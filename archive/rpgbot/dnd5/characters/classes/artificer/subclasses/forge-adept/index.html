Keith Baker’s Forge Adept Artificer Handbook: DnD 5e Subclass Guide

Keith Baker’s Forge Adept Artificer Handbook: DnD 5e Subclass Guide

Rocco Giusto December 16, 2022

Introduction

When Eberron was released, it included the Artificer and three subclasses for the new class. When Tasha’s Cauldron of Everything was later published, the Artificer and those subclasses were reprinted along with a new fourth subclass. It has remained as such since then: four official subclasses, the least of any class. But did you know that Keith Baker, the original author and creator of Eberron, published a third party supplement not long after Rising from the Last War? This book, Exploring Eberron , contains two new subclasses for the Artificer (as well as one each for Bard, Cleric, Druid and Monk).

In this handbook, we’ll be exploring the Forge Adept subclass for the Artificer, a frontline support combatant. This subclass is similar to the Battle Smith but gives up the go-go dog person sidekick for more personal power with a subclass exclusive Infusion, the Ghaal’Shaarat. While any Artificer can use the Enhance Weapon Infusion to get a magical weapon, this feature does not take up a daily Infusion slot for the Forge Adept.

One extra Infusion might not sound big, but avoiding the tax that a Battle Smith has to pay by Infusing a weapon to use Intelligence  to hit and damage is a big deal. We like to talk about Fundamental Math quite a lot around here, but we don’t usually explore just how much that math can be tilted sideways with even a +1 weapon. Assuming a four person party with three weapon users, a Forge Adept can get magic weapons on all of them from third level.

The math is a little messy, but at lower levels the DPR increase from those weapons is ever so slightly higher than the average +2.5 attack bonus increase from Bless because of the +1 to both hit and damage. With enough other added damage the calculation does swing back into Bless’ favor, but by the time that happens it might be reasonable to spend slots on Bless throughout an adventuring day. Not like anything is stopping you from stacking the pair either.

Disclaimer

RPGBOT uses the color coding scheme which has become common among Pathfinder build handbooks, which is simple to understand and easy to read at a glance.

  • Red : Bad, useless options, or options which are extremely situational. Nearly never useful.
  • Orange : OK options, or useful options that only apply in rare circumstances. Useful sometimes.
  • Green : Good options. Useful often.
  • Blue : Fantastic options, often essential to the function of your character. Useful very frequently.

We will not include 3rd-party content, including content from DMs Guild, in handbooks for official content because we can’t assume that your game will allow 3rd-party content or homebrew. We also won’t cover Unearthed Arcana content because it’s not finalized, and we can’t guarantee that it will be available to you in your games.

The advice offered below is based on the current State of the 2014 DnD 5e Character Optimization Meta as of when the article was last updated. Keep in mind that the state of the meta periodically changes as new source materials are released, and the article will be updated accordingly as time allows.

Forge Adept Features

  1. Tool Proficiency : Smith’s tools are alright, and you even get to pick any other tool if you already have Smith’s proficiency. There are plenty of nice tools to choose from so you’ll find something useful here.
  2. Forge Adept Spells : A good mix of Paladin and Cleric spells, with a few Warlock exclusives mixed in as well. These spells lean onto the defensive and supportive side of things, with a few damage options thrown in. This pairs nicely with other Forge Adept features that provide damage elsewhere.
    • 3rd Level : A pair of defensive spells that work well for us, providing either Temporary Hit Points or an increase to AC. These spells would rank higher, but our half slot progression inhibits our ability to upcast.
    • 5th Level : These spells are ranked orange not because they are bad spells but because they are somewhat more situational options. We could get some mileage out of the damage spell, but it eats up our bonus action for the duration and by this level the damage output is already starting to lag.
    • 9th Level : More defensive and restorative spells. In particular, one of these buffs makes us a healer’s best friend, not only for the effect it brings but also that they don’t have to be the one Concentrating on it.
    • 13th Level : A couple of non-Concentration longer duration defensive buffs, the spells at this level are nice but we have so few high-level spell slots. Like before, these are not bad spells, they are just harder to justify using.
    • 17th level : We come to a pair of very strong control options that work well even at this level. It helps that one of these spells is a 5th-level Paladin spell and those were all balanced around being only available at higher levels.
  3. Battle Ready : Exactly the same as the Battle Smith feature of the same name. As long as your weapon is magical, you can use Intelligence instead of Strength or Dexterity. This opens up plenty of possibilities by itself, but it pairs really well with the other 3rd level Forge Adept feature.
  4. Ghaal’Shaarat : Goblin for “mighty blade.” Put very simply, Ghaal’Shaarat is a special Infusion available to Forge Adepts that gives a weapon a scaling +X bonus as well as the Returning property if it happens to be a Thrown weapon. Where it differs from other Infusions is that it does not count against our Infusion limit and it only functions when held by the Forge Adept. This feature means we don’t sacrifice one of our Infusion choices every day just so Battle Ready can function. Because this eventually reaches +3, which is the value we use when talking about Advantage, it’s somewhat on par with a Reckless Raging Barbarian who can still cast spells.
  5. Extra Attack : Because a Forge Adept focuses on fighting with their Ghaal’Shaarat, extra attack is very helpful. Unlike the Battle Smith, the Forge Adept does gain on-hit damage bonuses, but we still have to wait till we’re 9th level to get the first one.
  6. Runes of War : AoE Elemental Weapons. While it does use our Concentration, this is a powerful effect in many parties and usable often enough to be available in most fights for the average adventuring day. This is more effective as your party makes additional weapon attacks, so in parties with classes like fighters, monks, and rangers this is great. In parties which rely on big single attacks (rogues, most casters) you will see less benefit.
  7. Perfect Weapon : This has two features. The first benefit is like a reverse Reckless Attack, taking a penalty to our offense to boost our defense. Players of 3.x may recognize this as the Defending magic weapon property or Combat Expertise. The second benefit is one of two persistent upgrades to the Ghaal’Shaarat that can be changed every morning after a long rest, one defensive and one offensive. Both are welcome additions that help keep us relevant at this level of combat.

Forge Adept Ability Scores

The Forge Adept doesn’t stray from the typical spread laid out in the Artificer Handbook . Thanks to Battle Ready we can focus on Intelligence for weapon attacks as well as Spell DCs.

Str : We don’t need this for anything.

Dex : We’re proficient with Medium armor and shields so we shouldn’t need more than 14.

Con : We’re blessed to have proficiency with this saving throw, but we still want to have hit points.

Int : This is our “do everything” ability score. It does everything.

Wis : Good for some skills and this kind of saving throw is dangerous to fail.

Cha : We’re not going to be the face of the team.

Forge Adept Races

For anything else not listed here, refer to the Artificer Race Breakdown .

  • Variant Human PHB : An extra feat goes a long way if you’re trying to get all the damage you can out of your attacks. Polearm Master and Crossbow Expert are stronger the sooner they are available, as are most feats.
  • Elves (and Eladrin, Shadar-Kai, Half Elves, Etc.) PHB, MMoM : I guess it would be odd if I forgot to point out that Battle Ready pairs incredibly well with Elven Accuracy .

Forge Adept Feats

For anything not listed here, please check the Artificer Handbook .

  • Elven Accuracy XGtE : It’s only green because not every Forge Adept is an Elf. Otherwise it would be Blue.
  • Great Weapon Master : The Ghaal’Shaarat scales pretty well, hitting +3 at 13th level. This can offset the -5 to-hit penalty quite well.

Forge Adept Weapons

Also check out the Artificer Handbook for anything not listed here .

  • Dart : The Ghaal’Shaarat does mimic the Returning Weapon Infusion and not the Repeating Weapon Infusion so in the narrow situation where you want to wear a shield and use the Sharpshooter feat at the same time, a Dart is your best option.
  • Longbow : Using the Bracers of Archery Infusion alongside the Ghaal’Shaarat is quite a lot of damage.

Forge Adept Armor

The advice in the Artificer Handbook is accurate for the Forge Adept.

Example Forge Adept Build – Arsenal

The example build presented below features 3rd-party content from author Keith Baker’s Exploring Eberron as the primary focus of the build. In addition, we’ll be using a feat from Keith’s more recent book Chronicles of Eberron . We don’t need to use that feat, but it’s fun and works for us. For argument’s sake, we’ll call out a replacement for the feat in case you either can’t use or do not have Chronicles of Eberron .

Abilities

We’ll prioritize Intelligence and Constitution, with Dexterity close behind. With leftover points we can bump our Wisdom up a tiny bit. Thanks to being a Half-Elf, we get to use their special +2/+1/+1 ability spread.

Race

We’re going with Half-Elf for the +2/+1/+1 ability spread as well as an extra two skill proficiencies. We’ll take Religion and Acrobatics.

Background

We’re taking the Sage background for the Arcana and History skills.

Skills and Tools

From Artificer we’ll gain Jeweler’s Tools, Tinker’s Tools, and Thieves’ Tools. We’ll also gain Investigation and Nature skills. Then from Forge Adept we’ll gain Smith’s Tools.

Feats

At fourth level, we’ll take the Psionic Egoist feat from Chronicles of Eberron, adding +1 Intelligence. If you’re unable to take the feat, Elven Accuracy is also fine.

At eighth level, we take Great Weapon Master. Thanks to the Ghaal’Shaarat, we’re already ahead of the Fundamental Math .

At twelfth level, we take an ASI for +2 Intelligence, putting it at 20 Intelligence.

At sixteenth level, we take an ASI for +2 Constitution, putting it at 18 Constitution.

At nineteenth level, we take an ASI for +2 Constitution, putting it at 20 Constitution.
