          Wild Talents<\/h2>\n\n

          Kinetic Blasts<\/h3>\n\n\n

          Your choices of blasts are extremely limited. You get one a 1st level, and you don’t get another until 7th level. The choice between an energy blast and physical blast is an important strategic choice. If you go for energy blasts, you’ll face increasingly common energy resistances after low levels, but you get to make ranged touch attacks, largely guaranteeing that you will hit afte rlow levels. If you go for physical, you’ll do more damage, but you’ll face damage reduction which you may be unable to bypass since “magic” weapons don’t bypass things like DR\/adamantine, and you need to make regular ranged attacks instead of ranged touch attacks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Air Blast<\/span> (Sp): Only deals bludgeoning\n\tdamage, so it can’t compete with Earth Blast or Telekinetic Blast. Regular\n\tranged attack.<\/p>\n

          Cold Blast<\/span> (Sp): Cold damage is fine, but\n\tit’s resisted by many creature types. Ranged touch attack.<\/p>\n

          Earth Blast<\/span> (Sp): You can choose among any\n\tof the three physical damage types, allowing you to bypass many forms of\n\tdamage reduction with ease. It’s the best basic physical blast, but it’s also\n\ta regular ranged attack, so it’s not as reliable as an energy blast.<\/p>\n

          Electric Blast<\/span> (Sp): The least commonly\n\tresisted type of energy blast. Ranged touch attack.<\/p>\n

          Fire Blast<\/span> (Sp): Fire is the most commonly\n\tresisted type of energy damage. However, it’s also extra effective against\n\tplants and it affects wooden objects easily. Ranged touch attack.<\/p>\n

          Telekinetic Blast<\/span> (Sp): The most complex\n\tof kinetic blasts. The basic usage is functionally identical to Earth Blast\n\texcept that you need to launch a pre-existing object (oh look, convenient\n\trocks on the ground). The complex usage of the blast involves real weapons.\n\tHowever, this method is largely useless. It allows you to use the special\n\tproperties of weapons, which could be helpful for bypassing DR, but by the\n\ttime this would be useful you will deal more damage using the basic version of\n\tthe blast and suffering the damage reduction. Either way, it’s still a regular\n\tranged attack so it won’t be as reliable as an energy blast.<\/p>\n

          Water Blast<\/span> (Sp): Essentially identical to\n\tair blast, but with water. Regular ranged attack.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Composite Blasts<\/h3>\n\n\n

          At 7th level you get access to composite blasts, which double your damage dice (and the +x bonus for physical blasts). However, most composite blasts also have a Burn cost. Until you pick up Supercharge or Composite Specialization, there’s no good way to get around this Burn cost without pointlessly wasting turns on Gather Power, so your Composite Blasts represent more of an occasional spike in damage output and less of a spammable option than your Basic Blasts. Unfortunately, almost all of the composite blasts are physical, so your options are extremely limited if you want to stick to reliable ranged touch attacks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Some of the blasts do multiple damage types (like Blizzard Blast dealing cold and piercing). Since this damage is split between the two types, you may suffer twice as many issues with damage reduction and energy resistance. However, it also means that you won’t be shut down by a single damage immunity.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Aetheric Boost<\/span> (Sp): The bonus damage is\n\tfar too small to justify two points of burn. In theory this is a way to keep\n\tthe utility of whatever Basic Blasts you’ve chosen while boosting their\n\tdamage. In reality, your choice of Composite Blasts is limited based on your\n\tBasic Blasts, so you’re still dealing the same types of damage. Even when you\n\tget the 15th-level advancement, the Burn cost may still be too high to justify\n\tusing this frequently.<\/p>\n

          Blizzard Blast<\/span> (Sp): There is no reason for\n\tthis to exist. It has identical effects to Ice Blast but worse\n\tprerequisites.<\/p>\n

          Blue Flame Blast<\/span> (Sp): It’s literally the\n\tsame as Fire Blast, but with more damage. It’s really boring, and doesn’t add\n\tany of the versatility that you’ll sorely need to deal with the fact that fire\n\tis the most commonly resisted energy type. Ranged touch attack.<\/p>\n

          Charged Water Blast<\/span> (Sp): Half bludgeoning,\n\thalf electricity. A fun combination of damage types, but it’s a physical\n\tblast, so it’s a regular ranged attack.<\/p>\n

          Force Blast<\/span> (Sp): Force damage is amazing,\n\tbut this only does as much damage as a basic blast. You need the extra damage\n\toutput more than you need to be able to hit ghosts. Ranged touch attack.<\/p>\n

          Ice Blast<\/span> (Sp): Identical to Blizzard Blast,\n\tbut you don’t also need Air Blast to use it. Half cold, half piercing.\n\tSkeletons will be a serious problem, and it’s still a regular ranged\n\tattack.<\/p>\n

          Magma Blast<\/span> (Sp): Half bludgeoning, half\n\tfire. A fine combination of damage types, but it’s still a regular ranged\n\tattack.<\/p>\n

          Metal Blast<\/span> (Sp): Any of the three\n\tphysical damage types. Regular ranged attack.<\/p>\n

          Mud Blast<\/span> (Sp): Only bludgeoning\n\tdamage.Regular ranged attack.<\/p>\n

          Plasma Blast<\/span> (Sp): Half bludgeoning, half\n\tfire. Functionally identical to Magma Blast. Regular ranged attack.<\/p>\n

          Sandstorm Blast<\/span> (Sp): Piercing\n\tand<\/em> slashing means that it’s automatically treated as the most\n\teffective of the two. Unfortunately, it’s still a regular ranged attack<\/p>\n

          Steam Blast<\/span> (Sp): Half bludgeoning, half\n\tfire. Functionally identical to Magma Blast. Regular ranged attack.<\/p>\n

          Thunderstorm Blast<\/span> (Sp): Half bludgeoning,\n\thalf electricity. Functionally identical to Charged Water Blast. Regular\n\tranged attack.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Defense Wild Talents<\/h3>\n\n\n

          You only get one of these most of the time, and it’s a crucial defense on top\n\tof your armor class.<\/p>\n

          Enveloping Winds<\/span> (Su): Once you pick up\n\tWings of Air this becomes very useful. Enemies shouldn’t be able to reach you,\n\texcept with ranged attacks, spells, or considerable effort. Of course, this\n\toffers no protection against melee attacks and non-ray spells.<\/p>\n

          Flesh of Stone<\/span> (Su): DR\/Adamantine is among\n\tthe best forms of DR in the game. Almost nothing bypasses it except Iron\n\tGolems and manufactured weapons, so it should work nearly any time you’re\n\tattacked.<\/p>\n

          Force Ward<\/span> (Su): Even better than normal\n\ttemporary hit points, and they regenerate fast enough that you can easily\n\trecover all of them between fights without needing to waste Burn to accelerate\n\tthe recharge rate. You get to treat hits as misses if they don’t pierce your\n\tbubble, which is especially useful against things like poison. You can also\n\trapidly refill the pool any time you accept Burn, so you can reliably get\n\tthrough an entire day without taking a serious amount of actual damage\n\tprovided that you manage your bubble well.<\/p>\n

          Searing Flesh<\/span> (Su): Kineticists usually do\n\tnot do well in melee, and this is only useful if you’re getting hit or\n\tgrappling. You should be avoiding being hit, and you should probably never be\n\tgrappling.<\/p>\n

          Shroud of Water<\/span> (Su): Pick the shield\n\toption. It’s basically a free animated heavy shield with no armor check\n\tpenalty. The armor bonus option can be easily and inexpensively replicated\n\twith a modestly enhanced chain shirt, and you can massively outpace the bonus\n\tif you pick up heavier armor.<\/p>\n

          Infusion Wild Talents<\/h3>\n

          Infusions are what make your blasts interesting. Remember that you can\n\tcombine one form infusion with one substance infusion, though the Burn cost\n\tfor doing so many be difficult until you have Infusion Specialization.<\/p>\n

          This section is organized by effective spell level to help selecting the best\n\toptions at each spell level. Remember that Infusions may be limited to certain\n\telements, and they only work with the specified “Associated Blasts”, so your\n\toptions may be more limited than you think. Be sure to thoroughly check the\n\toriginal entry for any Infusion you’re considering.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Spell Level 1<\/h4>\n\n\n

          Burning Infusion<\/span>: An excellent boost to\n\tyour damage output, especially at low levels, setting a creature on fire can\n\tbe a death sentence. The target is forced to waste a turn extinguishing itself\n\tonly for you to set it on fire again the next turn, or it can continue to\n\tslowly burn to death.<\/p>\n

          Draining Infusion<\/span>: A fantastic way to\n\tovercome damage resistances if you went for energy blasts. You deal full\n\tdamage (though I think this may be an error), or 1\/4 damage if the target\n\tsuccessfully saves (which is why I think that the full damage is an error). On\n\ttop of that, you get a free point of Burn to spend on the following turn.\n\tCombined with Gather Power you could get two free points of Burn on the\n\tfollowing turn, or 4 once you pick up Supercharge. Unfortunately, it requires\n\tthat the target have the subtype of your energy damage, which means that many\n\tcreatures with resistances, including those with resistances from spells,\n\twon’t be affected.<\/p>\n

          Extended Range<\/span>: As a class focused very\n\theavily on ranged combat, additional range is excellent. When you have the\n\tspace, stay as far away as you can manage. Use Gather Power to offset the Burn\n\tcost and wear your foes down from afar.<\/p>\n

          Fan of Flames<\/span>: Cones are great low-level\n\tAOEs, but at 15 feet you’re not going to hit more than one or two creatures at\n\tmost. If you pick this infusion, retrain it as soon as possible.<\/p>\n

          Gusting Infusion<\/span>: Too situational.<\/p>\n

          Kinetic Blade<\/span>: Tempting, but risky. You\n\tcan make a full attack, potentially allowing you to apply your blast multiple\n\ttimes in one turn. However, with 2\/3 BAB this will take quite a while to get\n\tadditional attacks. You can’t use this with two-weapon fighting because the\n\tInfusion specifies that you create “a<\/em> light or one-handed weapon”, so\n\tyou can’t create two weapons or a double weapon. Elemental Overflow will grant\n\ta crucial bonus to your attack rolls, but you don’t get the damage bonus.<\/p>\n

          Kinetic Fist<\/span>: Kinetic Fist provides\n\tadditional damage on top of your unarmed strike damage. Since kineticists\n\tdon’t have built-in unarmed strike bonus, this is objectively worse than\n\tKinetic Blade for a vanilla kineticist. If you want to use this, take the\n\tElemental Ascetic archetype. You’ll get it for free, its burn cost will be\n\tremoved, and its effectiveness will be improved.<\/p>\n

          Pushing Infusion<\/span>: You’re still stuck using\n\tyour 2\/3 BAB, and the 5-foot push limit without spending additional Burn is\n\tabsurd.<\/p>\n

          Quenching Infusion<\/span>: Too situational.<\/p>\n

          Thundering Infusion<\/span>: Too situational.<\/p>\n

          Spell Level 2<\/h4>\n

          Bowling Infusion<\/span>: Tripping an enemy can be\n\tvery useful if you have melee allies, but you’re bad at it. With only 2\/3 BAB\n\tyou can’t compete with a fighter. You do get to use your Constitution bonus,\n\twhich is nice, but it’s still not enough.<\/p>\n

          Entangling Infusion<\/span>: An excellent\n\tcrowd-control effect with a good duration, especially if you combine it with a\n\tForm infusion that makes your blast an AOE.<\/p>\n

          Spell Level 3<\/h4>\n

          Eruption<\/span>: The AOE is excellent, but the\n\tdamage is halved for physical blasts.<\/p>\n

          Extreme Range<\/span>: Fights rarely take place at\n\tdistances great enough that this will be useful. The Burn cost is low enough\n\tthat with Infusion Specialization 1 and Gather Power you can use this every\n\tround at no Burn cost.<\/p>\n

          Flurry of Blasts<\/span>: Do not combine this with\n\tComposite Blasts: it’s a huge waste of Burn. This is a hard infusion to use.\n\tIt sets your effective level to 1st for damage purposes, so your best bet is\n\tto use a physical blast to deal 1d6+1+Con damage to each target. If you hit\n\tthe same target you don’t get the +1 or your Constitution bonus, so you’ll\n\tdeal 2d6+1+Con total with two hits. Unless you’re trying to get around spell\n\tresistance or trying to force the target to fail a save, an AOE Infusion would\n\tbe a much better way to handle multiple targets.<\/p>\n

          Foe Throw<\/span>: Damage two targets, knock one of\n\tthem prone and<\/em> reposition it. This can be very powerful if you’re\n\tclever. However, it allows a save and<\/em> requires an attack roll so you\n\thave two chances to fail, and if you miss the attack roll the target has a lot\n\tof flexibility to position themselves.<\/p>\n

          Force Hook<\/span>: Melee is very hard for\n\tkineticists, but this is a great way to move around.<\/p>\n

          Impale<\/span>: Functinally a line AOE, but you\n\tneed to successfully attack and damage everything in the line to get the full\n\teffect.\n<\/p>\n

          Kinetic Whip<\/span>: If you’re building for melee,\n\tthis is a must. By the time you can pick it up, you have Infusion\n\tSpecialization 2 so the Burn cost is negligible, allowing you to make full\n\tattacks with two attacks at no Burn cost. The reach doesn’t prevent you from\n\tattacking adjacent enemies, so there’s little reason to use Kinetic Blade\n\tunless you want to mix in other Infusions.<\/p>\n

          Magnetic Infusion<\/span>: If you have melee allies,\n\ta +4 bonus is extremely significant. The bonus is untyped, too. This is an\n\texcellent go-to Infusion option if you don’t need anything else because it’s\n\talmost always useful and the Burn cost is low enough that Geather Power and\n\tInfusion Specialization can offset the cost.<\/p>\n

          Mobile Blast<\/span>: This both increases your\n\tper-round damage output and provides a small area denial effect. However, it\n\thas some strict limitations. If you want infinite duration, you’re limited to\n\tunmodified Basic Blasts, and the damage will be poor. In addition, the move\n\taction usage will mean that you can’t rely on Gather Power to reduce your Burn\n\tcosts every round. Your default usage should be to create a Mobile Blast based\n\ton your Basic Blast and walk around with it all day. If you have a good reason\n\tto do so, spend some Burn to throw a Substance Infusion onto your Mobile Blast\n\tand use it for a few rounds. If you can hold enemies in place by tripping\n\tand\/or entangling them, you can set the Mobile Blast on top of them and deal a\n\thuge amount of damage.<\/p>\n

          Rare-Metal Infusion<\/span>: Critical for melee\n\tkineticists and for physical blast users. This allows you to bypass any DR\n\texcept DR\/- and aligned DR.<\/p>\n

          Snake<\/span>: Amusing, but too situational.<\/p>\n

          Torrent<\/span>: Unlike Impale, this is a real line\n\teffect. However, it deals half damage with physical blasts, so physical blast\n\tusers may want to use Impale instead.<\/p>\n

          Spell Level 4<\/h4>\n

          Cyclone<\/span>: Half damage, and you need to be\n\treally close to multiple enemies for this to be appealing.<\/p>\n

          Flash Infusion<\/span>: Blinding an enemy is\n\tmassively crippling. By the level you get this, you can use Gather Power and\n\tInfusion Specialist to reduce the Burn cost to 0, allowing you to use this\n\tevery round. You won’t be able to use a Form Infusion for free until you get\n\tInfusion Specialist 3 at 11th level, but blinding a single foe every round is\n\tan excellent use of your time, especially if you have other allies to\n\tcapitalize on the handicap.<\/p>\n

          Spray<\/span>: A 30-foot cone is considerably more\n\tmanageable than the 15-foot cone allowed by Fan of Flames.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Spell Level 5<\/h4>\n\n\n

          Chain<\/span>: Nothing about the blast rules\n\tdictates that your targets need to be creatures, so if you need to bridge a\n\tgap wider than 30 feet you can target a square or object in the intervening\n\tspace and “bounce” the chain closer to the target at the expense of a die of\n\tdamage. Because you’re attacking in a chain, this is a great way to affect\n\tmultiple foes without worrying about things like hitting your allies.<\/p>\n

          Chilling Infusion<\/span>: A fantastic debuff for\n\tenemies that rely on full attacks, but often worthless against enemies that\n\trely on spells or special abilities.<\/p>\n

          Grappling Infusion<\/span>: This is fantastic,\n\tthough very difficult to use. You need to combine this with one of the three\n\tlisted Form infusions, making the Burn cost high, and the Form infusions are\n\tall at least as high level as Grappling Infusion so you’ll probably need to\n\tspend a feat on Extra Wild Talent to pick up Wall if you want to use this as\n\tsoon as it’s available. If you can get over the hurdles it takes to bring this\n\tonline, it’s absolutely amazing. You use your class level and Constitution\n\tinstead of your 2\/3 BAB and your Strength, and the Blast is treated as a Huge\n\tcreature, making your total CMB very impressive. You don’t get to apply\n\tSearing Flesh because the blast is doing the actual grappling, but you could\n\tin theory jump into the grapple yourself if you wanted to do so. Grappling the\n\ttarget would also allow you to plant Mobile Blast on top of them for easy\n\tdamage.<\/p>\n

          Unraveling Infusion<\/span>: A weird way to add\n\tutility to your blast. Even out of combat, this is really fantastic. Need to\n\tget through ongoing magical effects? Stand around and blast them until you\n\tmanage to pass the caster level check. Out of combat you can afford to use\n\tGather Power for a full turn to allow yourself a bunch of free Burn. In combat\n\tyou’ll likely reserve this for occasional use against spellcasters and their\n\tallies, but in those cases it’s pretty fantastic.<\/p>\n

          Wall<\/span>: AOE, area control, and you can add in\n\tSubstance Infusions to make the effect really scary. Grappling Infusion is an\n\tobvious combo, but there’s lots of room for creativity. You’ll want to use an\n\tenergy blast for the most damage, of course. A lot of people have trouble\n\tgetting their head around the placement for effects like this because of the\n\t“appears within 30 feet” wording. You can’t place a 120 ft. line within a 60\n\tft. diameter circle. It’s not possible. The description of the effect doesn’t\n\tsay that the wall can change direction, so I would assume that it can’t. The\n\tway I read it, you only need to have one square of the wall’s area within the\n\t30 ft. range. A cautious GM might reasonably require that the middle square of\n\tthe wall is used to place the effect, which seems like a reasonable\n\tcompromise.<\/p>\n

          Spell Level 6<\/h4>\n

          Brilliant Infusion<\/span>: Extremely situational,\n\tand the Burn cost is ridiculous. Since it’s effectively a 6th-level spell it\n\twill overcome most magical Darkness effects, but they’re rare enough that you\n\tprobably can’t justify spending one of your extremely limited Infusion\n\tslots.<\/p>\n

          Deadly Earth<\/span>: A decent AOE, good range, and\n\tit causes difficult terrain. The 1\/4 damage is disappointing, but it doesn’t\n\tallow a save. If you can force enemies to remain in the area, you can brings\n\tfoes down very quickly.<\/p>\n

          Disintegrating Infusion<\/span>: Force Blast’s\n\tbiggest problem is that it does half as much damage as other Composite Blasts.\n\tThis brings its damage in line with other Composite Blasts and adds the\n\tnon-damage benefits of the Disintegrate spell. However, the Burn cost is\n\textremely high and it allows a save to reduce the damage.<\/p>\n

          Spell Level 7<\/h4>\n

          Cloud<\/span>: The same radius as Deadly Earth, but\n\tit’s spherical, can be placed in the air, and does more damage to creatures\n\tstuck inside the effect. It lacks the difficult terrain component, but it also\n\tblocks line of sight. Combine this with Deadly Earth, and you can do an absurd\n\tamount of damage to foes who will have a lot of trouble doing anything about\n\tit.<\/p>\n

          Explosion<\/span>: No frills, no complications. Just\n\tan AOE Blast with a Reflex save for half damage. Allowing you to alter the AOE\n\tmeans that you can select the size you need to hit as many foes and as few\n\tallies as possible.<\/p>\n

          Fragmentation<\/span>: If you want a 20-foot AOE,\n\tExplosion will cover that need. Adding Fragmentation’s attack mechanic\n\tintroduces an additional point of failure and reduces the potential damage you\n\tcould deal.<\/p>\n

          Pure-Flame Infusion<\/span>: You need to take Spell\n\tPenetration. If you take Spell Pentration and Greater Spell Penetration, you\n\tshould be reliable enoguh that this is a massive waste of resources.<\/p>\n

          Spell Level 8<\/h4>\n

          Many Throw<\/span>: This only works with your basic\n\tTelekinetic Blast, which is unfortunate, but it’s still pretty good. Aether\n\thas almost no area effect options, so this is a must.<\/p>\n

          Utility Wild Talents<\/h3>\n

          A lot of the Kineticist is built around their Blast abilities. In fact,\n\teverything on this page until this point is about using Blasts to kill stuff.\n\tWhile blasts are neat, many problems can’t be solved with damage. That’s where\n\tutilities come in.<\/p>\n

          This section is organized by effective spell level to help selecting the best\n\toptions at each spell level. You get a talent at every even numbered class\n\tlevel, so expect to have one talent of each spell level, plus your “basic”\n\tutility talent(s).<\/p>\n\n\n

          Spell Level 1<\/h4>\n\n\n

          Aerial Adaptation<\/span>: Altitude sickness almost\n\tnever comes up in a campaign, but electricity resistance is nice.<\/p>\n

          Air Cushion<\/span>: Falling damage is an annoyance\n\tat any level, and this makes you permanently immune to it. It’s also a\n\tprerequisite for Wings of Air.<\/p>\n

          Air Shroud<\/span>: Unless your campaign involves a\n\tlot of time underwater, this is too situational.<\/p>\n

          Air’s Leap<\/span>: Potions of Jump cost 50gp and\n\tprovide a much better bonus. Alternatively, get Wings of Air.<\/p>\n

          Air’s Reach<\/span>: The 30-foot range on blasts\n\tcan be a problem, and locking yourself into Form Infusions which extend range\n\tgreatly limits your options. 60 ft. range is enough to keep you relatively\n\tsafe from enemies at no Burn cost. However, this requires that you use blasts\n\twhich include Air, so your options may be limited if you have access to\n\tmultiple elements.<\/p>\n

          Basic Aerokinesis<\/span>: Both options are very\n\tsituational, though I admittedly love the idea of having your hair constantly\n\tblowing in the wind despite a complete lack of actual wind.<\/p>\n

          Basic Geokinesis<\/span>: Situational. If you pick\n\tup Seismic Master, you can use this to unbury things you kill so that you can\n\tloot them.<\/p>\n

          Basic Hydrokinesis<\/span>: Situational, but the\n\tability to create water at will can be extremely useful if you’re clever.<\/p>\n

          Basic Pryokinesis<\/span>: Light is extremely\n\tuseful, but the fact that this version produces heat may actually make it\n\tworse. No more casting light on your hat unless you enjoy the smell of burning\n\thair.<\/p>\n

          Basic Telekinesis<\/span>: Mage hand is great, and\n\tthis adds some interesting uses and a scaling weight limit. This is the only\n\tbasic utility that scales, too.<\/p>\n

          Cold Adaptation<\/span>: Crucial in cold climates,\n\tand cold resistance is really nice.<\/p>\n
