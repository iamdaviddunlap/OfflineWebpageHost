Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Back in 3rd edition DnD, the Sorcerer was a twist on the Wizard, trading the\n\tWizard’s spellbook for the equivalent of a Spell Repertoire. With a well-curated Spell Repertoire and access to a spell\n\tlist of your choice, the Sorcerer is a diverse, capable, and exciting class\n\tcapable of filling any spellcasting-based role in a party.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Sorcerer Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Key Ability<\/strong>: Charisma is the Sorcerer’s Key Ability\n\tScore.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Hit Points<\/span>: 6+Con hit points. Fortunately you\n\thave plenty of room to boost your ability scores so your Constitution bonus\n\tcan compensate.<\/p>\n

            Initial Proficiencies<\/span>: The Sorcerer’s\n\tproficiencies are terrible. Except to get by on spellcasting and charm.<\/p>\n

              \n\t
            • Perception<\/span>: Start at Trained, and one of\n\t\tthe worst Perception proficiency progressions in the same.<\/li>\n\t
            • Saving Throws<\/span>: Your best save is Will,\n\t\tand the Sorcerer’s Will save progression is still below average.<\/li>\n\t
            • Skills<\/span>: One or more skills from your\n\t\tBloodline, plus 2+int Trained skills at 1st level, for a total of at least\n\t\t3+. That’s standard, but with no other need for Intelligence you may find\n\t\tthat you’re short on Skill Increases. Every bloodline in the Core Rulebook\n\t\tprovides two, so it’s likely that you’ll get 4+ unless Paizo comes up with\n\t\tbloodlines that don’t get two skills.<\/li>\n\t
            • Attacks<\/span>: Only Simple weapons, and among the\n\t\tworst proficiency progressions in the game. But you’re going to use spells,\n\t\tso it’s no great loss.<\/li>\n\t
            • Defenses<\/span>: No armor, and the worst unarmored\n\t\tdefense progression in the game.<\/li>\n\t
            • Spells<\/strong>: Standard progression for every full spellcaster\n\t\twith the exception of the Warpriest Cleric.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

              Bloodline<\/strong>: See my\n\t\tSorcerer Bloodlines Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Sorcerer Spellcasting<\/strong>: The Sorcerer’s spellcasting is unique in several ways. The Sorcerer gets one more leveled spell slot and one more spell known of each level than other full spellcasters at each spell level except 10th. Considering that most spellcasters get just 3 spell slots at each spell level, that’s fully a third more spells than anyone else.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                \n\t
              • Heightened Spells<\/span>: Heightening spells is\n\t\tan important mechanic in Pathfinder 2e. Many spells scale them. Since sorcerers\n\t\tuse a Spell Repertoire, you need to learn spells at multiple levels to\n\t\thighten them in most cases, though Signature Spells are an exception.<\/li>\n\t
              • Cantrips<\/span>: Fantastic every time, on any\n\t\tcharacter, in any amount. Sorcerers learn 5 cantrips at level 1 and never\n\t\tget more, but you can replace them by retraining or when you gain a level so\n\t\tyou’re not locked into whatever you pick at first level.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n

                Spell Repertoire<\/span>: Spell Repertoires are\n\tless versatile than spellcasting like the Cleric’s or the Druid’s. You know a\n\tfixed number of spells which grows as you gain levels, and while you don’t\n\tneed to prepare your spells ahead of time you also lose the flexibility of\n\tchanging your spells every day. You also need to learn the same spells as\n\tmultiple levels if you plan to heighten them, which is a difficult cost to\n\tpay. Fortunately, the Sorcerer’s Signature Spells feature (see below) allows you\n\tto heighten some of your spells without learning them multiple times.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Skill Feats<\/strong>: Standard for everyone except the Rogue.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Sorcerer Feats<\/strong>: See Sorcerer feats, below.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                General Feats<\/strong>: Standard.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Signature Spells<\/span>: A powerful and important\n\tfeature, this allows you to heighten a small set of spells without learning\n\tthem multiple times. As their name suggests, these spells will be your go-to\n\toptions in most situations, and choosing good spells will be absoutely\n\tcrucial.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Skill Increases<\/strong>: Standard for everyone except the Rogue.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Ability Boosts<\/strong>: Standard.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Ancestry Feats<\/strong>: Standard.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Magical Fortitude<\/span>: More saves is always\n\tgreat, but this is as high as you ever get.<\/p>\n

                Expert Spellcaster<\/span>: Standard and essential\n\tfor spellcasters.<\/p>\n

                Lightning Reflexes<\/span>: More saves is always\n\tgreat, but this is as high as you ever get.<\/p>\n

                Alertness<\/span>: Perception is absolutely\n\tcrucial, but this is the best you will ever get without spending General\n\tFeats.<\/p>\n

                Simple Weapon Expertise<\/span>: You should never\n\tneed this.<\/p>\n

                Defensive Robes<\/span>: More AC is great, but this\n\tis the best you will ever get.<\/p>\n

                Weapon Specialization<\/span>: You should never need\n\tthis.<\/p>\n

                Master Spellcaster<\/span>: Standard and essential\n\tfor spellcasters.<\/p>\n

                Bloodline Paragon<\/span>: 10th-level spells are\n\tcrazy. You can spend a class feat to get another, but it’s unlikely that\n\tyou’ll ever cast more than 2 10th-level spells in a day.<\/p>\n

                Legendary Spellcaster<\/span>: Standard and\n\tessential for spellcasters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                Str<\/span>: The Sorcerer has no meaningful use for\n\tStrenght. Dump it to 8.<\/p>\n

                Dex<\/span>: With no armor proficiencies and poor\n\tReflex saves, Dexterity is important if you want to survive.<\/p>\n

                Con<\/span>: Sorcerers get just 6+ hit points and\n\thave poor Fortitude saves. Constitution is essential.<\/p>\n

                Int<\/span>: Sorcerers get 3+ (realisitically 4+)\n\tTrained skills, so a little Intelligence helps if your party needs more\n\tskills.<\/p>\n

                Wis<\/span>: Perception and Will saves.<\/p>\n

                Cha<\/span>: Your Key Ability Score. Charisma will\n\tdefine what you do both in and out of combat, including both your spells and\n\tyour most viable skills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

The Sorcerer is also uniquely suited\n\tto explore innate spellcasting due to their ability to pick their own spell\n\tlist and becuase the Sorcerer is Charisma-based, making innate spellcasting\n\tessentially an extension of their class-based spellcasting.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Human<\/a>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Easy to fit to any build. The Adapted Cantrip feat chain is a great way to\n\tget some extra spellcasting, and Multitalented can help you multiclass into\n\tother classes to expand your spellcasting.<\/p>\n

                  Skills and Skill Feats<\/h2>\n\n\n

                  You get Skill Increases at 3rd and 5th level to raise skills to Expert, increases at 7th, 9th, 11th, and 13th level to raise skills to Master, and increases at 15th, 17th, and 19th level to raise skills to Legendary. That means that you can maximize at most three skills, and the rest of your skills might not advance beyond Trained.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  You get Skill Feats at even-numbered levels, giving you a total of 10 Skill Feats (and maybe another from your Background) by 20th level. Generally, you want to invest these feats in the same skills which you are choosing to maximize, though in some cases you may want to grab feats from skills which don’t require that you be more than Trained.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    \n\t
                  • Acrobatics<\/span> (Dex): Surprisingly important\n\t\tbecause it’s used for maneuvering while flying.\n\t\t
                      \n\t\t\t
                    • Cat Fall<\/span>: Being knocked prone while\n\t\t\t\tflying is an easy way to counter flying creatures, and enemies can do it\n\t\t\t\tjust as easily to you as you can do it to them. Cat Fall will reduce the\n\t\t\t\teffective distance you’ve fallen, allowing you to take less damage from\n\t\t\t\ta fall. However, the effects of Cat Fall scale based on your Proficiency\n\t\t\t\tlevel, so it may not be worth the skill feat unless you plan to increase\n\t\t\t\tyour proficiency in Acrobatics.<\/li>\n\t\t<\/ul>\n\t<\/li>\n\t
                    • Arcana<\/span> (Int): An essential knowledge\n\t\tskill, and it makes sense that the Sorcerer would be proficient, but without\n\t\thigh Intelligence you may not be very effective with it.\n\t\t
                        \n\t\t\t
                      • Arcane Sense<\/span>: Getting Detect Magic\n\t\t\t\tas a regular cantrip will be both easier and more effective. If you’re\n\t\t\t\thaving trouble fitting Detect Magic into your 5 cantrips, consider\n\t\t\t\tgetting more as innate spells from your Ancestry or take the Cantrip\n\t\t\t\tExpansion class feat.<\/li>\n\t\t<\/ul>\n\t<\/li>\n\t
                      • Athletics<\/span> (Str): You have little use for\n\t\tAthletics. Most problems which you might be forced to solved with Athletics\n\t\tcan be solved with spells.<\/li>\n\t
                      • Crafting<\/span> (Int): You don’t have the\n\t\tIntelligence to thrive with Crafting, and you won’t depend enough on items\n\t\tthat require repair to make Crafting necessary.<\/li>\n\t
                      • Deception<\/span> (Cha): An essential Face skill.\n\t\t
                          \n\t\t\t

                            \n\t\t\t
                          • Battlecry<\/span>: Demoralize for free when\n\t\t\t\tcombat starts. It might not be a good option if you’re hiding, but\n\t\t\t\totherwise it’s a free debuff at the beginning of every fight.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
                          • Intimidating Glare<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: A frustratingly large number of enemies won’t understand Common, so\n\t\t\t\tremoving the Auditory trait makes Demoralize much easier to use.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
                          • Terrified Retreat<\/span>: Counting on a\n\t\t\t\tcritical success is hard, but if your Charisma is very high it might\n\t\t\t\twork.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
