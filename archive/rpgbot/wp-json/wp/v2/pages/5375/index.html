Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Wizard Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Hit Points<\/span>: d6 hit points leaves you pretty\n\tsquishy, but you have a laundry list of defensive options to put in front of\n\tyour hit points. Because Transmuters spend a lot of time in melee while\n\tpolymorphed, you will need to spend some resources to address this issue.<\/p>\n

            Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: Your lack of base attack\n\tbonus hurts when you polymorph, so you become very dependent on your\n\tStrength\/Dexterity. You won’t lose additional attacks because BAB does not\n\tprovide extra attacks with natural weapons.<\/p>\n

            Saves<\/span>: Your only good save is Will, but\n\tbecause Wizards only “need” one ability, you can afford to invest some of your\n\tability score points in Dexterity and Constitution.<\/p>\n

            Proficiencies<\/span>: Wizards get very little in the\n\tway of proficiencies, but beyond very early levels you won’t need them. A\n\tlight crossbow is an excellent backup weapon for a Wizard, but stay away from\n\tBows unless you put points into Strength.<\/p>\n

            Skills<\/span>: Wizards only get 2+ skills, but you\n\tget every<\/em> knowledge skill as a class skill, and your high\n\tintelligence will give you plenty of skill points to throw around. Remember\n\tthat permanently increasing your intelligence will give you additional skill\n\tpoints retroactively.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

            Arcane Bond<\/span> (Ex or Sp): You have the option\n\tof a familiar or a bonded item, and both options have their merits. Keep in\n\tmind that you can’t use hand-held items while polymorphed because they meld\n\tinto your form, but your other items continue to function normally while\n\tpolymorphed.<\/p>\n

              \n\t
            • Familiar<\/span>: Always a solid choice, the\n\t\tfamiliar is an ever-present and ever-useful companion. Familiars can serve\n\t\tas scouts, assassins, couriers, assistants, and even meat shields in a\n\t\tpinch. Their passive bonuses to the wizard are roughly equal to a feat, and\n\t\tthe provide the benefits of Alertness when they are adjacent. Effectively, a\n\t\tfamiliar gives you two feats and a cool pet. Remember that you can use\n\t\tspells with a target of “You” on your familiar, which includes Polymorph\n\t\tspells. Polymorph your familiar into something useful and throw it around in\n\t\tcombat. Don’t forget that your familiar probably gets Weapon Finesse for\n\t\tfree, which they can continue to use while polymorphed.<\/li>\n\t
            • amulet<\/span>: Jewelry is nice an safe. It\n\t\tdoesn’t take up a hand, it’s hard to take from you in combat, and it’s a\n\t\tgood option for enchanting.<\/li>\n\t
            • ring<\/span>: The ring is really the gold standard\n\t\tof bonded items. It’s incredibly small, it’s easy to hide under a glove or\n\t\ton your person, it’s a very difficult target for sundering, and you can\n\t\tenchant is as per Enchant Ring without the feat. Magic rings have a lot of\n\t\tgreat options, but Enchant Ring is rarely worth the feat, so this gives you\n\t\tsome options to save some money.<\/li>\n\t
            • staff<\/span>: You generally can’t use it while\n\t\tpolymorphed.<\/li>\n\t
            • wand<\/span>: You generally can’t use it while\n\t\tpolymorphed.<\/li>\n\t
            • weapon<\/span>: You generally can’t use it while\n\t\tpolymorphed.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

              Arcane School<\/span>: You picked Transmutation. For\n\tinformation on other schools, see the\n\tWizard School Breakdown<\/a>\n\tFor information on the Transmutation school specifically, see below.<\/p>\n

              Cantrips<\/span>: Cantrips are amazing at every\n\tlevel. Prestidigitation is often called “minor wish”, and Detect Magic will\n\tnever stop being helpful. At low levels, Acid Splash provides easy, realiable\n\tdamage.<\/p>\n

              Scribe Scroll<\/span>: Scrolls are great for\n\twizards, and giving you the feat for free is nice. Use it to prepare scrolls\n\tof spells that you need infrequently so that you can cover all of your\n\tbases.<\/p>\n

              Bonus Feats<\/span>: 4 extra feats over the course\n\tof 20 levels isn’t game-breaking, but the Wizard would still be king if he\n\tdidn’t get any of these. The addition of\n\tArcane Discoveries<\/a>\n\tgives wizards a handful of unique and incredibly powerful options in place of\n\tmetamagic and item crafting feats.<\/p>\n

              Spells<\/span>: You have the best spell list in the\n\tgame, and every new book makes it better. Spells are why you play a wizard.<\/p>\n\n\n

              Transmutation School<\/h2>\n\n\n

              Physical Enhancement<\/span> (Su): The bonus is\n\tcertainly nice, and it greatly reduces the amount of money you need to spend\n\ton a belt. I would recommend constitution since your hit points are terrible,\n\tand transmuters spend a lot of time pretending to be a melee monster.<\/p>\n

              Telekinetic Fist<\/span> (Sp): Not spectacular\n\tcompared to similar school powers, but it’s bludgeoning damage so you don’t\n\thave to worry about energy resistance. It also provides you with a combat\n\toption at low levels before you can Polymorph.<\/p>\n

              Change Shape<\/span> (Sp): Some extra free\n\tshapeshifting. The duration is pretty short, so you’ll probably want to save\n\tthis for moving about as something with a special movement type, like flying\n\tacross a chasm or digging through the wall of a tunnel.<\/p>\n

              Enhancement School<\/span><\/h3>\n

              The Enhancement subschool suffers from duration issues. The buffs are too\n\tmediocre to justify their unbearably short durations.<\/p>\n

              Replacement Powers: Telekinetic Fist and Change Shape.<\/p>\n

              Augment<\/span> (Sp): The duration is pretty short\n\tuntil very high levels, but it can be nice to buff the fighter before you jump\n\tinto combat.<\/p>\n

              Perfection of Self<\/span> (Su): Sometimes you need\n\tto do a shit load of damage in one round. At those times, buff your strength a\n\twhole bunch and stomp on someone. This couples well with Polymorphing into\n\tsomething with Pounce.<\/p>\n

              Shapechange School<\/span><\/h3>\n

              The Shapechange subschool trades your low level attack option for the ability\n\tto gain additional natural attacks. This can be fantastic at high levels when\n\tyou can Polymorph, but it leaves you with very limited options until level\n\t5.<\/p>\n

              Replaced Power: Telekinetic Fist.<\/p>\n

              Battleshaping (Su)<\/span>: The real benefit here\n\tis using it while shapeshifted. Turn into a Dire Tiger and get a gore attack.\n\tLosing telekinetic fist hurts at low levels, especially due to your limited\n\tcombat options at low level, but this can do some great things for you once\n\tyou can Polymorph.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

                Because Polymorph augments your existing abilities instead of replacing them, it is very important to strike a balance between Intelligence and your physical ability scores. Your scores don’t need to be insanely high like a Fighter or a Rogue because they will be augmented, but every point helps.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  \n\t
                • Str<\/span>: Most Polymorph forms depend on\n\t\tStrength for attacks, damage, and combat maneuvers.<\/li>\n\t
                • Dex<\/span>: Important for saves, and for AC.\n\t\tBecause you will be a big scary target while Polymorphed, you need as much\n\t\tAC as you can get.<\/li>\n\t
                • Con<\/span>: Your hit points are garbage, and\n\t\tfortitude saves will be a problem for you. Being Polymorphed gives you\n\t\tadditional constitution, but if you fall out of Polymorph and your\n\t\tconstitution drops, you may die due to the sudden loss of maximum hit\n\t\tpoints, much in the same way raging Barbarians do.<\/li>\n\t
                • Int<\/span>: You get magic, skills, and a bonus to\n\t\tknowledge skills (which you will have a ton of). Because you need physical\n\t\tability scores you don’t need to maximize this, but it should probably still\n\t\tbe your highest ability score at start so that you can get all of your new\n\t\tspell levels as soon as possible.<\/li>\n\t
                • Wis<\/span>: You get high will saves, and more\n\t\tis nice, but you have too many other important abilities to heavily invest\n\t\tin Wisdom.<\/li>\n\t
                • Cha<\/span>: dump to 7 and keep your mouth shut\n\t\tuntil its time to throw some spells around.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                  Unlike most Wizards, Transmuters lose quite a bit by being middle-aged. Polymorph is a young person’s game.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                  Races<\/h2>\n\n\n

                  Bonuses to Intelligence are nice, but not strictly necessary. Improved saves, bonus physical stats, and defensive abilities are helpful.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  Dwarf<\/span>: Dwarves are a surprisingly good\n\tchoice for Transmuters. Bonus Constitution and Wisdom make them very durable,\n\tand a +2 on saves against spells is fantastic. Darkvision is always helpful,\n\tbut you lose it when you Polymorph.<\/p>\n

                  Elf<\/span>: Bonus intelligence is great, and the\n\tbonus to dexterity and penalty to constitution are roughly equal for our\n\tpurposes. The +2 bonus to penetrate spell resistance won’t come up much since\n\tPolymorph doesn’t deal with Spell Resistance. The other benefits are highly\n\tsituational, but certainly welcome.<\/p>\n

                  Gnome<\/span>: The bonus to Constitution is nice, but\n\tthe penalty to strength will hurt your damage. The other racial bonuses do\n\tbasically nothing for you.<\/p>\n

                  Half-Elf<\/span>: The flexible ability bonus goes\n\tright into Intelligence, but that’s really all you get. You won’t be\n\tmulticlassing, and skill focus won’t really matter to you. Some of the\n\tHalf-Elf’s alternate racial traits help a bit, but they don’t offer anything\n\tparticularly helpful for a Wizard. Humans are strictly better.<\/p>\n

                  Half-Orc<\/span>: The only thing you really get is\n\tthe flexible ability bonus. Half-Elves are better, and Humans are better than\n\tHalf-Elves.<\/p>\n

                  Halfling<\/span>: Bonus to dexterity helps with a\n\tbad save, and the +1 racial bonus to all saves is nice, but the strength\n\tpenalty hurts your damage. You don’t get anything from the other racial\n\tabilities, but Halfling isn’t totally awful.<\/p>\n

                  Human<\/span>: The flexible ability bonus can go in\n\tany of your physical ability scores, but I recommend Strength. Your bonus feat\n\tcan be used for a lot, but I would recommend Toughness so that you have lots\n\tof hit points even at first level.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
