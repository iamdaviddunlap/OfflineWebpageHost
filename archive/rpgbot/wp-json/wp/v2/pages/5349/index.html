Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Oh, thank whatever pantheon of gods your setting uses. Someone finally<\/em> fixed the Monk. Since 3.0, the Monk has been the laughingstock of classes. No amount of splat books, errata, archetypes, or alternate class features could make the Monk work. Monks have always faced two problems:<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

First, they are difficult to play. The Monks biggest class features were incompatible. The class was a front-line class with garbage AC and no hit points. Monks needed high scores in every<\/em> ability score. Second, Monks couldn’t do anything<\/em>. Their best trick was to punch things, and basically every martial class in the game could do that better. Brawlers were especially insulting since they dropped all of the Monk’s problems but kept their unarmed combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Unchained Monk dramatically improves the Monk’s customizability, versatility, and utility. A whole host of new options allow Monks to function as a Defender in addition to their existing abilities as a Striker, bringing them at least up to the capabilities of a Fighter.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    What’s New?<\/h2>\n\n\n

    The Unchained Monk is the most significantly changed Unchained class. It drops all of the lingering nastiness that monks retained from 3.5 and finally makes the monk simple to build without relying on 2+ archetypes to get a functional vanilla monk.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    • Ki Powers<\/strong>: Ki Powers add a talent-style customization option to Monks, allowing for much more diversity among Monk builds. The list of options includes powers which monks were previously locked into like Slow Fall and Quivering Palm, as well as a handful of exciting new options. Qinggong Monks players may find the mechanic familiar.<\/li>
    • Style Strikes<\/strong>: Unique strikes to add as rider effects once or twice each time you use Flurry of Blows, adding a lot of interesting options to the Monk’s favorite combat option.<\/li>
    • Flawless Mind<\/strong>: You’ll notice that Unchained Monks get low Will saves. This will fix it, but not until 19th level.<\/li>
    • Updated Numbers<\/strong>: d10 hit points, full BAB, and low Will saves. Altogether, a dramatic improvement that brings the monk on par with the fighter. You can fix bad will saves with Iron Will, while vanilla monks need to fix their hit points with Toughness and have no way to fix their medium BAB.<\/li>
    • No Archetypes<\/strong>: Any archetype published for the vanilla Monk is not compatible with the Unchained Monk. While the loss of variety is unfortunate, the improvement to the class is so significant that I think it’s a fair trade.<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n

      Unchained Monk Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

      Hit Points<\/span>: Finally! The Monk has normal\n\thit points for a martial character.<\/p>\n

      Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: The Monk’s primary\n\toffensive option, Flurry of Blows, pretended that the Monk had full BAB. Not\n\tactually giving Monks full BAB was an absurd and complicated aspect of the\n\tclass which had no discernable positive effect on the game.<\/p>\n

      Saves<\/span>: Good Fortitude and Reflex saves.\n\tVanilla Monks also get good Will saves, so this is a minor setback, but Monks\n\tneed Wisdom anyway so your Will save should be fine.<\/p>\n

      Proficiencies<\/span>: Monks get a really bizzarre\n\tcollection of exotic weapons, but many of them provide bonuses to combat\n\tmaneuvers, and the ability to use Flurry of Blows with monk weapons allows you\n\tto get the same number of attacks as a TWF build, but with the damage output\n\tof a two-handed build.<\/p>\n

      Skills<\/span>: No change here. Monks still have\n\tgarbage skills, which makes it very difficult for them to fill non-combat\n\troles in the party.<\/p>\n

      AC Bonus<\/span> (Ex): The Monk AC bonus helps to\n\toffset the monk’s lack of armor. The scaling is fairly negligible, but it\n\tstill feels good to have. However, your AC will still be awful without\n\tadditional items and AC improvements.<\/p>\n

      Flurry of Blows<\/span> (Ex): This is the Monk’s\n\ticonic attack. It follows the same progression as a fighter maximizing the\n\ttwo-weapon fighting feats, but you have no “off-hand” attacks. You don’t even\n\tneed free hands. You can use your feet if your hands are full. You can also\n\talternate freely between weapons and unarmed strikes, and you can substitute\n\tdisarm, sunder, and trip combat maneuvers for unarmed attacks.<\/p>\n

      Unarmed Strike<\/span>: This is why you generally\n\tplay a monk: because you want to hit things with your bare hands\/feet\/etc. The\n\tdamage dice starts off fairly small, but when you hit d8 at level 4, it begins\n\tto outpace whatever weapons you might be two-weapon fighting with. At level\n\t12, your fists are greatswords. You can also deal nonlethal damage without\n\tpenalty, which is situationally useful. It’s important to note that your\n\tunarmed strike damage is heavily size-based, so you want to do everything you\n\tcan to increase your effective size. Get enlarged and throw Impact on an\n\tAmulet of Mighty Fists. It’s also interesting to note that the damage\n\tprogression stops at 20th-level, so items like a Monks Belt stop being useful\n\tat high levels unless you multiclass.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
