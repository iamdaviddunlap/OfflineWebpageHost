Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Path of the Zealot Barbarian is about being a reckless bruiser, diving into the fray without a care in the world. Playing one is a careful balance of pushing out more damage without being too much of a burden on your party. Don\u2019t go bleeding your healing resources dry without reason because, make no mistake, you are no tank, just a heavy damage dealer that will not stop. So unleash your (un-)holy rage upon your foes and follow the Path of the Zealot in the name of [insert deity of choice here]!<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

While Zealots only have one feature for damage, Divine Fury, this feature scales well with Barbarian levels. Attacking twice with Reckless Attack, if we\u2019re following fundamental math<\/a>, we will apply that damage 98.5% of the time, 87% with Great Weapon Master. Features like Fanatical Focus and Rage Beyond Death, instead of directly increasing damage, make it nigh impossible to halt a Zealot\u2019s rage.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

By far the oddest feature of the Zealot is the Warrior of the Gods. The reward for devotion is to stay away from the gods as long as possible, freely called back to the life that one may never have to know the peaceful rest of death. Quite humorous, but also quite useful. If victory can be obtained through recklessness, then the price of victory may be as little as a 3rd-level spell slot, given an ally knows Revivify of course.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Our example build, the Starbarian, doesn\u2019t do anything too fancy. The most radical departure from a typical barbarian is that we\u2019ve chosen Astral Elf as the race because the flavor is too good. Astral Elves are elves that went to space to get closer to their gods, so of course a Zealot Barbarian is a great fit. Aside from the fun racial choice, we\u2019ve got an approachable build that doesn\u2019t have to make broad strategic decisions in most combat encounters. Divine Fury just automatically works while Raging and Astral Elves\u2019 Starlight Step gives some fun tactical options in combat allowing for some really approachable hack and slash meat grinder fun.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Path of the Zealot Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Like most Barbarians, we want Strength, Constitution, and Dexterity, and don\u2019t care too much for the other stats.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Str<\/span>: We do damage. This lets us do the damage.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: We want enough for medium armor. Even with Unarmored Defense, it\u2019s hard to get enough of both Constitution and Dexterity without sacrificing Strength.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: Not dying is still more optimal than relying on Warrior of the Gods.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: Someone else can know things.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: Someone else can be aware of things.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: Someone else can talk to things.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
