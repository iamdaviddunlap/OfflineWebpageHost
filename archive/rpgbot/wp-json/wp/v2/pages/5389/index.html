Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Master Chymist doubles down on the Alchemist’s mutagen, allowing the alchemist to turn into a hulking melee monster. At the expense of some of the Alchemist’s healing and support capabilities, the Master Chymist uses Mutagen to play a Defender and a Striker.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Much like a Barbarian, the Master Chymist has issues with AC due to their light armor. Unlike the Barbarian, the Master Chymist doesn’t have a d12 hit die to fall back on when their AC falls short. Be sure to invest in your AC and your Constitution bonus to be sure that you have enough hit points to survive melee combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Spells<\/strong>: You need at least 7 levels of Alchemist, and there is no way to get around that.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Races<\/h2>\n\n

            Bonuses to Strength and defensive abilities are good, and Medium size is
            \n\thelpful because Growth Mutagen allows you to be Enlarged for long periods of
            \n\ttime.<\/p>\n

            Dwarf<\/span>: The Dwarf’s racial traits make the\n\tMaster Chymist extremely durable. The Dwarf favored class bonus is especially\n\tuseful because it helps improve the Master Chymist’s AC.<\/p>\n

            Elf<\/span>: Normally good for an Alchemist, but\n\tthe Intelligence bonus is wasted and the Constitution penalty is dangerous.<\/p>\n

            Gnome<\/span>: Even if you plan to use Poison, the\n\tGnome is problematic due to their Strength penalty.<\/p>\n

            Half-Elf<\/span>: The Half-Elf is always good for\n\tprestige class builds because of Multitalented, but you might choose to trade\n\tit in for Dual Minded to boost the Master Chymist’s poor Will saves.<\/p>\n

            Halfling<\/span>: The Halfling could be viable for\n\ta Dexterity build, but most Master Chymists go for Strength over Dexterity.<\/p>\n

            Human<\/span>: The flexible ability bonus is nice,\n\tbut a bonus feat isn’t particularly important to the Master Chymist.<\/p>\n\n\n
