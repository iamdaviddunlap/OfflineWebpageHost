Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Inquisitor is, in many ways, the Divine equivalent of the Bard. Inquisitors get 6+ skills with a wide skill list, 6th level spells, medium BAB, and can fill nearly any role in a party.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Because teamwork feats generally only work in melee, Inquisitors are typically melee characters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Inquisitor Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points isn’t much for a\n\tcharacter on the front line, but it’s enough to take a few hits. Fortunately\n\tyou get medium armor and shields, and you have good saves.<\/p>\n

    Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: At medium BAB, the\n\tInquisitor can hold their own with a weapon, but don’t expect to fight it out\n\twith a fighter and win.<\/p>\n

    Saves<\/span>: With good Fortitude and Will saves,\n\tand with reasonable dexterity, the Inquisitor’s saves are good across the\n\tboard.<\/p>\n

    Proficiencies<\/span>: Simple weapons, shields, and\n\tmedium armor would be enough to get the job done, but the Inquisitor gets a\n\tfew other options as well. Most notably, the Inquisitor gets proficiency with\n\tthe repeating crossbow. I would never recommend using one, but it’s ammusing\n\tto know that the Inquisitor doesn’t need a feat to do so.<\/p>\n

    Skills<\/span>: 6+ skill points will let you do a\n\tlot of things. The Inquistitor’s skill list include all of the face skills,\n\tall of the most important knowledge skills, Perception, and Stealth. You can\n\tdo almost everyting but pick locks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

      Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

      Unfortunately, their wide range of abilities also makes the Inquisitor somewhat Multiple Ability Dependent (MAD). When planning your Inquisitor, be sure to decide which roles you wish to focus on and adjust your ability scores accordingly. This guide assumes that the Inquisitor is primarily a melee character, and these ability scores reflect that decision.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

      Str<\/span>: Likely your primary combat ability\n\tscore. Teamwork feats, which are a major part of the class, typically depend\n\ton being in melee combat alongside your allies. Because you have other\n\tcapabilities, you aren’t as dependent on strength as Fighters or other\n\tstrictly combat-focused classes, so you don’t need to dump all of your\n\tresources into Strength. Even if you prefer ranged weapons, Strength is an\n\timportant source of additional damage.<\/p>\n

      Dex<\/span>: Breastplate has a maximum dexterity\n\tbonus of +3, so a 16 in dexterity goes a long way to boost your AC, your worst\n\tsave, and your ranged attacks.<\/p>\n

      Con<\/span>: Always key for hit points, especially\n\twith your.<\/p>\n

      Int<\/span>: Not strictly necessary, but nice for\n\tskill ranks and Knowledge skills.<\/p>\n

      Wis<\/span>: Your spellcasting ability. Becuase\n\tyou’re a 2\/3 caster and don’t rely on direct spells to defeat opponents, you\n\tdon’t need to focus on Wisdom in order to boost your AC. You won’t need more\n\tthan 16 Wisdom, and you won’t need that much for quite a while, so don’t take\n\tmore than 15 Wisdom at start to save points.<\/p>\n

      Cha<\/span>: Unless you plan to be a Face, dump\n\tCharisma. Even if you need Intimidate, Stern Gaze gives you more than enough\n\tof a bonus to overcome an ability penalty. You can also take the Conversion\n\tInquisition, which allows you to use your Wisdom score in place of Charisma\n\tfor social skills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
