Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

I served a great ruler, powerful beyond measure, the world his dominion.<\/p>

Yet even he and his vast empire were destined to fall-to become one with oblivion. At the end of life’s journey…lies only death.<\/p>Amon – FFXIV: Endwalker<\/cite><\/blockquote>\n\n\n\n

The Oathbreaker is a different kind of Paladin and it\u2019s not for everyone, but in the right party it\u2019s great. Particularly, if your party wants to make use of undead minions, this is a great way to support that as Hellish Knight with Skeletal Squires. Just take note that Oathbreakers are required to be Evil, which isn\u2019t really a hindrance<\/a>, but some people will believe it to be. So if you\u2019re not opposed to using Evil powers and a Skeleton army to save the world, try the Oathbreaker.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Being Evil is a sticking point for many players and DMs, but it doesn\u2019t have to be. Evil in terms of Alignment can be expressed as a Surplus of Greed and Deficit of Empathy, as explained by this video from RPG Philosophy<\/a>. To paraphrase the explanation presented by Seth Skorkowsky in that video, the Evil hero is invested in what is theirs and pays no heed to what is not theirs.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

To that end, \u201cThese are my<\/em> friends. This is my <\/em>city.<\/em> Nobody hurts my<\/em> friends. Nobody attacks my<\/em> city.\u201d is a pretty normal line of thinking that works for motivating both Good and Evil heroes. When played that way, Evil PCs are fine, and Oathbreakers are a fine way to be Evil. For more thoughts on alignment in roleplaying, listen to this episode<\/a> with Dr. Terry MacMullin, department chair of philosophy at Eastern Washington University.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

As mentioned above, the key to being the best Oathbreaker is to already have fellow PCs utilizing undead minions. This can include any Wizard or Cleric as well as a handful of other classes with specific subclasses that grant access to Animate Dead<\/a>, such as Circle of Spores Druid<\/a> or Divine Soul Sorcerer<\/a> being able to pick Cleric spells. The example build below, Bones Malone and the Spooky Boys, will assume you\u2019re using this kind of teamwork.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
