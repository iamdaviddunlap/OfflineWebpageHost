Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

An occultist’s choice of implements is the most important class decision that they make. Implements grant an item buff (“Resonant Power”), a base focus power, access to a unique list of focus powers, and a spell of every level which the occultist can cast. Occult Adventures presents 8 implement schools, and since Occultists get just 7 choices you will need to omit at least one, and you’ll omit more if you choose to re-select the same implement school to get another set of spells from that school.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

When selecting implement schools remember that the only focus powers you are forced to take are the “base” focus powers. It’s perfectly fine to pick up a school for the resonant power, base focus power, and spells but never pick up any of the focus powers.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The specific implements for each implement school are intentionally not addressed below. The specific form of the implement generally doesn’t matter. Each school allows enough options that you shouldn’t have any conflicts. If your GM is a stickler, you can select the items that aren’t normal equipment and just hang them all from your character’s body on strings and walk around like a set of magical wind chimes.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Implement Schools<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Abjuration Implements<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    As you might expect, Abjuration centers on defensive options. All of the powers presented by the Abjuration Implements School can generally be handled with other means, many of which are already easily accessible to the Occultist. However, some of the better options also trigger on an Immediate action, allowing you to call up defenses quickly without cutting into your turn significantly.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Resonant Power: Warding Talisman<\/span> (Su): A\n\tresistance bonus is an absolute essential in Pathfinder. You want it to be as\n\tbig as possible as quickly as possible. That said, it’s also one of the\n\tcheaper bonuses to put on a magic item.<\/p>\n

    Base Focus Power: Mind Barrier<\/span> (Sp): The\n\tswift action option is essentially useless unless you’re taking ongoing damage\n\tor intend to walk into a damaging effect, but the immediate action option can\n\tprevent quite a bit of damage. The damage prevented scales with level, but the\n\tfocus cost never increases so this ability remains useful as you gain levels.\n\tThis really helps compensate for the Occultist’s d8 hit dice.<\/p>\n

    Focus Powers<\/span>: A few decent defensive\n\toptions, but nothing that you absolutely need.<\/p>\n

      \n\t
    • Aegis<\/span> (Su): It’s pretty rare that you\n\t\tneed to switch out armor abilities, and armor\/shield bonuses are among the\n\t\tleast useful AC bonuses.<\/li>\n\t
    • Energy Shield<\/span> (Sp): Activating this as a\n\t\tswift action means that you activate and re-activate it without\n\t\tmeainingfully cutting into your turn. The ability to activate it as an\n\t\timmediate action means that you can protect yourself from unexpected energy\n\t\tattacks and traps.<\/li>\n\t
    • Globe of Negation<\/span> (Sp): This is a\n\t\tfantastic defensive option, but it’s also situational. If you fight a lot of\n\t\tspellcasters you’re a fool not to consider this.<\/li>\n\t
    • Loci Sentry<\/span> (Sp): Very situational.<\/li>\n\t
    • Planar Ward<\/span> (Sp): Situational. The\n\t\tOccultist’s Magic Circles ability will generally be more effective,\n\t\tespecially once you can create circles as a full round action.<\/li>\n\t
    • Unraveling<\/span> (Sp): If no one else in your\n\t\tparty can cast Dispel Magic, this is absolutely essential.         Divination Implements<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

        The Divination Implements School is insanely useful. It provides scouting, information gathering, a flexible check bonus from Sudden Insight, and access to special senses which normally require high-level spells to access.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Base Focus Power: Sudden Insight<\/span> (Sp): An\n\teasily accessible scaling insight bonus on a wide variety of checks for just 1\n\tfocus.         • Mind Eye<\/span> (Sp): At fine size, the eye is\n\t\tsmall enough to fly under doors, through keyholes, and possibly through\n\t\tsmall cracks in walls and other barriers.             Evocation Implements<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

            Occultists are not blasters by any means. Their offensive spell options are pitifully slim, and with only 2\/3 spellcasting they don’t get the high level spells that evokers need to remain viable in a game where dealing damage is already one of the least effective ways to play a spellcaster. Energy Ray looks tempting, but its damage is too poor to justify spending precious mental focus.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Focus Powers<\/span>: There really isn’t anything\n\there that you need to get by.<\/p>\n

            • Energy Blast<\/span> (Sp): Aside from the\n\t\tability to change the energy type, this may actually be worse than fireball,\n\t\twhich is on the Occultist spell list.
