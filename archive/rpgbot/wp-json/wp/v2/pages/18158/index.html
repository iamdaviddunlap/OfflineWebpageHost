Graviturgy Magic Wizard Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

While Critical Role content is only sort of first-party, it\u2019s built into Dndbeyond, so we\u2019re going to count that for these purposes. We\u2019ve already covered the excellent Chronurgist<\/a> subclass, but it turns out that some people want to bend gravity instead of time. As the other subclass allowed to access Dunamancy<\/a>, we get several powerful options no one else can take without specific DM permission. What this means is that, like most Wizards, you\u2019re going to drop some big area control\/denial spell early in a fight, but then, even more than most Wizards, you\u2019re going to spend the rest of the encounter manipulating enemies into its range.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Regardless, it\u2019s still a subclass of the strongest class in the game, and we\u2019ll still make good use of that where appropriate. What you end up with is a strong Controller that\u2019s still capable of putting out good sustained damage while also being the single best archetype for forced movement in the game.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Graviturgy Magic subclass gains access to the Dunamancy spell list. See our Dunamancy Spell List Breakdown<\/a> for guidance on Dunamancy spells.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
