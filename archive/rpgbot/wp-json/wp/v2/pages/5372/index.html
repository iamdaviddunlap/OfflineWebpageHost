Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Wizard<\/a> sits at the top of the class tier list, and the Enchanter is perhaps the most powerful of the wizard schools. Boasting excellent buffs and debuffs, and many of the scariest save-or-suck spells, an Enchanter is a terrifying force to behold.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Enchanter is the ultimate save-or-suck caster. Every spell you cast should have immediate and dramatic results, and will very often end a fight with a word and gesture.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              Wizard Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

              Hit Points<\/span>: d6 hit points leaves you pretty\n\tsquishy.<\/p>\n

              Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: Fortunately, you’ll almost\n\tnever need to make an actual attack. Touch AC scales very little from level 1\n\tto level 20, going from an\n\t\n\t\taverage of 11.6 on CR 1 monsters to 12.7 on CR 20 monsters<\/a>.<\/p>\n

              Saves<\/span>: Well, at least you have decent\n\twill. Mind controlling a wizard is your job, and we don’t want anyone else\n\tdoing your job.<\/p>\n

              Proficiencies<\/span>: Wizards get very little in the\n\tway of proficiencies, but beyond very early levels you won’t need them. If\n\tyou’re not an Elf, a light crossbow is an excellent backup weapon for an\n\tEnchanter.<\/p>\n

              Skills<\/span>: Wizards only get 2+ skills, but you\n\tget every<\/em> knowledge skill as a class skill, and your absurd\n\tintelligence will give you plenty of skill points to throw around. Remember\n\tthat permanently increasing your intelligence will give you additional skill\n\tpoints retroactively, which will be nice when you hit levels 8 and 16.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

              Arcane Bond<\/span> (Ex or Sp): You have the option\n\tof a familiar or a bonded item, and both options have their merits.<\/p>\n

                \n\t
              • Familiar<\/span>: Always a solid choice, the\n\t\tfamiliar is an ever-present and ever-useful companion. Familiars can serve\n\t\tas scouts, assassins, couriers, assistants, and even meat shields in a\n\t\tpinch. Their passive bonuses to the wizard are roughly equal to a feat, and\n\t\tthe provide the benefits of Alertness when they are adjacent. Effectively, a\n\t\tfamiliar gives you two feats and a cool pet.<\/li>\n\t
              • amulet<\/span>: Jewelry is nice an safe. It\n\t\tdoesn’t take up a hand, it’s hard to take from you in combat, and it’s a\n\t\tgood option for enchanting.<\/li>\n\t
              • ring<\/span>: The ring is really the gold standard\n\t\tof bonded items. It’s incredibly small, it’s easy to hide under a glove or\n\t\ton your person, it’s a very difficult target for sundering, and you can\n\t\tenchant is as per Enchant Ring without the feat. Magic rings have a lot of\n\t\tgreat options, but Enchant Ring is rarely worth the feat, so this gives you\n\t\tsome options to save some money.<\/li>\n\t
              • staff<\/span>: If you like to use magic Staves,\n\t\tstaff might be a good option. If you only want one magic staff, this will\n\t\tsave you a feat. If you don’t enjoy magic staves, you have a big, obvious\n\t\tstick which people can either take from you or destroy.<\/li>\n\t
              • wand<\/span>: Wands are generally only good for low\n\t\tlevel spells which you plan to cast frequently, like Cure Light Wounds.\n\t\tWizards have very few options which can justify being on a wand, and a wand\n\t\tis very easy to disarm\/steal\/sunder.<\/li>\n\t
              • weapon<\/span>: A wizard should know better. An\n\t\tenchanter should never be using a weapon enough to justify actually carrying\n\t\tit around.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                Arcane School<\/span>: You picked Enchantment. For\n\tinformation on other schools, see the\n\tWizard School Breakdown<\/a>. For information on the Enchantment school specifically, see below.<\/p>\n

                Cantrips<\/span>: Cantrips are amazing at every\n\tlevel. Prestidigitation is often called “minor wish”, and Detect Magic will\n\tnever stop being helpful. At low levels, Acid Splash provides easy, realiable\n\tdamage.<\/p>\n

                Scribe Scroll<\/span>: Scrolls are great for\n\twizards, and giving you the feat for free is nice. Use it to prepare scrolls\n\tof spells that you need infrequently so that you can cover all of your\n\tbases.<\/p>\n

                Bonus Feats<\/span>: 4 extra feats over the course\n\tof 20 levels isn’t game-breaking, but the Wizard would still be king if he\n\tdidn’t get any of these. The addition of\n\tArcane Discoveries<\/a>\n\tgives wizards a handful of unique and incredibly powerful options in place of\n\tmetamagic and item crafting feats.<\/p>\n

                Spells<\/span>: You have the best spell list in the\n\tgame, and every new book makes it better. Spells are why you play a wizard.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Enchantment School<\/h2>\n\n\n

                Enchanting Smile<\/span> (Su): A bonus to social\n\tskills is weird for a wizard. If you expend resources to be a face, this might\n\tactually be useful. But most likely, you won’t be doing a lot of Bluffing. The\n\tlevel 20 ability is fantastic, but won’t see a lot of use.<\/p>\n

                Dazing Touch<\/span> (Sp): Dazed is a great status\n\tcondition and this power has no saving throws, but this is a\n\tmelee<\/em> touch attack, which you will be terrible at, and it only\n\taffects things with your hit dice of fewer, which is generally only going to\n\tNPCs or things with a CR lower than your level. Your familiar can’t deliver\n\tthe attack since it’s not a spell. It can be situationally useful if you get\n\tgrappled or if you ambush someone, but it’s not going to be your go-to\n\toptions.<\/p>\n

                Aura of Despair<\/span> (Su): The 30 foot range is\n\tproblematic, and the duration is fairly limited, but a -2 to saves against\n\teverything affected is brutal, The debuff matches the Shaken condition and\n\tstacks with it. If you can make a target Shaken, they’re looking at a total -4\n\tpenalty on saves against your absurd spell DCs. Sickened adds another -2.<\/p>\n

                Controller<\/span><\/h4>\n

                Force of Will<\/span> (Su): You give up charming\n\tsmile for the ability to communicate with your charmed\/dominated targets\n\ttelepathically. This is great for communicating with subjects which don’t\n\tspeak common, and lets you boss things around without revealing that they’re\n\tcharmed\/dominated. You could even use Charm Person on your party members to\n\tset up a cheap telepathic communication system.<\/p>\n

                Irresistible Demand<\/span> (Sp): This is strictly\n\tworse than Dominate Monster, the duration is extremely limited, the save is\n\taverage, and you have to concentrate as a standard action to maintain the\n\teffect. However, it’s Dominate Monster at 8th level. With careful application,\n\tthis is a really fantastic way to mimic a 9th level spell for a few rounds.<\/p>\n

                Manipulator<\/span><\/h4>\n

                Beguiling Touch<\/span> (Sp): You trade in the\n\tmediocre Dazing Touch for the considerably worse Beguiling Touch. Beguiling\n\ttouch allows a save, has a very short duration, and doesn’t work in combat or\n\ton hostile creatures. If you really need to charm something, cast Charm Person\n\tor Charm Monster. You’re an enchanter, so you’re really good at those.<\/p>\n

                Shape Emotions<\/span> (Sp): You can now use your\n\taura to give your party a +4 bonus on saves against fear, or your can give\n\tenemies a -2 penalty on saves against mind-affecting spells and effects. The\n\tbonus against feat can be replaced with Remove Fear, the -2 penalty is worse\n\tthan the save penalty from Aura of Despair, and<\/em> Aura of Despair\n\tapplies penalties to attacks, ability checks, and skill checks.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Opposing Schools<\/h3>\n\n\n

                • Conjuration<\/span>: Conjuration is one of the best\n\t\tschools. In addition to summoning effects, it includes teleportation\n\t\tspells.<\/li>\n\t

                  Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

                  Str<\/span>: Dump to 7. Make the fighter do the\n\tlifting.<\/p>\n

                  Dex<\/span>: Important for saves, and for the\n\toccasional touch attack.<\/p>\n

                  Con<\/span>: Your hit points are garbage, and\n\tfortitude saves will be a problem for you.<\/p>\n

                  Int<\/span>: You get magic, skills, and a bonus to\n\tknowledge skills (which you will have a ton of). Max at start, boost early,\n\tboost often.<\/p>\n

                  Wis<\/span>: You get high will saves, but with no\n\tother real ability dependencies, bumping wisdom to at least 12 for additional\n\tsaves is wise.<\/p>\n

                  Cha<\/span>: If you’re trying to be a face (which\n\tyou totally can), don’t dump charisma too hard. Otherwise, dump to 7 and keep\n\tyour mouth shut until its time to throw some spells around.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  In addition to normal ability considerations, Wizards can also make excellent use of the aging rules. Being middle-aged gives you a -1 penalty to your physical abilities, but gets you a pleasant +1 to your mental ability scores. However, this can be a problem for your already low reflex and fortitude saves.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
