                                                      Way of the Sun Soul<\/a> (SCAG<\/a>)<\/sup>\n<\/h3>\n\n\n\n
                                                      <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                                                      The Sun Soul Monk tries to bridge the gap between the Monk’s melee capacity\n\tand the ranged capacity of a Blaster or ranged Striker. The features are very\n\tsustainable since most of them don’t require Ki to use, but without spending\n\tKi their damage is pitiful, so you will frequently find yourself burning\n\tthrough your Ki pool early, then resorting to punching things or shooting at\n\tthem with Radiant Sun Bolt. Way of the Four Elements (possibly with Magic\n\tInitiate to pick up a ranged cantrip if you’re desperate for a ranged attack\n\toption) does essentially the same thing to much greater effect, and has the\n\tadditional ability to pick different spells.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                                      Way of Sun Soul has some really good ideas, but the execution of those ideas simply isn’t effective. Radiant Sun Bolt gives the monk a meaningful ranged option, but the only real improvement is that the Monk can attack from 30 feet away. It’s no more damaging or efficient than just walking up and punching something, and you actually lose<\/em> potential damage compared to using a spear at low levels. Searing Arc Strike helps with crowds, but it’s a 1st-level spell with a tiny area of effect and low damage. Searing Sunburst is basically Fireball, but less damage and a worse save, and even though it’s free you’ll typically get better results from punching things, which is also free.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                                      Sun Soul needs a lot of changes to make it work. Adding the Optional Class\n\tFeatures helps, but it is nowhere near enough. The core issue with the\n\tsubclass is that the features are usually at best<\/em> equivalent to just\n\tattacking and using Flurry of Blows. Here are some suggested fixes:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                                        \n
                                                      • Radiant Sun Bolt<\/strong>: You may replace any unarmed strike with a Radiant Sun Bolt attack, allow the monk to use either Dexterity or Wisdom for attack and damage rather than locking them into Dexterity, and increase the range by 10 feet every time the Martial Arts die increases, up to a maximum of of 60 feet at level 17. This allows the Monk to attack with their Bonus Action without spending Ki, allows them to build around Wisdom to focus on special features instead of just punching, and to function at range greater than the movement speed of nearly everything in the Monster Manual.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                                                      • Searing Arc Strike<\/strong>: Instead of using Burning Hands, this is a 15-foot cone which deals 1d8+Wisdom Modifier damage, and activating it costs no Ki. The Monk may spend Ki to enhance the effects, adding 1d8 damage and +5 feet to the size of the cone, up to a maximum number of Ki points equal to half their monk level. This gives the monk an inexpensive way to handle multiple foes, and the ability to expand the cone keeps the feature meaningful at high levels once Searing Sunburst comes online. Adding the Monk’s Wisdom modifier as a flat damage bonus helps keep the damage roughly consistent if you don’t spend Ki to improve the effects.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                                                      • Searing Sunburst<\/strong>: Change the base damage to 2d6+Wisdom Modifier.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                                                          Getting this feature at 6th level can feel frustrating. Cones work fine on\n\t\t\tmartial characters who can survive in melee, but it’s a 1st-level spell\n\t\t\tthat you get at level 6, and it has 15-foot range on a subclass that’s\n\t\t\tthat’s all about allowing the Monk to function at long range.<\/p>\n\t\t

                                                          Once you get Searing Sunburst, this may feel obsolete.\n\t\t\tFor the same two Ki points, Searing Sunburst deals 6d6 damage (avg. 21\n\t\t\tcompared to Searing Arc Strike’s 13.5) in a significantly larger AOE and\n\t\t\tat 150-foot range, though it does consume an Action. Adding more Ki widens the gap even further.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

                                                        3. Searing Sunburst<\/span>: The base damage isn’t\n\t\tgreat, but it scales reasonably well and doesn’t cost any Ki Points to use\n\t\tat the base damage. Unfortunately, because the base damage is so low that you\n\t\tneed to spend a ton of Ki to do any serious damage, and, since this is on a\n\t\tConstitution save your enemies are likely to pass the save, which means they take no damage, not half damage.\n\t\t

                                                          The best part of this is that you can use it for free. 2d6 damage still\n\t\t\tisn’t a ton of damage, but the range is great at 150 feet (compared to\n\t\t\tjust 30 feet for Radiant Sun Bolt), so if you’re fighting at long range\n\t\t\tyou can shell your enemies with this at the base damage until they either\n\t\t\tdie or get tired of your shenanigans and move closer.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

The\n\t\tbiggest frustration is that this consumes your Reaction so you can’t use it\n\t\tfor Opportunity Attacks or something like that.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n\n
