Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

If you like to get Critical Hits on your big Sneak Attacks but don\u2019t like the idea of Crit Fishing, perhaps the Assassin is the Rogue for you. You\u2019ve probably already looked at the Assassinate feature and want to capitalize on those guaranteed Critical Hits, but guaranteed is not the same as free. You\u2019ll have to work with your party to Surprise<\/em> your foes in order to earn those Critical Hits.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Surprised<\/em> is a simple condition that is much harder to achieve than you might think. If your whole party<\/em>\u2019s Stealth checks beat the Passive Perception of a foe, that foe is Surprised. Yes, even that Paladin waddling around in plate armor. If a foe detects even one of your allies, that foe is not Surprised<\/em>. That\u2019s the official definition for how Surprise works in Dungeons and Dragons 5e. We went into extreme detail on this in the Stealth episode<\/a> which also proposes some fixes if you want too.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Things get murky when we consider why the Assassin also has a pair of features designed around Deception and Infiltration. The rules of Surprise only mention Stealth and Perception, but we could (and do) argue that Deception versus Insight should also be a valid method of imposing Surprised<\/em> on a target. That stompy Paladin from earlier certainly has enough Charisma to lie through their teeth.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Assassin Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n

          The Assassin\u2019s primary scores will follow the scores in the Rogue Handbook<\/a>, but our features do steer us towards Face skills as a secondary concern.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Str<\/span>: It\u2019s always technically an option to use Strength as a Rogue, but typically impractical. For Assassins attempting to Stealth for Surprise, this is doubly true.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: AC in Light Armor and Attacks with Finesse or Ranged weapons. Important for us.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: Keeps us alive and these saves are really bad to fail.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: Technically we can use this with our Poisoner\u2019s Kit proficiency but that\u2019s about all we can get out of it.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: It\u2019s about as useful to us as it is to most people, i.e. Insight, Perception and Saves, but we don\u2019t get anything special out of it beyond that general utility.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: We gain some benefits to Deception and Disguise that would benefit from Charisma. If your DM agrees with my assertion above about Deception vs Insight for Surprise this might even jump to Blue.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
