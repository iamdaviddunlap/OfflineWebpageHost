Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Oracle is to the Cleric what the Sorcerer is to the Wizard. Oracles are Charisma-based spontaneous spellcasters that share a spell list (and several other aspects) with the Cleric. Because their abilities are so similar, the Oracle can fill any role that the Cleric can, and because they are Charisma-based, the Oracle makes for a much better Face than a Cleric.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Oracle Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points can be hard if\n\tyou’re on the front lines, especially with only medium armor and no real need\n\tfor Dexterity. If you prefer to stay in the back you’re probably fine, but if\n\tyou’re in the front be sure to boost your AC and Constitution.<\/p>\n

    Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/3 BAB and you can buff\n\tyourself to make up the difference.<\/p>\n

    Saves<\/span>: The Oracle’s only good save is Will,\n\tand with no other need for Wisdom even the Oracle’s best save likely won’t be\n\tspectacular.<\/p>\n

    Proficiencies<\/span>: Simple weapons are plenty,\n\tand medium armor and shield provide good AC. If you need more AC, take Heavy\n\tArmor Proficiency or cast some buff spells.<\/p>\n

    Skills<\/span>: 4+ skill ranks is great for a\n\tstraight caster, and your choice of mystery allows for some interesting class\n\tskills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Spells<\/span>: The Oracle uses the Cleric spell\n\tlist, which is excellent. You have a limited number of spells known which\n\tgreatly limits your versatility, but you still have access to all of the\n\tCleric’s best spells. Don’t forget that you get to select all Cure or all\n\tInflict spells as spells known for free. The text is buried in the “Spells”\n\tsection for Oracles, and it’s easy to miss.<\/p>\n

    Mystery<\/span>: Your choice of mystery is much like\n\tthe Cleric’s choice of domain, but more like a Sorcerer’s Bloodline. It\n\tgreatly determines the feel and flavor of your character, and greatly\n\tinfluences your play style. See my\n\tOracle Mystery Breakdown<\/a> for help selecting a\n\tmystery.<\/p>\n

    Oracle’s Curse<\/span>: The Oracle’s Curse is a\n\thandicap. Each option provides some other benefit which grows as you level,\n\tbut for the most part the curses are a burden. If you plan to multiclass,\n\tremember that half of your other class levels count toward your level for the\n\teffects of Oracle’s Curse, which can make for some very clever multiclassing\n\toptions.<\/p>\n

      Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

      The Oracle, like the Cleric, can be either a strict spellcaster or a front-line combat spellcaster. Your choice of abilities depends on what role you plan to fill in your party.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

      Str<\/span>: If you plan to be a combat caster, you\n\tneed Strength for attack and damage. If you’re a straight caster, dump\n\tStrength.<\/p>\n

      Dex<\/span>: Boosts AC and Reflex saves. If AC is\n\ta problem or if you plan to be a combat caster, pick up Heavy Armor\n\tProficiency so that a 12 is an acceptable score. For straight casters, pick up\n\ta little more dexterity to improve your AC and your ranged attacks.<\/p>\n

      Con<\/span>: Everyone needs hit points and\n\tFortitude saves.<\/p>\n

      Int<\/span>: With 4+ skill ranks and not a lot of\n\timportant skills, you can afford to dump Intelligence if you really need to.\n\tHowever, many Oracle Mysteries grant access to all knowledge skills, which can\n\tmake you an excellent Librarian with a little bit of Intelligence.<\/p>\n

      Wis<\/span>: Only required for Will saves, and\n\tWill is the Oracle’s good save.<\/p>\n

      Cha<\/span>: Your spellcasting ability.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
