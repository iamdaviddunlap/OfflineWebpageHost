Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

This article covers the legacy version of the Witch. For help with the remastered Witch, see our Remastered Witch Handbook<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Witch is a spellcaster granted power by a mysterious Patron. The Witch’s core features are very close to the Wizard, and their spellcasting works very similarly, but your choice of Patron Theme allows you to select the spell list of your choice similar to the Sorcerer, and the theme of the class’s features have a distinctly less scholarly bent. DnD 5e players might justifiably see thematic similarities to 5e’s warlocks, but the similarities end there.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Similar to the Wizard, the Witch’s ability to learn any number of spells from their spell list makes them a magical tool box with a tool for every specific problem one might encounter. Beyond that excellent foundation, the Witch’s ability to select their own spell list allows them to fill nearly any role in the party by selecting your Patron Theme and spells. Like the Wizard, the Witch’s high Intelligence gives you a lot of trained skills at 1st level, making it easy to play your party’s Librarian or to pick up skills to support other skill-based capabilities.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Beyond their spellcasting, the Witch is the uncontested master of familiars, and their Focus Spells (called “Hexes”) are truly fantastic, and include powerful Hex Cantrips which can often define your character’s best tactical options.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                  Witch Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

                  Key Ability<\/strong>: Intelligence is the Witch’s Key Ability Score, and like any spellcaster it’s used for your Spell Attacks and for your Spell DCs. Those are two of your most important stats, so keep your Intelligence as high as you can get it. Intelligence also grants you additional Trained skills at 1st level, allowing you to easily invest in numerous skills, including Lore options which other classes often need to ignore in favor of more important skills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  Hit Points<\/span>: 6+Con hit points. Fortunately you\n\thave plenty of room to boost your ability scores so your Constitution bonus\n\tcan compensate.<\/p>\n

                  Initial Proficiencies<\/span>: The Witch’s\n\tproficiencies are terrible. Your saving throw progression is very slightly\n\tworse than the Wizard’s, which you make up for by having better weapon\n\tproficiencies which you’ll almost certainly never use. Like the Wizard, your\n\thigh Intelligence will give you an abundance of Trained skills at first level,\n\tbut that’s the best part of your proficiencies beyond spellcasting.<\/p>\n

                    \n\t

                  • Saving Throws<\/span>: Only one good saving throw.\n\t\tWill is a great option, but only one good saving throw is still a serious\n\t\thandicap, and it never improves past Master.                   • Skills<\/span>: One skill from your Patron, then\n\t\t3+Int additional Trained skills for a total of 4+Int. 4+ is standard, and thanks to\n\t\thigh Intelligence you’ll start with numerous skills.<\/li>\n\t
                  • Attacks<\/span>: Simple weapons and unarmed\n\t\tattacks. You might make Unarmed Attacks if you take certain Witch Class\n\t\tFeats, but mostly you’ll rely on spells instead.<\/li>\n\t
                  • Defenses<\/span>: No armor proficiencies, and your\n\t\tproficiency in Unarmored Defense never increases past Expert. Expect to rely\n\t\ton spells like Mage Armor, and be sure that you have nice sturdy friends to\n\t\thide behind.<\/li>\n\t

Since spellcasting is the Witch’s primary feature, that’s a major choice that impacts your character very heavily. Witches also have Focus Spells called Hexes, including Hex Cantrips.                     Subclasses – Patron Themes<\/h2>\n\n\n

                    Your choice of Patron Theme gives you your spell list, a skill, a Hex Cantrip, and a free 1st-level spell that your familiar knows (remember that you get 5 more of your choice at 1st level). Every one of the skills is taken from Arcana, Nature, Occultism, and Religion, and while Nature and Religion are harder choices because they’re Wisdom-based, all four skills are still good choices for the Witch since you’re likely your party’s Librarian, and unless someone else in the party has better Wisdom you’re likely to pick up several or all of them.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    The Spell List is likely your most mechanically impactful decision, because while the Witch is more than just spellcasting your spellcasting is still your defining function in the party. Different spell lists have different strengths, but they’re all roughly equivalent in power.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n

                                        The Witch’s ability scores are mostly simple: You need Intelligence for your spells, and you need Dexterity, Constitution, and Wisdom for your defenses. However, the Witch has some interesting melee options like Living Hair and Eldritch Nails which place more reliance on ability scores to support melee combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                                        Conventional<\/h3>\n\n\n

                                        Most witches just need Intelligence for spells and enough in other Ability Scores to keep them alive. You might adjust these based on which skills you select, but the suggestions below are a good starting point.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        Str<\/span>: Dump. You’re not carrying heavy\n\tequipment, you’re not wearing armor, and you shouldn’t be using weapons.<\/p>\n

                                        Dex<\/span>: AC and Reflex saves. You’ll eventually\n\twant at least 16, but don’t bother getting more than 20.<\/p>\n

                                        Con<\/span>: Hit points and Fortitude saving\n\tthrows.<\/p>\n

                                        Int<\/span>: Your Key Ability Score.<\/p>\n

                                        Wis<\/span>: Perception and Will saves.<\/p>\n

                                        Cha<\/span>: Probably a dump stat unless you want\n\tto use your numerous skills to play the party’s Face. Unlike the Wizard, using\n\tyour third Action every turn to Demoralize foes isn’t a great choice for the\n\tWitch because so many Hex Cantrips have a 1-Action casting time.<\/p>\n\n\n

                                        Melee-Friendly<\/h3>\n\n\n

                                        The Witch’s proficiency in simple weapons and Class Feats like Living Hair and Eldritch Claws make melee combat a possibility. However, with no armor proficiencies this is still a very dangerous option that you’ll need to approach very cautiously. If you go this route, look for ways to boost your AC and your hit points.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        Before you go any further with this option: Strongly reconsider. Give this a real good think. The options built into the Witch do not<\/em> make melee a good idea. If you go this route, it will be hard and you will need to build your witch very carefully in order to be successful. You will likely need a level of insight into the game which goes beyond what I can cram into a single article. You will almost certainly need to consider a number of archetypes and feats not covered in this document. If you do it and it goes well, please email me<\/a> to brag about it because honestly it looks really hard and I would be very impressed. If you try it and it goes poorly, email me anyway because sometimes failure is a good learning opportunity.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        Str<\/span>: While Living Hair has the Finesse\n\ttrait, Eldritch Claws does not, and if you’re going for a melee build you’ll\n\tprobably be using Eldritch Claws rather than a Finesse weapon or Living Hair.\n\tHowever, having a higher AC is more important than a good attack bonus, so I\n\tstrongly recommend investing more in Dexterity than in Strength unless you can\n\tmake armor viable and can de-emphasize Dexterity.<\/p>\n

                                        Dex<\/span>: AC and Reflex saves. This is\n\tespecially important if you plan to be in melee, but if you can make armor a\n\tviable option you may be able to put less into Dexterity.<\/p>\n

                                        Con<\/span>: Hit points and Fortitude saving\n\tthrows.<\/p>\n

                                        Int<\/span>: Your Key Ability Score.<\/p>\n

                                        Wis<\/span>: Since you need good stats in every\n\tphysical ability score plus Intelligence, Wisdom will need to be largely\n\tdisregarded.<\/p>\n

                                        Ancestries<\/h2>\n\n\n

                                        An Intelligence boost is crucial, but since Free Ability Boosts are part of every Ancestry that’s easy to accomplish. Bonuses to other important ability scores are helpful, but not crucial, so Ancestry Feats are often as important as an Intelligence Increase.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        Catfolk<\/span>: Put the Free ability Boost into\n\tIntelligence and you have all that you need, but none of the Catfolk’s feats\n\tdirectly support the Witch’s capabilities. Interesting but unhelpful: you could take the Clawed Catfolk\n\tHeritage and the Eldritch Nails Witch Class Feat and have two totally\n\tindependent sets of claws\/nails with mostly identical stats.<\/p>\n

                                        Dwarf<\/span>: The Dwarf’s Ability Boosts work\n\tgreat if you put the Free Ability Boost into Intelligence, but Dwarf Ancestry\n\tFeats offer very little for the Witch. Strongly consider Adopted Ancestry.<\/p>\n

                                        Elf<\/span>: The Elf’s Ability Boosts are great,\n\tbut a Constitution Flaw is really problematic, so you may want to use the\n\tAlternate Ability Boosts rule to get two Free Boosts.
