The bard is among the most versatile classes in 3.5. With great skills,\n\tdeceptively good spellcasting, and decent combat abilities, the Bard can fill\n\tnearly any role in a party without depending entirely on spells to do so like\n\tthe Wizard does. They make an excellent addition to a large party which has\n\tevery role covered due to their effects as a force-multiplyer, but they also\n\tmake a fantastic member of a small party due to their wide-ranging\n\tskillset.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

A classic criticism of bards it that they are a “jack of all trades and a\n\tmaster of none.” Well, the full line is “Jack of all trades, master of none,\n\tbut often times better than a master of one”, and I can think of few things to\n\twhich that truism applies better than to the Bard.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The bard’s biggest roles are as a Face, Librarian, Support, and Utility\n\tCaster, though they can fill nearly any role with a handful of clever build\n\tchoices.<\/p>\n\n\n
