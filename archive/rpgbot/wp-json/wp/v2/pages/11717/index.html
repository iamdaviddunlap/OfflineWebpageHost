Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Inventor’s features center around your character’s Innovation, which is\n\tequal parts equipment and subclass, greatly influencing your tactics and your\n\trole in the party. Depending on your choices, your Inventor can be some\n\tcombination of Defender, Scholar, Scout, and Striker.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Inventor is a curious martial-adjacent character, falling into a role in\n\tthe party similar to the Fighter but leaning slightly toward the Rogue. With\n\thigh Intelligence you’ll start with abundant skills and you’re well suited to\n\tcrafting items of various types.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Inventor has a lot of decision points for a non-caster, making them\n\tcomplex to build and play. It can also reward deep understanding of PF2’s\n\tcrafting system. Weirdly, the Inventor also does a great job of making\n\tcomplexity “opt in”, allowing you to choose between options which aren’t\n\tstrictly better or worse but which might be more or less complex to build and\n\tplay. In Pathfinder 2e where building any character is complicated, this is\n\trefreshing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

              The Inventor is surprisingly MAD, even for Pathfinder 2e. You need\n\tIntelligence to support your class features and skills, but you’re also sort\n\tof a martial class so you need some combination of Strength, Dexterity, and\n\tConstitution. Your Will saves are poor, so you also need Wisdom. That leaves\n\tCharisma as a dump stat, but you’re going to have to weigh your other\n\tdecisions based on your other build decisions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Str<\/span>: If you’re using a Strength-based\n\tweapon (most melee weapons), you need all that you can get. If you’re using a\n\tfinesse weapon or a propulsion weapon you need a little bit for damage. If\n\tyou’re using a crossbow or a firearm or if you select the Construct\n\tinnovation, you may be able to dump Strength.<\/p>\n

              Dex<\/span>: Inventors get medium armor\n\tproficiency, so you likely need 12 at minimum. If you choose the Armor\n\tinnovation and choose the Subterfuge Suit or if you’re using a finesse weapon\n\tor a ranged weapon, you need more Dexterity than you would otherwise.<\/p>\n

              Con<\/span>: Hit points and Fortitude saves. This\n\tis especially important if you plan to be in melee because you only have 8+\n\thit points and medium armor.<\/p>\n

              Int<\/span>: Your Key Ability Score. Regardless of\n\tyour other build decisions, this is absolutely crucial.<\/p>\n

              Wis<\/span>: You need a bit to support your poor\n\tPerception and Will saves, but how much you can afford will depend on your\n\tother build choices.<\/p>\n

              Cha<\/span>: Dump.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                Skills and Skill Feats<\/h2>\n\n\n

That means that you can maximize at most three skills, and the rest of your skills might not advance beyond Trained.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                You get Skill Feats at even-numbered levels, giving you a total of 10 Skill Feats (and maybe another from your Background) by 20th level. Generally, you want to invest these feats in the same skills which you are choosing to maximize, though in some cases you may want to grab feats from skills which don’t require that you be more than Trained.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
