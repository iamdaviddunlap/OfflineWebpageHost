Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Rogue is a staple of DnD-style games, but as Pathfinder has grown, the vanilla Rogue has been left behind in a lot of ways. In the core rulebook, the Rogue was the unmatched master of stealth, skill, and surprise attacks. Over time these masteries have been eclipsed by other classes which overcame a lot of the issues which Rogues faces in core. The Unchained Rogue addresses many of Rogue’s biggest pain points, and gives the Rogue enough new and unique abilities to make it interesting in the face of all of the other exciting base classes.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    What’s New?<\/h2>\n\n\n

    • Finesse Training<\/strong>: Removes the Weapon Finesse feat tax.<\/li>

            Rogue Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Hit Points<\/span>: At d8 hit points, the Rogue is\n\tfairly squishy. Because many Rogues work best in melee, hit points are a\n\tserious concern and Rogues need to be sure to keep their AC high.<\/p>\n

            Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/3 BAB is difficult\n\tbecause many Rogues depend on two-weapon fighting, but flanking and high\n\tDexterity can make up the deficit. Debilitating Injury also allow you debuff\n\tand enemy’s AC, which can go a long way to compensate for your relatively low\n\tBAB.<\/p>\n

            Saves<\/span>: Good Reflex saves, high Dexterity,\n\tand Evasion make the Rogue exceptionally good at Reflex saves, but they often\n\thave issues with Fortitude and Will saves.<\/p>\n

            Proficiencies<\/span>: Limited to simple weapons,\n\thand crossbows, and a handful of martial weapons, Rogues have very few\n\toptions. Fortunately, they get all of the ones that they really need to get\n\tby.<\/p>\n

            Skills<\/span>: 8+ skill ranks and the longest class\n\tskill list in the game. Rogues can do a lot of things, but even with 8+ skill\n\tranks it can still be difficult to cover all of the essentials. Be sure to\n\tcoordinate with your party to know you’re going to need to invest your skill\n\tranks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Sneak Attack<\/span>: A pile of d6’s to drop on your\n\tfoes. Sneak Attack is the Rogue’s primary source of damage, but because it can\n\tbe hard to bring into play, Rogues occasionally find themselves useless in\n\tcombat. Unchained Rogue’s version of Sneak Attack notably allows you to Sneak\n\tAttack targets with concealment, though you still can’t Sneak Attack creatures\n\twith total concealment.<\/p>\n

            Trapfinding<\/span>: The bonus to disarming traps\n\tis good, but the ability to disarm magical traps is the key component of this\n\tfeature.<\/p>\n

            Finesse Training<\/span> (Ex): One of the most\n\timportant additions to the Unchained Rogue, Finesse Training give you Weapon\n\tFinesse for free at first level, and an effect similar to Slashing Grace at\n\t3rd level. Ranged Rogues will mostly only see this as a fun backup ability\n\twhen they’re forced into melee, but melee Rogues will save themselves a\n\trequired feat, and the 3rd-level ability nets them a massive boost to\n\tdamage.<\/p>\n

            Evasion<\/span> (Ex): With your high reflex saves,\n\tEvasion will save you from a lot of damage.<\/p>\n

            Danger Sense<\/span> (Ex): A minor improvement\n\tover Trap Sense, but the bonus is small and scales very slowly.<\/p>\n

            Debilitating Injury<\/span> (Ex): A fantastic rider\n\teffect which you add to your Sneak Attacks. Remember that you can only apply\n\tone penalty to a target at a time, but your successive attacks will each add\n\tan additional round to the duration, which gives you some nice padding if you\n\tcan’t hit the target for a round or two for some reason, or if you change\n\ttargets.<\/p>\n

              Compatible Archetypes<\/h3>\n\n\n

              Because the Unchained Rogue’s abilities are mostly the same as the vanilla Rogue’s, most Rogue archetypes are compatible. The only questionable part is the change from Trap Sense to Danger Sense. Because Danger Sense is strictly better than Trap Sense (it’s Trap Sense with an additional Perception bonus), any reasonable GM should allow you to use Danger Sense in place of Trap Sense because you’re giving up more for the same benefits.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

              The Rogue is very frequently MAD, especially if the party has very few other skills and no Face.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Str<\/span>: Only useful for a bit of damage until\n\tyou hit level 3. Dump to 8, but don’t dump to 7 unless you really need the\n\textra points.<\/p>\n

              Dex<\/span>: In light armor, Dexterity is absolutely\n\tcrucial. All but the strangest Rogue builds depend on Weapon Finesse or ranged\n\tweapons, so make sure to put a lot of resources into dexterity.<\/p>\n

              Con<\/span>: Essential for Fortitude saves and hit\n\tpoints, especially if you’re in melee.<\/p>\n

              Int<\/span>: Essential for skill ranks. 8+ seems\n\tlike a lot, but it’s nowhere near enough.<\/p>\n

              Wis<\/span>: Essential for Will saves.<\/p>\n

              Cha<\/span>: A little bit of Charisma is great if\n\tyou plan to play a Face, but otherwise you can dump Charisma to 7.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
