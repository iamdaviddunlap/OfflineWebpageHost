Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Swashbuckler is a very exciting take on non-magical combat in Pathfinder, which can become a boring war of attrition between stat blocks. The Swashbuckler has the ability to serve as a Face and a Striker, but could be turned into a Defender with a few feats.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Swashbuckler Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Hit Points<\/span>: d10 hit points is standard for\n\tmartial classes, but with only light armor and bucklers you will need to be\n\tcareful to defend yourself or you will burn through your hit points very\n\tquickly.<\/p>\n

    Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: Full BAB.<\/p>\n

    Saves<\/span>: Reflex is the Swashbuckler’s only\n\tgood save, and with good Dexterity it will be fantastic, but you really need\n\tto work to fill in the gaps in the Swashbuckler’s defenses. Charmed Life helps\n\ta bit, but it has limited uses per day.<\/p>\n

    Proficiencies<\/span>: You get martial weapons,\n\tbut with only light armor and bucklers, your AC isn’t going to be great until\n\tyou pick up Nimble.<\/p>\n

    Skills<\/span>: 4+ skill ranks is nice, and\n\tSwashbucklers get all of the skills required to be a Face.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

    Panache<\/span> (Ex): Panache fuels the\n\tSwashbuckler’s Deeds. You get a very limited pool, but you can recharge it by\n\tscoring critical hits or by killing worthy opponents. Because Panache is so\n\timportant, almost every Swashbuckler will use a weapon with a high threat\n\trange, such as a Rapier or Scimitar.<\/p>\n

    Deeds<\/span>: Swashbuckler Deeds, much like Gunslinger Deeds,\n\tare a list of special uses for Panache. Sweashbucklers get all Deeds\n\tautomatically, assuming that they are sufficiently high level. For more\n\tinformation on Deeds, see my\n\tSwashbuckler Deeds Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n

    Swashbuckler Finesse<\/span> (Ex): This is a\n\tfantastic option for a class dip for a ton of builds. You can use Weapon\n\tFinesse with any light or one-handed piercing weapons, which includes things\n\tlike heavy picks and spiked heavy shields. The ability to use Charisma in\n\tplace of Intelligence for Combat Feats means that you can get Combat Expertise\n\twithout the requisite 13 intelligence and pick up all of the cool combat\n\tmaneuver feats which require it. Remember that Slashing Grace allows you to\n\ttreat your selected slashing weapon as piercing for feats and such, so you can\n\tuse a scimitar or falcata.<\/p>\n

    Charmed Life<\/span> (Ex): Not quite as good as\n\tDivine Grace, but this will help compensate for the Swashbuckler’s poor saves\n\twhen save-or-suck effects arise.<\/p>\n

    Nimble<\/span> (Ex): Free AC is great.<\/p>\n

    Bonus Feats<\/span>: Swashbuckler levels count as\n\tFighter levels, which means you can take Fighter-only feats like Disruptive.\n\tThe retraining option is an often-overlooked benefit of Fighter bonus feats\n\twhich allows you to do ridiculous things like take Weapon Specialization at\n\t4th level, then retrain that choice at 8th level into Improved Critical, or\n\tsomething else with a level-dependent prerequisite. Don’t actually take\n\tImproved Critical because you get Swashbuckler Weapon Training.<\/p>\n

    Swashbuckler Weapon Training<\/span> (Ex): You are\n\ttreated as having Improved Critical 3 levels before you could get it, and\n\tprobably a level or two before you could afford a +1 keen weapon. The attack\n\tand damage bonuses are nice, too.<\/p>\n

    Swashbuckler Weapon Mastery<\/span> (Ex): Your\n\tRapier\/Scimitar now has 15-20\/x3 criticals with no enhancements or feats.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Str<\/span>: You might take 13 for Power Attack, but\n\tcertainly no more than that, and even that is difficult to justify. This is\n\tgenerally the Swashbuckler’s dump stat.<\/p>\n

    Dex<\/span>: The Swashbuckler’s primary ability;\n\tapplies to attacks, AC, Reflex saves, many important skills, and if you take\n\tSlashing Grace it applies to damage.<\/p>\n

    Con<\/span>: Swashbucklers need hit points to\n\tcompensate for their AC, and they need help with Fortitude saves.<\/p>\n

    Int<\/span>: Only needed for skills. If you plan\n\tto play a Face, don’t take less than 10, otherwise you can dump this.<\/p>\n

    Wis<\/span>: Only needed for Will saves.<\/p>\n

    Cha<\/span>: Determines the size of your Panache\n\tpool, and how many points you start the day with.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
