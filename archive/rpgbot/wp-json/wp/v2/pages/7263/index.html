              Angelic<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

              The go-to option for a sorcerer seeking to fill the role in a party typically filled by a cleric. The Granted Spells offer a good selection of iconic spells from the Divine Spell List, and while they’re not all amazing options, you get a lot of staples, leaving you plenty of room to diversify the rest of your Spell Repertoire. The Angelic Bloodline’s Focus Spells complement the rest on the subclass nicely, and provide some powerful tactical options that you can expect to use frequently and to great effect. Even the skills are good, and fit the theme and the role very well.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Taken as a whole, this is an excellent bloodline with a lot to offer. It does exactly what you want in a way that’s both effective and easy to play. You could drop an angelic bloodline sorcerer into a party in place of a back-line cleric with little difficulty.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Several of the Angelic Bloodline’s spells rely on your deity’s alignment,\n\tyour party members’ alignments, and your enemies’ alignments. For the maximum\n\tbenefits, choose a deity that’s at one of the extremes (lawful-good,\n\tchaotic-good, lawful-evil, chaotic-evil), play in a party that shares your\n\tdeity’s alignment, and face enemies that oppose your deity’s alignment.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
