Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Investigator is a clever fusion of the Alchemist and the Rogue, combining the Alchemist’s Extracts and Poison Use with the Rogue’s stealth and guile. Extracts provide excellent utility options, the Investigator’s skill list allows the Investigator to serve as a Face, Trapfinding allows him to serve as a Scout, and Studied Combat and Studied Strike make the Investigator a passable Striker. However, Studied Combat and Studied Strike both require melee attacks, which forces the Investigator to fight in melee combat with light armor and d8 hit points, and you won’t be able to match the damage output of a single-class Rogue.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Investigator Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points is always hard\n\tfor a melee character.<\/p>\n

          Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/3 BAB, the same as\n\tboth Alchemists and Rogues. Fortunately, Investigators depend heavily on\n\tStudied Combat in combat, so you would never be able to use iterative attacks\n\tanyway, and the Studied Combat attack bonus will exceed the gap between 2\/3\n\tand full BAB at every level at which it;s available.<\/p>\n

          Saves<\/span>: Good Reflex and Will saves.<\/p>\n

          Proficiencies<\/span>: Light armor and simple\n\tweapons, plus a short list of martial weapons similar to a Rogue. Your best\n\tbet is likely a Rapier.<\/p>\n

          Skills<\/span>: 6+ skill points is great, but the\n\treal appeal is the incredible breadth of the Investigator’s skill list.\n\tInvestigators get all of the skills available to Rogues, plus all knowledge\n\tskills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Alchemy<\/span> (Su): Just like the Alchemist\n\tability of the same name. You get just as many extracts per day as an\n\talchemist, which provides you a lot of really great options.<\/p>\n

          Inspiration<\/span> (Ex): Inspiration opens up a lot\n\tof really exciting options. Adding 1d6 to skill\/ability checks is pretty dull,\n\tbut can help when the stakes are high. You can use two Inspiration points to\n\tboost an attack or saving throw, but be careful not to burn through your\n\tInspiration too quickly.<\/p>\n

          Trapfinding<\/span>: This allows the Investigator\n\tto completely replace the Rogue as the party’s Scout.<\/p>\n

          Poison Lore<\/span>: Ridiculously situational. If you\n\tneed to neutralize poison, there are several spells which do it, or you can\n\tjust dump it on the ground.<\/p>\n

          Poison Resistance<\/span>: Poison becomes very\n\tcommon at high levels. The scaling resistance is plenty at low levels, but\n\timmunity at high levels is even better.<\/p>\n

          Investigator Talents<\/span> (Ex or Su):\n\tInvestigator Talents open up a lot of really great options, including some\n\tAlchemist Discoveries and Rogue Talents. For help with Investigator Talents,\n\tsee my\n\tInvestigator Talent Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n

          Keen Recollection<\/span> (Ex): You have all\n\tKnowledge skills as class skills and 6+ skill ranks. Throw a rank in each\n\tknowledge skill for the +3 class skill bonus, and pretend no one ever wrote\n\tthis ability.<\/p>\n

          Trap Sense<\/span> (Ex): Situational, and not very\n\tgood in the rare situations where it applies.<\/p>\n

          Studied Combat<\/span> (Ex): The insight bonus to\n\tattacks will easily make up the difference between 2\/3 and full BAB, and the\n\tbonus to damage is nice. This will only last a few rounds, so be sure to\n\tmaintain your Intelligence to keep this up for as long as possible, or you\n\twill spend a lot of inspiration re-studying your enemies in combat. The bonus\n\tis good enough that you might consider sacrificing attack bonus for things\n\tlike Power Attack or Combat Expertise. Be sure to take the Quick Study talent\n\tto drop the action from a Move to a Swift so that you can make a full attack\n\tor use poison in the same round.<\/p>\n

          Studied Strike<\/span> (Ex): Not quite as powerful\n\tas Sneak Attack, and it ends your Studied Combat, so use this sparingly,\n\tespecially at low levels when the dame will barely exceed the bonus from\n\tStudied Combat. Re-studying a target consumes Inspiration, so only use this if\n\tyou plan to change targets, or you think that you can kill the target, or if\n\tyou can spare the Inspiration points.<\/p>\n

          Swift Alchemy<\/span> (Ex): Crafting alchemical\n\titems faster is nice, but crafting typically happens off-screen in most\n\tcampaigns. Poisoning an item as a move action is nice so that you can apply\n\tpoison and attack in the same turn, but it will conflict with Studied Combat,\n\twhich is usually a better option.<\/p>\n

          True Inspiration<\/span> (Ex): Add 1d6 to all of\n\tyour ability and skill checks forever, and you can spend inspiration for\n\tanother d6.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

          The Investigator’s abilities look a lot like those of a melee Rogue.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Str<\/span>: Only gives you damage, but don’t dump\n\tit if you can avoid it. Without the Rogue’s big pile of Sneak Attack, you want\n\tevery bit of damage you can get. 13 opens up Power Attack, which may be\n\tworthwhile.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: With light armor, you want to depend on\n\tDexterity for Weapon Finesse and AC.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: Essential to compensate for the\n\tInvestigator’s poor hit points and bad Fortitude saves.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: Important for that awesome skill list\n\tand many of the Investigator’s abilities, including Alchemy and Inspiration.\n\tHowever, since Investigators are only 2\/3 spellcasters and are not dependent\n\ton DCs of any kind, you don’t strictly need more than 16 Intelligence.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: You need a bit for Will saves, but\n\tthat’s all that the Investigator gets.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: Only required if you plan to be a\n\tFace, and your skill ranks and Inspiration can offset a poor Charisma\n\tscore.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
