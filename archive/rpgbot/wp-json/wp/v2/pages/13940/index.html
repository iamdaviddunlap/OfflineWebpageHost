Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Summoner is the ultimate pet class in Pathfinder 2e, granting you a\n\tpowerful, customizable creature called an “Eidolon”. The Eidolon allows you to\n\tchange your role within the party, allowing your dynamic duo to serve as a\n\tDefender, Scout, and Striker, while the Summoner can serve as a Face with some\n\tlight Blaster, Healer, and Support options from their Bounded Spellcasting.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

While building an effective Summoner can be complicated, it’s not so complicated as building two wholly independent characters. Keep in mind the role you want your Eidolon to play, and look for options which clearly play to that role. Plan your spellcasting around your Eidolon’s capabilities, and be sure to bring some cantrips to fill various needs.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The complexity in the Summoner comes from managing their action economy. A typical character gets three Actions, potentially stretching that with things like Minions. But the Summoner’s Act Together action lets you overlap actions with your Eidolon, allowing you to take up to 4 Actions in a single turn without involving Minions or the Quickened condition (5 with Tandem Movement!). But you also need to consider positioning both yourself and your Eidolon in order to keep both of you safe, but still a position to be useful.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

If you look at the Summoner and think “I want a cool monster but I don’t want to juggle all the complicated action stuff”, look at the Meld With Eidolon<\/a> feat. It changes the function of the Summoner from a player-pet setup into a transformation sequence thing, allowing you to split your focus depending on whether you’re in or out of combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                    Summoner Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
                    <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                    The typical Summoner’s ability scores closely resemble the Sorcerer. However,\n\tsince the Summoner isn’t a full spellcaster, their Charisma isn’t applied as\n\tfrequently as it is for Sorcerers and other spellcasters. This gives you room\n\tto reduce your investment in Charisma and put those resources elsewhere,\n\tespecially if you want to fill in gaps in the Summoner’s defenses.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    However, not every summoner necessarily needs to be built this way. If you\n\ttake the Meld With Eidolon feat, your own ability scores are less impactful\n\tdefensively. You can likely afford to dump Dexterity and Wisdom since you’ll\n\tbe relying on your Eidolon’s defenses instead, leaving you room to invest\n\tthose ability scores however you like, such as investing more in Intelligence\n\tto support your skills. Regardless, you do need Constitution to support your\n\tshared pool of hit points, and you need enough Charisma to support whatever\n\tyou intend to do with your spells. Though, even that may be a minor need if\n\tyou don’t intend to your spells offensively.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    Str<\/span>: You do not belong in melee, and even if\n\tyou’re in melee you can use Gouging Claw rather than a weapon. Dump this.<\/p>\n

                    Dex<\/span>: AC and Dexterity saves. Crucial for\n\tstaying alive.<\/p>\n

                    Con<\/span>: You have terrible armor and you share\n\thit points with your Eidolon. Your Constitution score should be as good as you\n\tcan get it.<\/p>\n

                    Int<\/span>: Generally all that you get is skills,\n\tbut if you take an Eidolon with the Arcane or Occult tradition and want to use\n\tExtend Boost, you’ll need enough Intelligence to support the appropriate skill\n\tcheck.<\/p>\n

                    Wis<\/span>: Perception nad Will saves.<\/p>\n

                    Cha<\/span>: Your Key Ability score. If you plan to\n\trely on offensive spellcasting in combat (cantrips, spells cast by your\n\tEidolon, etc.), you want to maximize this. If you’re going to rely more\n\theavily on your Eidolon’s Strikes, there’s much less pressure so you can\n\tafford to distribute your boosts elsewhere. It’s technically possible to play\n\ta Summoner with low Charisma if your spellcasting is all buffs and utility\n\toptions, but that will strictly limit your spellcasting options, so I don’t\n\trecommend it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                      Summoner Skills and Skill Feats<\/h2>\n\n\n

                      You get Skill Increases at 3rd and 5th level to raise skills to Expert, increases at 7th, 9th, 11th, and 13th level to raise skills to Master, and increases at 15th, 17th, and 19th level to raise skills to Legendary. That means that you can maximize at most three skills, and the rest of your skills might not advance beyond Trained.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      You get Skill Feats at even-numbered levels, giving you a total of 10 Skill Feats (and maybe another from your Background) by 20th level. Generally, you want to invest these feats in the same skills which you are choosing to maximize, though in some cases you may want to grab feats from skills which don’t require that you be more than Trained.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      Skills for the Summoner are a little bit odd because your Eidolon uses your\n\tProficiency but their own ability scores, meaning that the skills which work\n\tfor you and the skills that work for your Eidolon may differ. It’s entirely\n\tpossible that between you and your Eidolon you could be effective at any\n\tskill. However, Intelligence is a dump state for the Summoner and none of the\n\tpubished Eidolon options have great Intelligence, so Int-based skills are a\n\tdifficult choice.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      Despite using your proficiency in skills, your Eidolon does not<\/em> get\n\tto use your Skill Feats.
