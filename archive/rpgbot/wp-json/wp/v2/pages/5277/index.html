Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Mesmerist does two things: It talks to creatures, and it stares at creatures. In a party it serves as a Face, and with a little bit of help it can serve as a Scout. It can also do some support stuff, but can’t compete with a normal spellcaster. Hypnotic Stare is the Mesmerist’s signature ability, allowing the Mesmerist to significantly debuff enemies. Unfortunately, the Mesmerist is capable of doing very little on its own, so if you play one you’ll need strong allies to do the heavy lifting in combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Mesmerist Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points is probably\n\tenough as long as you’re smart. Hypnotic Gaze locks you into fighting within\n\t30 feet, but you’re in light armor with no shields and mediocre Dexterity, so\n\tyour AC is going to be terrible.<\/p>\n

          Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/3 BAB is fine,\n\tespecially since you only need to attack when you have nothing better to do\n\twith your turn.<\/p>\n

          Saves<\/span>: Two good saves, but you’ll want to\n\tinvest in your Fortitude save.<\/p>\n

          Proficiencies<\/span>: Light armor, simple\n\tweapons, and a handful of other weapons. Except to spend a lot of time in a\n\tchain shirt wielding a sword cane.<\/p>\n

          Skills<\/span>: While not so versatile as the Bard\n\tor the Rogue, the Mesmerist gets 6+ skill ranks and has good skill list with a\n\tgood mix of knowledge and face skills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Spells<\/span>: 2\/3 spontaneous Charisma-based\n\tcasting. The Mesmerist’s spell list is almost exclusively mind-affecting like\n\tother psychic casters, and at only 2\/3 casting you’ll have issues with save\n\tDCs. Fortunately, Hypnotic Stare applies a big enough penalty to offset the DC\n\tgap between you and a full caster.<\/p>\n

          Consummate Liar<\/span>: The bonus to bluff is\n\treally nice, but the Mesmerist has absolutely no reason to feint.<\/p>\n

          Hypnotic Stare<\/span> (Su): -2 to Will saves is\n\tsignificant, and upgrading to -3 at 8th level is even better. For a class\n\twhich focuses so heavily on mental effects, this is absolutely essential.\n\tAnything with a high Will save might be out of your reach, and this penalty\n\tcould make or break you. This ability also sets the target for Painful Stare,\n\tBold Stare, and Stare Feats, which are the bulk of how you’ll contribute in\n\tcombat.<\/p>\n

          Knacks<\/span>: Cantrips are fantastic regardless\n\tof their name. Unfortunately your list of options is very short, but you still\n\tget essentials like detect magic and prestidigitation.<\/p>\n

          Mesmerist Tricks<\/span> (Su): Most Trick options are terrible,\n\tand the limitation of one implanted trick at a time makes the ability really\n\tdifficult to use in any meaningful way until you get Manifold Tricks at 5th\n\tlevel. See my\n\tMesmerist Trick Breakdown<\/a>\n\tfor help selecting tricks.<\/p>\n

          Painful Stare<\/span> (Su): It’s a bit like sneak\n\tattack, but you can only do it once per round and the damage is terrible.<\/p>\n

          Towering Ego<\/span> (Su): Will saves are important,\n\tand putting your Charisma bonus on top of your high Will saves should make you\n\tnice and resilient.<\/p>\n

          Bold Stare<\/span> (Su): You need Psychic Inception.\n\tYou will die if you do not take it. I cannot emphasize that enough. The other\n\toptions are cool, and some of them are even worth taking, but Psychic\n\tInception is too important for you to not have it. Take that first, and just\n\tpretend that you didn’t actually get a choice because any other choice is like\n\tleaning on the point of a sword and hoping it’s not sharp enough to run you\n\tthrough.<\/p>\n

              Dex<\/span>: In light armor you need Dexterity to\n\tboost your AC.<\/p>\n

              Int<\/span>: More skill points can be really\n\tuseful depending on your party makeup.<\/p>\n
