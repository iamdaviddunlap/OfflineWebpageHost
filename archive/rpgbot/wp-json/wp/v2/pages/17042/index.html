Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The\n\tHerbalist Archetype<\/a>\n\tis all about healing with Alchemical elixirs. Taking the dedication feat\n\tgrants\n\tbasic alchemy benefits<\/a>,\n\tallowing preparation of a limited subset of the Alchemist’s options. For\n\tHerbalist, this subset is any Alchemical item with the “Healing<\/a>” trait. This gives anyone that takes the Herbalist Archetype a bit of\n\tadditional healing to provide to the party, although it will be very small\n\twithout further investment into the later Herbalist feats.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Herbalist is great for anyone that wants to add a little extra healing to\n\tthe party, but it is unlikely to be sufficient on its own. Don’t take this\n\tarchetype expecting it to make you the party’s primary healer, but instead\n\ttake it to add some additional healing to a class which otherwise wouldn’t\n\thave any. The cleric is on the floor, but here comes the barbarian with eleven\n\therbs and spices to patch things up.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n

Herbalist vs Alchemist Multiclass<\/h3>\n\n\n

You might want to compare the Herbalist to the Alchemist Multiclass. For\n\trequirements, instead of a skill (Nature) and skill feat (Natural Medicine)\n\tthe Alchemist MC Dedication requires 14 Intelligence. Both dedications grant\n\tthe\n\tAlchemical Crafting<\/a> feat\n\tand a number of reagents per day. Notably, Alchemist does not require anything\n\tspecial to get your full level worth of reagents as opposed to Herbalist only\n\tgetting half for not being in the wilderness. On top of this, the Alchemist MC\n\tis not limited to any subsection of Alchemical Items and has an array of feats\n\tthat grant some of the Alchemist’s class features, such as\n\tQuick Alchemy<\/a> or other\n\tAlchemist feats.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

However, unlike Herbalist, the Alchemist MC requires two feats at 6th and\n\t12th to eventually gain an Advanced Alchemy level of Level-5. If you’re just\n\tlooking to add Alchemical Healing to your toolset, Herbalist will do more for\n\tyou with less investment in the long run. If you want a broader array of\n\tAlchemical power, you might look into the Alchemist MC or just take the\n\tAlchemical Crafting feat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        • Herbalist Dedication<\/span><\/a>\n\t\t2: The Dedication feat grants basic alchemy benefits, which in turn grant\n\t\tthe\n\t\tAlchemical Crafting<\/a>\n\t\tfeat.           \n\t\t\tAs for the rest of the basic alchemy benefits, we have a version of the\n\t\t\tAlchemist’s ability to make stuff for free each day, limited to healing\n\t\t\titems of 1st-level.         • Gravelands Herbalist<\/span><\/a>\n\t\t4 (Uncommon): This feat has multiple extra prerequisites of being trained in\n\t\tReligion and being a member of the Knights of Lastwall, but the benefit it\n\t\tprovides does not really justify being a feat, let alone having the extra\n\t\trequirements.           • Endemic Herbs<\/span><\/a>\n\t\t6: While the benefits are nice, it does unfortunately require spending an\n\t\textra batch of reagents to add the effect to one item, effectively giving\n\t\tyou half as many uses per day for a small extra bonus dictated by where you\n\t\thappen to be. It might have some uses, but I wouldn’t recommend taking this\n\t\tunless you’re using Free Archetype and you want to take both of the level 6\n\t\tfeats here.<\/li>\n\t
          • Expert Herbalist<\/span><\/a>\n\t\t6: Very simple, very useful. This allows your free daily items to finally be\n\t\thigher than 1st-level, and level-3 is actually quite fantastic. If you refer\n\t\tagain to the list of \u201cHealing<\/a>\u201d trait Alchemical items, you’ll be able to make the 2nd-level ones now,\n\t\tassuming you’ve been\n\t\tfilling out your formula book<\/a>. Technically you can wait an extra three levels to automatically learn the\n\t\timproved Elixirs of Life, but I would recommend learning those formulas as\n\t\tyou reach their minimum levels in order to craft a proper supply with the\n\t\tnormal crafting rules.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n

            Who Should Use This?<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Anyone who wants a little additional healing ability and can afford to miss a\n\tfew levels of class feats to get this minor in Alchemy would do well to pick\n\tthis up. The quickest route into meeting the\n\tNatural Medicine<\/a> feat\n\trequirement of the Dedication are to either take the\n\tHerbalist<\/a>\n\tbackground, with options of Constitution and Wisdom, or\n\tPlant Whisperer<\/a>,\n\twhich instead gives the options of Wisdom or Charisma.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
