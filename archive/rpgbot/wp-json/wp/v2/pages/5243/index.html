Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

While not particularly powerful, the Cad<\/a> fighter archetype is one of my favorites. The idea of a fighter who forgoes traditional training in favor of dirty tricks seems like a fun concept. The Cad is excellent at locking down high priority targets like casters or major enemies, but isn’t spectacular at damage output. if combined with allies who can benefit from crowd control effects (rogues, etc.), the Cad can be a major asset to the party.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Fighter Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Hit Points<\/span>: Between your d10 hit points and\n\tyour heavy armor, you are very durable.<\/p>\n

          Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: The biggest and best in\n\tthe game.<\/p>\n

          Saves<\/span>: Your fortitude saves are good, but\n\tyou will need to invest in your other saves.<\/p>\n

          Proficiencies<\/span>: You get the best\n\tproficiencies of any class.<\/p>\n

          Skills<\/span>: Fighters only get 2+ skills, and very\n\tfew of your skill choices are worthwhile, so you will likely dump\n\tintelligence.<\/p>\n

          Bonus Feats<\/span>: The main reason to play a\n\tfighter. Everything a fighter would want to take is a combat feat, and there\n\tare enough options that you never have to play the same fighter twice.<\/p>\n

          Armor Mastery<\/span>: DR\/- is great, and you should\n\talways be wearing armor.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Archetype Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Proficiencies<\/span>: The Cad gives up proficiency\n\tin medium armor, heavy armor, and tower shields. It may be worth spending a\n\tfeat on medium armor proficiency so that you can use mithral breastplate, but\n\twithout Armor Training, you won’t be able to move at full normal speed in\n\tmedium and heavy armor. You need to get into combat quickly, so your movement\n\tspeed is important.<\/p>\n

          Skills<\/span>: The Cad add Acrobatics, Bluff,\n\tEscape Artist, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth to their skill list. These fit the\n\tflavor of the Cad well, but the fighter’s limited skill points don’t give him\n\ta lot of room to enjoy these bonuses.<\/p>\n

          Dirty Maneuvers<\/span>: This is the Cad’s\n\treplacement for bravery, and fits the flavor of the archetype perfectly.\n\tDisarm and Dirty Trick are crucial for the Cad, and this provides an extremely\n\trare bonus to the Dirty Trick maneuver. Steal can be situationally useful, but\n\tI find that there are generally more important things to do in combat than\n\tpick people’s pockets.<\/p>\n

          Catch Off-Guard<\/span>:\n\tCatch Off-Guard<\/a>\n\tis a weird feat, but can be very powerful in a Cad’s hands. Proficiency with\n\timprovised weapons is fairly dull, but treating unarmed opponents as\n\tflat-footed opens up some options for the Cad, especially once he gets Deadly\n\tSurprise at level 7. This is particularly useful against NPC wizards\/sorcerers\n\twho don’t typically need weapons. Keep in mind that natural weapons count, so\n\tthis isn’t particularly useful against most monsters.<\/p>\n

          Payback<\/span>: While certainly in keeping with\n\tthe flavor of the archetype, this is strictly worse than Weapon Training. The\n\tCad is good at locking down enemies and preventing them from attacking you (or\n\tdoing anything else interesting). If payback comes into play frequently, the\n\tfight likely isn’t going well for you.<\/p>\n

          Deadly Surprise<\/span>: Level 7 is basically\n\tchristmas. At this point you should have all of the Dirty Trick feats, which\n\tis great because Deadly Surprise is probably the best thing that has ever\n\thappened to the Dirty Trick maneuver. Dirty trick is, by default, a Standard\n\tAction. With\n\tQuick Dirty Trick<\/a>, you can make a single dirty trick attempt per turn in place of your attack\n\twith highest base attack bonus, a limitation which isn’t placed on other\n\tcombat maneuvers like Trip and Disarm. However, your foe needs to be denied\n\ttheir dexterity bonus to use this ability. Use Quick Dirty Trick to blind your\n\tfoe, then use Deadly Surprise to pile on other status effects.<\/p>\n

          If you are planning to multiclass into other classes, now is the time to do\n\tit. Deadly Surprise is easily the defining feature of the Cad archetype, and\n\tthe higher level features do little to improve your maneuvers further.<\/p>\n

          Razor-Sharp Chair Leg<\/span>: Changing the damage\n\ttype for your improvised weapons is cute, but rarely necessary. The 19-20\n\tthreat range with improvised weapons is nice, but isn’t a game changer. It’s\n\tmarginally more important when you get Treacherous Blow later, but still not a\n\thuge bonus.\n<\/p>\n

          Craven Combatant<\/span>: You’re going to have\n\tCombat Expertise for your combat maneuver feats, and you will likely be using\n\tit to compensate for your lack of medium and heavy armor. If you are using\n\tcombat expertise, this effectively becomes Improved Uncanny Dodge.<\/p>\n

          Sweeping Prank<\/span>: At this point, you should\n\tbe getting to use dirty trick frequently due to Deadly Surprise, which makes\n\tSweeping Prank only useful on surprise rounds in which you start adjacent to\n\ttwo foes or in rounds in which you need to move. Even then, you could simulate\n\tthis effect by using Cleave and have the added benefit of dealing damage. At\n\t17th level you can use this ability against 2+dex modifier targets. This is\n\tmarginally better, but still only situationally useful.<\/p>\n

          Treacherous Blow<\/span>: Because you’re limited\n\tto a single immediate action per round, this won’t let you use Dirty Trick\n\tmore often, it simply provides a way to use it when your target isn’t denied\n\ttheir attack bonus.<\/p>\n

          Ultimate Payback<\/span>: This is a great\n\timprovement on Payback, but isn’t going to be particularly helpful unless your\n\tbuild focuses on critical hits.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Str<\/span>: Any melee character’s\n\tbread-and-butter.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: Potentially more important than\n\tConstitution on a Cad, dexterity adds to your relatively low AC, improves your\n\tCMD, and improves your reflex saves. You need at least 13 for Combat\n\tExpertise. Of course, if you need AC you can always use Combat Reflexes.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: You have d10 hit points and strong\n\tfortitude saves, but more constitution can only help.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: You need 13 for Combat Expertise, but\n\twon’t benefit much from anything past that. 14 might be nice for skill points,\n\tbut isn’t worth the points if you’re using point buy.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: The only real benefit you get here is\n\twill saves. While will saves are certainly important, your other abilities\n\tneed more focus. Plus, you can always take Iron Will if you need it.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: Dump to 7. Completely useless on a Cad.\n\tYour archetype is about fighting dirty, not about making friends.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
