Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Blood Hunter is a martial striker mechanically similar to the Ranger. It has a similar skill set, both in and out of combat, and building a Blood Hunter feels very similar.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

This handbook is for the 2020 version of Matthew Mercer’s Blood Hunter class, published on DMsGuild.com<\/a> (affiliate link<\/a>). The class’s original version is still available, and so is my Classic Blood Hunter Handbook<\/a>. While the two classes are thematically the same and share nearly all of their major design elements, Mr. Mercer has clearly learned a lot about class design in the years since he published the original Blood Hunter, and the jump in quality between versions is apparent. The 2020 version of the class is slick, well-written, mechanically novel, and extremely playable.<\/p>\n\n\n

                  What’s new in the 2020 Version?<\/h2>\n\n\n

                  If you’re transitioning from the previous version of the Blood Hunter to the 2020 version, it can be helpful to identify exactly what changed between versions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                  <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
                    \n
                  • Blood Maledict and Crimson Rite switched places; Blood Maledict is now awarded at 1st level, and Crimson Rite at 2nd.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Crimson rite no longer reduces your maximum hit points, both reducing the cost to use it (use it then drink a potion if you’re desperate to be at full hp) and making it easier to manage.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Players now learn one less Blood Curse, to a maximum of 5 at 18th level rather than 6 at 20th. The number of curses known is now listed in the class features table for your convenience.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Crimson Rite now lists when you gain additional options (Primal Rite, Esoteric Rite) in the class features table<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Blood Maledict’s 3rd use per rest is moved two levels higher, though all other increases to your use pool remained where they were.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • New Feature: Brand of Castigation \/ Brand of Tethering<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Grim Psychometry got a significant rewrite. It’s much more flexible now.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Dark Velocity renamed to Dark Augmentation; effects altered slightly<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Sanguine Mastery was totally rewritten<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Subclasses: The subclasses have seen a lot of changes. Shuffling around the levels at which the Blood Hunter gets subclass benefits necessitates some design changes, but the degree to which the subclasses have changes is considerable. Where the class’s core features recieved some polish and some fine-tuning, the subclasses are almost completely different in mechanical terms.\n
                      \n
                    • Ghostslayer: Almost everything has changed. Aside from the name and damage type of Rite of Dawn, none of the Ghostlayer’s mechanics were retained from the original version of the subclass.  <\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • Lycan: Originally published as a supplement for the Blood Hunter, Order of the Lycan made it into the revised version of the class alongside the other subclasses. Predatory strikes has a lower damage cap, Bloodlust no longer requires you to keep a running tally of damage you’ve taken in a turn, and Cursed Weakness is gone. Improved Predatory Strikes now adds a scaling attack bonus that used to come from Beastly Precision several levels later. Advanced Transformation got a rewrite, but Lycan Regeneration is unchanged. The higher-level features got reworked. Brand of the Voracious is new and it is very powerful.  <\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • Mutant: The Mutation Score mechanic is gone, significantly simplifying the subclass. In general, Order of the Mutant is easier to read and play. The list of formulae has expanded significantly, and the only option which was removed was Wariness.   <\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • Profane Soul: The spellcasting is unchanged, and while the patron benefits haven’t changed conceptually they’ve been rebalanced slightly. Diabolic Channel is replaced by Brand of the Sapping Scar so Order of Profane Soul is a bit less of an Eldritch Knight clone. Soul Syphon is replaced by Blood Curs eof the Souleater. <\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • Blood Curses: Every Blood Curse changed. Four were removed, 7 new options were added, and 4 existing options were altered to some degree. There are now Blood Curses which have prerequisites, including level requirements an subclass requirements.  
                      • New: Blood Curse of the Anxious<\/li>
                      • Blood Curse of Binding: Now also disallows Reactions. The Amplified version now has a 1-minute cap on duration so you can’t permanently bind creatures with poor Strength.  <\/li><\/ul>
                        • New: Blood Curse of Bloated Agony<\/li>
                        • New: Blood Curse of Corrosion<\/li>
                        • New: Blood Curse of The Exorcist<\/li>
                        • Blood Curse of The Eyeless: Range reduced, and now subtracts your hemocraft die from the attack rather than imposing Disadvantage   <\/li>
                        • Blood Curse of The Fallen Puppet: amplifying the effect no longer adds a damage bonus, but the creature can move up to half its speed.   <\/li><\/ul>
                          • New: Blood Curse of The Howl<\/li>
                          • Removed: Blood Curse of the Fending Rite<\/li>
                          • Blood Curse of The Marked: Totally rewritten. No longer tied to Crimson Rite.   <\/li><\/ul>\n

                            • Removed: Blood Curse of Mutual Suffering<\/li>\n\n\n\n

                              What’s new in the 2022 version?<\/h2>\n\n\n

                              The forum post on DnDBeyond<\/a> has the (mostly) full details, but I’ll cover the highlights.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              First, we got three new patrons to reflect new patrons added in sourcebooks released since the 2020 version of the Blood Hunter: The Fathomless, The Genie, and The Undead.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              Second, and perhaps most important, we now get to choose between Intelligence and Wisdom as your Blood Maledict stat. Unless you’re planning to multiclass into artificer or wizard, choose Wisdom.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              The rest is just rewording of the existing text for clarity. One notable change is that wording around effects which trigger from Crimson Rite damage have been updated to read “whenever you hit a creature with a weapon for which you have an active crimson rite”. In practice little has changed, but the text is more explicit which hopefully removes some arguments around potential abuse cases.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              There were some minor rules tweaks, so be sure to check the specific text of things like Blood Curses for wording changes. As an example: Blood Curse of Binding now affects Large or smaller creatures, where previously you cold Enlarge yourself to potentially target larger enemies.<\/p>\n\n\n

                              Blood Hunter Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

                              Hit Points<\/span>: d10 is standard for front-line\n\tmartial classes, and since the Blood Hunter sacrifices their own hit points\n\tit’s important to have as many hit points as possible.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              Saves<\/span>: Dexterity saves are important for\n\tmitigating damage from area damage effects, but Intelligence saves are rare.\n\tYou’ll also gain the ability to add your Intelligence modifier to your physical\n\tsaving throws, which will go a long way to keep you alive.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              Proficiencies<\/span>: Martial weapons, medium\n\tarmor, and shields. The Blood Hunter matches the Ranger’s proficiencies, but\n\tcan’t match the AC of the Fighter or the Paladin unless you find a way to get\n\tproficiency with heavy armor. The Blood Hunter also gets proficiency with\n\tAlchemist’s Supplies, allowing you to craft things like Alchemist’s Fire, as\n\twell as three skills from a list of class options.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              Hunter’s Bane<\/span>: The Blood Hunter is a class\n\tthat looks for any excuse to track a monster, so Advantage on Wisdom\n\t(Survival) checks to track some monsters is helpful.<\/p>\n

                              When you gain Hunter’s Bane at level 1, you choose your Hemocraft ability score,\nchoosing between Intelligence and Wisdom. Choose Wisdom. This ability score sets your\n“Hemocraft Modifier”, which is crucial for many of your class features.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              Blood Maledict<\/span>: Blood Maledict feels a\n\tlittle bit like spellcasting. It’s not spellcasting, but it certainly feels\n\tthat way. You get a maximum of four uses per short rest, so when you resort to\n\ta Blood Curse it needs to be a situation that really justifies it rather than\n\tthrowing it at the first enemy you see. Blood Curses notably only affect\n\tcreatures with blood unless you spend hit points to amplify your Blood Curse,\n\tso if you run into elementals, constructs, or some types of undead get ready\n\tto spend some hit points.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                              Fighting Style<\/span>: Fighting Style is a welcome\n\taddition for any martial character. The Blood Hunter gets all of the offensive\n\toptions, but unlike the Ranger they get no defensive options. With only medium\n\tarmor, the option to take Defensive would be nice, but the Blood Hunter is\n\tapparently too aggressive for things like AC. There’s also nothing appealing\n\tfor the Order of the Lycan to use while transformed, so if you’re considering\n\tOrder of the Lycan look for something that you can use while you’re not\n\ttransformed.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                \n\t
                              • Archery<\/span>PHB<\/a><\/sup>: The obvious choice for ranged builds. +2 to hit is a big deal in a game\n\t\twhere a 20th-level character can expect a maximum of +11 to hit.<\/li>\n\t
                              • Dueling<\/span>PHB<\/a><\/sup>: Note that this works while using a shield. 2 damage closes the damage gap\n\t\tbetween a longsword and a two-handed weapon (4.5->6.5 vs. 6.5\/7).<\/li>\n\t
                              • Great Weapon Fighting<\/span>PHB<\/a><\/sup>: This adds\n\t\tan average of just over 1 damage per attack on average<\/a>, and even then the only with a greatsword. Tragically, the Blood Hunter\n\t\tdoesn’t get a better option if you plan to use two-handed melee weapons.<\/li>\n\t
                              • Two-Weapon Fighting<\/span>PHB<\/a><\/sup>: The Blood Hunter’s attacks more closely resemble the Ranger’s than the\n\t\tFighter’s. You only get two attacks, and you can apply Crimson Rite to both\n\t\tweapons to get the on-hit damage boost. However, beware the stacking costs\n\t\tof Crimson Rite, and remember that many of the Blood Hunters abilities use\n\t\tyour Bonus Action so you’ll often need to give up your additional attack to\n\t\tdo something else.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n

                                Crimson Rite<\/span>: Crimson Rite adds a scaling\n\tdamage bonus which does similar damage to Hunter’s Mark or Hex, but costs you\n\thit points rather than spell slots. It’s a crucial part of the Blood Hunter,\n\tand many of your other class features will depend on you having Crimson Rite\n\trunning. If you’re going somewhere that you expect to encounter enemies, draw\n\tyour weapon, activate Crimson Rite ahead of time, and don’t put your weapon\n\tdown until you’re ready to rest. The effect expires when you take a short or\n\tlong rest, but otherwise if you don’t lose hold of your weapon you can get a\n\tlot of bonus damage for a fairly minor quantity of damage. <\/p>

                                It’s also interesting to note that the bonus damage is magic, but it doesn’t\n\tmake attacks with the weapon magic, so creatures which you attack might resist\n\tyour weapon damage but not the bonus damage from Crimson Rite. The damage\n\tbonus scales with level, and it feels really good on top of Extra Attack. The\n\tability is also worded to disadvantage two-weapon fighting and thrown weapons,\n\tbut it works really well for Crossbow Expert users. You need to spend\n\tadditional hit points and<\/em> an extra Bonus Action to affect additional\n\tweapons, so be very cautious about two-weapon fighting builds.<\/p>\n

                                  \n\t
                                • Primal Rites<\/strong>: You get just two of the three Primal Rites\n\t\toptions, so be sure to pick options which will be effective against as many\n\t\topponents as possible. Blood Hunters typically face evil creatures like\n\t\tfiends, so you want to take options which aren’t commonly resisted by those\n\t\tcreatures.\n\t\t
                                    \n\t\t\t
                                  • Rite of the Flame<\/span>: Nearly all fiends\n\t\t\t\thave resistance to fire damage, and it’s commonly resisted by other\n\t\t\t\tcreatures too.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
                                  • Rite of the Frozen<\/span>: Many fiends have\n\t\t\t\tresistance to cold, but not so many as have resistance to fire.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
                                  • Rite of the Storm<\/span>: Fiends usually\n\t\t\t\tdon’t have resistance to lightning damage.<\/li>\n\t\t<\/ul>\n\t<\/li>\n\t
                                  • Esoteric Rites<\/strong>: You only get one, and Rite of the Roar is\n\t\tconsiderably better than the other options.\n\t\t
                                      \n\t\t\t
                                    • Rite of the Dead<\/span>: Most things don’t\n\t\t\t\tresist necrotic damage, but the things that do resist it are evil\n\t\t\t\tcreatures which blood hunters can expect to face.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
                                    • Rite of the Oracle<\/span>: Psychic damage\n\t\t\t\tis resisted by very few creatures, but many undead and constructs are\n\t\t\t\toutright immune to it.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
                                    • Rite of the Roar<\/span>: Almost nothing\n\t\t\t\tresists thunder damage. The only better option is Force damage.<\/li>\n\t\t<\/ul>\n\t<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                                      Blood Hunter Order<\/strong>: See “Subclasses – Blood Hunter Orders”,\n\tbelow.<\/p>\n

                                      Extra Attack<\/span>: A crucial feature of almost\n\tany martial class.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                      Brand of Castigation<\/span>: A small but helpful\n\tdamage boost against large singular foes, especially if you can keep your\n\ttarget’s focus on yourself. Your Intelligence\/Wisdom likely isn’t higher than 16, so\n\tthe damage bonus won’t win fights on its own, but against foes with numerous\n\tsmall attacks it can add up quickly. Unfortunately you’ll need to be hit by\n\tattacks to trigger Brand of Castigation, so manage your hit points\n\tcarefully.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                      Grim Psychometry<\/span>: Make an Intelligence\n\t(History) check to ask the DM about the plot or when to find nearby monsters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                      Dark Augmentation<\/span>: The bonus speed is nice\n\tbut not particularly significant. The bonus to saves is the real appeal here.\n\tEven if your Intelligence\/Wisdom is just 14, +2 to three types of saving throws is\n\tsignificant, and if your Hemocraft Modifier improves later this gets even\n\tbetter.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                      Brand of Tethering<\/span>: Situational, but a\n\thelpful counter against enemies which can easily move between planes like\n\tspellcasters and some fiends. Brand of Castigation has no duration, so you can\n\tleave this in effect and make it much easier to chase across the planes. It’s\n\tunclear how this works against creatures using the Blink spell, but I can’t\n\timagine that it goes well for them.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                      Hardened Soul<\/span>: Fear and Charm effects become\n\tcommon at high levels, and many effects that don’t make you frightened and\n\tdon’t charm you are also considered “fear” and “charm” effects, so resistances\n\tlike this are much more effective than they appear at first glance.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                      Sanguine Mastery<\/span>: Rerolling a Hemocraft die\n\tonce per turn will be helpful nearly every turn. The most likely usage is to\n\treroll the bonus damage from Crimson Rite, and rerolling a d10 damage die is\n\tstill satisfying even it’s not a huge mathematical advantage. You can also\n\tuse it to reduce the damage which you take to activate Crimson Rite or to\n\tamplify a Blood Curse, and to improve the effects of many of your other Blood\n\tHunter abilities.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                                      Subclasses – Blood Hunter Orders<\/h2>\n\n\n

                                      Order of The Ghostslayer<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                                      This is the baseline for the Blood Hunter. It’s a solid archetype that broadens the Blood Hunter’s possible targets by allowing Blood Maledict to affect creatures without blood, and while it’s especially good at killing undead the Ghostslayer is still good at fighting everything else.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        \n\t
                                      • Rite of the Dawn<\/span>: Radiant damage is very\n\t\trarely resisted, making this your best Crimson Rite option until a least\n\t\t14th level when you would normally choose your only Esoteric Rite. The\n\t\tglowing weapon is helpful, but it also discourages you from activating\n\t\tCrimson Rite ahead of time if you’re trying to avoid notice. The damage\n\t\tresistance and bonus damage against undead are situationally useful, but\n\t\tobviously they’re fantastic against undead enemies which are common in many\n\t\tcampaigns.<\/li>\n\t
                                      • Curse Specialist<\/span>: An extra use of Blood\n\t\tMaledict is fantastic on its own, and the ability to affect creatures\n\t\twithout blood without spending hit points to amplify is really nice.\n\t\tConstructs, most elementals, plants, and many types of undead don’t have\n\t\tblood, so normally they’re immune to Blood Curses.<\/li>\n\t
                                      • Ethereal Step<\/span>: The ability to enter the\n\t\tEthereal plane and move about typically isn’t available until much higher\n\t\tlevel. For example: the Etherealness spell is a 7th-level spell. Use this to\n\t\twalk through walls and to avoid all sorts of other obstacles.<\/li>\n\t
                                      • Brand of Sundering<\/span>: At this level your\n\t\tHemocraft Die increases to 1d8, so the extra damage is nice. However, since\n\t\tBrand of Castigation is only usable once per rest you may be tempted to\n\t\twithold Brand of Castigation in case you encounter enemies which can move\n\t\tthrough creatures or walls like ghosts. It’s never fun to sit on such a good\n\t\tresource in case you need it, but I don’t see a way around it.<\/li>\n\t
                                      • Blood Curse of the Exorcist<\/span>: Charm and\n\t\tFear effects are very common as you reach high levels, so this will see a\n\t\tlot of use. The damage from Amplifying is automatic and exceeds the amount\n\t\tof damage that you take, so it’s nearly always worth the hit points to\n\t\tAmplify this.<\/li>\n\t
                                      • Rite of Revival<\/span>: You should always have\n\t\tCrimson Rite running in combat, which means that this is always an option.\n\t\tWell, unless you’re already at such low hit points that you can’t activate\n\t\tCrimson Rite. At that point it’s time to make a hasty retreat and live to\n\t\tfight another day.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                                        Order of The Lycan<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                                        Order of the Lycan is only slightly more complicated than the order of the Ghostlayer, but in a lot of ways it’s stronger. It has several numeric advantages which other characters can’t replicate without magic items, and Order of the Lycan is extremely difficult to kill.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        Even with its many strengths, Order of the Lycan still has limitations. The subclass is entirely melee-focused, and does not benefit directly from any of the Combat Style options available to the Blood Hunter. Bloodlust can also force you into a dangerous state where you might attack your allies unless you give up your lycanthrope form, which you can only use a few times before resting until very high level.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                        Fortunately, you can easily overcome these challenges: The Archery style offers an easy way to fight at range when your Order of the Lycan features aren’t useful, nicely addressing both melee-only nature of the subclass and giving you a meaningful way to use Combat Style. Bloodlust will remain a problem until you acquire Lycan Regeneration at 11th level unless your party has ample healing available. If someone in your party can cast Healing Spirit you should be just fine.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                          \n\t
                                        • Heightened Senses<\/span>: Hearing isn’t\n\t\ttypically as useful as sight, and smell is rarely used for Perception\n\t\tchecks. But Advantage on nearly any Perception check is still good.<\/li>\n\t
                                        • Hybrid Transformation<\/span>: For one hour, turn\n\t\tinto a combat monster. Well, more of a combat monster than you already were.\n\t\tYour hybrid form emphasizes fighting unarmed, but you can still use a shield\n\t\tand you should absolutely do so because you’ll need the extra AC to avoid\n\t\tbeing subject to Bloodlust for as long as possible. The hour-long duration\n\t\tcan carry you through several fights with sufficient healing options\n\t\tavailable, but if Bloodlust comes into effect and you don’t have enemies\n\t\tnearby for you to attack you’ll need to end your transformation early to\n\t\tavoid murdering your friends.\n\t\t

                                          Much like the Barbarian’s Rage feature, this is your go-to option in\n\t\t\tcombat, but since the usage is limited you technically can’t use it\n\t\t\twhenever you want. The hour-long duration is good, but you still need to\n\t\t\tbe careful to manage the effects unless you want to get into a fight\n\t\t\twithout the ability to transform.<\/p>\n\t\t

                                            \n\t\t\t
                                          • Feral Might<\/span>: A modest, but still\n\t\t\t\tuseful bonus to melee damage. Curiously, it’s not limited to weapon\n\t\t\t\tattacks so you can use it with spells if you want to do that for some\n\t\t\t\treason. Advantage on Strength checks is great for things like grappling\n\t\t\t\tand shoving. Strength is normally a dump stat, but if you want to Shove\n\t\t\t\tor Grapple things a little bit of Strength won’t cost you too much.<\/li>\n\t\t\t
                                          • Resilient Hide<\/span>: +1 AC will close the\n\t\t\t\tgap between medium and heavy armor, and since the bonus only applies\n\t\t\t\twhen not wearing heavy armor it removes any incentive to get heavy armor\n\t\t\t\tproficiency. You also gain resistance to common weapon damage types,\n\t\t\t\tthough unlike the Barbarian this resistance is bypassed by silvered\n\t\t\t\tweapons or magic.<\/li>\n\t\t\t

                                                Order of The Mutant<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                                                Order of the Mutant saw a lot of improvement from the original version, but it’s still held back by the severely limited number of mutagens that you can have readied at any one time. Because you can never have more than 3, there’s little incentive to prepare anything except your absolute favorite options unless you can find out what you’re facing well ahead of time.                                                 Exalted Mutation solves Order of the Mutants biggest problem, but it doesn’t come online until 18th level, at which point you’ve suffered so long that it hardly matters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
