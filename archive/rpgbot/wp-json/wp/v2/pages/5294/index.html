Oracle Archetypes often supplement or partially replace the abilities and spells provided by Oracle Mysteries, which can be a great way to cherry-pick the abilities you want from Mysteries which might have several bad options.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Archetypes<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Dual-Cursed Oracle<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    Dual-Cursed Oracle takes a second curse (without the benefits), gives up bonus class skills, and trades in three Mystery bonus spells for two extra Revelations and access to two extremely powerful revelations which allow you to make and force rerolls. The penalties can be fairly gentle if you plan for them, and the new Revelations are absolutely amazing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Oracle’s Curse<\/span>: Taking another curse hurts,\n\tbut a couple of the curses are fairly gentle.<\/p>\n

    Class Skills<\/span>: None? Really? This hurts, but\n\tif you’re going to dump intelligence it probably doesn’t matter.<\/p>\n

    Bonus Spells<\/span>: None of the spell options\n\tare hugely powerful, but they’re not completely terrible either.<\/p>\n

      \n\t
    1. ill omen<\/span>: It’s really hard to time this\n\t\tspell. Forcing enemies to reroll might make them more likely to fail a\n\t\tcrucial saving throw, or they might just burn the reroll on an attack. There\n\t\tis no saving throw, which is fantastic for really tough enemies, but be sure\n\t\tnot to use this on targets with Spellcraft.<\/li>\n\t
    2. oracle’s burden<\/span>: Because you have two\n\t\tcurses, this is twice as versatile as it is for most Oracles. Of course,\n\t\tit’s also much more useful if your curse is one of the more debilitating\n\t\toptions.<\/li>\n\t
    3. bestow curse<\/span>: Fantastic debuff.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

      Revelations<\/span>: Two additional revelations, and\n\tthe two new revelations made available are absolutely incredible.<\/p>\n

        \n\t
      • Misfortune<\/span> (Ex): Imagine this scenario:\n\t\tYou are a straight caster Oracle. Your Charisma is insanely high, and your\n\t\tspell DCs are nearly impossible to overcome. You cast Dominate Person or\n\t\tsomething on an enemy, and he rolls a natural 20, his only change of saving\n\t\tagainst your spell. You use Misfortune, force him to reroll, and watch as he\n\t\tproceeds to murder his former allies. You can use this as many times per day\n\t\tas you want, and there is almost no reason to not use it when an enemy\n\t\tpasses a saving throw. On top of that, you can use this on your allies, so\n\t\tyou can force them to reroll failed saves which would otherwise kill them.\n\t\tThis is also a great option if you are planning to class dip into Oracle for\n\t\twhatever reason because it doesn’t depend on your level in any way.<\/li>\n\t
      • Fortune<\/span> (Ex): A universal d20 reroll,\n\t\tusable multiple times per day. Absolutely fantastic.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

        Replaced Features: mystery class skills, mystery spells (2nd, 4th, 6th)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Compatible Archetypes: None<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Enlightened Philosopher<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

        Enlightened Philosopher resembles the Lore Mystery in many ways. Because this archetype depends on both Intelligence and Charisma, I would never use this for combat caster Oracle. However, for a straight caster Oracle it allows you to be the party’s Librarian in addition to the capabilities provided by the Oracle’s spells.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Alignment<\/span>: Not a problem in most parties.<\/p>\n

        Class Skills<\/span>: ALL knowledge skills.\n\tLinguistics can be mostly replaced with the Tongues spell, but opening up\n\tknowledge skills allows you to serve as your party’s Librarian.<\/p>\n

        Bonus Spells<\/span>: Enlightened Philosopher\n\treplaces every bonus spell except the one you gain at 2nd level. If your\n\tMystery’s spell list is bad, this may be a good choice.<\/p>\n

          \n\t
        1. owl’s wisdom<\/span>: The best you can really\n\t\tget from Owl’s Wisdom is a +2 to Will saves.<\/li>\n\t
        2. water walk<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
        3. discern lies<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
        4. true seeing<\/span>: A bit expensive to cast, but\n\t\textremely powerful.<\/li>\n\t
        5. wind walk<\/span>: Great long distance travel\n\t\tspell.<\/li>\n\t
        6. ethereal jaunt<\/span>: Great as an infiltration\n\t\tor escape mechanism.<\/li>\n\t
        7. moment of prescience<\/span>: Fantastic and very\n\t\tversatile.<\/li>\n\t
        8. astral projection<\/span>: Excellent way to\n\t\t“safely” travel to other planes.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

          Revelations<\/span>: Mental Acuity is fantastic.\n\tEnlightened Philosopher gives you access to all knowledge skills, so this gets\n\tyou extra skill ranks to fill out your skills and a bigger intelligence bonus\n\tto all of them.<\/p>\n

            \n\t
          • Mental Acuity<\/span> (Ex): A permanent bonus to\n\t\tIntelligence. Because this is an inherent bonus it won’t stack with Wish,\n\t\tbut by the time you could get wish you will already have a +4 bonus.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

            Final Revelation<\/span>: Charisma is a dump stat,\n\tso you’re not likely to get anything added to your saves, but the list of\n\timmunities is nice. You can take 20 on all knowledge checks, which means at\n\tthis point that you basically know everything, and you get a nice list of\n\timmunities. Being reincarnated is super cool, but probably won’t come up\n\twithin the scope of the campaign.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Replaced Features: Mystery class skills, Mystery spells (4th, 6th, 8th, 10th, 12th, 14th, 16th, 18th), Revelation (7th), Final Revelation<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Compatible Archetypes: None<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            The Planar Oracle is a logical complement to the elemental Mysteries, but not a very good one.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            4. planar adaptation<\/span>: Situational, but\n\t\trequired if you want to go to other planes with crazy climates like the\n\t\tplane of fire.<\/li>\n\t
            5. plane shift<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
            6. mass planar adaptation<\/span>: See Planar\n\t\tAdaptation.<\/li>\n\t

            8. etherealness<\/span>: Excellent for infiltration\n\t\tor escape.<\/li>\n\t

                Possessed Oracle<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                Possessed is a reasonably good archetype. The abilities and effects are vaguely related to being possessed, but not in the “I’m a vesel for an otherwordly being” way. More of the “slightly spooky shit happens around me and I find it just as unsettling as you do” sort of way. If your Mystery has a bad spell list, Possessed replaces most of it with some decent options, but otherwise leaves the Mystery in place.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Oracle’s Curse<\/span>: Haunted and Tongues are two\n\tof the least harmful curses, but it’s still annoying to need to take two\n\tcurses.<\/p>\n

                Bonus Spells<\/span>: Some very good options, some\n\tvery bad ones.<\/p>\n

                  \n\t
                1. ventriloquism<\/span>: Very situational, and easily\n\t\treplaced by Ghost Sound. Not on the Cleric\/Oracle spell list. Normally you\n\t\tdon’t get the first bonus spell until 2nd level, so I assume that this is a\n\t\ttypo.<\/li>\n\t
                2. spider climb<\/span>: Somewhat situational, but\n\t\tyou can bring this into play very easily, and it’s not on the Cleric\/Oracle\n\t\tspell list.<\/li>\n\t
                3. screech<\/span>: Nice way to get your allies some\n\t\tfree attacks, but the range is small and it still allow a will save before\n\t\tyour allies have to roll to hit.                 4. sleepwalk<\/span>: Very situational.<\/li>\n\t
                5. telekinesis<\/span>: Fantastically useful. Not on\n\t\tthe Cleric\/Oracle spell list.<\/li>\n\t
                6. animate objects<\/span>: Excellent way to get\n\t\tsome temporary minions without summoning, and it scales with level by\n\t\tallowing you to animate more\/better objects.<\/li>\n\t
                7. divine vessel<\/span>: Divine Power three levels\n\t\tbetter. Better bonuses, bigger buffs, and a whole bunch of resistances based\n\t\ton your alignment. Because it’s a Transmutation effect, but not a polymorph\n\t\teffect, you can combine it with effects like Enlarge Person. Exclusive to\n\t\tOracles.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

                  Revelations<\/span>: Two Minds is an excellent\n\tdefence against Enchantment effects.<\/p>\n

                  1. Two Minds<\/span> (Su): Enchantment effects are\n\t\tamong the most dangerous in the game, and the ability to reroll a Will save\n\t\twill save your life, even if it’s only once per day.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n

                    3. divination<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
                    4. find the path<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
                    5. discern location<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
                    6. foresight<\/span>: Fantastic buff. Insight bonuses\n\t\tare rare and expensive, and a 10 minute per level duration will get you a\n\t\tfew hours of protection. Not on the Cleric spell list.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n
