Magical Secrets Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

In 5th edition, bards have finally been upgraded to full caster and boy are they happy about it. No longer are bard players left feeling like they can’t enjoy the upper echelons of absurd spellcasting enjoyed by their friends. But, like every other Charisma caster, they have to lock in the spells they pick and, even though they have access to more and higher level spells now than in previous editions, there are still places where they could absolutely use some help. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

WotC decided to give them something unique to do just that: magical secrets. Every so often they can purloin a spell off someone else’s list and make it their own forever. So, how do we make best use of that?<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

I will break this document down into two and a half sections: one for most\n\tbards, a quick note for lore bards (who get these far earlier than other\n\tsubclasses), and one for swords bards because their playstyle is different\n\tenough to warrant its own discussion.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

      Most Bards<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
      <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

      Every bard will get to take two leveled spells or cantrips from any class (including bard) at each of levels 10, 14, & 18 for a total of 6 additional spells over the course of the character. To boil the entire premise of this entire article down to a single sentence I will pose a question: what do you need? Take that. If you’re in a party with many spellcasters you may have everything covered and may want to fill in your own solo capabilities or lean into your specialization. <\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

      <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

      If (more likely), you aren’t, look at the spell list of a caster not in your group and see what unique capacities you can pull from there to cover something. Magical secrets are usually best spent acquiring utility that is otherwise inaccessible to your party, and we particularly want to look at spell lists that are intended to provide power later than when we can get access to it: paladins and rangers.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

      <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

      Half casters have some great options that aren’t intended to be accessed by\n\tthese classes until levels 13 or 17 that you can take immediately at level 10.\n\tThese also tend to be the most unique spells as they’re balanced for just\n\tthose classes and can therefore do some interesting things when taken at lower\n\tlevels. Let’s get into some of the best options in my opinion for our first\n\tsecrets. For this first section, I will cover every cantrip and spell up to\n\tspell level 5 since this is the first time most bards will be able to take\n\tthem.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                  There’s a lot <\/em>to cover there, so let’s go over some things.                   You’ve probably spent most of your life using your cantrips to insult people for psychic damage.                   If you’re the primary healer in the group, Aura of Vitality is\n\tludicrous<\/em> amounts of healing for a level 3 spell, with the caveat\n\tthat you should basically never use it in combat.                   If you don’t have anything else to do with your bonus actions yet, Spiritual\n\tWeapon remains king among spells that should be concentration but aren’t.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  Revivify is so good that you should take it even if someone else in the party\n\talready has it because, if they die, you can use it on them. I would honestly\n\tonly not consider this a priority if your party already has two instances of\n\tit.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                                If you’re playing college of swords or valor, you’re not likely to be the primary healer in the party. This frees you up to be a little more selfish with your spells. One of the reasons I mention this is that, if you take Find Greater Steed (which you really should), you should pay special note to the line \u201cWhile mounted on it, you can make any spell you cast that targets only you also target the mount.\u201d This means that several of the buff spells listed above you can get double value out of, especially Armor of Agathys. Applying a ton of temp hp to your mount is a great way to increase its survivability, and getting free damage back on it is an amazing bonus. Woe betide any creature that tries to melee your pegasus out from under you. It’s also not concentration so you can run something else on both of you too.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
