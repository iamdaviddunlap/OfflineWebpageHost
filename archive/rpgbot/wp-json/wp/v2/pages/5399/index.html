Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

As characters grow in level, their Base Attack Bonus (BAB) increases. With\n\thigher BAB, characters gain additional attacks when making a full attack\n\taction. Fighter type classes with full BAB progression get their first\n\titerative attack at 6th level. The addition of a second attack vastly improves\n\tthe character’s effectiveness in combat, and can potentially double the\n\tcharacter’s damage.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Using your iterative attacks typically requires a Full Attack action. If you\n\tneed to move to reach your enemy, this means you must sacrifice your attacks\n\tin order to move. This tactical limitation is one of the biggest shortcomings\n\tof melee fighter characters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Enter Vital Strike. Vital Strike allows you to forego additional attacks in\n\tfavor of making a single attack with high damage. Because Vital Strike only\n\tmultiplies the weapon’s base damage (just the dice; no enhancements), Vital\n\tStrike seems like a poor tactical option. However, with some focus, Vital\n\tStrike can be an excellent option around which to center your character.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                  Benefits of Vital Strike<\/h2>\n\n\n
                    \n
                  • Accuracy<\/strong>: You always attack at your full BAB, which means that you should rarely miss.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Good Damage<\/strong>: You may need to buy more dice to construct the tower of d6s or d8s that you’re going to drop on your enemies. If you use miniatures at the table, I strongly encourage you to build towers of dice and then very deliberately topple them onto enemy miniatures when you roll for damage. Slightly impractical, but it really gets your point across.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Mobile<\/strong>: You get to use your move action every round. Move around a bit. Pull something out of your pocket. Open a door. Enjoy yourself as a move action.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Weapon Versatility<\/strong>: Is the BBEG flying out of range of your oversized sword? Switch to an oversized crossbow and shoot tent poles at him. You can use Vital Strike with melee and<\/em> ranged weapons without investing additional feats.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n
                    <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                    Drawbacks of Vital Strike<\/h2>\n\n\n
                      \n
                    • All or nothing<\/strong>: If you miss, you’re out of luck for the turn.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • Critical Hits are Boring<\/strong>: When you deal 16d8 damage, adding 4d8 won’t feel very exciting on a critical hit.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • No Charging<\/strong>: You can’t Vital Strike on a charge. If enemies are more than twice your speed away from you, Vital Strike with a javelin or something while you move up.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • Single Target<\/strong>: Your attacks are huge, but you can only kill one thing per turn.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n
