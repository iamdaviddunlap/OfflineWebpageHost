            Familiars in the Party<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Because Familiars determine their abilities based on their master’s hit points, BAB, and skills, familiars can fill many roles in the party as well as or sometimes better than their master.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Basic Familiars<\/h3>\n\n\n

            Common among spellcasters who make no effort to capitalize on their Familiars, a basic Familiar is still an excellent tool. They can serve as a delivery mechanism for touch spells, and because of their small size and good Dexterity they are often excellent at Stealth, allowing them to move into position to attack before the party kicks in the door. They can also act as couriers for Exploding Runes and Sepia Snake Sigil, or for delivering messages non-magically to nearby recipients.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Apprentice Familiars<\/h3>\n\n\n

            If your GM allows it, your Familiar may be able to use magic items, especially if you have Use Magic Device. This can make your familiar extremely useful if you hand them wands of Cure Light Wounds, or scrolls\/wands offensive spells which don’t allow saving throws, like Grease.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Remember that Spell Trigger items like wands and Command Word items both require that the user be able to speak, which limits your options to familiars which can speak like the Raven or the Thrush unless you take Improved Familiar or get items like a Ring of Eloquence<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Skill Familiars<\/h3>\n\n\n

            Especially useful if they have opposable digits, familiars can serve as Scouts nearly as well a Rogue. They lack Trapfinding, and can’t get the ability from their master, so be wary of traps.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            The exact mechanics of other skills are somewhat suspect. A wizard’s familiar gets the same modifier to Knowledge skills, but I don’t think any reasonable GM would allow a familiar to make a Knowledge check. Similarly, if your familiar can speak you might be able to use your familiar as the party’s Face if your GM allows it. You might also let your familiar make spot\/listen\/search checks by itself, but the ability to provide the Alertness feat seems to be intended to reflect your familiar aiding you.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Combat Familiars<\/h3>\n\n\n

            The biggest reason to pick up Improved Familiar (or any of the other special familiar feats) is because you want to turn your familiar into a fighting animal. There is a fantastically long list of wonderful options (see “Improved Familiar Options”, below), many of which can be very capable combatants. Because familiars use their master’s BAB and calculate their HP based on their masters, combat familiars generally work best for combat classes like Duskblade and Hexblade.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Archaeopteryx<\/a><\/h4>\n

            The worst flying familiar, the archaeopteryx is fast and can fly, but it has\n\tpoor maneuverability and an ability which makes its flight even worse. It has\n\tno notable special abilities. If you want something that flies, the bat, hawk,\n\tand owl are all better options.<\/p>\n

            Bat<\/a><\/h4>\n

            With flight, low-light vision, and blindsense the Bat is a portable sensor.\n\tOnce your familiar gains the ability to speak to you at level 5, it can locate\n\tnearby invisible enemies and point them out for you and your allies to attack.\n\tThe bonus to fly is largely worthless (you need to make fly checks about as\n\toften as swim checks), but it doesn’t hurt to have..<\/p>\n

            Blue-ringed Octopus<\/a><\/h4>\n

            An octopus is a scary predator to its prey, but when most things are medium\n\t(and therefore bigger than the octopus), its Grab ability is worthless. The\n\tblue-ringed octopus’s poison is very weak, and doesn’t make it any more\n\teffective.<\/p>\n

            Cat<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Hilariously effective at killing 1st-level Commoners and other weak\n\thumanoids, the cat is a good generic familiar. Cats get racial bonuses to\n\tstealth, making them excellent Scouts if their master doesn’t invest in\n\tstealth. Unfortunately the +3 bonus to Stealth will be wasted on most\n\tspellcasters, but Arcane Tricksters and other Rogue\/Caster hybrids may enjoy\n\thaving a cat. Cats also have Scent, which allows them to detect nearby\n\tcreatures.<\/p>\n

            Compsognathus<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            The compsognathus is fast, has low-light vision and scent, an grants a +4\n\tbonus to initiative. It lacks any useful skills, and its poison is very weak.\n\tAs a familiar it’s unremarkable, but the +4 bonus to initiative makes it\n\tappealing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

            Dodo<\/a><\/h4>\n

            The dodo is Small, so it can threaten squares, carry tiny riders, and attack\n\tinto adjacent squares, but it’s otherwise really useless. The +4 bonus to\n\tinitiative is fantastic for spellcasters, but there are several other (and\n\tbetter) familiars which provide the same bonus.<\/p>\n

            Donkey Rat<\/a><\/h4>\n

            The donkey rat strongly resembles the normal rat. Donkey rats are Small (and\n\tcan therefor threaten squares, carry tiny riders, and attack into adjacent\n\tsquares), they’re slightly less stealthy than normals rats, and they lack a\n\tclimb speed, but they have slightly better perception than rats, and have the\n\tsame special senses (Scent, low-light vision), and double the rat’s land\n\tspeed. Assuming you don’t want to keep your familiar in your pocket when\n\tyou’re not using it, the donkey rat might be a minor improvement to the\n\trat.<\/p>\n

            Fox<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Decent stealth, decent perception, and Scent. The fox is a decent, though not\n\tvery exciting, choice. It trades the rat’s versatility for a better default\n\tperception bonus, so if your party is good at perception the rat is a better\n\tchoice.<\/p>\n

            Goat<\/a><\/h4>\n

            The goat is small, which allows it to threaten adjacent squares, but its\n\tattacks aren’t good enough for it to be a viable combat option. The goat\n\tdoesn’t have any useful abilities, and the bonus to survival is probably\n\twaster on you.<\/p>\n

            Greensting Scorpion<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Less versatile than the scarlet spider, and with an even weaker poison, but\n\t+4 initiative is so useful that you might take this solely for the initiative\n\tbonus.<\/p>\n

            Hawk<\/a><\/h4>\n

            With a +13 to perception, the Hawk will easily outstrip its master’s\n\tperception checks until mid levels, even with the +3 bonus to Spot from having\n\ta Hawk familiar. Hawks fly, which is great for delivering touch attacks, but\n\tother flying options are better at stealth (owls) or can detect invisible\n\tcreatures (bat).<\/p>\n

            Hedgehog<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            Adorable, but totally useless. Even the +2 bonus to Will saves is bad because\n\tspellcasters nearly always have good Will saves.<\/p>\n

            King Crab<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Only useful for polymorph builds that intend to use grapple forms.<\/p>\n

            House Centipede<\/a><\/h4>\n

            The house centipede is fast and has darkvision (like every vermin), and its\n\tpoison dazes targets, which is absolutely fantastic. The bonus to Stealth can\n\tbe nice for stealthy casters, and the centipede’s +19 stealth is impressive.\n\tThe poison DC is half of the centipede’s HD plus its constitution modifier,\n\tbut with only 10 constitution it will never be particularly effective.<\/p>\n

            Lizard<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            The lizard has no redeeming qualities except a climb speed, and plenty of\n\tfamiliar options have climb speeds.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

            Monkey<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            The monkey has human-like hands, so it can do cool things like hold and use\n\tweapons and magic items, making it one of the best Apprentice Familiar options\n\tavailable without Improved Familiar. It has a climb speed, but doesn’t have\n\tscent and it’s not particularly stealthy or skilled. If you are skilled\n\t(Rogue\/caster hybrids typically are), the monkey can be a fantastic\n\tassistant.<\/p>\n

            Otter<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Adorable, but only useful in an aquatic campaign, and if you’re in an aquatic\n\tcampaign you probably want something that can breath underwater.<\/p>\n

            Owl<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Basically a stealthy version of the Hawk, and the Owl’s Spot bonus applies in\n\tdim light conditions, like those common to dungeons.<\/p>\n

            Pig<\/a><\/h4>\n

            At small size, the pig can carry a tiny rider (such as a Halfling affected by\n\tReduce Person), and threatens adjacent squares, but it’s certainly not going\n\tto win any fights. It has Scent, and the +3 to Diplomacy checks is nice, but\n\tit has to compete with numerous better options which provide Scent and with\n\tthe Thrush, which can fly.<\/p>\n

            Pig<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            Only useful in aquatic campaigns, and even then there are much better\n\toptions.<\/p>\n

            Raccoon<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Slightly stronger and slightly faster than the rat, but considerably less\n\tstealthy and lacking the rat’s swim speed. A bonus to Sleight of Hand is also\n\tconsiderably less helpful than the rat’s bonus to Fortitude saves.<\/p>\n

            Rat<\/a><\/h4>\n

            The rat is stealthy and has both climb and swim speeds, allowing it to get\n\taround a lot of places unnoticed. It has Scent and low-light vision, allowing\n\tit to help notice enemies. While it’s certainly not exciting, the Rat is a\n\tgreat baseline from which to measure other familiar options. The bonus to\n\tFortitude saves is also great for arcane spellcasters who typically have poor\n\tFortitude saves.<\/p>\n

            Raven<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            If you want to be talkative, the Thrush’s bonus to Diplomacy will be more\n\thelpful. Appraise is garbage. However, because the raven can speak it may be\n\table to use magic items.<\/p>\n

            Scarlet Spider<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Mechanically very similar to the viper, but its poison deals Strength damage\n\tinstead of Constitution damage, making it considerably less effective. It also\n\thas high Acrobatics, allowing it to more easily enter enemy’s squares, and\n\tit’s an excellent climber.<\/p>\n

            Sloth<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            Iconic for being slow, the sloth’s stats are true to its species. It is slow,\n\tits attacks are garbage, and its only redeeming quality is Scent, which you\n\tcan find on plenty of other familiars.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

            Snapping Turtle<\/a><\/h4>\n

            The snapping turtle is slow, and doesn’t really have anything special but\n\tScent. If you want something with Scent you can do considerably better.<\/p>\n

            Thrush<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            Basically a smaller raven, so it’s harder to notice and to hit, but it’s also\n\tless capable of carrying things. The ability for your Thrush to speak is\n\tsituational, but if you’re playing a Face the +3 bonus to Diplomacy can be\n\treally nice. The Thrush is one of only two basic familiars which can speak,\n\twhich may allow it to use wands.<\/p>\n

            Toad<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Dismally slow, no useful abilities, and 3 hit points is garbage. It can’t\n\teven swim. Somehow the toad gets a +21 to Stealth, but the ability to sit\n\tstill and not be noticed is not enough to redeem the toad.<\/p>\n

            Tuatara<\/a><\/h4>\n

            Basically a lizard (but technically a dinosaur!), the Tuatara has both\n\tlow-light vision and Darkvision, but is otherwise just a scaly weasel. A\n\tSurvival bonus is very hard for most spellcasters to use.<\/p>\n

            Turtle<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            Though the snapping turtle is technically stronger, both turtle are bad\n\tenough that they are functionally identical. The natural armor bonus to AC\n\tmight be appealing for melee casters and polymorph builds, but I’m not certain\n\tif it stacks with other natural armor bonuses.<\/p>\n

            Viper<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            The viper’s big appeal is its poison. The poison’s DC scales with your\n\tfamiliar’s effective hit dice, but with only 10 constitution you’re going to\n\thave a lot of trouble getting enemies to fail their saving throws. You may be\n\table to harvest poison from your familiar if someone in the party can use it.\n\tAlso note that in Pathfinder, multiple doses increase the save DC of poison\n\taffecting the target by 2 instead of forcing separate saves, so you may be\n\table to improve the poison’s usefulness by applying it multiple times.<\/p>\n

            Weasel<\/a>\n<\/h4>\n

            A climb speed and scent are the weasel’s biggest advantages, but the Rat has\n\tthose plus a swim speed, and Reflex saves aren’t as important as Fortitude\n\tsaves. The Attach special ability is amusing, but in practice it won’t do\n\tanything useful.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
