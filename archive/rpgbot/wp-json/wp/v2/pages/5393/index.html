Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Veiled Illusionist is a fun take on Illusionists. The core mechanic is the Veil pool, which powers a collection of abilities based on whatever disguise you adopt. It’s not screamingly powerful, but the flavor is great and with some creativity the Veiled Illusionist can add some exciting new options for illusionists.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Veiled Illusionist is a full caster closest to a Wizard, but because of their emphasis on illusions their skillset can fall somewhere between a Rogue and a Wizard, which can make them hard to fit into a party which required specific roles to be filled.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Much of the Veiled Illusionist’s decisions (including gear) are essentially identical to those of a Wizard, so be sure to check out my Wizard Handbook<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n

        Veiled Illusionist Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

        Hit Points<\/span>: d6 hit points is common for\n\tstraight casters.<\/p>\n

        Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 1\/2 BAB, but you should\n\tnever need it.<\/p>\n

        Saves<\/span>: Will is the Veiled Illusionist’s\n\tonly good save, which probably matches the good saves of your base class.<\/p>\n

        Proficiencies<\/span>: None.<\/p>\n

        Skills<\/span>: 2+ skill ranks and a\n\tvery<\/em> small skill list. Fortunately Pathfinder’s skill system is\n\tfairly flexible, so Veiled Illusionists with lots of skill points can continue\n\tto easily invest in the same skills which they took with their base class.<\/p>\n\n

        Spells per Day<\/span>: Like most prestige classes\n\twhich advance your spellcasting, you only get additional spells per day (and\n\tspells known for spontaneous casters). Arcanists, Witches, and Wizards all\n\tneed to go spend money to learn additional spells.<\/p>\n\n

        Veil Pool<\/span> (Sp): The Veil Pool is the source\n\tof all of the Veiled Illusionist’s abilities, and the class is limited by the\n\tpoints in the pool. Any Veiled Illusionist should do everything they can to\n\tboost the size of their pool, or they will find themselves depending solely on\n\ttheir spells.<\/p>\n

Using this ability is basically the “on switch” for your class.\n\t\tThe duration is hours\/level, so at early class levels you’ll spend many of\n\t\tyour Veil Pool points just keeping this turned on, but this will taper off\n\t\tquickly as you gain class levels, just in time for you to make use of your\n\t\tother class abilities.<\/li>\n\t
        2. I’m not sure exactly what this entails with Disguise Self. Most of the time\n\t\tyou’re going to change from something human-like to something else\n\t\thuman-like. It might matter when you can change into a Cyclops and want to\n\t\tsound like a big stompy giant.<\/li>\n\t
        3. The touch component is weird, but the ability to mimic voices is\n\t\timportant.<\/li>\n\t

Bards will find \n\t\tthis particularly helpful because their spell list is more limited than \n\t\tother base class options. You don’t get \n\t\tto shuffle your spells known whenever you change veils.<\/li>\n\t
          • Halfling<\/span>: Very situational, and very \n\t\tlimited. If you don’t want people to know what you’re casting, cast \n\t\tInvisibility.<\/li>\n\t
          • Elf<\/span>: Spell Resistance is hugely \n\t\tproblematic at higher levels, and rerolling a d20 roughly equals a +5 \n\t\tbonus. Combined with Spell Penetration, you shouldn’t have any issues.<\/li>\n\t
          • Gnome<\/span>: This means that you can maintain \n\t\ta spell like Silent Image and move it around while still casting other \n\t\tspells. However, remember that the cost is one point for each time you \n\t\tconcentrate, so you’re going to burn through your entire pool in a few \n\t\trounds.               Note regarding poison DC’s for polymorph effects<\/strong>: I’m not sure how to calculate the DC of the Naga’s poisons. Since the veils are a spell-like ability, it should use the universal monster rules for Spell-like Abilities<\/a>. The spell replicated by True Veil is unclear for the Cyclops\/Naga forms, but Veil Pool uses disguise self, which means that the poison DC is 10 +1 (Disguise Self’s spell level) +Charisma. Alternatively, you might rule that the DC is based on the DC of the originating effect (which makes sense based on the general Polymorph rules), so your DC is 15 + the number of points in your Veil Pool.               Base Classes<\/h2>\n\n\n

              Full casters are an obvious choice.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                \n\t
              • Arcanist<\/span>: The Arcanist’s unique spell \n\t\tpreparation and casting mechanic works very well for the Veiled \n\t\tIllusionist because illusionists depend on a very fixed set of spells to \n\t\tsolve all of their problems. The Arcanist’s dependence on Charisma for \n\t\texploits and spell DC’s is frustrating, but it works well for Bluff and \n\t\tDisguise.<\/li>\n\t
              • Bard<\/span>: Bard is a tempting option as an \n\t\tinteresting build, but it simply can’t match the effectiveness of a full \n\t\tcaster. The Bard’s skills will fall off sharply once they’re stuck with \n\t\tthe Veiled Illusionist’s 2+ skill points per level, and Bardic Music will \n\t\tstop being a good option.<\/li>\n\t
              • Cleric<\/span>: A Trickery domain cleric gets \n\t\tenough illusion spells to qualify for the class. Cleric doesn’t really \n\t\tplay to the concept of the Veiled Illusionist, but I could see some \n\t\tinteresting characters coming out of a Trickery Cleric.<\/li>\n\t
              • Sorcerer<\/span>: While not as powerful or \n\t\tversatile as an Arcanist of Wizard, the Sorcerer’s huge pile of spells\n\t\tper \n\t\tday allow you to cast illusions frequently without running out of spell \n\t\tslots. Unfortunately none of the bloodlines play into the Veiled \n\t\tIllusionist particularly well, but Shadow is a decent choice if you’re \n\t\talso your party’s Scout.<\/li>\n\t
              • Witch<\/span>: The Witch’s big draw is Hexes, \n\t\tand since you’re not sticking to Witch your Hexes won’t be great. If you \n\t\topt for Witch, stick to utility Hexes over those with a save DC like \n\t\tsave-or-suck Hexes.<\/li>\n\t
              • Wizard<\/span>: The Wizard is an obvious\n\t\tchoice, \n\t\tespecially with the Illusionist school, and the Wizard’s natural \n\t\tversatility allows the Veiled Illusionist to do a lot of utility casting \n\t\tbeyond their illusions.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n

                Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

                The Veiled Illusionist’s abilities will be identical to those of your base class.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Races<\/h2>\n\n\n

                Any race which works well for your base class will work well for the Veiled Illusionist. Gnomes are a particularly good choice for their racial bonus to Illusion DC’s.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Traits<\/h2>\n\n\n

                Traits which work for your base class will work for Veiled Illusionist. Resist the urge to pick up traits which get you Bluff and Disguise as class skills because you will get the class skill bonus from your first level of Veiled Illusionist, which essentially erases the benefit for the traits.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Skills<\/h2>\n\n\n
                  \n\t
                • Bluff<\/span> (Cha): Essential for any Face, \n\t\tespecially one who gets by on illusion and disguises.<\/li>\n\t
                • Disguise<\/span> (Cha): The Veil abilities\n\t\twill \n\t\tgive you a hefty bonus to Disguise checks, but a few ranks can put you \n\t\twell out of reach of other creature’s Perception checks.<\/li>\n\t
                • Knowledge (arcana)<\/span> (Int): One of the \n\t\tmost important Knowledge skills.<\/li>\n\t
                • Knowledge (religion)<\/span> (Int): One of the \n\t\tmost important Knowledge skills.<\/li>\n\t
                • Spellcraft<\/span> (Int): Someone in your party \n\t\tneeds to have it, and it might as well be you.<\/li>\n\t

                  Example Build – Elf Wizard Veiled Illusionist<\/h2>\n\n\n

                  I honestly have no idea how many people are in this adventuring party. Every time I turn around there’s some new guy there. Sometimes a giant snake shows up for no readily apparent reason.<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  This is an extremely generic build, but it’s a great baseline from which to work. Because this is a Wizard-based build, this build is intended to fill the role of your party’s primary arcane spellcaster.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                  We’ll take the Illusionist school (obviously), but we’ll avoid the subschools because their lower level abilities are bad and you won’t get enough Wizard levels to get their better abilities until extremely high level. Give up Necromancy or Evocation.<\/p>\n\n\n

                      Additional Skills<\/h4>\n\n\n

                      As you improve your Intelligence, you gain skill points retroactively, so you’ll pick up additional skills at 8th level and 16th level. In additon, the Headband of Vast Intelligence grants you ranks in one skill equal to your hit dice, so your +6 Headband of Vast Intelligence gets you three free skills!<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      • Diplomacy: You already have bluff and a splash of Charisma, so why not be the Face?<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                          Traits<\/h3>\n\n\n

                          Almost anything will work really. Reactionary provides a fantastic +2 bonus to Initiative, and Life of Toil helps with our garbage Fortitude saves.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                          If your party needs a Face more than a Librarian, drop two of your Knowledge skills and pick up the Ease of Faith and Outcast’s Intuition traits to get Diplomacy and Sense Motive as class skills.<\/p>\n\n\n
