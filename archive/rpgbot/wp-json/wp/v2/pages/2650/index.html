Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

This handbook is for version 2.1 of Matt Mercer’s Blood Hunter<\/a> (Affiliate Link<\/a>). I am planning to cover the newer version released in January of 2020<\/a>, but have not yet had the opportunity to so. Please check back periodically for updates, or follow me on Facebook<\/a>, Patreon<\/a>, and Twitter<\/a> to see site updates as they’re pubished.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Blood Hunter is a martial striker mechanically similar to the Ranger. It has a similar skill set, both in and out of combat, and building a Blood Hunter feels very similar. You could easily take any Ranger and replace all of their class levels with Blood Hunter levels and have a perfectly functional character.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

That said, the Blood Hunter is a complicated class. It has several resource pools to track, and Crimson Rite allows you to temporarily alter your own hit point maximum. If you’re intimidated by math, or if you find spellcasters difficult to play, you may not enjoy the class. But if you’re comfortable managing resource pools and like making life-or-death risk-reward decisions in the thick of combat, the Blood Hunter has a lot to offer.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

While the flavor of the class is interesting and the abilities are thematic and exciting, the text of the class is somewhat rough and may be difficult to interpret at times. I’ll make an effort to clarify mechanics where I think it’s helpful.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Blood Hunter Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Hit Points<\/span>: d10 hit dice are standard for a\n\tfront-line martial class.<\/p>\n

            Saves<\/span>: Strength saves are fairly rare, but\n\tWisdom saves are common and typically very problematic.<\/p>\n

            Proficiencies<\/span>: Martial weapons, shields,\n\tbut only medium armor.
