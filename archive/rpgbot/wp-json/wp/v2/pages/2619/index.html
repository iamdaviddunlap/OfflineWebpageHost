Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Artificer is a class with a tool for every job and a solution to every\n\tproblem. They excel as a Support character, but make decent Defenders,\n\tHealers, and Strikers, too. With the right infusions and spells, they can fill\n\tnearly any role in the party, making the Artificer’s versatility rival that of\n\tthe Bard.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Artificer has been a popular concept since at least 3rd edition, where\n\tthe Artificer first appeared as a class unique to the Eberron campaign\n\tsetting. Since then, the class has reappeared in 4th edition, and was in high\n\tdemand when 5th edition was released, but didn’t see a final release until\n\tfully 5 years after 5th edition’s initial release. The concept of a character\n\twho performs magic by binding it to items and who crafted all sorts of\n\ttechnological or magical gadgets is a fun novelty in a game where magic rarely\n\ttakes those forms.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

However, the Artificer is complicated. This is not a class I would recommend\n\tfor new players or for players who suffer from analysis paralysis. The\n\tArtificer has more decision points than any class to date, including the\n\tWizard. Every time you finish a long rest you can reset your prepared spells,\n\tshuffle where you apply all of your infusions, and pick magic items from a\n\tlist of some 40+ options. While some of these decisions may remain static for\n\tlong periods of time, the intent of the class is that you will tailor your\n\tabilities day-to-day to suit the challenges you expect to face. While that\n\tversatility and adaptability is very powerful, it also requires a great deal\n\tof micromanagement of your character.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This is the sort of class that can be rewarding for players who enjoy\n\t“crunch” and fiddling with their character’s build, but which will be\n\tabsolutely punishing for players who don’t like to spend hours agonizing over\n\tthe differences between individual character options. The need to manage\n\tspells and infusions plus the mental load of trying to make tool proficiencies\n\tuseful is often frustrating for many players, but I have always enjoyed\n\texactly those sorts of challenges, so I’ve enjoyed artificers every time I’ve\n\tplayed one.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Several of the Artificer’s features are related to crafting and to magic\n\titems. If your game does not allow item crafting or does not use magic items,\n\tyou’re going to miss out on those features. Of course, you may also be the\n\tonly source of magic items, which makes you a very popular member of your\n\tparty. Most published games don’t leave space for downtime and crafting and\n\talso provide pre-determined magic items which might not appeal to your\n\tparty.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In those situations I’ve found that changing your infusions to fill gaps for your party is immensely effective, and your party may be willing to trade permanent magic items among the group if everyone ends up happy with whatever items and infusions they’re using at any given moment.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This guide is for the latest version of the Artificer class. The full version\n\twas originally published in Eberron: Rising from the Last War, and updated in\n\tTasha’s Cauldron of Everything. If you are currently using the version\n\tpublished in Eberron, I recommend checking the\n\tErrata document<\/a>\n\tfor the latest improvements, as the changes are significant.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Artificer Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nHit Points<\/span>: With d8 hit points and medium\n\tarmor, the artificer is not a tank by any means. You’ll probably want to\n\tremain at range, but if you find yourself in melee a lot be sure to pad your\n\thit points with False Life, plenty of Constitution, and as many AC boosts as\n\tyou can manage.<\/p\n>\nSaves<\/span>: Constitution is great, especially\n\tsince many artificers fight on the front lines where you’ll be repeatedly\n\tmaking saves to maintain Concentration. Intelligence saves are exceptionally\n\trare.<\/p\n>\nProficiencies<\/span>: Medium armor and shields\n\twill keep you alive in melee, but with only simple weapons your best Finesse\n\toption is a dagger. That’s fine for most artificers since fighting with ranged\n\tspells is your best option, but the Battlesmith will eventually take up\n\tmartial weapons and use Intelligence for their attacks and damage. The\n\tArtificer’s skills are mostly Intelligence and Wisdom-based, and most are\n\tknowledge skills, but Sleight of Hand is an option. You also get three tool\n\tproficiencies, which gives you room to tailor your character to the theme\n\tyou’re going for. Your Artificer Specialist (your subclass) will grant you proficiency in an\n\tadditional set of tools relevant to the subclass at 3rd level.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n\n
              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nMagical Tinkering<\/span>: This is very similar to\n\tcantrips like Prestidigitation. The effects are interesting and unique, and if\n\tyou’re clever you can come up with all kinds of uses for Magical Tinkering.\n\tYou can have multiple objects affected at the same time, so consider carrying\n\taround a few prepared items which you can quickly produce and use.<\/p\n>\nSpellcasting<\/span>: Artificers are a 2\/3 caster\n\tthat prepares and casts spells like a cleric (prepare daily from the full\n\tclass list). You get ritual casting, which is always great, and the spell list\n\tis a combination of options from the cleric and wizard spell lists, allowing the Artificer\n\tto serve as a Blaster, a Healer, and a Support caster. Notably, the Artificer\n\tcan retrain a cantrip every level. To the best of my knowledge, the Artificer\n\tis the only class with the ability to replace cantrips unless you allow\n\toptional class features.<\/p\n>\nThe Artificer’s spellcasting foci are also unique. Rather than a wand or\n\tsomething, you use Thieves’ Tools or a set of Artisan’s Tools. You can wave a\n\tset of lockpicks around to cast fireball, which I think will inevitably lead\n\tto some laughs at the table and countless goofy characters using weird tools\n\tto perform magic.<\/p\n>\nYou can also use any item that’s the subject of one of your infusions, which\n\tmeans that if you have infused a weapon or a shield you can easily have a\n\tfocus in hand without dropping your weapon or shield to pull out a wand or\n\tsomething. Your choice of subclass will add additional focus options, but\n\tthey’re typically no better than what the Artificer gets by default, though\n\tthey’ll certainly fit the theme of your subclass.<\/p\n>\nIt’s also very important to note that the Artificer must always<\/em> use\n\ta focus when casting spells. Errata and the updated version of the Artificer\n\tclass explain that this adds a Material component to all of the Artificer’s\n\tspells. Thanks to the core rules for spell components, this means that you can\n\talways use the hand holding your focus to perform somatic components. That\n\tmakes the Artificer the only spellcaster who can perform every one of their\n\tspells with items in both of their hands. (Typically you can’t perform somatic\n\tcomponents with a focus in your hand unless the spell also requires an\n\tinexpensive material component. I complain about this rule frequently.)<\/p\n>\nArtificers get surprisingly few prepared spells (level\/2+Int), which can be\n\tvery limiting and makes good spell selection crucial. For help selecting\n\tspells, see my\n\tArtificer Spell List Breakdown<\/a\n\t>.<\/p\n>\n\nInfuse Item<\/span>: This may be the Artificer’s\n\tmost iconic ability. You get to start with two infused items chosen from four\n\tInfusion known, which is like getting two magic items at 2nd level. For\n\tadvice on Infusions, see my\n\tArtificer Infusions Breakdown<\/a\n\t>.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

              Artificer Specialist<\/strong>: Artificer subclasses are briefly\n\tsummarized below. See my\n\tArtificer Subclasses Breakdown<\/a>\n\tfor help selecting your subclass.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                \n
              • Alchemist<\/a>: Add new support and healing options, as well as bonus healing and bonus\n\t\tdamage with spells which deal certain damage types.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              • Armorer<\/a>: Don a suit of magic armor and smash or blast your foes in combat.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              • Artillerist<\/a>: Emphasize the Artificer’s abilities as a Blaster, and add the ability to\n\t\tsummon magical canons to aid you in combat.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              • Battle Smith<\/a>: Focus on fighting with weapons alongside your new Steel Defender, a\n\t\tsturdy pet construct designed for combat.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nThe Right Tool for the Job<\/span>: You technically\n\tnever receive a free set of tools except for the Thieves’ Tools included in\n\tthe Artificer starting equipment. If you’re short on gold, you might not be\n\table to afford the tools related to your subclass. Instead, you get this. I\n\tthink the expectation is that you can use this to craft the tools for your\n\tsubclass for free, and trade them out for other tools when you need them.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\nTool Expertise<\/span>: There’s a reason WotC is\n\tcomfortable granting universal expertise with tools: unless the DM is going\n\tout of their way or you are making a truly impressive effort, most tool\n\tproficiencies rarely matter beyond the flavor of your character. I can’t think\n\tof an instance where a character made frequent checks with Brewer’s Tools over\n\tthe course of a campaign, for example. However, the notable exception of\n\tThieves’ Tools means that Tool Expertise has at least one important use case\n\tin a typical campaign.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nFlash of Genius<\/span>: A bonus of up to +5 on a\n\tsave can easily turn a failed save or check into a successful one, and using\n\tthis up to 5 times a day means that it’s a powerful and reliable part of your\n\tskillset.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\nMagic item Adept<\/span>: Attuning one additional\n\titem is typically not a huge difference, but considering many Infusions\n\trequire attunement, this can be very important in campaigns which include\n\tmagic items. The ability to craft your own magic items faster and for less\n\tgold improves this ability further because you can craft items which require\n\tattunement with less concern about the limited number of items you can attune.\n\tIf you don’t have an All-Purpose Tool yet, build one now. It will take half a\n\tweek and 100 gold. Congratulations, you now have a magical tool that grants\n\tproficiency in all artisan tools. Use that to craft more magic items for\n\teveryone.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nSpell-Storing Item<\/span>: It may only be a 1st- or\n\t2nd-level spell, but you can cast it up to 10 times per day at 20\n\tIntelligence. Obvious options include Cure Wounds, False Life, Invisibility,\n\tand other restorative or protective spells, buffs, and utility options which\n\tyou’re going to cast repeatedly throughout a normal day of adventuring.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

                Unfortunately, it appears that you can’t choose a 1st-level spell cast with a\n\t2nd-level slot, but that’s probably fine.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                Remember that any creature can use this, so consider passing this off to an\n\tally (the Homunculus Servant and the Battle Smith’s Steel Defender could both\n\tsuffice if you need more precise control than letting another player use it)\n\tif the spell you choose makes more sense coming from someone else, if the\n\tspell targets the caster, or if you chose a spell which requires Concentration\n\tand someone in your party is a non-spellcaster so their Concentration isn’t\n\tbeing utilized.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Strangely, the text of Spell-Storing Item doesn’t use the phrase “Cast a\n\tSpell” to describe the action, so unlike a wand or similar item, creatures may\n\tbe able to use this when they normally can’t cast spells (like when raging).\n\tYou also notably don’t need to provide components, so you can use spells\n\tsilently, without moving, and without providing material components (including\n\texpensive ones!) so you can get away with all kinds of trickery.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nMagic item Savant<\/span>: One more attuned item,\n\tand you can ignore class\/race\/spell\/level requirements on magic items. Those\n\trequirements are rare, but maybe you want to use a Holy Avenger or\n\tsomething.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nMagic item Master<\/span>: One more attuned\n\titem.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nSoul of Artifice<\/span>: You can (and should) be\n\tattuned to 6 items, giving you a +6 bonus to all of your saves. Add Flash of\n\tGenius to that, and you can add +11 to any save on top of your normal\n\tbonus.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n\n

                Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                Artificers live and die by their Intelligence score, but Dexterity and\n\tConstitution are just as helpful to the Artificer as they are to everyone\n\telse. The Artificer has an impressive three total dump stats, allowing you to\n\tdump all of your points into the abilities which we care about and leave the\n\trest at 8. Replicate Magic Item gives you access to ability score boosting\n\titems and items which boost all of your saving throws, so you can often offset\n\tor override incredibly low ability scores with little effort.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\nStr<\/span>: Typically a dump stat. You don’t need\n\tStrength for anything unless you spend a feat on proficiency with heavy armor,\n\tand considering how many AC buffs you can get from your Infusions you really\n\tdon’t need to do that. Only the Battlesmith will invest heavily in fighting\n\twith weapons, and they can rely on Intelligence for attack and damage.<\/p\n>\nDex<\/span>: You’ll want some Dexterity to fill out\n\tyour AC and to help with weapons at low levels, but you’ll never need more\n\tthan 14. Armorer artificers planning to go all-in on Guardian armor can\n\tsurvive with 8 Dexterity, though getting to level 3 will require a great deal\n\tof caution if you’re starting at level 1.<\/p\n>\n

                Con<\/span>: Always essential.<\/p>\nInt<\/span>: Your primary stat. Fuels your spells\n\tand all of your class features.<\/p\n>\nWis<\/span>: Technically a dump stat, but it\n\tcomplements many of your skills nicely so it may be helpful to put some points\n\tinto it.<\/p\n>\n

                Cha<\/span>: Dump stat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
