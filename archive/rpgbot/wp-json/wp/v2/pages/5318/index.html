Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Shaman is a divine spellcaster, and can work as a Blaster (depending on your choice of Spirit), Healer, and Support. The Shaman is slightly less durable than the Oracle due to lack of shield proficiency, and isn’t particularly suited to melee combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        Shaman Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

        Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points makes you\n\t\tdurable for a primary caster, but don’t go drawing fire.<\/p>\n

        Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/3 BAB, but Shamans\n\t\tare full casters, so you really don’t need it.<\/p>\n

        Saves<\/span>: Will is the Shaman’s only good\n\t\tsave, and with a dependency on Wisdom for spells, a Shaman should have\n\t\texcellent Will saves. Be sure to put resources into Dexterity and\n\t\tConstitution to boost the Shaman’s poor saves.<\/p>\n

        Proficiencies<\/span>: Simple weapons give you\n\t\tplenty of options to supplement your spells, and medium armor will give you\n\t\ta decent AC. Unfortunately, the Shaman doesn’t get proficiency with shields.\n\t\tBecause the Shaman’s AC won’t be great, expect to spend most of your time\n\t\tfighting at range.<\/p>\n

        Skills<\/span>: 4+ skill ranks and a few good\n\t\toptions. Four ranks will cover all of the Shaman’s Knowledge skills, plus\n\t\tHeal.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

        Spells<\/span>: Works just like a Cleric, but with\n\t\ta different spell list. Access to your full spell list without needing to\n\t\tactually learn spells makes the Shaman very versatile, and gives you a lot\n\t\tof really great options.<\/p>\n

        Orisons<\/span>: Orisons are fantastic, reliable,\n\t\tand versatile.<\/p>\n

        Spirit<\/span> (Su): The Shaman’s Spirit defines\n\t\tthe Shaman’s character theme, and offers a collection of interesting\n\t\tabilities and unique hexes. For help selecting your Spirit, see my\n\t\tShaman Spirit Breakdown<\/a><\/p>\n

        Spirit Animal<\/span> (Ex): The Spirit Animal is a\n\t\tfamiliar, but it can also deliver hex spells which work as a touch attack.\n\t\tYour Spirit will give your familiar a buff of some sort, but very few of\n\t\tthem provide a meaningful improvement over how useful a familiar is on its\n\t\town.<\/p>\n

        Spirit Magic<\/span>: Much like Cleric Domain\n\t\tspells, this adds an extra spell slot per day, but with very limited\n\t\toptions. Be sure to consider your Spirits’ spell lists when selecting your\n\t\tprimary Spirit and your Wandering Spirit.<\/p>\n

        Hex<\/span>: Hexes are fantastic, and Shamans get\n\t\ttheir own list, plus access to the entire list of Witch Hexes. This offers\n\t\tan enormous set of great options. For help selecting Hexes, see my\n\t\tShaman Hex Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n

        Wandering Spirit<\/span> (Su): Adds quite a bit of\n\t\tversatility to the Shaman, and allows you to select a Spirit for its Spirit\n\t\tAnimal and Hexes, then switch to another for its abilities and spell\n\t\tlist.<\/p>\n

        Wandering Hex<\/span>: Get a free Hex that you can\n\t\tchange every day from your Wandering Spirit.<\/p>\n

        Manifestation<\/span> (Su): Varies.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
