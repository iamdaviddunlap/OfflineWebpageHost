Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Cleric is an iconic member of any dungeon fantasy party, providing powerful magic to both heal and empower their allies since the earliest editions of Dungeons and Dragons, but over the years clerics have taken on more ability to assail, destroy, smite, and otherwise blast their foes. That versatility is very apparent in PF2.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

In Pathfinder 2nd edition, Paizo has reimagined the Cleric in two varieties:\n\tThe Cloistered Cleric, a casting-focused, unarmored divine spellcaster; and\n\tthe Warpriest, an armored divine warrior splitting their capabilities between\n\tdivine spellcasting and wielding their deity’s favored weapon in battle.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

With two doctrines, dozens of deities, and numerous domains, the Cleric has\n\tnumerous major decision points, allowing players to build an impressively\n\tdiverse array of characters within the same class.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              Cleric Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

              Key Ability<\/strong>: Wisdom.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Hit Points<\/span>: 8+ is great for cloistered\n\tclerics, giving them more hp than the sorcerer or the wizard, but for\n\twarpriests 8+ hit points is difficult since you’re likely going to be on the\n\tfront lines of combat alongside champions, fighters, and other characters with\n\tboth better armor and more hit points.<\/p>\n

                \n\t

              • Saving Throws<\/span>: Will saves are the\n\t\tCleric’s only good save. Warpriests get better Fortitude progression, but\n\t\tnot by a lot.<\/li>\n\t
              • Skills<\/span>: A total for 4+ Trained skills,\n\t\tbut you’ll likely dump Intelligence so expect something like 3 Trained\n\t\tskills at first level from your class.<\/li>\n\t
              • Attacks<\/span>: The Cloistered Cleric’s weapon\n\t\tproficiencies are basically worthless. You’re barely more capable than a\n\t\twizard. The Warpriest’s proficiencies are better, but not quite as good as a\n\t\tmartial class like the Champion.<\/li>\n\t
              • Defenses<\/span>: By default the Cleric gets no\n\t\tarmor proficiencies. The Warpriest gets medium armor, but clerics only\n\t\tadvance to Expert in armor proficiencies, and even then only at 13th level.\n\t\tStrongly consider putting feats into Armor Proficiency.<\/li>\n\t
              • Spells<\/strong>: The Cloistered Cleric gets the standard\n\t\tprogression for every full spellcaster, but the Warpriest Cleric’s\n\t\tspellcasting proficiency doesn’t advance as much or as quickly.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                    Warpriest<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                    With medium armor and better weapon options, the Warpriest splits their\n\tcapabilities between the Cleric’s spellcasting and some martial capabilities\n\twhich allow you to fight effectively in melee. You give up the standard\n\tspellcasting proficiency progression which most full spellcasters recieve, but\n\tin exchange you get medium armor and Shield Block, both of which considerably\n\timprove your durability.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    The Warpriest’s biggest problem is that their proficiencies don’t keep up with other characters. You never go past Expert in armor, and while your weapon proficiencies advance reasonably well, you never go past Weapon Specialization, so your attack bonus and damage will always lag behind martial characters. To make up the gap, you’ll need to take feats like Align Weapon and cast spells like Magic Weapon. Fortunately, you get plenty of those options.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      If your deity’s weapon is Simple, such as a quarterstaff, getting the\n\t\t\tDeadly Simplicity feat for free boosts the damage die to make up the\n\t\t\tdamage gap.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

                      Cloistered Cleric<\/h3>\n\n\n

                      Like any full spellcaster, your ability score needs are determined by your\n\tspellcasting, and you can spend remaining boosts to support skills which you\n\tmay want to use. Since the Cloistered Cleric is less MAD than the Warpriest,\n\tyou can afford to invest more in Charisma to support Divine Font.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      Str<\/span>: Dump.<\/p>\n

                      Dex<\/span>: AC and Reflex saves. Your AC is going\n\tto be terrible, so unless you’re planning to invest several feats in armor\n\tproficiencies you’ll need some Dexterity to keep yourself alive.<\/p>\n

                      Con<\/span>: Hit points and Fortitude saves. You\n\tdon’t need a lot because you’re so well-equipped to heal yourself and fix any\n\tproblematic status conditions, but you don’t want to dump it.<\/p>\n

                      Int<\/span>: More skills are great, but the Cleric\n\tis not a skill-based class so there’s little incentive to invest in a lot of\n\tIntelligence.<\/p>\n

                      Wis<\/span>: Spellcasting and Will saves.<\/p>\n

                      Cha<\/span>: Divine Font.<\/p>\n\n\n

                      Warpriest (Strength-based)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                      The Warpriest is inherently MAD. You need enough Strength to make using a\n\tweapon effective, you need enough Dexterity to fill out your armor’s Dex Cap\n\t(though you should strongly consider upgrading to heavy armor at level 3), you\n\tneed enough Constitution to compensate for your 8+ hit points, and you still\n\tneed enough Wisdom to make your spellcasting meaningful. Things get even worse\n\tif you intend to use Divine Font.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      Str<\/span>: You likely want 16 Strength at level\n\t1. You won’t quite match martial classes like the Fighter, but you’re close\n\tenough that you’re still a threat.<\/p>\n

                      Dex<\/span>: You need either 12 if you plan to\n\tstick to medium armor, but 10 will suffice if you want to upgrade to heavy\n\tarmor at level 3 when you get your first General Feat.<\/p>\n

                      Con<\/span>: With just 8+ hit points and medium\n\tarmor, you need extra hit points.<\/p>\n

                      Int<\/span>: You need to have a dump stat, and this\n\tis the Warpriest’s best option. You’re already doing too many things to focus\n\ton managing numerous skills.<\/p>\n

                      Wis<\/span>: Your spellcasting proficiency will\n\tadvance slower than most full casters, so using spells offensively is less\n\tappealing, and if you’re not using spells offensively as your go-to option,\n\tyour spell attack modifier and spell DC are less crucial, so you don’t need to\n\tmax out Wisdom.<\/p>\n

                      Cha<\/span>: If you plan to use Divine Font, you\n\twant a little bit of Charisma for the free spells, but you likely can’t afford\n\tmuch due your other ability score needs.<\/p>\n\n\n

                      Con<\/span>: With just 8+ hit points and medium\n\tarmor, you need extra hit points.<\/p>\n

                      Int<\/span>: You need to have a dump stat, and this\n\tis the Warpriest’s best option. You’re already doing too many things to focus\n\ton managing numerous skills.<\/p>\n

                        Skills and Skill Feats<\/h2>\n\n\n

                        You get Skill Increases at 3rd and 5th level to raise skills to Expert, increases at 7th, 9th, 11th, and 13th level to raise skills to Master, and increases at 15th, 17th, and 19th level to raise skills to Legendary.                         You get Skill Feats at even-numbered levels, giving you a total of 10 Skill Feats (and maybe another from your Background) by 20th level.
