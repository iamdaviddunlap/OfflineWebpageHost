Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The wizard sits at the top of the class tier list. With a stunning array of\n\tspells and abilities, there are very few problems that a wizard can’t solve. A\n\twizard’s school is the lense through which he sees the world, and determines\n\thow he is played and how he approaches problems.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Despite their limited base skill ranks, Wizards’ dependence on Intelligence\n\tgives them a large enough pool of skill ranks that they can fill a number of\n\tskill-based roles depending on their build. However, many will find that the\n\twizard is best served by investing in Knowledge skills to capitalize on their\n\tstellar intellect. In addition to their “Librarian” role, Wizards can also\n\tfill a variety of other roles depending on their school and spell\n\tselections.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Abjuration School<\/a\n\t>\n<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    The abjuration school features most defensive buff spells, some miscellaneous\n\tdefensive utility spells (such as Alarm), and Dispel Magic. Many of these\n\tspells are critical to the success of an adventuring party, but the school\n\tabilities offer very little.<\/p>\n\n\n\nResistance<\/span> (Ex): Energy resistance is nice,\n\tbut it can be hard to predict what you need. Since you’re an abjurer, you’re\n\tdefinitely going to have access to energy resistance spells, but this can save\n\tyou a spell. If you can’t pick an option on a given day, pick fire because\n\tit’s the most common. The immunity at level 20 is nice, but a little bit late\n\tto matter.<\/p\n>\nProtective Ward<\/span> (Su): Deflection bonuses are\n\tnice, but most characters will have a ring of protection. After very low\n\tlevels. The 10 foot range also means that you have to be right in the thick of\n\tcombat for this to have any effect.<\/p\n>\nEnergy Absorption<\/span> (Su): Energy absorption\n\tallows you to resist any type of energy damage without needing to prepare for\n\tit beforehand. It’s a nice ability, but you shouldn’t ever need it.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Banishment School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    The Banishment school is intended to handle summoned creatures. Because\n\tsummoning things is generally reserved for spellcasters and certain outsides,\n\tyour school powers will see very little use. In addition, they force you into\n\tuncomfortably close quarters with your enemies, which is never a good place\n\tfor a wizard.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers: Energy Absorption and the Protective Ward.<\/p>\n\n\n\nUnstable Bonds<\/span> (Su): As a melee touch attack,\n\tyou can partially disable a target creature. There is no save, which is nice,\n\tbut this is miles worse than the school power for Enchantment.<\/p\n>\nAura of Banishment<\/span> (Su): If you are in a\n\tcampaign with a lot of summoners, this is worthwile. A fairly small portion of\n\tenemies rely on summoning multiple creatures, so this will not see frequent\n\tuse. If you need to get rid of a single creature, Banishment is a much better\n\toption.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Counterspell School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    Counterspelling is rarely a good option for a spellcaster, as any round that\n\tyou spend sitting around waiting to counterspell is another free round that\n\tyour enemies can do whatever they like. The Counterspell subschool offer a\n\tcouple of really good options for shutting down enemy spellcasters. If you are\n\tin a setting or game which features a large number of spellcasters, it’s very\n\teasy to find a place for the Counterspell Abjurer.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers: Energy Absorption and the Protective Ward.<\/p>\n\n\n\nDisruption<\/span> (Su): If this didn’t require a\n\tmelee touch attack, it could easily be green. The concentration DC is\n\textremely high, and the duration on this effect may very well outlast a normal\n\tcombat at high levels. However, the round you use it is a round that you’re\n\tnot casting spells.<\/p\n>\nCounterspell Mastery<\/span> (Su): If you want to\n\tcounterspell things, you probably already have Improved Counterspell, so you\n\tbasically get a bonus feat. Allowing you to counterspell as an immediate\n\taction is the real ability here. The limited uses per day are dissappointing,\n\tbut if you consider spellcasters as a subset of all of the enemies you might\n\tface in a day, you probably have enough uses to change the outcome of a battle\n\tor two.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Conjuration School<\/a\n\t><\/h3\n>\n\n\n\n

    Conjuration is one of the best schools. It features direct damage spells with\n\tno spell resistance, teleportation, summoning, and creation spells. At high\n\tlevels, summoning spells allow you to summon spellcasters to expand your\n\tspellcasting capacity, and you can even summons some creatures which have\n\tlimited healing abilities. The Conjuration school powers nicely supplement the\n\tschools abilities. With the wide range of creatures which you can summon, you\n\tcan do basically everything but hold a conversation.<\/p>\n\n\n\nSummoner’s Charm<\/span> (Su): One of the biggest\n\tlimitations on Summon Monster spells is their duration. Improving it by 50% is\n\thugely beneficial, especially at high level when your summons can cast\n\tspells.<\/p\n>\nAcid Dart<\/span> (Sp): This is better than the\n\tAcid Splash cantrip about 90% of the time. However, it’s a Spell-Like Ability,\n\twhich means that spell resistance applies. Acid Splash’s biggest draw is that\n\tit’s a conjuration spell so it doesn’t allow spell resistance. This is a weird\n\tirony, but rarely important. Having this ability largely supplants the need\n\tfor offensive cantrips, which opens up space for more interesting choices.<\/p\n>\nDimensional Steps<\/span> (Sp): Conjuration\n\tincludes teleportation, which gives you lots of options to get out of trouble.\n\tSince this is a standard action, it means moving instead of casting spells. In\n\tsome situations this is fine, but you will likely only use this in cases where\n\tyou are otherwise unable to cast spells.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Creation School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    Depending on what your DM will let you make with creation spells, this can be\n\ta very powerful option. Theorycrafters and muchkins will generally suggest\n\tpoisons as an excellent option for creation spells, and so long as your DM\n\tallows it, poisons are a good choice. Creation effects can be very versatile\n\tin the hands of creative player, but this subschool’s effectiveness depends\n\theavily on your ability to be creative under pressure.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers: Acid Dart and the Dimensional Steps.<\/p>\n\n\n\nCreate Gear<\/span> (Su): The flavor on this\n\tability is cool, but the limitation on moving parts is dissappointing. If your\n\tDM is particularly flexible (read: “stupid”), you could use this to create\n\texpensive items like gold. You could also do something silly like creating\n\tpoison for your rogue, or create a bunch of oil and set it on fire. Get\n\tcreative, and see what your DM will let you get away with.<\/p\n>\nCreator’s Will<\/span> (Sp): You get the Minor\n\tCreation and Major Creation spells as spell-like abilities. You can cast the\n\tspells anyway, but the large number of uses is nice. Use creation to create\n\tpoisons, special ammunition, and other things which are expensive with limited\n\tuse. Or, create a giant ball of steel and roll it over your enemies. Again,\n\tsee what your DM will let you get away with.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Teleportation School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    The teleportation school is a very small change from the Conjuration school.\n\tIt gives up the Acid Dart power for a swift action teleport ability. At high\n\tlevels where Acid Dart isn’t useful, Shift gets progressively more powerful,\n\tmaking this subschool a better option at higher levels.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Power: Acid Dart.<\/p>\n\n\n\nShift<\/span> (Su): Teleport a few squares as a\n\tswift action. The range starts of at 5 feet, which is a swift action for a\n\tsecond 5 foot step. Even if I weren’t a wizard, I would be happy spending a\n\tswift action for a second 5 foot step.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Divination School<\/a\n\t>\n<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    The Forewarned ability is really the best part of this school. Despite how\n\tincredible it is, the rest of the school powers are highly situational, which\n\tseverely limits the school’s appeal.<\/p>\n\n\n\nForewarned<\/span> (Su): Being able to act in every\n\tsurprise round means you get to cast an extra spell even if you are surprised.\n\tThe initiative bonus itself would probably make this ability blue.<\/p\n>\nDiviner’s Fortune<\/span> (Sp): The 1 round\n\tduration seems fairly limiting, so you probably won’t want to waste your time\n\tusing this in combat. Instead, use that insight bonus to buff your team for\n\timportant skill checks like disarming traps and opening locks. Insight bonuses\n\tare fairly rare, so this is easy to stack with other buffs.<\/p\n>\nScrying Adept<\/span> (Su): If you have recurring\n\tenemies, this could be bumped up to green. If you don’t have recurring\n\tenemies, it will be pretty rare for anyone to be scrying you, and you probably\n\twon’t have a lot of familiar enemies to scry on.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Foresight School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    The Foresight subschool gives up the Divination school’s lousy higher level\n\tabilities in favor of two really amazing abilities. If you want to play a\n\tdiviner, this subschool is clearly the better option.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers: Diviner’s Fortune and Scrying Adept.<\/p>\n\n\n\nPrescience<\/span> (Su): The applications for this\n\tability are numerous. Roll it on turns you’re expecting to make a saving throw\n\tor a touch attack, and you can plan your turn before knowing the results. At\n\tworst, this is effectively a free reroll 3+int times per day.<\/p\n>\nForetell<\/span> (Su): A +2 luck bonus to attacks\n\tand saving throws is absolutely incredible. Luck bonuses are among the rarest\n\tin the game, and allowing you to turn this ability on and off is like a power\n\tswitch for awesome. The 30 foot range is concerning, but for a luck bonus the\n\trisk is likely worth the reward.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Scryer School<\/a\n\t>\n<\/h4>\n\n\n\n

    Sense Senses is potentially more useful than Diviner’s Fortune, but not\n\tnotably. This is really a side-step for the Diviner, but the potentially\n\tincreased awareness fits the school’s theme better than Diviner’s Fortune.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers: Diviner’s Fortune.<\/p>\n\n\n\nSend Senses<\/span> (Sp): Line of sight\n\tClaraudience\/Clairvoyance. With some clever use, this can be fairly helpful.\n\tHowever, you are completely shut down by something as simple as a door or a\n\tcurtain.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Enchantment School<\/a\n\t><\/h3\n>\n\n\n\n

    The Enchanter is the ultimate save-or-suck caster. Every spell you cast\n\tshould have immediate and dramatic results, and will very often end a fight\n\twith a word and gesture. With some fairly simple trait and skill selections,\n\tyou can ever serve as the party’s face despite potentially terrible\n\tcharisma.<\/p>\n\n\n\nEnchanting Smile<\/span> (Su): A bonus to social\n\tskills is weird for a wizard. If you expend resources to be a face, this might\n\tactually be useful. But most likely, you won’t be doing a lot of Bluffing. The\n\tlevel 20 ability is fantastic, but won’t see a lot of use.<\/p\n>\nDazing Touch<\/span> (Sp): Dazed is a great status\n\tcondition and this power has no saving throws, but this is a\n\tmelee<\/em> touch attack, which you will be terrible at, and it only\n\taffects things with your hit dice of fewer, which is generally only going to\n\tNPCs or things with a CR lower than your level. Your familiar can’t deliver\n\tthe attack since it’s not a spell. It can be situationally useful if you get\n\tgrappled or if you ambush someone, but it’s not going to be your go-to\n\toptions.<\/p\n>\nAura of Despair<\/span> (Su): The 30 foot range is\n\tproblematic, and the duration is fairly limited, but a -2 to saves against\n\teverything affected is brutal, The debuff matches the Shaken condition and\n\tstacks with it. If you can make a target Shaken, they’re looking at a total -4\n\tpenalty on saves against your absurd spell DCs. Sickened adds another -2.<\/p\n>\n

    Controller<\/span><\/h5>\nForce of Will<\/span> (Su): You give up charming\n\tsmile for the ability to communicate with your charmed\/dominated targets\n\ttelepathically. This is great for communicating with subjects which don’t\n\tspeak common, and lets you boss things around without revealing that they’re\n\tcharmed\/dominated. You could even use Charm Person on your party members to\n\tset up a cheap telepathic communication system.<\/p\n>\nIrresistible Demand<\/span> (Sp): This is strictly\n\tworse than Dominate Monster, the duration is extremely limited, the save is\n\taverage, and you have to concentrate as a standard action to maintain the\n\teffect. However, it’s Dominate Monster at 8th level. With careful application,\n\tthis is a really fantastic way to mimic a 9th level spell for a few rounds.<\/p\n>\n
    Manipulator<\/span><\/h5>\nBeguiling Touch<\/span> (Sp): You trade in the\n\tmediocre Dazing Touch for the considerably worse Beguiling Touch. Beguiling\n\ttouch allows a save, has a very short duration, and doesn’t work in combat or\n\ton hostile creatures. If you really need to charm something, cast Charm Person\n\tor Charm Monster. You’re an enchanter, so you’re really good at those.<\/p\n>\nShape Emotions<\/span> (Sp): You can now use your\n\taura to give your party a +4 bonus on saves against fear, or your can give\n\tenemies a -2 penalty on saves against mind-affecting spells and effects. The\n\tbonus against feat can be replaced with Remove Fear, the -2 penalty is worse\n\tthan the save penalty from Aura of Despair, and<\/em> Aura of Despair\n\tapplies penalties to attacks, ability checks, and skill checks.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Evocation School<\/a\n\t>\n<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    Evocation is the blunt hammer of magic. Straight damage lacks the elegance of\n\tless direct schools, but it’s hard to argue with the effectiveness of\n\tFireball. Most theorycrafters will decry that dealing damage is the less\n\teffective than save-or-suck spells, but evocation spells like fireball are\n\tbetter at handling crowds of enemies which save-or-suck spells typically have\n\ttrouble with.<\/p>\n\n\n\nIntense Spells<\/span> (Su): If all you want is to\n\tdo damage, this ability is pretty fantastic. The ability notes that the damage\n\tonly applies once per spell, but fails to mention area of effect spells. Your\n\tDM may choose to apply the damage to everyone in an AOE (it’s only one damage\n\troll), or he may make you pick the recipient. Work this out with your DM ahead\n\tof time, as it will define how you play your character. This ability also has\n\tthe amusing affect of keeping your low level damage spell relevant into higher\n\tlevels.<\/p\n>\nForce Missile<\/span> (Sp): Similar to magic\n\tmissile, this ability is the best of the damage dealing school powers. Because\n\tit is a force effect, it can affect incorporeal creatures, and you don’t have\n\tto worry about energy resistance.<\/p\n>\nElemental Wall<\/span> (Sp): Fire wall is a\n\treasonably good spell, and being able to move this around easily makes it\n\tconsiderably more powerful and versatile.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Admixture School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    Losing force missile is hard for an evoker since you won’t have many combat\n\toptions at low levels, but Intense Spells applies to cantrips, so you can fall\n\tback on Acid Splash. Versatile Evocation fixes the evoker’s biggest problem,\n\tand elemental manipulation gives your party a very solid defensive option\n\tagainst energy damage.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers: Force Missile and Elemental Wall.<\/p>\n\n\n\nVersatile Evocation<\/span> (Su): One of the biggest\n\tproblems for evokers is energy resistance. This effectively removes that\n\tcomplication.<\/p\n>\nElemental Manipulation<\/span> (Su): Get your team\n\tenergy resistance to one type of energy, then make all energy that type of\n\tenergy. Easy energy resistance to every energy type.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Generation School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    The Generation subschool powers don’t really fit the theme of the school, and\n\tthe don’t do anything particularly helpful.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers:Intense Spells and Force Missile.<\/p>\n\n\n\nLingering Evocations<\/span> (Su): There aren’t a\n\tlot of evocation spells with a duration greater than instantaenous, and most\n\tof them are fairly dull.<\/p\n>\nWind Servant<\/span> (Sp): This is what mage hand is\n\tfor.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Illusion School<\/a\n\t><\/h3\n>\n\n\n\n

    Illusion is a hard school to play. The effects are highly subjective, so your\n\teffectiveness is almost entirely dependant on how creative you and your DM\n\tare. Your DM needs to be very good at ignoring metagame knowledge in order to\n\tplay enemies correctly while illusions are in effect. Discuss this school with\n\tyour DM before you play, or you may find that your DM is unintentionally\n\truining your attempts at illusion.<\/p>\n\n\n\nExtended Illusions<\/span> (Su): A lot of\n\tillusions have a duration of concentration, especially low levels ones. You\n\tmay not need this ability often, but it’s good for covering a getaway or\n\tmaintaining a distraction while you cast other spells.<\/p\n>\nBlinding Ray<\/span> (Sp): Blinding a target is\n\tpotentially crippling. If you have a rogue in your party, this is will get a\n\tlot of use. Keep in mind that it only meaningfully affects creatures with HD\n\tequal to or less than your wizard level, so you will need a fairly good\n\tknowledge of monster stats to use this reliably.<\/p\n>\nInvisibility Field<\/span> (Sp): Absolutely\n\tfantastic. In addition to the obvious stealth benefits, invisibility is great\n\tfor escaping melee, and for attacking creatures with high dexterity. Creatures\n\twhich are flat-footed to you will often have a touch AC of 10 (adjusted for\n\tsize and deflection), which can make them a great target for touch attacks\n\tlike your blinding ray or disintegrate.<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Phantasm School<\/a\n\t><\/h4\n>\n\n\n\n

    The Phantasm subschool can be a lot of fun with a good rogue in your party,\n\tbut it doesn’t do much for you as a caster.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Powers: Blinding Ray and Invisibility Field.<\/p>\n\n\n\nTerror<\/span> (Su): This makes you weirdly good\n\tat flanking with a rogue, but you generally shouldn’t be in melee enough to\n\tmake use of this. You may be able to do some clever class dipping to abuse\n\tthis.<\/p\n>\nBedeviling Aura<\/span> (Su): Auto-flanking for\n\trogues! Woo! Sneak attacks everywhere!<\/p\n>\n\n\n\n

    Shadow School<\/a\n\t>\n<\/h4>\n\n\n\n

    The Shadow subschool gives up some of the Illusion school’s offensive options\n\tin exchange for some mobility-centric powers. The powers are interesting, but\n\tnot particularly helpful.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replacement Powers: Blinding Ray and Invisibility Field.<\/p>\n\n\n\nBinding Darkness<\/span> (Sp): Entangling\n\tdifficult foes can be helpful, but this also gives the target concealment,\n\twhich makes it difficult for your allies to attack, and impossible to sneak\n\tattack unless they have darkvision.<\/p\n>\nShadow Step<\/span> (Sp): This is a cool movement\n\toption similar to the Conjuration school’s Dimensional Steps.
