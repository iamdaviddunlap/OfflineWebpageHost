Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Inventor’s Innovation feature serves double duty as both subclass and\n\tequipment, which makes sense considering what the Inventor does. Your choice\n\tof Innovation will greatly define your character’s tactics and role within the\n\tparty.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Your choice of Innovation is a huge opportunity to opt into or out of\n\tcomplexity in your character. Armor is mostly passive, making it low\n\tcomplexity to build and low complexity to play. Construct is low complexity to\n\tbuild because so many of the decision points are essentially mandatory, but\n\tit’s high complexity to play because you’re managing a minion. Weapon is\n\tcomplex both to build and to play because Pathfinder 2e’s weapons are so\n\tintricate, and many of the modifications will add even more complexity to your\n\tStrikes by giving you new things to do with your weapon.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Armor Innovation<\/h3>\n\n\n\n\n
    <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

    The Armor Innovation offers the choice between the Power Suit (basically\n\tmedium armor) and the Subterfuge Suit (light armor). Focusing on your armor\n\toffers access to passive skill bonuses and numerous damage resistance options,\n\tallowing you to be exceptionally durable for a class in medium armor with only\n\t8+ hit points. However, it’s very easy to fall into the Tank Fallacy here\n\tsince the Inventor isn’t quite so effective offensively as more conventional\n\tmartial classes.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    If you want to be tanky, Power Suit is a good choice. However, without a\n\tReaction like Attack of Opportunity, you lack the “stickiness” that makes the\n\tFighter an effective Defender, so your tactics need to be different. Expect to\n\tspend time blocking spaces, grappling, and otherwise making yourself a\n\tnuisance for your enemies.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    If you want to be sneaky, Subterfuge Suit is the natural choice. Grab a\n\tranged weapon or a finesse weapon, pick up Subtle Dampeners, and get\n\tsneaky.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Construct Innovation<\/h3>\n\n\n\n\n
          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          The most complex Innovation to play since you need to manage a second\n\tcharacter constantly. The Construct Innovation is unique among minion features\n\tin that it allows you to give your Minion three Actions immediately, which\n\ttypically requires a class feat for other characters like the Druid and the\n\tRanger. Weirdly, Construct Innovation is also by far the easiest Inventor to\n\tbuild. There is an obvious feat choice at basically every level where you gain\n\ta Class Feat, so you rarely need to agonize over your build choices.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Repairing your construct is crucial to making it effective. Sure, every\n\tInventor is going to maximize Craft, but you as the Inventor need to be able\n\tto use the Repair action to much greater effect because your construct’s\n\tcontinued existence depends on it. Expect to take feats like Quick Repair and\n\tSpecialty Crafting.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Unfortunately, it’s unclear what the DC is to repair your companion. Unlike\n\tTreat Wounds which allows you to optionally, Repair’s effectiveness is\n\tmultiplied based on your Proficiency, so the ideal situation is for the DC to\n\tbe low enough that Assurance makes a Critical Success a guarantee, but that\n\tseems unlikely.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          The Inventor’s class entry doesn’t require a Craft check to do anything built\n\tinto the class (such as rebuilding your destroyed construct) except to trigger\n\tthe Overdrive Action, which uses the “Standard” DC for your level, and that\n\tseems to be the best answer available.           Your Construct Companion is limited in how it can benefit from items. “The\n\tonly item bonuses they can benefit from are to Speed.” This means no item\n\tbonuses to attack, AC, damage, saves, skills, etc. Improvements to your\n\tcompanion’s stats come almost exclusively from advanced along the Advanced,\n\tIncredible, Paragon track.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
