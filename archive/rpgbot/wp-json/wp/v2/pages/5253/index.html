        Hunter Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

        Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points isn’t great, but\n\tthe Hunter’s Animal Companion should be durable enough to protect the\n\tHunter.<\/p>\n

        Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/d BAB.<\/p>\n

        Saves<\/span>: The Hunter has good Fortitude and\n\tReflex saves, and due to the Hunter’s dependence on Wisdom for spells also has\n\tdecent Will saves.<\/p>\n

        Proficiencies<\/span>: Simple and martial weapons,\n\tmedium armor, and shields. This offers plenty of options for a variety of\n\tbuilds.<\/p>\n

        Skills<\/span>: 6+ skills and some excellent skill\n\toptions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

        Spells<\/span>: Hunters are divine spontaneous\n\tspellcasters, and get access to both the Ranger spell list and the Druid spell\n\tlist. Ranger spells, especially 4th-level Ranger spells, are absolutely\n\tfantastic, and the Hunter gets them earlier and in greater quantity than\n\tanyone else in the game. The Hunter’s spells are dependent on Wisdom.<\/p>\n

        Orisons<\/span>: Fantastic and versatile.<\/p>\n

        Animal Companion<\/span> (Ex): The Hunter gets the\n\tbest version of Animal Companion in the game. On top of the Druid’s version\n\t(the previous gold standard), the Hunter can teach their companion tricks from\n\tthe Skirmisher Ranger’s list of “Hunter’s Tricks”. Some of these tricks don’t\n\tquite make sense for an Animal Companion, but a few of them are very helpful.\n\tIn addition, if the Hunter loses of foregos their animal companion, Summon\n\tNature’s Ally spells extend their duration from rounds to minutes per\n\tlevel.<\/p>\n

        Animal Focus<\/span> (Su): This is a fantastic way\n\tto quickly buff yourself and your Animal Companion, and the bonuses can be\n\tused to address a variety of challenges. Remember that the bonus to your\n\tcompanion doesn’t consume your minutes per day duration, so you can leave it\n\tin place permanently.<\/p>\n

          \n\t
        • Bat<\/span>: Not always important, but fantastic\n\t\tto have available when you do need it. At high levels the blindsense is\n\t\tgreat for detecting invisible foes.<\/li>\n\t
        • Bear<\/span>: Temporary bonuses to Constitution\n\t\tare difficult to justify because they can kill you when they expire.<\/li>\n\t
        • Bull<\/span>: This can largely replace your belt’s\n\t\tbonus to Strength.<\/li>\n\t
        • Falcon<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
        • Frog<\/span>: Very situational.<\/li>\n\t
        • Monkey<\/span>: Very situational.<\/li>\n\t
        • Mouse<\/span>: Evasion is fantastic on any\n\t\tcharacter.<\/li>\n\t
        • Owl<\/span>: Situational.<\/li>\n\t
        • Snake<\/span>: Situational, but this can be\n\t\tfantastic if you or your companion are built as a Defender.<\/li>\n\t
        • Stag<\/span>: The bonus is tiny.<\/li>\n\t
        • Tiger<\/span>: This can largely replace your\n\t\tbelt’s bonus to Dexterity.<\/li>\n\t
        • Wolf<\/span>: Situational, and the range is\n\t\tgarbage.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          Nature Training<\/span> (Ex): There are very few\n\tfeats where Druid or Ranger levels matter.<\/p>\n

          Wild Empathy<\/span> (Ex): Very situational.<\/p>\n

          Precise Companion<\/span> (Ex): If you plan to be an\n\tarcher, you need Precise Shot. If you plan for your Hunter to be in melee,\n\tOutflank can be a great way to team up with your Animal Companion, especially\n\tbecause your companion gets Outflank for free. If you plan to ride your\n\tcompanion but use melee weapons, consider the Pack Flanking feat; once you get\n\tHunter Tactics, it will be granted to your Animal Companion for free.<\/p>\n

          Track<\/span> (Ex): Very situational.<\/p>\n

          Hunter Tactics<\/span> (Ex): Teamwork Feats are very\n\tpowerful, but requiring multiple characters to select them can often be\n\tproblematic. Granting the Hunter a guaranteed ally for teamwork makes them\n\tmuch more viable. This also allows the Animal Companion to get very difficult\n\tteamwork feats without meeting prerequisites which are already difficult for a\n\tplayer character.<\/p>\n

          Teamwork Feat<\/span>: Free Teamwork Feats, and the\n\tHunter can change their most recent choice as a standard action any number of\n\ttimes. Remember that with Hunter Tactics the Hunter’s feat choice is\n\tautomatically shared with their Animal Companion, which makes this even\n\tbetter.<\/p>\n

          Improved Empathic Link<\/span> (Su): Once your\n\tfamiliar becomes intelligent, you can give them complex instructions to scout\n\tfor you. Allowing the Hunter to see through their companion’s eyes allows the\n\thunter to see things first-hand without putting themselves in danger.<\/p>\n

          Woodland Stride<\/span> (Ex): Very Situational<\/p>\n

          Bonus Tricks<\/span> (Ex): Most characters probably\n\tcan’t make use of a lot of extra tricks, but remember that Hunters can teach\n\ttheir companion Hunter’s Tricks from the Skirmisher Ranger ability.<\/p>\n

          Second Animal Focus<\/span> (Su): In addition to the\n\tscaling bonuses from Animal Focus, 8th level allows the Hunter to gain two\n\tbonuses (three if the Hunter’s companion is dead), and grant two to their\n\tAnimal Companion.<\/p>\n

          Swift Tracker<\/span> (Ex): Very situational.<\/p>\n

          Raise Animal Companion<\/span> (Sp): A “permanent”\n\tnegative level to raise the Hunter’s Animal Companion is a cheap price to pay\n\tconsidering that the negative level goes away after 24 hours. Because this is\n\ta spell-like ability it takes a Standard Action to use, and has no material\n\tcomponent. Raise your companion in combat if you need to.<\/p>\n

          Speak with Master<\/span> (Ex): This makes it\n\tconsiderably easier to share information and instructions with your\n\tcompanion.<\/p>\n

          Greater Empathic Link<\/span> (Su): Even better than\n\tspeaking, now you can exchange information and commands silently. Combined\n\twith the ability to see through the companion’s eyes, the Hunter can now\n\tessentially remote-control their Animal Companion.<\/p>\n

          One with the Wild<\/span> (Ex): Situational.<\/p>\n

          Master Hunter<\/span> (Ex): The tracking bit is\n\tworthless, but adding a third animal focus which lasts all day is absolutely\n\tfantastic.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Wisdom is the Hunter’s spellcasting ability, but other abilities are largely\n\tdependent on your build. Since they roll in medium armor and have a big\n\tdurable pet to stand behind, I recommend opting for archery over melee\n\tweapons.<\/p>\n

          Str<\/span>: If you plan to use melee weapons,\n\tStrength is essential. If you plan to use a bow, pick up a bit for damage, but\n\tI wouldn’t go with more than 14 at level 1.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: In only medium armor, a bit of\n\tDexterity will be a good supplement to the Hunter’s AC, and it’s obviously\n\tcrucial if you plan to use a bow.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: Hit points and Fortitude saves.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: Only needed for skill ranks.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: The Hunter’s spellcasting ability, and\n\tcompensates for the Hunter’s “bad” will save. However, the Hunter is only a\n\t2\/3 caster, so don’t think that you need to compete with a Cleric or a\n\tDruid.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: Only useful for Handle Animal and Wild\n\tEmpathy, and you can improve your modifier easily enough that dumping Charisma\n\twon’t hurt much.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
