Evoker Wizard Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Evoker Wizard\u2019s job is to indiscriminately cover the battlefield in heavily damaging magic. The School of Evocation is all about getting to use those big dangerous spells like Fireball without having to worry about friendly fire. Never worry about your allies\u2019 positioning again, as they simply become immune to your Sculpted Spells.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

One of the many great things about being an Evoker Wizard is being a Wizard<\/em>. We\u2019re better than other Wizards at damage magic, but we\u2019re also still great at all the area control effects that come standard with all Wizards. Throw down a Hold Person one round, then drop a Fireball on everyone next round without killing all your friends. That\u2019s teamwork.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The example Evoker build down below plays the role of a Blaster, picking as many save for half damage AoE spells as possible to make the most of Sculpt Spell. If you want to hang back and get to use all of those great big damage spells like Fireball without having to worry about your allies, give Evoker a try.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        School of Evocation Features<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
        <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
          \n\t
        1. Evocation Savant<\/span>: Standard for PHB wizard\n\t\tsubclasses. Strangely, this feature incentivizes you to use your two free\n\t\tspells learned per level to learn literally anything except spells from your\n\t\tfavorite school.<\/li>\n\t
        2. Sculpt Spells<\/span>: Friendly fire happens\n\t\tinevitably when you have an evoker in the party. This goes a long way to cut\n\t\tdown on the problem, and you should abuse the fact that your allies are immune to your spells wherever possible.\n\t\t

          There’s some weird nuance here that’s easy to overlook: While this is\n\t\t\tclearly intended to protect allies from Burning Hands, Fireball, and other\n\t\t\tsuch AOE damage options, the protection doesn’t end after the initial\n\t\t\teffect of the spell. Creatures are protected from the spell’s effects for\n\t\t\tthe spell’s full duration. This means that your allies can comfortably\n\t\t\twalk around in the areas of spells like Sickening Radiance,\n\t\t\tpotentially dragging enemies about inside the area to force them to\n\t\t\ttake damage. You could even cast Otiluke’s Freezing Sphere, designate your\n\t\t\tallies as protected by Sculpt Spells, then send them off with pockets full\n\t\t\tof frozen spheres to smash at their feet in a room full of enemies.\n\t\t\tHonestly, I don’t think this was intended when WotC wrote School of\n\t\t\tEvocation. Much like Empowered Evocation, I think that they meant for\n\t\t\tSculpt Spells only to apply to the initial effects. But it’s been 5+ years\n\t\t\tand we haven’t gotten errata or tweets from Jeremy Crawford, so it appears\n\t\t\tthat Sculpt Spells works as I’ve described it until someone tells us\n\t\t\totherwise.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

        3. Potent Cantrip<\/span>: Acid Splash and Poison\n\t\tSpray are the only damaging Wizard cantrips in the PHB which require a\n\t\tsaving throw, but other supplements have dramatically increased your\n\t\toptions.\nAdditionally, this feature is not limited to Wizard cantrips, in case you happen to have something from another class, feat, or a racial feature.\n This is nice insurance, and it encourages you to use saving throw\n\t\tcantrips like Frostbite instead of attack cantrips like Fire Bolt because\n\t\tthe guaranteed half damage means that your DPR is calculated with the formula ([Dice Average]*([Chance to Fail Save]+0.5*(1-[Chance to Fail Save]))). This formula applies to any save for half effect, so it’s also relevant to a lot of leveled spells.\n

          An example at the level we get this: with Toll the Dead (2d12, avg. 13) and a 60% Chance the target fails: (13*(0.6+0.5*(0.4)))=10.4 Expected DPR. If instead the target only failed the save 50% of the time: (13*(0.5+0.5*(0.5)))=9.75 Expected DPR. That\u2019s only a difference of 0.65 DPR.<\/p>\n

          A firebolt at the same level, following the expected math for an attack roll with 2d10 damage on a hit, comes to 7.7 DPR and does nothing on a miss.<\/p>\n<\/li>\n\t

        4. Empowered Evocation<\/span>: A bonus to damage\n\t\tlike this is a considerable boost, especially for your low-level spells like\n\t\tBurning Hands, which can still be decent damage sources at a low spell slot\n\t\tcost.\n\t\t

          According to\n\t\t\tJeremy Crawford<\/a>\n\t\t\tback in 2015, this bonus applies to each missile of magic missile and to\n\t\t\tall targets of an AOE, but only to one attack for multi-attack spells like\n\t\t\tScorching Ray. If Empowered Evocation applies to each missile, Magic\n\t\t\tMissile becomes very powerful. This conflicts with Crawford’s more recent\n\t\t\trulings, where he has ruled that each missile from Magic Missile is a\n\t\t\tseparate damage roll, so similar damage bonuses only get applied to one of\n\t\t\tthe missiles. Personally I think the more-recent ruling makes more sense,\n\t\t\tbut discuss it with your DM.\nFor the magic missile case, see our Practical Guide to \nMagic Missile<\/a>.<\/p>\n<\/li>\n\t

        5. Overchannel<\/span>: This is a great way to get\n\t\tmore mileage out of reliable lower-level spells like Magic Missile and\n\t\tFireball at a level where your higher-level spells would generally eclipse\n\t\tthem. Just be sure to have a Cleric handy when you start taking damage. RAW,\n\t\tthe damage is maximized every time that the spell deals damage, which makes\n\t\tspells like Wall of Fire especially powerful. However, according to\n\t\tJeremy Crawford<\/a>\n\t\tthe effect was intended to only apply to the first damage roll for the\n\t\tspell, so sticking to Fireball is likely your best choice. Check with your\n\t\tDM about how they want to handle things.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n\n

          School of Evocation Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Wizards need Intelligence for Save DCs, Constitution for HP and Concentration saves, and Dexterity, for AC. Evokers follow this trend.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Str<\/span>: Dump it. We got Wizard powers.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: We want some of this for AC so we can maybe sometimes not get hit.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: More HP. Better Concentration saves. Good all around.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: Setting Save DCs and Spell Attack Bonus, as well as improving Empowered Evocation. Get this to 20 quickly.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: These saving throws generally suck to fail, so if we have anything left after the rest, put a point here.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: Dump it. Let someone else chit chat. We\u2019re here for Explosions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
