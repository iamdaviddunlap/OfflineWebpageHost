Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Crossbowman is a challenge. Archers, including other crossbow builds, typically rely on making numerous attacks in order to deal damage. The Crossbowman flies in the face of this by depending on single shots made as readied actions. To really understand this archetype, make sure to read the Ready Action<\/a> rules.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Crossbowman Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Deadshot<\/span> (Ex): This ability defines how the\n\tarchetype will be played. Essentially, the Crossbowmen depends on readied\n\tactions. Applying half of your dexterity to damage is nice, but it’s hard to\n\tmake it match up to the huge damage gap provided by using a composite bow with\n\trapid shot.<\/p>\n

    Crossbow Expert<\/span> (Ex): Weapon training with\n\tcrossbows. If you’re only going to get one attack off of a prepared action,\n\tyou had better hit with it.<\/p>\n

    Improved Deadshot<\/span> (Ex): Very few enemies\n\tdepend on dexterity for AC, so this will rarely help you hit. If you had sneak\n\tattack, this would be nice, but after 7 levels a dip into Rogue won’t be very\n\thelpful.<\/p>\n

    Quick Sniper<\/span> (Ex): The bonus to sniping is\n\tnice, but Stealth isn’t a class skill for you, and the archetype doesn’t add\n\tit. The ability to return fire is situationally useful, but it also unloads\n\tyour crossbow so that you can’t take your prepared action shot unless you’re\n\tusing a repeating crossbow or have Rapid Reload.<\/p>\n

    Greater Deadshot<\/span> (Ex): Finally. This is a\n\tsmall damage boost for Deadshot. Crossbow damage still isn’t great, but every\n\tlittle bit helps. By this point your Dexterity should be fairly impressive.<\/p>\n

    Safe Shot<\/span> (Ex): Saves you the trouble of\n\ttaking Point Blank Master.<\/p>\n

    Pinpoint Targeting<\/span>: Pinpoint Targeting is\n\ta tempting feat, especially against high AC enemies. Armor, Natural Armor, and\n\tShields are the sorts of bonuses which a typical tank relies on, and this will\n\tlet you get past their AC. However, it prevents you from using things like\n\tVital Strike, which might mean you sacrifice damage for reliability.<\/p>\n

    Meteor Shot<\/span> (Ex):\n\tBull Rush<\/span> or\n\tTrip<\/span> with a ranged attack. Neither help you\n\tmuch, but they might help your allies.<\/p>\n

    Penetrating Shot<\/span> (Ex): I certainly hope\n\tyou took Improved Critical. This makes critical hits with crossbows really\n\texciting, but it’s fairly difficult to set up, and it’s certainly not reliable\n\tuntil you get Weapon Mastery.<\/p>\n

    Weapon Mastery<\/span> (Ex): With 19-20×3 crit,\n\tyour critical hits won’t be spectacularly often, but they will be better than\n\twith a bow. Combined with Penetrating Shot, this might see a fair bit of\n\taction.<\/p>\n\n\n

      Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

      The Crossbowman is remarkably SAD compared to most fighters. You can dump 3 abilities and probably won’t even notice.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

      Str<\/span>: Crossbows don’t touch strength at all,\n\tso you can dump it to 7 without affecting anything but your carrying\n\tcapacity.<\/p>\n

      Dex<\/span>: Your bread and butter.<\/p>\n

      Con<\/span>: Keep those hit points high.<\/p>\n

      Int<\/span>: Your skills are garbage, and you don’t\n\tneed Combat Expertise..<\/p>\n

      Wis<\/span>: Will saves are fighter’s biggest\n\tshortcoming, and even a little bit of Wisdom goes a long way to keep you\n\talive.<\/p>\n

      Cha<\/span>: Less talking, more shooting.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
