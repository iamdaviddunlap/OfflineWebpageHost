Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

A Patreon supporter challenged me to write an article comparing the available classes based on their ability to heal their party. Specifically, which class makes the best “Healbot”.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

You might reasonably ask “What is a Healbot?” Well, the answer is pretty\n\tsimple: A “Healbot” is a character whose primary function is to provide hit\n\tpoint restoration (“healing”) for the rest of the party. Historically, this\n\trole has been considered on of the primary functions of clerics, a tragic\n\tstereotype that clerics have been railing against since at least 2nd edition\n\tAD&D.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

In this competition (for lack of a better word) we’ll be examining available\n\tclasses and subclasses to assess them for their ability to heal their party\n\tmembers in order to judge which class makes the best Healbot. This is not an\n\tassessment of each builds general viability or effectiveness: solely their\n\tability to restore hit points to other characters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Rules<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Builds examined this competition will abide by the following rules:<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
            \n
          1. No Healer Feat: Each class is assumed to have the same access to feats. Healer provides a healing option that restores hit points, but if every class can get it, it doesn’t differentiate any one build from another.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          2. No Multiclassing: Each build will be a single class. While multiclassing could absolutely produce a better Healbot, multiclassing is beyond the scope of this competition.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          3. No Magic Items: Magic items will not be considered unless they are granted by a class feature.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          4. Self-Harm is Penalized: Any hit point damage dealt to the competitor or a member of their party shall be subtracted from their final score.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          5. Official Rules Rulings: Errata, Sage Advice, including tweets from Jeremy Crawford are considered official rulings on any rules matters. Any errata which changes the affects of spells or abilities applies.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          6. Adventuring Day Rules Apply: In order to balance classes which recharge on Short Rests against those which recharge on Long Rests, the “Adventuring Day” rules apply: Players will benefit from 2 Short Rest between each Long Rest.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          7. 4-Character Parties: For the purposes of spells which heal multiple creatures, the party will be assumed to include the competitor and three allies.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          8. Dice Are Averaged: All dice shall use their average values.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          9. No Unearthed Arcana: I still don’t cover Unearthed Arcana, so UA options are off-limits.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          10. Recipients Can Always Be Healed: For simplicity, it is assumed that recipients of healing can always receive healing, and competitors do not need to worry about healing being wasted by going over the targets’ hit point maximums.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          11. Heal up to Half Counts as 3\/4 Points<\/strong>: Effects which can raise a creature to “no more than half of its hit point maximum” shall count only as 3\/4 of their full hit point restoration value. This is a compromise between the idea that they’re still valid hit points, but also that the cap makes that healing less readily useful than other sources of healing.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          12. Attacks Always Hit<\/strong>: Where applicable, any attack made is assumed to hit its target.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          13. Ability Score Increases go 16 -> 18 -> 20<\/strong>: All competitors will start with a score of 16 in their spellcasting ability, and their score will increase by 2 at 4th and 8th level.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          14. Tied Scores are Shared<\/strong>: Competitors with the same score will share the same ranking.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          15. Full Healing is Worth 440<\/strong>: A 20th-level hill dwarf barbarian with 24 Constitution and the Tough feat has a maximum possible hit point maximum of 440 hit points. That seems to be the highest number of hit points achievable without magic items, so we’ll consider that the value for Power Word Heal, Wish (per target), and any other effect which restores a creature to maximum hit points.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          16. Optional Class Features<\/strong>: All optional class features are allowed. This adds some new healing spells to several class’s spell lists.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
          17. Aura of Life<\/strong>: Aura of Life could, in theory, restore up to 300 hp total given the other established rules, but that depicts an unlikely scenario so Aura of Life will be disregarded entirely.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n\n
            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Scoring<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Scoring will be assessed on a group of competition categories:<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
              \n
            1. One-Round Healing (Singles)<\/strong>: The maximum amount of hit\n\t\tpoints that the competitor can restore to a single allied creature (not the\n\t\tcompetitor) in a single round, starting at the beginning of the competitor’s\n\t\tturn and ending at the beginning of their next turn. Hit points restored to\n\t\tthe competitor do not count.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
            2. One-Round Healing (Party)<\/strong>: Same as singles, but the total\n\t\tamount of healing granted to the competitor’s party (including the\n\t\tcompetitor) will be totaled.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n

              More categories may be added in the future.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

              In addition, scoring will be performed based on results over the course of\n\tthe competition running from levels 1 through 20:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                \n
              1. Best In Category<\/strong>: Scoring in the top three in one of the\n\t\tabove competition categories shall award each competitor points (3 for 1st,\n\t\t2 for 2nd, 1 for 3rd). Highest score is declared “Best In Category” for that\n\t\tcategory.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              2. Best Overall<\/strong>: Scoring in the top three in the per-level\n\t\tcategories shall award each competitor points (3 for 1st, 2 for 2nd, 1 for\n\t\t3rd). Highest score wins. Winner claims the coveted title of “Best\n\t\tHealbot”.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n\n

                Competitors<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Artificer (Alchemist)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                A relative newcomer to DnD 5e, the Artificer is the first full class\n\tpublished outside of the Player’s Handbook. While the Artificer only enjoys\n\t2\/3 spellcasting, they get access to crucial healing options like Cure Wounds\n\tand their Infuse Items ability allows them to produce magic items to\n\tsupplement their built-in capabilities. The Alchemist subclass adds\n\tExperimental Elixir and Alchemical Savant, both of which add additional\n\thealing output.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Bard (College of Lore)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                Bards are an unusual choice for a healer, but in a small party they can be a good go-to option because their spell list shares elements with both the Cleric and the Wizard, allowing them to fill their party’s spellcasting needs with one spellcaster. While their healing options aren’t as good as the Cleric’s, College of Lore offers access to spells outside of the Bard’s spell list which may make up the difference.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Cleric (Life Domain)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                So iconic that it’s in the SRD, the Life Domain Cleric is the iconic healbot,\n\tand they’re favored to win across the board. With the best healing spells in\n\tthe game, and subclass features like Disciple of Life and Channel Divinity:\n\tPreserve Life. However, Preserve Life hits the “Heal up to Half” to\n\tlimitation, which may make a difference in competition.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Druid (Circle of Dreams)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                While their healing spells aren’t as numerous as the Cleric’s the Druid does\n\tenjoy nearly-exclusive access to Healing Spirit, Goodberry, and some other\n\tuseful spells. Circle of Dreams offers Balm of the Summer Court, which grants\n\ta pool of dice which the Druid can use to heal their allies.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Druid (Circle of the Shepherd)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                The Druid’s Circle of the Shepherd offers Spirit Totem (Unicorn Spirit), a\n\ttemporary buff which adds a massive spike of additional healing on top of the\n\tDruid’s spells for a short duration. This huge numerical buff may enough to\n\tput them high in the rankings in parts of the competition.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Monk (Way of Mercy) (Not competing)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                Hand of Healing offers the Way of Mercy Monk a minor healing option. This\n\tallows starts at 2d4+6 per turn at level 3, than maxes out at 2d10+10 at high\n\tlevel. That’s not enough to exceed the Paladin’s Lay on Hands, so the Way of\n\tMercy Monk has been omitted from the competition.<\/p>\n\n\n

                Paladin (Any)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                While their healing spells are limited, paladins have unique access to Lay on\n\tHands, a pool of healing which they can provide on a moments notice, allowing\n\tfor big bursts of reliably healing. However, it’s not clear if Lay on Hands\n\twill be enough to compete with heavy hitters like the Cleric and the Druid.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Ranger (Any) (Not competing)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                Rangers can cast Cure Wounds and Healing Spirit, but that just makes them a\n\tworse version of the Druid for the purposes of this competition. They’re here\n\tas an honorable mention and nothing else.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Sorcerer (Divine Soul)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                The Sorcerer’s Divine Soul subclass grants the Sorcerer access to both the\n\tSorcerer’s spell list and the Cleric’s spell list, giving them access to the\n\tlargest spell list in the game, including all of the Cleric’s powerful healing\n\toptions. On top of that, Font of Magic and Metamagic allow the Sorcerer to\n\tmanipulate their spells to maximum effect, offering a totally unique\n\tspellcasting options. Subclass features like Empowered Healing and Unearthly\n\tRecovery add additional options for hit point restoration.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Warlock (The Celestial)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                The Warlock normally has no built-in healing abilities beyond Vampiric Touch,\n\tbut The Celestial offers access to some healing options including Cure Wounds\n\tand Healing Light.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Competition<\/h2>\n\n\n

                Everything below takes the form of a table, but if that’s no fun you can\n\timagine a bunch of adventurers dilligently taking 8-hour naps, leaping up to\n\tfrantically cast a bunch of spells, then lying back down for another 8-hour\n\tnap.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                Competition: One-Round Healing (Singles)<\/h3>\n\n\n

                “Singles” is a weird competition, but it’s also the easiest to understand.\n\tCompetitors have one round to do as much healing as they can to one creature.\n\tThis isn’t a great indication of a character’s effectiveness as a healer in a\n\treal game because in most cases you won’t go from a full rest to immediately\n\tneeding to pour everything you can muster into healing one party member.<\/p>\n\n\n
