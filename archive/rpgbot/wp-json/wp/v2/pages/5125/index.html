Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Mechanically, humans are a blank template. They have the ability to convert\n\tevery one of their racial feats into general feats, and can even dip into\n\tother classes with Multitalented. They serve as a fantastic baseline for\n\tcomparison to other races, allowing us to clearly look at a race and say “I\n\twant this thing from this race that I can’t get from the Human”. Their\n\tbuilt-in customizability also means that they work in every class and nearly\n\tevery build.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

While they fall under the human ancestry, we’ve addressed the\n\thalf-elf<\/a>\n\tand\n\thalf-orc<\/a>\n\tin their own handbooks because their mechanical complexity justifies\n\tconsidering them separately.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Disclaimer<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

RPGBOT uses the color coding scheme which has become common among Pathfinder build handbooks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n\t
  • Red<\/span>: Bad, useless options, or options which\n\t\tare extremely situational. Nearly never useful.<\/li>\n
  • Orange<\/span>: OK options, or useful options\n\t\tthat only apply in rare circumstances. Useful sometimes.<\/li>\n
  • Green<\/span>: Good options. Useful often.<\/li>\n
  • Blue<\/span>: Fantastic options, often essential\n\t\tto the function of your character. Useful very frequently.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

    Human Racial Traits<\/h2>\n\n\n
      \n\t
    • Hit Points<\/span>: 8 hit points is average. Be\n\t\tsure to invest in Constitution, and consider taking Toughness at first\n\t\tlevel.<\/li>\n\t
    • Size<\/span>: Medium. Medium and small size have\n\t\tfew functional differences in Pathfinder 2e.<\/li>\n\t
    • Speed<\/span>: 25 ft. is standard.<\/li>\n\t
    • Ability Boosts<\/span>: Among the best reasons to\n\t\tplay a human, 2 free ability boosts means that you can be good at any class.\n\t\tUnfortunately, with the addition of the Alternate Ability Boosts rule, the\n\t\tHuman is not unique in this capability. You also notably can’t get three\n\t\tboosts from your Ancestry as a human.<\/li>\n\t
    • Languages<\/span>: Common plus a free bonus\n\t\tlanguage of your choice, and you can learn any common language rather than\n\t\tpicking from a list of languages which your race has access to.<\/li>\n\t
    • Senses<\/span>: Boring, normal senses. If this is a\n\t\tproblem for you, consider a Versatile Heritage which grants better\n\t\tsenses.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

      Human Heritages<\/h2>\n\n\n

      Your go-to option is Versatile Heritage, which will cover most builds.\n\tHowever, other Heritages offer useful options if you have a specific build in\n\tmind.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

      <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
        \n\t
      • Half-Elf<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Unless you specifically want to mix human and elf ancestry feats, there’s\n\t\tlittle reason to do this. You do gain low-light vision, which is nice.\n\t\t

        For more on the Half-Elf, see our\n\t\t\tHalf-Elf Handbook<\/a>.\n\t\t<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

      • Half-Orc<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: With the current available source material, this is the only way to get\n\t\taccess to Orc racial feats, and some of the options in the Core Rulebook are\n\t\tvery tempting. You also gain low-light vision.\n\t\t

        For more on the Half-Orc, see our\n\t\t\tHalf-Orc Handbook<\/a>.\n\t\t<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

      • Skilled Heritage<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Essentially two skill increases. Probably not as good as a feat, but if\n\t\tyour character is going to be heavily invested in skills, this may help\n\t\tquite a bit.<\/li>\n\t
      • Versatile Heritage<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: General feats offer a lot of options, and getting an extra feat at 1st\n\t\tlevel gives you a lot of room to customize your character. Characters with\n\t\tpoor weapon or armor proficiencies can take Armor Proficiency or Weapon\n\t\tProficiency, or you could take Toughness to pad your hit points. Unlike\n\t\tother heritage options which will remain unchanged as real-world time\n\t\tpasses, every new rules supplement which includes new general feats will\n\t\tmake this heritage better. However, unlike Pathfinder 1st Edition, a single\n\t\tadditional feat at first level is not a crucially important build\n\t\toption.<\/li>\n\t
      • Wintertouched Human<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Scaling resistance to a common damage type.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n

        Human Feats<\/h2>\n\n\n

        This section does not cover Elf feats (which are available to half-elves) or\n\tHalf-Orc\/Orc feats (which are available to half-orcs). Look forward to\n\tseparate handbooks which will explore those options in the future.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        Level 1<\/h3>\n\n\n
          \n\t
        • Adapted Cantrip<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: This allows you to take powerful combat cantrips like Electric Arc (CRB), Gouging Claw (SoM), or Needle Darts (RoE)\n\t\ton any spell list.         • Arcane Tattoos<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Where Adapted Cantrip lets you take one cantrip from outside of your\n\t\tspell list, Arcane Tattoo just gives you an arcane cantrip.         • Cooperative Nature<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Aid is generally not a good use of an Action in combat unless you’re\n\t\tinvesting a lot into making it effective.           If you do plan to build around Aid, the Half-Elf Heritage will do a lot\n\t\t\tto improve your options because it has exclusive feats related to Aid.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

        • Courteous Comeback<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: The benefit is good, but it’s just too restrictive.<\/li>\n\t
        • Devil’s Advocate<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Unless you’re playing almost exclusively in Chelaxia, this won’t matter\n\t\tenough to justify the feat.<\/li>\n\t
        • Dragon Spirit<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: All good offensive cantrips. This is an easy choice for sorcerers with an\n\t\tarcane bloodline, but remember that innate spells are Charisma-based.         • General Training<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: There are a lot of very good General Feats. Remember: Skill Feats are\n\t\tGeneral Feats, so those are options, too.<\/li>\n\t
        • Gloomseer<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Good, but you could get this from another ancestry\/heritage and still\n\t\thave the feat.         • Haughty Obstinacy<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Too situational.<\/li>\n\t
        • Keep Up Appearances<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Only situationally useful, and heavily subject to GM interpretation.<\/li>\n\t
        • Know Oneself<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Emotion effects like fear are common enough to be an issue, and sometimes\n\t\tthe critical failure effects will take you out of a fight. This mitigates\n\t\tthat risk.<\/li>\n\t
        • Natural Ambition<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Class Feats determine much of what your character can do, and they’re\n\t\tsome of the best feats available in many cases. Even while limited to\n\t\t1st-level Class Feats, you still have several excellent options.<\/li>\n\t
        • Natural Skill<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Trained in a skill gets you quite a bit, and for many skills being\n\t\tTrained is all that you’ll ever need. You do get Skill Increases as you gain\n\t\tlevels, but the list of skills is long and getting more Trained skills at\n\t\t1st level means that you can use your Skill Increases to improve your\n\t\texisting skills rather than learning new ones.<\/li>\n\t
        • Quah Bond<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Not amazing, but a skill and a Skill Feat is a good trade for one\n\t\tAncestry Feat.<\/li>\n\t
        • Saoc Astrology<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Not quite as reliable as Guidance, but similarly useful.<\/li>\n\t
        • Tupilaq Carver<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Summon spells are decent and Summon Construct is only on the Arcane spell\n\t\tlist by default, so for other spellcasters this can be very useful.<\/li>\n\t
        • Unconventional Weaponry<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Only Fighters are Trained in Advanced Weapons, so this is a great way for\n\t\tother classes to get access to a weapon which is a defining part of your\n\t\tbuild, like the Sawtooth Saber for a two-weapon fighting build.           Level 5<\/h3>\n\n\n
            \n\t
          • Adaptive Adept<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: Taking a multiclass archetype’s feats is often a better option.           • Clever Improviser<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: If your character has few Trained skills and you’ve chosen to invest your\n\t\tskill increases into improving the few skills that you have, you’ll be\n\t\tUntrained in a wide variety of skills.           • Darkseer<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Get this from another ancestry\/heritage, from a spell, or from an\n\t\titem.<\/li>\n\t
          • Ornate Tattoo<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Buy a wand.<\/li>\n\t
          • Sense Allies<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: This might be useful if you have multiple allies who like to be stealthy\n\t\tduring combat so that you can still target them with buffs, healing, and\n\t\tother support effects.<\/li>\n\t

            • Incredible Improvisation<\/span>CRB<\/a><\/sup>: The bonus is nice, and once per day is likely enough to cover important\n\t\tskill checks in skills where no one in your party is proficient, such as\n\t\tLore skills.             • Virtue-Forged Tattoos<\/span>CG<\/a><\/sup>: Seriously, just multiclass. Multitalented is the same level and will get\n\t\tyou so much more than this.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n
