Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Warmage is a simple, straight-forward take on spellcasting. A dedicated Blast and Striker, the Warmage forgoes all subtlety in favor of blowing stuff up and dealing piles of d6’s.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

To be completely honest, the Warmage is a bad class. Its damage output is trivially higher than a Sorcerer, and the ability to equip armor can be easily replicated by a variety of other classes. In order to improve the warmage, look for ways to expand your spell list: Arcane Disciple, items like Runestaves, and classes like the Rainbow Servant.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Warmage Class Features<\/h3>\n\n\nHit Points<\/span>: With d6 hit points the Warmage can’t take many hits, but it’s a bit better than the Wizard.\n\nBase Attack Bonus<\/span>: 1\/2 BAB, but BAB doesn’t really matter. The few attacks which the Warmage will make will be touch attacks.\n\nSaves<\/span>: Will is the Warmage’s only good save, and with no major dependencies on Dexterity or Constitution you will need to make a point to boost your bad saves..\n\nProficiencies<\/span>: Simple weapons give the warmage some nice backup options when you run low on spells, and access to light armor and light shields will give you a better AC than most arcane spellcasters. Armored Mage allows the Warmage to cast spells unhindered, and at level 8 the Warmage can move up to medium armor.\n\nSkills<\/span>: 2+ skill ranks, and a very thin skill list. The Warmage needs a bit of Intelligence for Warmage Edge, but 12 Intelligence will get you all of the Warmage’s interesting skills.\n\nSpells<\/span>: The Warmage is a Charisma-based spontaneous caster, similar to the Sorcerer. However, unlike the Sorcerer, the Warmage’s spell list is limited to a very small set of offensive spells.\n\nArmored Mage<\/span> (Ex): Wearing armor and shields while casting spells will give you a somewhat survivable AC.\n\nWarmage Edge<\/span> (Ex): This bonus is nice at very low levels, but it will be quickly eclipsed by caster-level based damage. It is the Warmage’s only ability which depends on Intelligence, so it’s hard to justify putting a lot of resources into boosting Intelligence. It’s especially nice at first level because it makes cantrips do respectable damage.\n\nAdvanced Learning<\/span> (Ex): Limited to Evocation spells, but this opens up a lot of fantastic spells which aren’t on the Warmage spell list. This is especially nice because the Warmage got no new spells added to their spell list after Complete Arcane was published.\n\nSudden Empower<\/span>: 50% more damage for free once per day.\n\nSudden Enlarge<\/span>: Frequently mistaken for Widen spell. Enlarge improves a spell’s range. Very few spells have range problems, which makes this very situational.\n\nSudden Widen<\/span>: Excellent for high level spells with small areas of effect.\n\nSudden Maximize<\/span>: Maximum damage always feels good.\n\n\n

          Alternate Class Features<\/h3>\n\n\n
            \n \t
          • Eclectic Learning<\/span>PHB2<\/a><\/sup>: The warmage really needs access to non-blaster spells like Fly to stay relevant at high levels. Casting spells a full spell level behind everyone else is painful, but the added versatility is fantastic.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

            Abilities<\/h3>\n\n\n

            The Warmage’s abilities largely resemble a Sorcerer; strong emphasis on Charisma, and the rest is mostly focused on survivability.<\/p>\n\n\n\nStr<\/span>: Dump.\n\nDex<\/span>: Essential for saves, AC, and ranged touch attacks.\n\nCon<\/span>: Hit points are crucial.\n\nInt<\/span>: Take a bit for Warmage Edge, but don’t bend over backwards to improve it. The damage bonus is fairly small.\n\nWis<\/span>: Take a bit for Will saves.\n\nCha<\/span>: The Warmage’s spellcasting ability.\n\n

            Races<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

            Bonuses to Charisma are are incredibly rare, so look for other bonuses. Dexterity is nice, and small size is always good for strict spellcasters.<\/p>\n\n\n\nDwarf<\/span>: The penalty to Charisma really hurts, but the defensive abilities are tempting.\n\nElf<\/span>: The bonus to Dexterity is nice, but the penalty to Constitution is annoying.\n\nGnome<\/span>: A bonus to Constitution adds some survivability, and the penalty to Strength doesn’t matter. The Gnome’s other abilities are largely wasted on the Warmage.\n\nHalf-Elf<\/span>: Awful.\n\nHalf-Orc<\/span>: Penalties to both Intelligence and Charisma.\n\nHalfling<\/span>: Small size and a bonus to Dexterity make the Halfling great with rays and ranged touch attacks.\n\nHuman<\/span>: A bonus feat is always welcome.\n\n\n

            Skills<\/h3>\n\n\n
              \n \t
            • Concentration<\/span> (con): Essential for almost any spellcaster.<\/li>\n \t
            • Intimidate<\/span> (cha): The Warmage has the Charisma to be a Face, but lacks access to Bluff and Diplomacy.<\/li>\n \t
            • Knowledge (Arcana)<\/span> (int): One of the most important Knowledge skills.<\/li>\n \t
            • Knowledge (History)<\/span> (int): Situational.<\/li>\n \t
            • Spellcraft<\/span> (int): Great for identifying spells.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

              • Knowledge Devotion<\/span>CC<\/a><\/sup>: Normally reserved for melee classes, Knowledge Devotion can be a great option for Warmages who depend on rays and touch attacks, and the bonus class skill can be helpful for qualifying for prestige classes.<\/li>\n \t

                        Armor<\/h3>\n\n\n

                        Armor is presented in the order in which you should acquire it, rather than alphabetical order.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                          \n \t
                        • Studded Leather<\/span>: Starting armor.<\/li>\n \t
                        • Light Shield<\/span>: Starting armor if you want a shield, but you probably want two hands to use a crossbow. Once you have enough spell slots that you don’t want a crossbow, the light shield is essentially the same as a buckler because it still allows you to hold items in your shield hand.<\/li>\n \t
                        • Chain Shirt<\/span>: Permanent armor. Consider upgrading to Mithral if you have a lot of Dexterity, but it’s not essential.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

                          Introduced in the Magic Item Compendium, runestaves are a fantastic supplement to any spontaneous caster’s spell list. Many runestaves offer utility options which will make nice improvements to the Warmage’s spell list.<\/p>\n\n\n
