Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Wielding the manifestation of inner will as a weapon in battle are those Monks who follow the Way of the Astral Self. Powered by Wisdom, these Monks are able to push their Attack stat and Stunning Strike DC early on, giving an advantage over other Monks who must push Dexterity first to keep up with the fundamental math of the game<\/a> while their DC atrophies.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But maybe you\u2019re still undecided, so ask yourself this? Should a master of unarmed martial arts be able to grapple an opponent and wrestle them to the ground? Well most Monks end up dumping Strength and can\u2019t Grapple their way out of a paper bag. Or well, they usually can escape the Grapple with Dex and Acrobatics. But with the Way of the Astral Self, Wisdom is used in place of Strength for Grappling, and anything else you could possibly use Athletics for. Is that portcullis the only thing between your party and freedom? Lift it, with Wisdom.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Perhaps though, you\u2019re not here to be a Monk. Perhaps you\u2019re just perusing multiclass options. Good news, we have a couple suggestions down in that section about leveraging the Wisdom-first way this subclass handles things. Maybe a little Druid, maybe a little Cleric.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              Astral Grapplemancy<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

              Because the Arms of the Astral Self allow us to use Wisdom for Athletics, for all Strength Checks and Strength Saves really, we have better access to Grappling than other Monks. But what is the right way to go about a Grapple attempt? We could always read the Practical Guide to Grapple<\/a>, and, while that is a great place to start, we can be more Monk specific.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

              Assuming our arms are already active from a previous turn and we are high enough level for an Extra Attack, we declare the Attack Action. We then replace our first attack to make an opposed Athletics Check to Shove and we choose Target is Prone. We then replace our second attack with another Athletics Check to Grapple. For most classes, we\u2019re out of attacks, but we are Monk and we made the Attack Action on our turn. Flurry of Blows doesn\u2019t care what our Attack Action did, Flurry gonna punch twice regardless, and our target is Prone next to us, so we have Advantage. We may not be the best at grappling<\/a>, but we\u2019re in the top five at least.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

              Way of the Astral Self Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
              <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

              The Way of the Astral Self builds much like any other Monk. We still want both Dexterity and Wisdom, but we are able to use our first few ASIs on Wisdom over Dexterity thanks to Arms of the Astral Self.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Str<\/span>: We can dump this, we\u2019re not some hulking Barbarian with a Maul.<\/p>\n

              Dex<\/span>: Half of our AC calculation and our Attack stat if Astral Self is down.<\/p>\n

              Con<\/span>: We like hit points and not dying.<\/p>\n

              Int<\/span>: Dump. Let someone else be the Scholar.<\/p>\n

              Wis<\/span>: Half of our AC calculation, our Attack stat when running Astral Self, and sets the DC for a few of our abilities.<\/p>\n

              Cha<\/span>: Dump. Let someone else be the Face.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
