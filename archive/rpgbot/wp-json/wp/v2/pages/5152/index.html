Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Champion is a big, tanky brute with some divine flavor. Capable of both\n\twithstanding and dealing great amounts of damage, the champion is a\n\twell-rounded Defender with a great theme and interesting mechanics. However,\n\tthe champion adheres to either end of the good\/evil alignment axis, so the\n\tChampion can often feel unwelcome in a party of adventurers who are less\n\tideologically extreme.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In Dungeons and Dragons and in Pathfinder 1e, the Champion was called the\n\t“Paladin”, and was historically locked into Lawful-Good alignment, along with\n\ta strict code of conduct which made it difficult to include paladins in a\n\tparty of characters who might not be good-aligned, and even in good-aligned\n\tparties, paladins were frequently seen as an inflexible and frustrating moral\n\tcompass. The Paladin’s strict moral code has been a subject of derision for\n\tdecades, and only in recent years did DnD and Pathfinder entertain the idea of\n\tpaladins of less rigid alignments.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

DnD now allows paladins of any alignment in the core rules, Pathfinder 1e\n\tpublished archetypes for other alignments, and Pathfinder 2e’s Core Rulebook\n\tallows three types of Good-aligned Champions, which was then expanded in the\n\tAdvanced Player’s Guide to cover the three evil alignments. Still, your allies\n\tmay find your character’s rigid morality (or immorality) frustrating, so\n\tdiscuss the matter with your party before brining a champion into the game.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In a party, the Champion’s primary role is a Defender, and few classes can\n\tfill that role so well with so little effort. Depending on your selection of\n\tskills and class feats, the Champion can also serve as a Face, a Healer, and a\n\tStriker, though they may not be so effective at healing as the Cleric since\n\tthey lack a broad list of spells. The Champion is notably the only class that\n\tgets proficiency in spell attacks and spell DCs without getting an actual\n\tspell list, which makes it easy for the Champion to multiclass into classes\n\twith the Divine spell list like the Cleric and the Sorcerer (with the right\n\tbloodline, of course).<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                    Subclasses – Deity and Cause<\/h2>\n\n\n

                    The first, and possibly most important benefit of your subclass is the\n\tChampion’s Reaction. This is a powerful option for using your Reaction which\n\tcan have a huge impact on the way your character works in combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    These Reactions all occur when enemies target your allies, and the conditions\n\tof the Reactions require that your ally and your enemy be within 15 feet of\n\tyou. This forces the Champion into melee, and it means that your Champion’s\n\tReaction may not see consistent use if your allies prefer to stay far out of\n\tmelee combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    Antipaladin<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                    The Antipaladin is an offense-focused evil subclass, encouraging a highly\n\trisky play style where you capitalize on enemies hitting you, then willingly\n\ttake additional damage to harm your attacker in kind. Since the Antipaladin’s\n\tversion of Champion’s Reaction only occurs when you’re hit with an attack by\n\tan enemy within 15 feet, you’re encouraged to forgo a shield and get into\n\tmelee as quickly as possible and encourage enemies to focus their attacks on\n\tyou instead of your allies.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    Perhaps the Antipaladin’s biggest problem is the “Tank Fallacy”. You’re big\n\tand durable and hard to kill, but little about the subclass makes it easier to\n\tkill your enemies unless they choose to attack you and trigger Destructive\n\tVengeance. As soon as they figure out how Destructive Vengeance works, there’s\n\ta huge disincentive to continue attacking you instead of your allies. Look for\n\tways to force enemies to remain in melee with you.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    This is not a subclass for the cautious or the faint-hearted, and not just\n\tbecause you’re likely playing a murderous psycopath. Be sure that your party\n\tcan provide plenty of healing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      \n\t
                    • Champion’s Reaction: Destructive Vengeance<\/span>: Choosing to take damage so that your enemy takes an equal amount of\n\t\tdamage is usually a bad trade for player characters. If you go this route,\n\t\tmake sure that you have the ability to heal yourself.<\/li>\n\t
                    • Focus Spell: Touch of Corruption<\/span>: A great\n\t\toffensive option, and it can heal undead creatures.<\/li>\n\t
                    • Divine Smite<\/span>: Persistent damage is really\n\t\tgood, and it makes Destructive Vengeance a much better option since your\n\t\tenemy will take considerably more damage than you.                     • Exalt<\/span>: Divine Smite already made\n\t\tDestructive Vengeance a good trade, and this makes it even better.
