Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Gunslinger is a weird, complicated class. It’s a martial Striker which\n\tcan branch out as a Face and Scout depending on your skills. But if you look\n\tat the Gunslinger and think “this is a fighter with guns (or crossbows,\n\ttechnically)”, you’re missing a lot of what makes the Gunslinger special.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The fundamental difficulty with using Firearms, much like crossbows, is that\n\tit costs actions to reload them. The Gunslinger goes to absurd lengths to\n\tStretch the action economy (and the hand economy in some cases) in order to\n\tmake firearms as effective as using a bow. Gunslingers will frequently have\n\tturns where they do 5 or more Actions worth of things. Several of those things\n\tare reloading, but it’s still pretty cool.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Between the diversity of firearms and the four subclasses published in Guns\n\tand Gears (5 if you count the Spellshot archetype), there are enough options\n\tto cater to a wide variety of gunslinger concepts and builds, and there is\n\tplenty of room to experiment with different weapons as your character\n\tadvances.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                Gunslinger Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

                Key Ability<\/strong>: Dexterity.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Hit Points<\/span>: 8+ hit points is plenty for a\n\tback-line Striker like the Gunslinger. You won’t hold up if enemies focus\n\ttheir attacks on you, but at least you’re not running 6+ hit points like a\n\twizard.<\/p>\n

                Initial Proficiencies<\/span>: Gunslingers are\n\toffense-heavy, but with only light armor, 8+ hit points, and poor Will saves,\n\tthey’re vulnerable both to focused attacks and to many spells.<\/p>\n

                  \n\t
                • Perception<\/span>: Among the best progressions in\n\t\tthe game, though a few classes like the Ranger and the Rogue hit proficiency\n\t\tadvances earlier than the Gunslinger.<\/li>\n\t
                • Saving Throws<\/span>: Roughly average Fortitude\n\t\tsaves, really good Reflex saves (not quite as good as the Rogue), and among\n\t\tthe worst Will save progressions in the game. Don’t neglect your Wisdom.<\/li>\n\t
                • Skills<\/span>: A total of 4+Int Trained skills\n\t\tat level 1, which is average.<\/li>\n\t
                • Attacks<\/span>: You match the Fighter for the\n\t\tbest weapon proficiency progression in the game, though only for firearms\n\t\tand crossbows. You even get Advanced firearms and crossbows, but the\n\t\tproficiency progression is one step behind Simple\/Martial firearms and\n\t\tcrossbows. Proficiency with other weapons is also one step behind firearms,\n\t\tand the Singular Expertise feature prevents you from changing that.<\/li>\n\t
                • Defenses<\/span>: Light armor and medium, but\n\t\tthe Gunslinger is totally dependent on Dexterity, so light armor is your\n\t\tbest option. Your proficiency progression is typical for most non-tanky\n\t\tclasses.<\/li>\n\t
                • Class DC<\/strong>: The Gungslinger’s class DC is used for very feat\n\t\tthings, but the Critical Specialization Effect for firearms calls for a\n\t\tFortitude save and gives the target Stunned 1 if they fail, which is really\n\t\tgood. Crossbows don’t care about your class DC.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                    \n\t

                      • Deeds<\/strong>:\n\t\t

                                  Drifter, Vanguard, and Kickback Weapon Enthusiasts<\/h3>\n\n\n

                                                                                  Introduction The Gunslinger is a weird, complicated class. It’s a martial Striker which can branch out as a Face and Scout depending on your skills. But if you look at the Gunslinger and think “this is a fighter with guns (or crossbows, technically)”, you’re missing a lot of what makes the Gunslinger special.
