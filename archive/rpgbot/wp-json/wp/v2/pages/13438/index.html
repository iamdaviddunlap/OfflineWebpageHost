Though aasimars are classically the offspring of\n\tangels, a narrative advantage of Pathfinder that aasimars benefit from is the\n\tnumber of good-aligned outsiders that characters could be descended from.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Aasimar Racial Traits<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Ability Scores<\/span>: Gaining a +2 to two stats\n\twithout a racial penalty to a third stat is rare in Pathfinder. Wisdom is\n\talways good, but a charisma bonus encourages specific class choices.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Native Outsider<\/span>: As outsiders with the\n\tnative subtype, Aasimars are immune to a number of punishing effects that only\n\ttarget humanoids, but also can not be the recipient of a number of buffs.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Medium<\/span>: Medium size is the size that most\n\tthings are and offers no real benefits or penalties.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Normal Speed<\/span>: A movement of 30 feet. Not\n\tspecial, but not bad either.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Senses<\/span>: 60 feet of darkvision is fantastic\n\ton any character, but it’s best if you pick a class where you expect to scout\n\tin darkness.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Skilled<\/span>: A +2 in perception is great\n\tregardless of your stats, but it’s even better combined with the wisdom bonus\n\tfrom your racial ability modifiers. You also get a +2 to diplomacy, which is\n\tfun if you plan on being the party face.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Spell-Like Ability<\/span>: Daylight is a\n\tsituational spell that is most useful for countering magical darkness and\n\timposing penalties on enemies with light sensitivity. However, it lasts a long\n\ttime and you can slap it on your weapon long before you get into a fight.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Celestial Resistance<\/span>: A broad range of\n\tresistances against commonly-encountered damage types. This is great, but it\n\tdoesn’t scale well so there are better options in the Alternate Traits.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Languages<\/span>: Common and Celestial. Celestial\n\tcomes up often enough that it’s nice to have.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          <\/div>\n\n\n\n

          Alternate Racial Traits<\/h3>\n\n\n\n
          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Celestial Crusader<\/span>: Replaces Celestial\n\tResistance and Skilled. A +1 insight bonus to attack rolls against a\n\tcommonly-encountered enemy type is useful, but you’re giving up some\n\tincredible defensive options for a situationally useful bonus that’s not all\n\tthat great even when it matters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Deathless Spirit<\/span>: Replaces Celestial\n\tResistance. This trait is exceptionally useful in an undead-themed campaign.\n\tNegative levels are one of the most punishing effects in the game. With this\n\ttrait you don’t lose hit points when you are subject to negative levels, which\n\tapplies even when you are resurrected. A +2 to saves against an entire school\n\tof magic stays useful forever.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Exalted Resistance<\/span>: Replaces Celestial\n\tResistance. While the spell resistance only applies to spell effects with the\n\tevil descriptor and to spells cast by evil outsiders, this is a benefit as it\n\twon’t apply to your friends’ spells along with your enemies. Pick this if you\n\texpect to fight evil outsiders in your campaign, especially if you expect to\n\tfight them at low levels when spell resistance is exceptionally hard to come\n\tby.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Halo<\/span>: Replaces Darkvision. If you’re not\n\tplanning on scouting in the dark, and you’re going to be the party face, the\n\tlight cantrip and an intimidate bonus is a good trade for darkvision. Plus,\n\tyou can always get Darkvision from a spell or an item if you do need it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Immortal Spark<\/span>: Replaces Skilled and\n\tSpell-like Ability. Lesser Age Resistance lasts all day, so if you play a\n\tmiddle-aged aasimar you can get a +1 ability score increase to all your mental\n\tscores without suffering any of the physical score penalties, which is a huge\n\tbenefit if you’re playing a spellcaster.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Incorruptible<\/span>: Replaces Spell-like Ability.\n\tCorruption Resistance is a bad spell.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Heavenborn<\/span>: Replaces Skilled and Spell-like\n\tAbility. There are very few spells which would benefit from this in a\n\tnoticeable way so it mostly affects spell duration for spells like Daylight,\n\twhich ironically you longer get to cast.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Scion of Humanity<\/span>: Replaces Languages and\n\talters Native Outsider. If there are buffs that you’d like to be able to be\n\table to benefit from, this is worth taking. Additionally, if you take the\n\tRacial heritage feat, you can count as an aasimar, human and one other race,\n\twhich is neat if you have a build in mind that requires race specific\n\tarchetypes or feats.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Truespeaker<\/span>: Replaces Skilled. Learning\n\ttwice the amount of languages per rank invested in linguistics can result in a\n\thuge number of languages known, but you could also buy a Wand of Tongues and\n\tkeep the ranks and +2 bonus to Perception.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Aasimar Feats<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Angel Wings<\/span>: You should have regular\n\taccess to fly by now, but it’s nice to gain a class skill and a flight speed\n\twithout bothering the party caster, using an action, or spending money on\n\titems.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Angelic Blood<\/span>: This is a feat tax towards\n\tgaining Angel\/Metallic Wings, but the ability to deal bleed to yourself to\n\tcause evil creatures to take damage is so funny that it’s almost worth it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Angelic Flesh<\/span>: You choose one option that\n\tchanges your skin and grants you some kind of defense. Gaining fire resistance\n\t5 from Brazen, or +1 natural armor from Steel are both nice, but not so good\n\tthat it’s worth the investment unless you’re attempting to pick up Metallic\n\tWings.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Celestial Servant<\/span>: Spell resistance on a\n\tfamiliar or animal companion is wonderful, and smite evil on it increases\n\ttheir damage output a great deal. However, you’ll want to look for an animal\n\tcompanion with more than 11 charisma to really make use of it, and few regular\n\tanimals meet that criteria.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Channel Force<\/span>: This is a great addition to\n\ta build centered around\n\tChannel Energy<\/a>. Gain the benefits of a bull rush or drag without having to go through an\n\tenemy’s CMD. Area control is always worth your time.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            \n
          • Improved Channel Force<\/span>: Forced movement in\n\t\tan AoE cone or line is incredible.<\/li>\n
          • Greater Channel Force<\/span>: You’re going to\n\t\tbe hard-pressed to find a situation where Improved Channel Force won’t be\n\t\tsufficient, but if you find that situation, it’s nice to have.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

            Consecrate Spell (Metamagic)<\/span>: If you’re\n\tregularly fighting evil creatures, which you likely will be, this is great. If\n\tyou are in an evil campaign or have an evil party member, this is ironically\n\talso good for healing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Heavenly Radiance<\/span>: Flare burst and Wake of\n\tLight are situational at best, but Searing Light, Wandering Star Motes and\n\tSunbeam are all reliable spell-like abilities.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Metallic Wings<\/span>: This takes a huge feat\n\tinvestment just to increase your damage output by a fraction. If you have\n\tsmite, sneak attack, or some other way to add a lot of damage to every attack\n\tin a round, this feat is green<\/span>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Aasimar Classes<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
            <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

            If you do decide to swap out the Skilled trait and the daylight spell-like ability for Immortal Spark and play as a middle-aged character, you have the combined ability score bonuses of a +1 to intelligence, +3 to wisdom and a +3 to charisma with no real penalties unless you’re hit by a dispelling effect. This method of play lends itself to full casters, but every class can make use of it with a little risk involved.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Alchemist<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            With a +2 to wisdom and charisma and no favored class bonuses, the best case\n\tI can make for an aasimar alchemist is that they can lessen the impact of the\n\talchemical penalty to your will saves for consuming a mutagen.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Antipaladin<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            As an alternate version of the paladin, this is very similar to its companion\n\tclass. While the aasimar’s charisma increase improves a few more offensive\n\toptions with the antipaladin, there are a number of features that you are no\n\tlonger able to benefit from. Unlike with the paladin, there is no favored\n\tclass bonus. Additionally, you do not get the option of an animal companion,\n\tso you cannot apply the celestial template to it with the Celestial Servant\n\tfeat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Arcanist<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            A +2 to wisdom helps your will saves, a +2 to charisma helps raise the DC of\n\tyour arcanist exploits, as well as increases the number of times per day you\n\tcan consume spells. If you take Immortal Spark and play a middle-aged\n\tcharacter, the arcanist is blue<\/span> as you gain\n\tability score increases to both of your requisite class abilities.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Barbarian<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Don’t build an aasimar barbarian without a real plan in mind. Otherwise, your\n\tbarbarian is just a little harder to mind control.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Barbarian, Unchained<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            See above.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Bard<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Aasimars make for an excellent bard, gaining a very useful +2 to charisma for\n\tnearly all their features, and the +2 to wisdom improves their will saving\n\tthrow a bit. The favored class bonus additionally lets you pick one bardic\n\tperformance and make it a bit better every 6 levels.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            For flavor I suggest being the descendant of an azata, chaotic-good outsiders\n\twho live to support freedom and the arts.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Bloodrager<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            With a +2 to charisma, you have a nice little bump to your spellcasting stat\n\twhich increases an otherwise small pool of spells per day. Try to find a nice\n\tmiddle ground with how much you invest in charisma, as bloodragers generally\n\tshouldn’t be casting spells that require saves.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Brawler<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            +2 wisdom and charisma don’t give the brawler a lot to work with.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Cavalier<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            The wisdom and charisma bonuses don’t directly make for a good cavalier, but\n\tthe Celestial Servant feat gives your mount a bit more oomph, and the favored\n\tclass bonus allows you to deal extra damage to the target of your challenge,\n\twhich is the primary way you’ll be dishing out damage.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Cleric<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            The most classic aasimar class next to paladin, and there’s a reason for\n\tthat: It’s good. The wisdom bonus is necessary for a full wisdom caster, and\n\tthe favored class bonus lets you smack a little extra damage when channeling\n\tenergy against evil outsiders and undead. If you grab yourself the Channel\n\tForce feat chain, you can really bully them by literally pushing them around.\n\tBe sure to read our\n\tPractical Guide to Channel Energy<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Druid<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Perhaps not what you think of when you envision an aasimar, but a great\n\toption regardless. There’s no favored class bonus for aasimar druids, but the\n\twisdom increase and the ability to gain a celestial animal companion with\n\tCelestial Servant make up for that. The charisma increase helps with using the\n\thandle animal skill to control your companion and on wild empathy checks.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Fighter<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Aasimar doesn’t offer a lot to the fighter that makes it especially exciting,\n\tand fighters traditionally need decent scores across their physical stats.\n\tHowever, with the Scion of Humanity trait and the Racial Heritage feat, you\n\tmay be able to assemble some chimeric hybrid of racial feats and archetypes\n\tthat benefit from the extra combat feats you gain.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Gunslinger<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Gaining a wisdom increase means you’ll have an extra use of grit to use in\n\tyour fights. With a d10 hit die on a ranged character, you can survive through\n\tmost fights with only a few scratches, but a dexterity increase is too crucial\n\tfor the gunslinger to merit a green.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Hunter<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            A great wisdom increase for the hunter, and if you build a ranged character\n\tyou can focus on dexterity otherwise. The charisma is useful for the handle\n\tanimal skill and wild empathy ability. Additionally you can buff your animal\n\tcompanion with the Celestial Servant feat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Investigator<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            With the investigator’s inspiration dice, you can build a shockingly adept\n\tface, but it will likely suffer in combat.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Inquisitor<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            As a martial with wisdom-based spellcasting, this is a solid choice. The\n\tbuild will be quite MAD so you’ll want to pick up heavy armor proficiency. The\n\tfavored class bonus gives you an increase to intimidate, knowledge, and sense\n\tmotive checks against outsiders, which stacks nicely with the intimidate bonus\n\tgained from the alternate Halo trait.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Kineticist<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            You shouldn’t play a kineticist with any race that doesn’t have a racial\n\tconstitution increase.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Magus<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            An intelligence spellcaster and martial hybrid isn’t going to benefit that\n\tmuch from wisdom and charisma racial bonuses, other than slightly better will\n\tsaves.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Medium<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Medium is normally a pretty weird class, as it sort of revels in its MADness.\n\tHowever, with the Immortal Spark trait and being middle aged, you can really\n\tlean into the jack of all trades aspect of the class.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Mesmerist<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Invest heavily into charisma. Never miss a feint, have great will saves, and\n\thigh spell DCs. A solid choice for the aasimar, but a shame that there’s no\n\tfavored class bonus for it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Monk<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Monks are a notoriously MAD class, and aasimars don’t fix that issue.\n\tHowever, the racial wisdom score bonus is good on a monk, and you can make up\n\tthe damage loss from a mediocre or bad strength score by using ki to take\n\tadditional attacks. Your stunning strike will have a decent DC, so you should\n\tuse it often.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Monk, Unchained<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Like the \u201cchained\u201d version of the monk, except it’s better in nearly every\n\tway. Ki powers like qinggong power and elemental fury let you focus even less\n\ton strength in favor of wisdom.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Ninja<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            An aasimar ninja does nearly all the things an aasimar rogue does, but your\n\tcharisma score governs your ki pool, which means you can gain extra attacks\n\tfrom it. The +2 to diplomacy and perception synthesizes nicely with a wisdom\n\tand charisma focused skill monkey.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            If you are willing to invest the feats to get Metallic Wings and take a -2\n\tpenalty from stealth from Angelic Flesh, you can dish out some serious damage\n\tat 11th level by adding sneak attack to your natural attacks on a full\n\tattack.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Occultist<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            A +2 to wisdom and charisma doesn’t do a lot for the occultist, other than\n\thaving slightly better will saves.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Oracle<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            As a charisma caster, the oracle shines from the aasimar’s ability score\n\tbonuses. The favored class bonus is incredible, allowing you access to higher\n\tlevel revelation abilities sooner. This was heavily nerfed by errata (adding\n\t1\/2 to the oracle’s level for the purposes of determining the effects of one\n\trevelation became 1\/6 to the oracle’s level instead), but gaining revelation\n\tfeatures early is still so good that this is entirely worth it. You can, for\n\texample, apply this bonus to the Ancestor mystery revelation Spirit of the\n\tWarrior and get a BAB that is higher than your level for a few rounds a\n\tday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Additionally, there’s an Aasimar-specific archetype for the oracle called the\n\tPurifier, which grants you alignment channel as if you were a cleric. You can\n\talso turn evil outsiders, and command good outsiders as if they were undead,\n\twhich are exceptionally cool abilities.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Paladin<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            The most iconic class for the aasimar, and it holds up to the hype. The\n\tracial charisma bonus interacts positively with a number of paladin features,\n\tand the aasimar doesn’t need to worry about constitution too strictly because\n\tof lay on hands. The favored class bonus grants the aasimar stronger morale\n\teffects from their aura. If the paladin selects an animal companion as their\n\tdivine bond they can get the celestial template six levels early with\n\tCelestial Servant, which they can retrain into another feat at 11th level.

            Additionally,\n\tbuilding towards metallic wings with a paladin is a solid option, as it grants\n\tyou two additional attacks to deliver smite damage without cutting into a\n\tpaladin’s normal full attack.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Psychic<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Aside from slightly better will saves, the aasimar doesn’t do anything for\n\tthe psychic.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Ranger<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            As a primarily ranged martial and wisdom caster, the ranger lends itself well\n\tto the aasimar, and, a ranger with an animal companion as their hunter’s bond\n\tcan pick Celestial Servant to make their companion a good deal stronger. The\n\tcharisma increase is useful for handle animal checks to persuade your animal\n\tcompanion to follow your commands.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Rogue<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            The rogue is a tempting option, but without a racial bonus to strength or\n\tdexterity it’s an uphill battle for an optimizer. As a skill monkey, the\n\twisdom and charisma bonuses are nice. The +2 to perception and diplomacy from\n\tthe skilled trait synergizes nicely.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            You can also get a pretty dramatic damage increase at 11th level with the\n\textra natural attacks gained via the metallic wings feat tree, however, you\n\twill have to invest four feats to get it, taking a -2 penalty to stealth with\n\tthe Angelic Flesh feat. If you really want to play a sneaky aasimar who\n\tattacks with bladed wings, try the ninja.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Rogue, Unchained<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Almost the same as the base rogue, except marginally better. You receive\n\tweapon finesse for free, so it’s a little easier to justify the metallic wings\n\tfeat tree.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Samurai<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            Even though the samurai shares many features with the cavalier, without the\n\tfavored class bonus it is a much less appealing option. You can still apply\n\tthe celestial template to your mount with the Celestial Servant feat, which\n\tmakes them tankier.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Shaman<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            As a wisdom-based full caster, the shaman is a solid choice for the aasimar.\n\tThe shaman’s spirit animal counts as a wizard’s familiar for the purposes of\n\tfeats, which means an aasimar can take the Celestial Servant feat and buff\n\ttheir little friend.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n

            Skald<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            The aasimar works perfectly fine as a skald, with their racial charisma bonus\n\tbenefitting their spellcasting and raging songs. They struggle with physical\n\tattacks, but that’s true of skalds generally.<\/p>\n\n\n

            Slayer<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

            The same logic that applies to the aasimar rogue applies to aasimar slayers,\n\texcept they get fewer skills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Sorcerer<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n
