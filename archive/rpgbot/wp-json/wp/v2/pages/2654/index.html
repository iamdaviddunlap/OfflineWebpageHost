Gunslinger Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

This handbook is for version 1.3 of Matt Mercer’s Gunslinger<\/a> (Affiliate Link<\/a>). While there are numerous homebrew incarnations of a gunslinger class or archetype, Matt Mercer’s has gained a great deal of traction in the DnD community, and I have received numerous requests to cover it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Gunslinger is in many ways similar to the Battle Master Fighter<\/a>. You choose a set of “Trick Shots” which let you expend a limited resource pool (grit points) to do some extra stuff on top of attacking and dealing damage. The Gunslinger was originally adapted from the Pathfinder RPG when the Critical Roll podcast migrated from Pathfinder to 5th Edition DnD, and there are still a handful of things which call back to the Pathfinder rules.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

It’s important to note that Mercer’s Gunslinger was published several years prior to the official Gunner feat’s publication in Tasha’s Cauldron of Everything, and as such the two are not designed to work alongside each other. This handbook assumes that you are not combining the two options.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

This guide is specifically for the Gunslinger Fighter, and omits sections of my typical class handbooks when those sections aren\u2019t meaningfully different from other members of the subclass. For more information on the Fighter, see my Fighter Handbook<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                Gunslinger Fighter Ability Scores<\/h2>\n\n\n

                The Gunslinger runs on Dexterity, but you need some Wisdom for Grit Points.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Str<\/span>: Dump.<\/p>\n

                Dex<\/span>: Dexterity is everything to the\n\tGunslinger. Get as much as you can as fast as you can. It also makes Dexterity-based skills like Stealth a viable choice, allowing you to also serve as your party’s Scout.<\/p>\n

                Con<\/span>: Essential on any adventurer.<\/p>\n

                Int<\/span>: Dump.<\/p>\n

                Wis<\/span>: More Wisdom means more Grit Points and makes Perception a viable skill option.<\/p>\n

                Cha<\/span>: Dump.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
