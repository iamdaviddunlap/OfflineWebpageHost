Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Antipaladin is mechanically very similar to the Paladin, but the\n\tdifferences in their abilities reflect the difference in their alignments.\n\tWhere the Paladin is a capable warrior supplemented by healing and support\n\tabilities, the Antipaladin drops all pretense of healing and support in favor\n\tof additional damage output and debuff options. The Antipaladin gets every\n\tsocial skill except Diplomacy, so if you plan to play a Face be sure to use a\n\ttrait to pick up Diplomacy as a class skill.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Antipaladin is an alternate class of the Paladin, so any archetypes,\n\tfeats, or class features for which the Antipaladin has the correct ability\n\tapplies to the Antipaladin. Many of the Antipaladin’s abilities are identical\n\tto Paladin abilities but with different names, so consult your GM about using\n\toptions which apply to Paladins with your Antipaladin.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Antipaladin Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Hit Points<\/span>: d10 hit points is standard for\n\tfront-line melee characters.<\/p>\n

          Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: Full BAB.<\/p>\n

          Saves<\/span>: Despite a “bad” Reflex saves, Unholy\n\tResilience gives the Antipaladin some of the best saves in the game.<\/p>\n

          Proficiencies<\/span>: Heavy armor, shields, and\n\tmartial weapons.<\/p>\n

          Skills<\/span>: 2+ skill ranks and not a lot of\n\toptions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Aura of Evil<\/span> (Ex): This hardly ever\n\tmatters.<\/p>\n

          Detect Good<\/span> (Sp): Situational.<\/p>\n

          Smite Good<\/span> (Su): Your enemies will primarily\n\tbe good, and Smite Good gives you a pile of damage and a nice bonus to AC. It\n\tremains useful at any level, and is limited only by the small number of uses\n\tper day.<\/p>\n

          Unholy Resilience<\/span> (Su): The equivalent of\n\tDivine Grace, Unholy Resilience<\/p>\n

          Touch of Corruption<\/span> (Su): The damage behind\n\tTouch of Corruption is not the primary draw. Instead, look at the effects of\n\tCruelties. Your GM may allow\n\tyou to do so if you are healed by negative energy, but check with your GM to\n\tbe sure.<\/p>\n

          Aura of Cowardice<\/span> (Su): While this is\n\tnormally not very helpful, it becomes very useful when you pick up the Shaken\n\tand Frightened cruelties.<\/p>\n

          Plague Bringer<\/span> (Ex): Immunity to disease\n\tbecomes important at high levels when disease and poison become more common,\n\tbut not as important as it is for good-aligned characters because good-aligned\n\toutsiders don’t typically use poison or disease.<\/p>\n

          Cruelty<\/span> (Su): Cruelties turn Touch of\n\tCorruption into a powerful single-target crowd-control effect, and eventually\n\tinto a save-or-suck effect once you get the high-tier cruelties. You only get\n\tto apply one at a time, unfortunately, but that’s often enough.<\/p>\n

                    Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

                    The Antipaladin’s abilities are much the same as any heavy armored melee\n\tcharacter, but the Antipaladin’s divine abilities add a dependence on\n\tCharisma.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                    Str<\/span>: The Antipaladin is a strictly melee\n\tclass, which means that you need Strength.<\/p>\n

                    Dex<\/span>: You need 12 to fill out full plate,\n\tbut even that isn’t strictly necessary, especially if you plan to use a\n\tshield.<\/p>\n

                    Con<\/span>: As a front-line character, hit points\n\tare always important.<\/p>\n

                    Int<\/span>: The Antipaladin’s skill list is very\n\tshort, and more skill ranks won’t fix that. Unless you plan to play a Face,\n\tdump Intelligence.<\/p>\n

                    Wis<\/span>: Only required for will saves, and Unholy\n\tResilience and the Antipaladin’s good Will saves greatly reduce this\n\tdependence.<\/p>\n

                    Cha<\/span>: Powers many of the Antipaladins\n\timportant abilities, include Unholy Resilience.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
