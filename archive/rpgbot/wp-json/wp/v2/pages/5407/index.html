A Note About CRs below 1<\/h2>\n\n\n

When counting CR down from 1, the CR progresses by fractions: 1 > 1\/2 >\n\t1\/3 > 1\/4 > 1\/8. If a threat’s CR ever falls by “-1”, and would fall\n\tbelow 1, move it one step along this track.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Example: NPC characters’ CR is their level minus 2. A level 1 Warrior is CR\n\t1\/3, while a level 2 warrior is CR 1\/2.<\/p>\n\n\n

Scaling Encounters for More Than 4 Players<\/h2>\n\n\n

If you’re anything like me, you have almost never fortunate enough to run a\n\tgame exactly 4 players. My ongoing games typically include 5 or 6 players.\n\tWhile I certainly enjoy having my friends around the table, larger parties\n\tprovide some mathematical challenges when designing encounters.<\/p>\n\n\n

Why More Players Requires Adjustment<\/h3>\n\n\n

Adding even one player above 4 can be hugely destabilizing. One extra player\n\tcan be an extra person to flank with, an extra save-or-suck spell every round,\n\tan extra chunk of damage, or a whole mess of buffs (I’m looking at you,\n\t5th-man bard). Any good party is greater than the sum of its parts, and adding\n\textra players does not change that.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Even from level 1, extra players can do a lot to upset the balance of\n\tencounters. As of this writing, I am running\n\tWay of the Wicked<\/a>\n\tfor a party of 6. Without going into any real spoilers, the party begins\n\tunarmed and unarmored in prison. Several of the early encounters pit the party\n\tagainst two guards (human warrior 2), which makes for a CR 1 encounter. For 4\n\tcharacters, this would be a scary fight without normal gear. With 6 players,\n\tthis encounter is a joke, even without weapons or armor.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

To handle scaling, the rulebook suggests adding 1 to the party’s “average\n\tplayer level”. I find that this rule does not typically solve the issue of\n\tadditional players. As an example: The “Advanced Creature” template adds to\n\tthe stats of a creature without cosmetically changing it, and increases its CR\n\tby 1, which should compensate for 6 or more players according to the rulebook.\n\tAny solo monster (a dragon, a beholder, etc.) would still be hard pressed to\n\tdefeat 6 well organized players. This is due in great part to the huge\n\tdifference in the number of actions which the players take compared to the\n\tnumber of actions which the monster could take. A reasonably well built party\n\tof 6 with decent initiative rolle can easily kill a solo monster of their CR\n\t(or sometimes several CRs higher) before it can take a turn.<\/p>\n\n\n
