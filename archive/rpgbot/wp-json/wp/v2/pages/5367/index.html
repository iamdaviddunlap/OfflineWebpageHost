Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Hexes are a great way to customize your Witch. Similar to Rogue Talents or Barbarian Rage Powers, you get one at every even numbered Witch level. However, unlike Rogues, Barbarians, and many other classes with similar customization mechanics, Witches get a Hex at first level in addition to the typical every-even-level progression. This makes single-level class dips into Witch very appealing for many Hexes which don’t depend on a DC or scale with level.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Unless otherwise noted, Hexes can be used an infinite number of times in a day, which really extends the Witch’s usefulness beyond its spells per day.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Hexes<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Beast of Ill-Omen<\/span> (Su): Bane isn’t very good,\n\tand the penalty doesn’t scale with level. However, this can be thrown on your\n\tfamiliar long beforehand, and gives you a free single-target debuff, which is\n\tcool.<\/p>\n

    Blight<\/span> (Su): Hugely situational, and not very\n\tgood even in situtions where it would apply.<\/p>\n

    Cackle<\/span> (Su): Cackle’s effectiveness is\n\tdefined entirely by the effectiveness of the Hex you are extending. Agony is\n\tthe only applicable Hex from the Major Hex list, but it isn’t the best option.\n\tEvil Eye and Misfortune are the best options: their durations are short, but\n\ttheir effects are crippling. Extending the duration of these effects\n\tfunctionally multiplies the usefulness of those hexes.<\/p>\n

    Cauldron<\/span> (Ex): The Witch gets access to a\n\tfantastic list of spells, including Cure spells, so potions can be an\n\texcellent choice. You can also take this at first level and start pumping out\n\tpotions of Cure Light Wounds right off the bat. The +4 bonus to Craft\n\t(Alchemy) is just icing on the cake.<\/p>\n

    Charm<\/span> (Su): The duration is very short,\n\tand improving a targets disposition by one step isn’t much. Casting Charm\n\tPerson will yeild much better results. This can be extended with Cackle, which\n\tcan lead to you standing and cackling like a mad man while your new friend\n\tstares at you uncomfortably.<\/p>\n

    Child-Scent<\/span> (Ex): Unless you are playing\n\tpedo-bear or stealing puppies, this is not useful.<\/p>\n

    Coven<\/span> (Ex): Potentially useful for NPC\n\twitches or for cool roleplaying, but otherwise not very useful for players.<\/p>\n

    Disguise<\/span> (Su): Excellent for infiltration,\n\tbut somewhat situational. Disguise Self isn’t on the Witch spell list, so this\n\tcan add an extra option otherwise not available to the Witch.<\/p>\n

    Evil Eye<\/span> (Su): Adding a -2 to attacks or\n\tsaving throws can be very debilitating. Even with a successful save, the\n\ttarget still takes the penalty for 1 round, which can really help weaken a\n\ttough enemy. Rules as written, you can extend the duration with Cackle even if\n\tthe target passes the initial save. Increasing the penalty to -4 makes this\n\teven better. However, you may find that you are often better served by casting\n\ta spell.<\/p>\n

    Feral Speech<\/span> (Su): Animals very rarely\n\thave anything interesting to discuss.<\/p>\n

    Flight<\/span> (Su): The bonus to swim seems a\n\tlittle but out of place, but makes sense as flavor because you are supposedly\n\tlighter. Fly is not on the Witch spell list, and flight is crucial for\n\tspellcasters, especially at high levels. Splitting the duration into\n\tone-minute increments helps to stretch the usage throughout the day more than\n\tyou could expect from a single Fly spell. Keep in mind that you can still use\n\tFeather Fall at will to save any falling allies.<\/p>\n

    Fortune<\/span> (Su): Because it costs your\n\tstandard action to use this, don’t expect to use this in combat. Instead, use\n\tit for skill checks on things like disabling traps, and on saving throws\n\tagainst ongoing effects outside of combat.<\/p>\n

    Healing<\/span> (Su): This provides a bit of free\n\thealing every day. While this is incredibly useful at first level, it will\n\tquickly be eclipsed by a Wand of Cure Light Wounds. The scaling at 5th level\n\tis a nice nod, but not enough to keep this relevant as your level\n\tincreases.<\/p>\n

    Misfortune<\/span> (Su): Pick the biggest enemy in\n\tthe fight, and drop this on them in round one, then extend it with Cackle.\n\tRerolling every attack will greatly improve your party’s chances of survival,\n\tand rerolling saving throws will make the target considerably more vulnerable\n\tto effects of all kinds.<\/p>\n

    Nails<\/span> (Ex): You really shouldn’t be\n\tspending a lot of time in melee combat. However, this can be a good choice for\n\tclass dipping or for the Hexcrafter Magus.<\/p>\n

    Poison Steep<\/span> (Sp): Getting access to the\n\tPoison spell as early as 2nd level is great, but this is very difficult to\n\tbring into play. You need to find some way force your enemies to eat whatever\n\tyou poison. Granted, this could work pretty easily by throwing poisoned meat\n\tinto rooms with unintelligent (and hungry) monsters or animals.<\/p>\n

    Prehensile Hair<\/span> (Su): This is fantastic\n\tfor class dips for Rogues or characters focuses on natural weapons. I can’t\n\tthink of any other source which adds a Hair attack, so this is a great way way\n\tto add an extra attack to your full attacks. However, a typically witch will\n\thave trouble using this for anything but flavor.<\/p>\n

    Scar<\/span> (Su): This has some interesting\n\toptions. The most obvious is to scar a recurring enemy, but a better option is\n\tto scar your allies. With the ability to cast Hexes on your allies from up to\n\ta mile away, you can use Hexes like Healing, Fortune, or Ward while you are\n\toff doing other things. Heck, you could sit outside while your party goes into\n\tthe dungeon and support them with Hexes at long range.<\/p>\n

    Slumber<\/span> (Su): Single target save or suck.\n\tThe DC scales to keep pace with your spells, and affects creatures of any hit\n\tdie. There is no Witch for whom this is not a fantastic option.<\/p>\n

    Swamp Hag<\/span> (Sp): Hugely situational.<\/p>\n

    Tongues<\/span> (Su): Comprehend Language and\n\tTongues are situational, but come up frequently. Having both always available\n\tis nice, but both are already on the Witch’s spell list.<\/p>\n

    Unnerve Beasts<\/span> (Su): Hugely situational, and\n\twill probably hurt you more than anyone else.<\/p>\n

    Water Lung<\/span> (Su): Hugely situational, but\n\tWater Breathing isn’t on the Witch spell list.<\/p>\n

    Ward<\/span> (Su): Throw this on your party’s\n\tDefender before and after every fight. Losing the bonus every time it is\n\tbypassed is annoying, but this can hugely reduce the subject’s need for a Ring\n\tof Protection or a Cloak of Resistance.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Major Hexes<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Agony<\/span> (Su): Nauseated largely disables the\n\ttarget, and makes them very vulnerable. However, Slumber puts things to sleep\n\tand will have the same save DC.<\/p>\n

    Beast Eye<\/span> (Su): Conceptually very cool,\n\tbut Arcane Eye is probably better. You do gain the animals senses, which is\n\tnice for Scent, but you don’t get control of the creature.<\/p>\n

    Cook People<\/span> (Su): A one-hour duration is\n\tpretty great for spells like Bull’s Strength, which typically have durations\n\tin minutes per caster level. Age resistance is already on the Witch spell list and has a\n\tconsiderably longer duration than one hour, so don’t waste your time on that\n\teffect. You might consider keeping cookies of Neutralize Poisong and Cure\n\tDisease hand because they don’t cost anything to create. Keep in mind that\n\tcooking and eating a sentient creature is generally considered an evil act, so\n\tcheck with your GM if your character is not evil.<\/p>\n

    Hag’s Eye<\/span> (Su): Arcane Eye is a very solid\n\tdivination, but it’s already on the Witch spell list.<\/p>\n

    Hidden Home<\/span> (Sp): Very cool for NPC\n\twitches or for campaigns where you spend a lot of time in one place, but\n\totherwise not useful for a typical adventurer.<\/p>\n

    Hoarfrost<\/span> (Su): Horrifying and deadly, but\n\tbecause the damage only ticks once per minute, this is totally useless in\n\tcombat.<\/p>\n

    Ice Tomb<\/span> (Su): This can serve as either a\n\tsave or suck effect to disable and capture enemies, or as a suspended\n\tanimation effect to protect and preserve your allies. For strict combat\n\tpurposes, Slumber is better.<\/p>\n

    Infected Wounds<\/span> (Su): Totally useless in\n\tcombat, and disease effects can be removed very easily, so this won’t be very\n\teffective.<\/p>\n

    Major Healing<\/span> (Su): Free healing is always\n\tnice, but by this level, a Cleric will have Heal available, and you will get\n\tit before this Hex scales up to Cure Critical Wounds.<\/p>\n

    Nightmares<\/span> (Su): Hugely situational. If you\n\tdislike someone enough to curse them with this, it’s generally better to just\n\tkill them.<\/p>\n

    Retribution<\/span> (Su): This is great for big\n\tdamage-dealing monsters. It’s unfortunate that you or your allies need to take\n\tdamage for the Hex to take effect, but the reality of combat is that your\n\tallies are going to take damage at some point.<\/p>\n

    Speak in Dreams<\/span> (Sp): Situational, but\n\tvery cool.<\/p>\n

    Vision<\/span> (Su): Entirely dependant on your\n\tGM.<\/p>\n

    Waxen Image<\/span> (Su): This is a cool way to\n\tcontrol or debuff a target. The individual effects aren’t super powerful, and\n\tspending a standard action to activate them is probably not as good as casting\n\ta spell, but the effects could be very powerful if used well. Consider making\n\tthe target move in combat to draw attacks of opportunity, or making your enemy\n\tstaggered before he can charge.<\/p>\n

    Weather Control<\/span> (Su): Situational, but\n\tpotentially very powerful.<\/p>\n

    Witch’s Brew<\/span> (Ex): By this level, potions\n\tare usually not relevant. Also, many GMs allow the creation of multiple items\n\tin a day as a house rule because spening 8 hours to create one potion which\n\ttakes up 5% of the gold worth of magic items that you can create in a day\n\tseems completely ridiculous.<\/p>\n\n\n
