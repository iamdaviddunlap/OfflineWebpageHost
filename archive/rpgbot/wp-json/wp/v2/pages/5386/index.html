Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Arcane Trickster is a very popular concept: a trickster who meshes the\n\tstealth and skill of a Rogue with the power and versatility of an arcane\n\tspellcaster. There is little that an Arcane Trickster can’t do, and with a\n\tdecent build an Arcane Trickster can fill any role in the party except\n\tDefender and Healer. Depending on build, the Arcane Trickster can supply a\n\thuge quantity of damage output, and can exceed the Rogue in stealth,\n\tversatility, and scouting ability.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

However, the Arcane Trickster has trouble keeping pace with the rest of the\n\tparty at mid levels before it has a few levels of Arcane Trickster. During\n\tthis middle period, you will need to be clever and careful to stay useful and\n\talive.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Requirements<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Alignment<\/strong>: Shouldn’t be a problem.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Skills<\/strong>: 4 ranks in three skills is trivial, and you will\n\twant to have both Disable Device and Knowledge (Arcana) on most builds.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Spells<\/strong>: Almost any arcane spellcasting class gets access to\n\tMage Hand, but access to 2nd level spells is a crucial component in deciding\n\tyour base class. See “Base Classes”, below.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Special<\/strong>: 2d6 Sneak Attack is easily achieved by a variety of\n\tclasses, and you should expect to have 2 Sneak Attack dice by level 6. You\n\tcould take one level in two classes which offer Sneak attack to get into\n\tArcane Trickster early, but you will give up a lot of useful low level class\n\tabilities, and a much-needed +1 BAB. See “Base Classes”, below.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Arcane Trickster Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Hit Points<\/span>: The lowest hit points available.\n\tArcane Tricksters need to be careful not to draw fire.<\/p>\n

          Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 1\/2 BAB.<\/p>\n

          Saves<\/span>: Good Reflex and Fortitude saves. Be\n\tsure to invest in Constitution to boost the Arcane Trickster’s lousy Fortitude\n\tsaves.<\/p>\n

          Proficiencies<\/span>: None. You should get\n\teverything you need from your base classes.<\/p>\n

          Skills<\/span>: The Arcane Trickster only gets 4+\n\tskill ranks, and has a very long list of very important skills.<\/p>\n

          Spells per Day<\/span>: The Arcane Trickster\n\tadvances spellcasting at every level, meaning that you only give up\n\tspellcasting levels for your Sneak Attack class. Note that, like every other\n\tprestige class with spell advancement, classes which have a specific “Spells\n\tKnown” table will learn new spells, but classes like the Witch and Wizard\n\twhich can learn spells on their own do not gain new spells known. This is\n\tinconvenient, but you can always buy new spells.<\/p>\n

          Ranged Legerdemain<\/span> (Su): Situational, but\n\tvery flashy.<\/p>\n

          Sneak Attack<\/span>: Increases at the same rate as\n\tthe Rogue. Note that if you took the Knife Master Rogue Archetype, only your\n\tRogue Sneak Attack dice are d8’s; your Arcane Trickster dice are still\n\td6’s.<\/p>\n

          Impromptu Sneak Attack<\/span> (Ex): The Arcane\n\tTrickster gets a lot of great tricks which allow them to use Sneak Attack,\n\tlike casting Invisibility. Sometimes you need a little extra help, which is\n\twhere Impromptu Sneak Attack comes in. You only get it once a day (twice at\n\t7th level), but it’s good to have this in a pinch.<\/p>\n

          Tricky Spells<\/span> (Su): Situational, but\n\tfantastic in a grapple or if you need to do something clever like casting an\n\tillusion while someone is staring at you.<\/p>\n

          Invisible Thief<\/span> (Su): Wizards can cast\n\tGreater Invisibility by 7th level, and other full arcane casters can cast it\n\tby 8th level. With 3 levels given up to get into Arcane Trickster, an Arcane\n\tTrickster can expect to cast Greater Invisibility at 10th or 11th character\n\tlevel. You get Invisible Thief at 15th or 17th character level. The rounds per\n\tday are .<\/p>\n

          Surprise Spells<\/span>: This is where the Arcane\n\tTrickster overtakes nearly every other Blaster in the game. Drop a Cone of\n\tCold on a group of enemies and you will exceed other spellcasters by 6 or 7d6\n\tdamage. Scorching Ray will still be your bread and butter, but a big AOE is a\n\tfantastic opener.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Base Classes<\/h2>\n\n

          The Arcane Trickster requires two base classes: One to provide arcane spells,
          \n\tand one to provide Sneak Attack.<\/p>\n

          <\/p>\n

          Spellcasting Class<\/h3>\n\n\n

          Bloodrager<\/h4>\n

          Literally the worst option you can find. It takes a full 8 levels to meet the\n\tArcane Trickster’s spellcasting requirements, and the Bloodrager’s paltry\n\tnumber of spell slots isn’t nearly enough to fuel your need to constantly cast\n\tScorching Ray. If you wanted to build a weird melee trickster it may look\n\ttempting, but giving up 8 levels of Sneak Attack progression largely\n\tinvalidates the purpose of the class.<\/p>\n

          Arcanist<\/h4>\n

          The Arcanist receives spells at the same levels as the sorcerer, but gets as\n\tmany spell slots as a wizard, combining the drawbacks of both classes. The\n\tarcanist’s big draw is the ability to change their spell selection on a daily\n\tbasis, then cast from that pool as though they were a spontaneous caster.\n\tArcane tricksters typically cast from a small set of favored spells which\n\tnever changes, so the appeal of the Arcanist is largely wasted on the arcane\n\ttrickster.<\/p>\n

          Bard<\/h4>\n

          Bards get second level spells at the same level as the Sorcerer, and have\n\tmuch better skills. Unfortunately, the Bard doesn’t have access to offensive\n\tspells which can be used to Sneak Attack. If you plan to be more of a melee\n\ttrickster, the Bard offers some interesting archetypes.<\/p>\n

          Archetypes<\/h5>\n

          Only archetypes which take effect before you switch to Arcane Trickster\n\tlevels are included.<\/p>\n

          Arcane Duelist<\/span>: A more melee-focused Bard,\n\tthe Arcane Duelist gives up much of the Bard’s skill abilities to focus on\n\tdealing damage. Bardic Music won’t be very helpful, but the Arcane Duelist\n\tgets both Arcane Strike and a bonus feat by level 3.<\/p>\n

          Archaeologist<\/span>: The Archaeologist’s Luck\n\tability is a much better option for the Arcane Trickster than Bardic\n\tPerformance. Clever Explorer helps with scouting, and Uncanny Dodge can be\n\thelpful on a lightly armored character.<\/p>\n

          Archivist<\/span>: Still stuck with a variant of\n\tBardic Performance, but Lore Master lets you take 20 on one Knowledge check in\n\ta day, and Magic Lore can largely replace Trapfinding.<\/p>\n

          Magus<\/h4>\n

          The Magus gets second level spells at the same level as the Sorcerer, and\n\toffers some very interesting options for a melee trickster. 4 levels brings\n\tArcane Pool, Spell Combat, Spellstrike, one Magus Arcana, and Spell Recall.\n\tWith a bit of focus, this combination of abilities can make for a fantastic\n\tpile of damage by combining Spellstrike with Sneak Attack. In addition, the\n\tMagus allows you to cast spells in light armor, which helps with the Arcane\n\tTrickster’s AC problems.<\/p>\n

          Archetypes<\/h5>\n

          Only archetypes which take effect before you switch to Arcane Trickster\n\tlevels are included.<\/p>\n

          Bladebound<\/span>: The blade’s abilities won’t scale\n\twhile you take Arcane Trickster levels, so it will quickly become useless.<\/p>\n

          Eldritch Scion<\/span>: The Eldritch Scion is\n\tCharisma-based, which can be a good option if you plan to use the Ninja\n\tbecause it will help with your Ki Pool.<\/p>\n

          Hexcrafter<\/span>: If you don’t plan to use Spell\n\tRecall, gaining a free Witch Hex can be very helpful.<\/p>\n

          Kensai<\/span>: You are already giving up a lot of\n\tspellcasting to be an Arcane Trickster. Don’t give up more.<\/p>\n

          Myrmidarch<\/span>: You are already giving up a lot\n\tof spellcasting to be an Arcane Trickster. Don’t give up more.<\/p>\n

          Skirnir<\/span>: A shield helps with the Arcane\n\tTrickster’s AC problem, but the diminished spellcasting is crippling.<\/p>\n

          Soul Forger<\/span>: You are already giving up a lot\n\tof spellcasting to be an Arcane Trickster. Don’t give up more.<\/p>\n

          Spellblade<\/span>: Two-Weapon Fighting is very hard\n\tfor the Arcane Trickster because their BAB is terrible.<\/p>\n

          Staff Magus<\/span>: This doesn’t really improve\n\tthe Magus in this case.<\/p>\n

          Sorcerer<\/h4>\n

          The Sorcerer gets 2nd-level spells one level behind the Wizard, so it tends\n\tto be used less than the Wizard. However, the sorcerer’s larger pool of spell\n\tslots can be appealing for arcane tricksters because they typically rely on\n\tsuch a small pool of favored spells.<\/p>\n

          Archetypes<\/h5>\n

          Only archetypes which take effect before you switch to Arcane Trickster\n\tlevels are included.<\/p>\n

          Crossblooded<\/span>: Giving up spells known is very\n\thard when you’re already giving up 3 levels of spellcasting to become an\n\tArcane Trickster.<\/p>\n

          Eldritch Scrapper<\/span>: You won’t gain enough\n\tlevels to make use of Martial Flexibility.<\/p>\n

          Mongrel Mage<\/span>: Because you will only take 4\n\tlevels of Sorcerer, you will get very few bloodline powers, and the special\n\tabilities will quickly stop mattering.<\/p>\n

          Witch<\/h4>\n

          Witches get spells at the same rate that wizards do, and give you a familiar\n\t(familiars are great for skill-heavy characters). Three levels of Witch gets\n\tyou two Hexes, which gives you a lot great utility options. Fortune is great\n\tfor your skills, Prehensile Hair will let you dual-weild crossbows if that’s\n\tsomething you want, and Nails can give you a decent backup melee option. See\n\tmy\n\tWitch Hex Breakdown<\/a>\n\tfor more help picking hexes.<\/p>\n

          Archetypes<\/h5>\n

          Most witch archetypes are bad, and none of them help the Arcane Trickster in\n\tany way. Gravewalker looks tempting, but Possess Undead is the only thing that\n\tworks well for tricksters, and it doesn’t come online until 8th level.<\/p>\n

          Patrons<\/h5>\n

          Wizard<\/h4>\n

          Wizards get 2nd-level spells earlier than any other Arcane spellcaster,\n\tmaking them the obvious choice for the Arcane Trickster’s spellcasting class.\n\tThe ability to buy spells known dramatically improves the trickster’s\n\tversatility. The only issue with the Wizard is the relatively limited number\n\tof spells per day.<\/p>\n

          Archetypes<\/h5>\n

          Only archetypes which take effect before you switch to Arcane Trickster\n\tlevels are included.<\/p>\n

          Arcane Bomber<\/span>: Literally never.<\/p>\n

          Exploiter Wizard<\/span>: Arcane Bond and Arcane\n\tSchool offer a lot of great options, but the ability to boost your spell DC\n\tand caster level can help you keep up with dedicated spellcasters. An Arcanist\n\tExploit offers some interesting options.<\/p>\n

          Scrollmaster<\/span>: Save this for straight\n\tcasters.<\/p>\n

          Siege Mage<\/span>: An Arcane Trickster is not using\n\tsiege weapons.<\/p>\n

          Spell Sage<\/span>: Focused Spells will get you a\n\tfree ray with Scorching Ray, which is nice, but Spell Study uses spell slots\n\ttoo quickly for the Arcane Trickster.<\/p>\n

          Spellslinger<\/span>: Not as awful for the Arcane\n\tTrickster as it is for a normal Wizard, the Spellslinger’s access to firearms\n\tis an interesting option. This greatly improves the number of spells with\n\twhich you can Sneak Arrack because you can turn cone and line spells into\n\tattacks. However, this limits you to a single shot per round when you can use\n\tScorching Ray to make several.<\/p>\n

          Spirit Whisperer<\/span>: If you planned to take a\n\tfamiliar, you can trade your arcane school for a small buff to your\n\tfamiliar.<\/p>\n

          Schools<\/h5>\n

          Abjuration<\/span>: The Arcane Trickster isn’t much\n\tof a support character, and defensive buffs don’t help much.<\/p>\n

          Conjuration<\/span>: Summoning isn’t typically in\n\tthe Arcane Trickster’s wheelhouse, but the Teleportation subschool can be\n\tfantastically helpful for the Arcane Trickster.<\/p>\n

          Divination<\/span>: Forewarned ensures that, even if\n\tyou’re surprised, you can probably Sneak Attack in the surprise round. The\n\tForesight subschool offers a fantastic reroll mechanic.<\/p>\n

          Enchantment<\/span>: Save or suck effects don’t\n\treally work for the Arcane Trickster because their spell DC’s won’t keep up\n\twith full casters.<\/p>\n

          Evocation<\/span>: Intense Spells offers a tiny\n\tdamage boost to your spells, and the Admixture subschool makes it easier to\n\tuse Scorching Ray when you encounter enemies which are resistant to fire. This\n\tdoesn’t seem like much, but Scorching Ray is important to the Arcane\n\tTrickster’s most common tactic, and it can be completely shut down by\n\tresistance or immunity to fire.<\/p>\n

          Illusion<\/span>: Extended Illusions won’t really\n\thelp because you only get 3 levels of it. Blinding ray is helpful in a pinch,\n\tbut it means that you’re only Sneak Attacking every other round. While you use\n\tit.<\/p>\n

          Necromancy<\/span>: Arcane Tricksters can’t do much\n\twith undead pets.<\/p>\n

          Transmutation<\/span>: Physical Enhancement will\n\tonly give you a +1 to one ability. Battleshaping out of the Shapechange\n\tsubschool can be nice to get an extra natural attack to Sneak Attack with if\n\tyou’re in melee.<\/p>\n

          Universalist<\/span>: Hand of the Apprentice is\n\tmediocre. Arcane Tricksters are just as good at throwing a weapon normally.<\/p>\n

          Air<\/span>: Garbage.<\/p>\n

          Earth<\/span>: Garbage.<\/p>\n

          Fire<\/span>: Garbage.<\/p>\n

          Water<\/span>: Garbage.<\/p>\n

          Metal<\/span>: Garbage.<\/p>\n

          Wood<\/span>: On par with Transmutation, and you\n\tget some Druid spells which don’t really help the Arcane Trickster.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Sneak Attack Class<\/h3>\n\n\n

          Alchemist<\/h4>\n

          Only usable with the Vivisectionist archetype (see below).<\/p>\n

          Archetypes<\/h5>\n

          Vivisectionist<\/span>: A very interesting option,\n\tthe Vivisectionist gets +2d6 Sneak Attack at level 3, Brew Potion, Mutagen,\n\tThrow Anything, a Discovery, Poison Resistance, Poison Use, Swift Alchemy,\n\tTorturer’s Eye, Cruel Anatomist, and 3 1st-level extracts per day. However,\n\tthe Alchemist only gets 4+ skill ranks, and skill ranks will be a problem for\n\tmany Arcane Trickster builds attempting to fill in for a conventional rogue.\n\tIf you combine alchemist with Wizard you can double-dip on Intelligence, which\n\twill help compensate for the lack of skill ranks from your class.<\/p>\n

          Ninja<\/h4>\n

          On par with the Rogue, the Ninja gets +2d6 Sneak Attack at level 3, and\n\tbrings Poison Use, the Ninja’s Ki Pool, a Ninja Trick, and No Trace. The size\n\tof the Ki Pool won’t be very large because it’s level-dependent, but it offers\n\tsome useful options like Vanishing Trick (turn Invisible as a swift action).\n\tThe reliance on Charisma works well if you plan to use Sorcerer as your\n\tspellcasting class, but you’ll lag behind other Arcane Trickster builds in\n\tskills, so this option may work best in parties with another high-skill\n\tcharacter to fill the gaps.<\/p>\n

          Rogue<\/h4>\n

          The gold standard since Arcane Trickster was written, the Rogue gets +2d6\n\tSneak Attack at level 3 and brings Evasion, Trapfinding, Trap Sense, and a\n\tRogue Talent. The vanilla and unchained rogues will both work fine, but the\n\tthe unchained version is outright better in nearly every way, with the\n\tpossible exception of the removal of some of my favorite Rogue Talents, like\n\tOffensive Defense. The biggest difference you’ll see in the three levels we\n\tcare about is whether or not you get Finesse Training, which only matters if\n\tyou want to use melee weapons.<\/p>\n

          Archetypes<\/h5>\n

          Only archetypes which take effect before you switch to Arcane Trickster\n\tlevels are included.<\/p>\n

          Counterfeit Mage<\/span>: Magical Expertise is the\n\tonly ability which the Arcane Trickster will see from this archetype until\n\tvery high level, and because the Arcane Trickster can already cast spells UMD\n\tis much less important.<\/p>\n

          Knife Master<\/span>: The Arcane Trickster will\n\tfrequently Sneak Attack with spells, and we don’t want to give up that\n\tdamage.<\/p>\n

          Poisoner<\/span>: The Arcane Trickster already has\n\tplenty of tricks without Poison, but if you really want to use Poison the\n\tPoisoner archetype is a must.<\/p>\n

          Rake<\/span>: Intimidating a target makes them\n\tShaken, and therefore more vulnerable to your other spells, so Bravado’s Blade\n\tcan be very effective for the Arcane Trickster.<\/p>\n

          Sniper<\/span>: You trade your only Rogue Talent for\n\t10 feet more Sneak Attack range. Slightly useful, but getting a Rogue Talen\n\tallows you to take the Extra Talent feat to get more.<\/p>\n

          Slayer<\/h4>\n

          Takes far too long to get enough Sneak Attack.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

          The Arcane Trickster’s abilities depend heavily on your choice of base class, especially Intelligence and Charisma.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Str<\/span>: Dump to 7 on nearly any Arcane\n\tTrickster.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: Important for AC, saves, and\n\tattacks.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: Crucial for the Arcane Trickster’s bad\n\tFortitude saves and lousy hit points.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: If you use the Arcanist, Magus, or Wizard, this is\n\tblue.<\/span>. If not, it’s\n\torange<\/span> because you need it for skills.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: Only needed for Will saves, and the\n\tcombination of multiclassing with spellcasting classes should give you a\n\tdecent base Will save.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: If you use the Bard or Sorcerer, this is\n\tblue<\/span>, otherwise it’s\n\torange<\/span> because it’s helpful for social\n\tskills.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Races<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Bonuses to Dexterity and\/or your spellcasting ability are great, but technically not essential because the Arcane Trickster doesn’t depend on spell DC’s.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Dwarf<\/span>: Nothing helpful to the Arcane\n\tTrickster.<\/p>\n

          Elf<\/span>: With bonuses to both Dexterity and\n\tIntelligence, the Elf is a fantastic option for the Arcane Trickster. The\n\tElf’s other racial abilities all contribute to the Arcane Trickster’s\n\tspellcasting abilities.<\/p>\n

          Gnome<\/span>: The Gnome is fine, but the Halfling\n\tis strictly better for the Arcane Trickster.<\/p>\n

          Half-Elf<\/span>: Everyone immediately thinks\n\tHalf-Elf when considering multiclass characters, and for good reason. However,\n\tdual-minded will get you at most 3 levels of favored class bonuses, so it’s\n\treally not worth it. The Half-Elf’s other racial bonuses are still good,\n\tespecially if your Arcane Trickster is a Face. If you want Darkvision (and you\n\tdo), consider the Drow-Blooded alternate class feature.<\/p>\n

          Half-Orc<\/span>: The flexible ability bonus is\n\tnice, and Darkvision is essential for any stealth character.<\/p>\n

          Halfling<\/span>: The best option for a small Arcane\n\tTrickster, the Halfling’s bonuses to Dexterity and Charisma are fantastic. The\n\tHalflings other racial abilities contribute to the Arcane Trickster’s stealth\n\tcapacity.<\/p>\n

          Human<\/span>: Always fantastic, Humans make for an\n\texcellent Arcane Trickster.<\/p>\n\n\n
