          Alchemist Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

          Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points is good for a\n\tprimarily magical character, but not great if you spend a lot of time in close\n\tcombat.<\/p>\n

          Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/3 BAB.<\/p>\n

          Saves<\/span>: Good Fortitude and Reflex saves, but\n\tWill saves will be a problem, especially if you use your Mutagen to boost your\n\tDexterity.<\/p>\n

          Proficiencies<\/span>: Simple weapons and light\n\tarmor. Barely better than a Wizard.<\/p>\n

          Skills<\/span>: 4+ skill ranks, and Craft\n\t(Alchemy) is basically required. The Alchemist is Intelligence-based, so they\n\tshould have plenty of bonus skill ranks to pick up the few good skills\n\tavailable to the Alchemist, and you might consider picking up additional class\n\tskills using traits.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

          Alchemy<\/span> (Su): The Alchemist works a bit like\n\ta 2\/3 prepared caster, but they drink all of their spells. Be sure to pick up\n\tthe Infusion Discovery so that you can share your Extracts with the party.<\/p>\n

          Bomb<\/span> (Su): An excellent blast at first\n\tlevel, the Bomb’s damage drops off quickly compared to spells. If you plan to\n\tuse bombs, be sure to invest some Discoveries to improve Bomb. Also consider\n\t“Bomb Admixture” spells which supplement the effects of your bombs.<\/p>\n

          Brew Potion<\/span> (Ex): Potions aren’t fantastic\n\tin terms of item crafting, but it never hurts to hand out cheap healing\n\tpotions to the party.<\/p>\n

          Mutagen<\/span> (Su): 10 minutes per level will\n\tlast a reasonably long time. The bonuses to your abilities are fantastic, and\n\tstack with enhancement bonuses. Be careful of which ability you choose to\n\tincrease: improving Strength will improve damage with thrown weapons, but will\n\thandicap your Bombs and Extracts. Dexterity will improve AC, attack rolls with\n\tranged weapons, and Reflex saves, but will handicap your already low Will\n\tsaves.<\/p>\n

          Throw Anything<\/span> (Ex): The Throw Anything\n\tfeat won’t really help much unless you like throwing garbage or non-ranged\n\tweapons, but the bonus damage with splash weapons dramatically improves the\n\tdamage of alchemical weapons like Acid. Note that this bonus damage doesn’t\n\tget added to the Alchemist’s Bomb damage because Bomb already adds the\n\tAlchemist’s intelligence bonus to damage.<\/p>\n

          Discovery<\/span> (Su): Alchemist Discoveries are\n\tpowerful and unique ways to customize your alchemist, and a class dip into\n\tAlchemist is a common choice for many builds (especially those that need an\n\textra arm to reload weapons). For help selecting Alchemist Discoveries, see my\n\tAlchemist Discovery Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n

          Poison Resistance<\/span> (Ex): Situational, but\n\tpoison becomes common at high levels where your bonus is substantial.<\/p>\n

          Poison Use<\/span> (ex): The Alchemist isn’t a big\n\tweapon user, so it’s difficult for them to find a way to make use of poison.\n\tHowever, if you have someone in the party who doesn’t mind using poison you\n\tcan poison their weapons for them and send them into combat.<\/p>\n

          Swift Alchemy<\/span> (Ex): Crafting alchemical\n\titems more quickly further improves your access to alchemical items like Acid\n\twhich you will expend quickly. Poisoning weapons as a move action is nice in\n\tcombat, but the Alchemist still isn’t combative enough to be notably scary\n\twith poison on their own weapons.<\/p>\n

          Swift Poisoning<\/span> (Ex): Poison weapons even\n\tfaster! Of course you probably still need to pass them to someone who’s good\n\tenough with weapons for it to matter.<\/p>\n

          Persistent Mutagen<\/span> (Su): The Mutagen’s\n\tbiggest limitation is its duration, and multiplying the duration by 6 is a\n\thuge improvement.<\/p>\n

          Instant Alchemy<\/span> (Ex): It’s pretty rare\n\tthat you need to whip up an item in the middle of combat, and the only reason\n\tto poison a weapon as an immediate action is to use it for an attack of\n\topportunity.<\/p>\n

          Grand Discovery<\/span> (Su): Grand Discoveries are\n\tamazing. For help selecting Alchemist Discoveries, see my\n\tAlchemist Discovery Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

          Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

          The vanilla Alchemist is a ranged character with 2\/3 Intelligence-based
          spellcasting. Focus on Dexterity and Intelligence. If you want to use
          polymorph, or plan to go into melee combat, you may instead want to focus on
          Strength.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

          Str<\/span>: Only important if you plan to use\n\tconventional thrown weapons.<\/p>\n

          Dex<\/span>: The Alchemist is primarily a ranged\n\tcharacter.<\/p>\n

          Con<\/span>: Hit points are always important.<\/p>\n

          Int<\/span>: Required for the Alchemist’s Extracts,\n\tbut not quite as crucial as it is for a full spellcaster. You can get away\n\twith starting with 16 if you want to emphasize other abilities.<\/p>\n

          Wis<\/span>: Only required for Will saves, but\n\tdon’t dump it.<\/p>\n

          Cha<\/span>: The Alchemist has no social skills, and\n\tno abilities that depend on Charisma, so dump it to 7.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
