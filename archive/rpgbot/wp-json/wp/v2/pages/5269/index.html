Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Magus is everything that the Eldritch Knight wants to be; a potent combination of the Wizard’s offensive magic and the Fighter’s martial prowess. In combat, the Magus is defined by Spellstrike, which allows the Magus to deliver spells through a weapon attack. The Magus can serve the party as a Striker and Librarian, but has the spells to serve as a Support should the need arise.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Magus Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

            Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points is hard for a\n\tstrictly melee class, so be sure to keep your AC high.<\/p>\n

            Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: 2\/3 BAB is\n\tacceptable,\n\tand if you keep your weapon well enhanced and your Dexterity high, it will\n\tbe plenty.<\/p>\n

            Saves<\/span>: Strong Fortitude and Will saves\n\tare great, and your high Dexterity will compensate for the low Reflex\n\tsave.<\/p>\n

            Proficiencies<\/span>: All of the weapon and\n\tarmor proficiencies that you will need. The late introduction of medium and\n\theavy armor proficiency are annoying because they introdcue components to\n\tthe build much later than you need them in order to take advantage of\n\tthem.<\/p>\n

            Skills<\/span>: 2+ skill ranks isn’t much, but\n\tyour high Intelligence will let you get whatever skills you need. Don’t\n\tforget that increasing your intelligence retroactively awards you skill\n\tranks, and Headband of Vast Intelligence grants you one rank per character\n\tlevel in one skill for every 2 points of enhancement bonus. What? Didn’t\n\tknow that? Yeah, no one beleives me when I tell them that, but it’s\n\tright there<\/a>\n\tin the core rules.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

            Spells<\/span>: The Magus gets 2\/3 casting,\n\tsimilar to a Bard, but is much more dependant on spells. Spell Combat and\n\tSpellstrike are the Magus’ two most iconic combat options, and the\n\teffectiveness of those abilities is entirely defined by the Magus’ choice\n\tof\n\tspells.<\/p>\n

            Spellbooks<\/span>: Spellbooks are what make the\n\tMagus and Wizard so much more versatile than the Bard and Sorcerer. You can\n\tcompletely change your list of tricks every day to fit whatever you need,\n\tand much like a Wizard you can have a functionally infinite number of\n\tspells\n\tknown.<\/p>\n

            Arcane Pool<\/span> (Su): Arcane pool fuels a\n\tlot\n\tof really cool abilities for the Magus, especially his ability to enhance\n\this weapon. If you really like enhancing your weapon this way, be sure to\n\tpick up the Enduring Blade Magus Arcana to help stretch your Arcane Pool\n\tpoints. Avoid using Arcane Pool to get the Keen property: you should have a\n\t+1 keen rapier without Arcane Pool, and supplement it with Arcane Pool.<\/p>\n

            Cantrips<\/span>: The Magus doesn’t get a lot of\n\tcantrips, but that doesn’t make them any less awesome. Be sure to bring\n\tDisrupt Undead when you get Spellstrike because you can channel Disrupt\n\tUndead into a Spellstrike for a free attack even if you aren’t actually\n\tattacking an undead.<\/p>\n

            Spell Combat<\/span> (Ex): This is the reason the\n\tMagus uses a Rapier instead of using an Elven Curve Blade. Being able to\n\tcast a spell and use a weapon at the same time gives you a lot of potential\n\tfor damage output. You can also take a penalty to your attacks to get a\n\tbonus to Concentration, which is nice at low levels when your Concentration\n\tchecks haven’t begun to outpace your spell levels.<\/p>\n

            Spellstrike<\/span> (Su): This is why you want to\n\tuse a rapier. I can’t think of another way to get a 15-20 critical threat\n\trange on a spell, and the idea of doing 20d6 damage with an Intensified\n\tShocking Grasp is very enticing. You want to make sure that you’re going to\n\thit with this attack, so don’t let your attack bonus fall behind. If you’re\n\thaving too much trouble hitting, switch to using regular Spell Combat so\n\tthat you can use your spells as touch attacks.<\/p>\n

            Magus Arcana<\/span>: The Magus’ pseudo-feat.\n\tSimilar to Rogue Talents and any number of similar abilities, Magus Arcana\n\tare a great way to customize your Magus, and many options are better than\n\tmany feats. For help selecting your Magus Arcana, see my\n\tMagus Arcana Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n

            Spell Recall<\/span> (Su): This will eat your\n\tArcane Pool very quickly, so don’t plan to use this on a regular basis.<\/p>\n

            Bonus Feats<\/span>: This really helps with our\n\tearly feat dependency, especially if you’re not a human. Combat Feats and\n\tMetamagic Feats make up most of your important feats, and 5th level is\n\tright\n\twhen you want to get Intensify Spell and use it on Shocking Grasp to keep\n\tthe damage relevant for another 5 levels.<\/p>\n

            Knowledge Pool<\/span> (Su): You can go out and\n\tbuy\n\tscrolls to learn knew spells. Unless you’re somewhere that you can’t do\n\tthat, this really isn’t useful. If you can’t find a scroll or spellbook\n\tthat\n\tcontains spells you want, you can use this to bypass the requirement for\n\tsource material.<\/p>\n

            Medium Armor<\/span> (Ex): Medium Armor is a\n\tsmall improvement to your AC. See the Armor section below for helping\n\tselecting your armor.<\/p>\n

            Improved Spell Combat<\/span> (Ex): By now your\n\tConcentration checks should be decent. Your highest DC is 18 (15+3), and\n\twith at least 16 intelligence you have a better than even chance. If you\n\thaven’t found other ways to improve your Concentration checks (Spellguard\n\tBraces are a fantastic choice), taking a -1 penalty to your attacks for a\n\t+3\n\tto your Concentration check is a pretty fantastic trade.<\/p>\n

            Fighter Training<\/span> (Ex): There aren’t a\n\tlot of Fighter feats that you really want at this point.<\/p>\n

            Improved Spell Recall<\/span> (Su): This hugely\n\timproves your versatility because you can call up all of those weird\n\tobscure\n\tspells that you learned thinking you might use them once or twice in your\n\tlife. You can also recharge an Intensified Shocking Grasp for a single\n\tArcane Pool point.<\/p>\n

            Heavy Armor<\/span> (Ex): Unless your Dexterity\n\tbonus is +10 or greater, Heavy Armor is your best option, and upgrading to\n\tMithral Tatami-Do will get you a nice +2 AC boost.<\/p>\n

            Greater Spell Combat<\/span> (Ex): The offer is\n\tcertainly tempting, but by now your Concentration check is 14+intelligence.\n\tAssuming a very modest 18 intelligence, you can roll a 1, score a 19, and\n\tcast a 4th level spell guranteed. At level 15 you are guranteed to succeed\n\twhen casting 5th level spells, and at level 16 you are guranteed to succeed\n\twith 6th level spells. This ability is completely unnecessary unless you\n\tare\n\tfighting someone with the Disruptive feat.<\/p>\n

            Counterstrike<\/span> (Ex): The free attack is\n\tnice, but not being able to disrupt the spell really handicaps this\n\tability.\n\tIf you don’t have Step Up, you probably will never get to use this.<\/p>\n

            Greater Spell Access<\/span> (Su): The Wizard has\n\ta much better spell list than the Magus, and access to a whole bunch of\n\tthose spells gives you a ton of great options.<\/p>\n

            True Magus<\/span> (Su): The ability to forego\n\tcasting defensively is a token gesture because at this point you could\n\tautomatically cast any of your spells defensively with an Intelligence\n\tscore\n\tof 10. The flexible bonus is nice, though.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

            The Magus has some problems with MAD, especially if you want a lot of Strength for damage, but it’s manageable if you understand that you really don’t need a ton of Strength.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

            Str<\/span>: Because the Magus starts with only\n\tlight armor proficiency, you need to invest in Dexterity. If you’re going\n\tto\n\tinvest in Dexterity, you should use weapon Finesse. If you’re using Weapon\n\tFinesse, you don’t need Strength. You might take a bit of strength for\n\tdamage and to open up Power Attack, but it’s not strictly necessary because\n\tyour damage primarily comes from your spells.<\/p>\n

            Dex<\/span>: Your primary combat stat. Because\n\tthe\n\tMagus starts with only light armor, Dexterity is crucial, and the Magus’\n\tonly bad save is Reflex.<\/p>\n

            Con<\/span>: As a front-line character, the\n\tMagus\n\tneeds all of the hit points it can get.<\/p>\n

            Int<\/span>: Your spellcasting ability, and it\n\tfuels your skill ranks and knowledge skills. While you don’t need it as\n\tmuch\n\tas a Wizard because you only get 6 levels of spells, it’s helpful for bonus\n\tspells, especially at low levels.<\/p>\n

            Wis<\/span>: Helpful for Will saves, but Will\n\tis\n\tone of the Magus’ good saves, so don’t invest too heavily..<\/p>\n

            Cha<\/span>: Useless for a Magus. It only affects\n\tyour Use Magic Device skill, and if you want to use UMD, take the Pragmatic\n\tActivator trait. Dump to 7.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
