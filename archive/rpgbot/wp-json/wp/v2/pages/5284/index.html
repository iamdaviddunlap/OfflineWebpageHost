Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Style feats are really cool. In fact, they’re one of my favorite ways to customize the Monk, both for flavor reasons and for mechanical reasons. Technically the feats are available to anyone willing to invest in Improved Unarmed Strike, but they are by far more appealing to the Monk than to other classes.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Enter the Master of Many Styles. They give up the Monk’s signature attack\n\toption (Flurry of Blows), but in exchange they get the ability to mix and\n\tmatch style feats in a way that literally no one else can do. This gives you a\n\tton of flexibility, but it also means that you need to look for ways to close\n\tthe gap in damage output between the Master of Many Styles and the vanilla\n\tMonk.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Monk Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Hit Points<\/span>: d8 hit points is hard for a\n\tstrictly melee class.<\/p>\n

    Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: The Monk’s primary\n\toffensive option, Flurry of Blows, pretends that the Monk has full BAB, which\n\tis usually enough in combat but can be frustrating when qualifying for\n\tfeats.<\/p>\n

    Saves<\/span>: All good saves.<\/p>\n

    Proficiencies<\/span>: The Monk is proficient with\n\tMonk weapons (which make up a collection of strange, exotic, and often foreign\n\tweapons) and a couple of other odds and ends like hand axes, crossbows, and\n\tspears. Strangely, the Monk weapons tend to be the best options since they can\n\tbenefit from Flurry of blows. Monks gain no armor or shield proficiencies, and\n\tgenerally rely on their Monk AC bonus instead.<\/p>\n

    Skills<\/span>: The Monk skill list has several\n\texcellent options, several of which are difficult for the monk to capitalize\n\ton. By splashing a few skills and focusing on the best ones, the Monk can make\n\texcellent use of their 4+ skill points.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    AC Bonus<\/span> (Ex): The Monk AC bonus helps to\n\toffset the monk’s lack of armor. The scaling is fairly negligible, but it\n\tstill feels good to have. However, your AC will still be awful without\n\tadditional items and AC improvements.<\/p>\n

    Unarmed Strike<\/span>: This is why you generally\n\tplay a monk: because you want to hit things with your bare hands\/feet\/etc. The\n\tdamage dice starts off fairly small, but when you hit d8 at level 4, it begins\n\tto outpace whatever weapons you might be two-weapon fighting with. At level\n\t12, your fists are greatswords. You can also deal nonlethal damage without\n\tpenalty, which is situationally useful. It’s important to note that your\n\tunarmed strike damage is heavily size-based, so you want to do everything you\n\tcan to increase your effective size. Get enlarged and throw Impact on an\n\tAmulet of Mighty Fists.
