Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Kineticists have a couple of interesting options, but nothing that’s especially exciting. None of the archetypes do a good job at fixing problems with the class, and they generally fail to execute the concepts that they’re shooting for in any meaningful way. You’re probably better off playing a vanilla Kineticist.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Archetypes<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Blood Kineticist<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    Remember that one thing in “Avatar: The Last Airbender” where water benders could bend peoples’ blood and do crazy stuff like that? Well, here you go. Blood Kineticist is a mixed bag; some of the options are amazing, and some of them are bad. Overall, it balances out to a fair trade for what you give up.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Blood Focus<\/span> (Ex): Locking you into water is\n\t\tfine. Limiting the archetype’s Infusions to things with blood is really\n\t\tannoying since so many enemies are constructs, plants, undead, or things\n\t\twhich arguably don’t have blood (Abberations? Vermin?).<\/p>\n

    Wrack<\/span> (Su): You won’t always need this,\n\t\tbut untyped damage is extremely rare and very powerful. Even if you run into\n\t\tsomething totally immune to your blast, this will hurt it.<\/p>\n

    Bleeding Infusion<\/span> (Su): Bleed damage\n\t\tusually isn’t great, but it also usually doesn’t do this much damage.\n\t\tCombine this with Wrack to get around resistances, and you can wait for an\n\t\tenemy to bleed to death instead of wasting all of your Burn to kill it with\n\t\tWrack.<\/p>\n

    Blood Tell<\/span> (Sp): Blood Biography is very\n\t\tsituational.<\/p>\n

    Blood Blast<\/span> (Su): Blood Blast is terrible\n\t\ton its own because it’s identical to water blast, but with more damage. The\n\t\tonly draw is that you get to use the Infusions from the archetype. Still,\n\t\tthat shouldn’t prevent you from considering other elements.<\/p>\n

    Blood Throw<\/span> (Su): Foe throw is good, and\n\t\tit’s typically only available to aether users.<\/p>\n

    Gut-Wrenching Infusion<\/span> (Su): At 3 points\n\t\tof Burn this is way too expensive for the effect. Sickened is an excellent\n\t\tdebuff, but 3 points of Burn is just too high.<\/p>\n

    Vampiric Infusion<\/span> (Su): Healing in combat\n\t\twithout cutting into your action economy is really nice. The Burn cost is\n\t\treally high, so be sure to use Gather Power to reduce it. You get\n\t\tSupercharge at the same level, so between Gather power and Infusion\n\t\tspecialization, you can easily reduce the Burn cost to 0. You still need to\n\t\taccept 1 Burn to heal, unfortunately.<\/p>\n

    Blood Mastery<\/span> (Su): Living forever is\n\t\treally great in real life, but rarely matters in a campaign. The free age\n\t\tbonuses are also nice, but since Kineticists have so little dependence on\n\t\tmental ability scores you don’t have a good way to abuse them.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Replaced Features: Infusions (1st, 5th, 9th, 11th), Utility Wild Talents (6th, 8th), Expanded Element (Altered), Omnikinesis.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Elemental Annihilator<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    This archetype is garbage. There are clear design holes which make it functionally unplayable. The concept is fine: give up utility options for even more damage output. The execution means that you actually deal less<\/em> damage than a vanilla Kineticist, in addition to giving up all of your non-combat options. The whole archetype is build around Devastating Infusion and Flurry of Devastation. Devastating Infusion cripples your damage output, and expects that your damage output will scale based on Flurry of Devastation, but without full BAB Flurry of Devastation is really weak. Imagine a fighter, but with half as meany feats, bad proficiencies, and only 2\/3 BAB. That is the amount of damage output you can expect from this archetype.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    • “…and can be used with any physical blast (but not energy blasts).”: If\n\t\t\tyou want to use this Infusion, you’ll need to pick an element with a\n\t\t\tphysical basic blast. That means no fire.<\/li>\n
    • “Unlike with kinetic blade, the elemental annihilator can use Vital\n\t\t\tStrike with devastating infusion.”: This means that Vital Strike is the\n\t\t\tonly way to scale this Infusion’s damage. Even if you did that, you would\n\t\t\tstill max out at 4d8+Con damage, which is pitiful and a massive waste of\n\t\t\tfeats.                 Elemental Ascetic<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                The Elemental Ascetic merges the Kineticist’s elemental powers with the martial style of the Monk. The flavor is really cool, and some built-in melee mechanics make melee Kineticists much simpler. However, like the Monk, the Elemental Ascetic is very MAD. You need four good ability scores to function, which is hard to manage. Playing this archetype will be mechanically difficult, and you’ll need to invest heavily in ability score enhancement items and items to compensate for your relatively poor AC due to lack of armor.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Elemental Flurry<\/span> (Su): This is not the\n\t\tMonk’s Unarmed Strike. You don’t get the damage progression. Instead, you\n\t\trely on Kinetic Fist for damage, which is unfortunate because Kinetic Fist\n\t\tapplies 1\/3 of the damage dice you get from a normal Blast. That means that\n\t\tyou’ll be dealing 1d6 energy damage until 7th level. Fortunately, you get\n\t\tFlurry of Blows as a Monk of your level, so you’ll get to make multiple\n\t\tattacks, potentially allowing you to exceed the damage you would deal with a\n\t\tsingle Blast. Unfortunately, you also give up access to every form infusion\n\t\twhich isn’t an AOE, so your ability to use blasts at range is massively\n\t\treduced.<\/p>\n

                Elemental Wisdom<\/span> (Su): Switching your\n\t\tprimary ability score to Wisdom is a serious handicap. Focusing on\n\t\tConstitution increases the size of your Burn pool, not to mention addressing\n\t\tyour problematic hit points. A normal Kineticist needs a bit of Wisdom to\n\t\tcompensate for their poor Will saves, but they don’t need to emphasize it\n\t\tsignificantly. If nothing else, the emphasis on Wisdom means that your Will\n\t\tsaves will be decent.<\/p>\n

                AC Bonus<\/span> (Ex): The Monk’s AC bonus is\n\t\tpretty great.<\/p>\n

                Powerful Fist<\/span> (Su): One damage die size\n\t\tincrease means 1 more damage per die on average. Since this costs Burn,\n\t\tyou’ll generally use this as an occasionaly damage boost. Infusion\n\t\tSpecialization will reduce the cost, but it doesn’t stay in sync with the\n\t\tcost of the ability, and using the damage boost reduces your ability to use\n\t\tSubstance Infusions, so using the damage boost carries an opportunity\n\t\tcost.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Replaced Features: Kinetic Blast (Altered), Elemental Overflow, key ability scores of wild talents, Elemental Defense, Infusions (5th, 9th, 13th)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Kinetic Chirurgeon<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                The Kinetic Chirurgeon gives up all of the flexibility of Infusions in exchange for the ability to be a mediocre healbot. If you want to play a healbot, play a Cleric. Also, don’t play a healbot because they’re boring and not very useful. Just buy a wand of cure light wounds.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Kinetic Chirurgery<\/span> (Su): Mercies are\n\t\tpretty great, and they allow you to solve problems well beyond simple\n\t\tdamage.<\/p>\n

                Metahealer<\/span> (Su): Access to Breath of Life\n\t\tis amazing, even if your target needs to accept Burn to get it. A bit of\n\t\tBurn is an easy trade for your life.<\/p>\n

                Healing Buffer<\/span> (Su): Free healing is\n\t\tnice, but you give up a ton of versatility to get it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Replaced Features: Infusions, Metakinesis, Infusion Specialization, Internal Buffer (Altered)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Compatible Archetypes:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Overwhelming Soul<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                The Overwhelming Soul is a surprisingly large change to the class, considering it only has three abilities. The end result is that the class is easier to play, involves less resource management, and doesn’t need to balance the risks of Burn. If you’re new to Pathfinder and want to play a Kineticist, this archetype is a great option.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Mind Over Matter<\/span> (Ex): Switching your\n\t\tprimary ability is a weird trade. You’re turning a dump stat into your\n\t\tprimary stat, which makes a very SAD class more MAD. However, I think the\n\t\tKineticist can handle it, especially since this makes Elves and Halflings\n\t\tmuch more appealing. The additional class skills also make the Kineticist\n\t\tinto an effective Face, giving you something to do with your 4+ skill\n\t\tranks.<\/p>\n

                Mental Prowess<\/span> (Su): Giving up the\n\t\tability to accept Burn is a potentially huge handicap, but it also makes the\n\t\tKineticist much easier to play.<\/p>\n

                Overwhelming Power<\/span> (Ex): Elemental\n\t\tOverflow with a smaller bonus, you don’t need to accept Burn to get it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                Replaced Features: Class skills (Altered), the key ability score of wild talents, Burn (Altered), Internal Buffer.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
