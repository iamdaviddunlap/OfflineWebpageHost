    Rules<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Skill unlocks come into play every 5 skill ranks (5, 10, 15, 20), and provide\n\tgradually more powerful abilities beyond a linear improvement of your\n\tcharacter’s ability to use a skill successfully.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Who can use skill unlocks?<\/h3>\n\n\n

    Your group might choose to allow all characters to use skill unlocks for all\n\tof their skills, which disproportionately rewards highly skilled characters.\n\tIn groups which lean toward casters and other low-skill classes, this might be\n\tacceptable and might help balance out class usage.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    You might instead require that characters take the\n\tSignature Skill<\/a>\n\tfeat, which allows characters to use skill unlocks for a single skill. You\n\tmight also use the Unchained Rogue, which gives Rogues easy access to skill\n\tunlocks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Personally, I recommend allowing both the Signature Skill feat and the\n\tUnchained Rogue. There is a huge variance in the quality of skill unlocks, and\n\tgranting them for free to everyone disproportionately rewards a subset of\n\tcharacters who frequently take the skills with the best unlocks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

            Craft<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

            Craft is for NPCs and poison enthusiasts. There are options to make magic item crafting available to non-casters using Craft, but they’re all terrible compared to regular magic item crafting. Poison crafters might be able to make use of Craft’s skill unlocks, especially since Rogues make up a disproportionate section of the poison-using population. Poison is very expensive, which means that it takes a long time to craft, and combining the 5-rank and 15-rank unlocks effectively multiplies your craft speed by 14. Combined with the existing rule which allows you to willingly increase your craft DC by 10, you can churn out poisons at a dizzying rate. Unfortunately, none of the unlocks do anything to address the problematic cost of poisons.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              \n\t
            • 5 Ranks<\/span>: Functionally doubles your craft\n\t\tspeed.<\/li>\n\t
            • 10 Ranks<\/span>: By this level it should be nearly\n\t\timpossible to fail a craft check.<\/li>\n\t
            • 15 Ranks<\/span>: Multiply your crafting speed by\n\t\t7.<\/li>\n\t
            • 20 Ranks<\/span>: Blacksmithing, the most\n\t\tversatile craft skill in the game, now allows you to partially replace both\n\t\tCraft Magic Arms and Armor, Forge Ring, and big chunks of Craft Wondrous\n\t\tItem. Combined with the 5-rank and 15-rank abilities you can churn out magic\n\t\titems at a fair pace, possibly even faster than the normal 1000gp per day\n\t\tcraft speed typical of magic item crafting.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                      Fly<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                      Despite the importance of flight at high levels, Fly is a nearly worthless skill.                       • 20 Ranks<\/span>: Two sizes larger will make a\n\t\tmedium creature nearly immune to wind effects, and flying straight up at\n\t\tfull speed is a great way to get a tactical advantage over other flying\n\t\tenemies.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n

                          Heal<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                          In 3.5 Heal was a joke skill on par with Use Rope. You took it to qualify for shitty feats and prestige classes. In Pathfinder the “Treat Deadly Wounds” option makes Heal a fantastic and cost-effective way to boost your party’s healing abilities. Heal’s skill unlocks further improve the Treat Deadly Wounds to provide additional healing. The max DC you need to hit is 25, which is fairly easy with a few ranks, decent Wisdom, and items. Beyond that, skill unlocks are the only reason to continue investing ranks. Each unlock provides a better version of the same effect, so I believe the intent is that they do not stack, though the rules don’t say so specifically.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                            \n\t
                          • 5 Ranks<\/span>: 2 hp per hit die, and 2 ability\n\t\tdamage off of each damaged ability. At this level that’s a huge chunk of\n\t\thealing, and will save precious spell slots and\/or costly wand charges.<\/li>\n\t
                          • 10 Ranks<\/span>: 4 hp per hit die, and 4 ability\n\t\tdamage off of each damaged ability.<\/li>\n\t
                          • 15 Ranks<\/span>: 6 hp per hit die, and 6 ability\n\t\tdamage off of each damage hit die.<\/li>\n\t
                          • 20 Ranks<\/span>: 9 hp per hit die, and 9 ability\n\t\tdamage off of each damage hit die. That will completely heal most\n\t\tcreatures.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n\n
