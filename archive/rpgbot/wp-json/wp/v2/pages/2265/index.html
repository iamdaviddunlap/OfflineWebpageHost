The Swashbuckler is a great concept, but falls short in implementation. The\nSwashbuckler attempts to be a charismatic Fighter with a sense of Panache, but\ncomes out as a lightly armored Fighter with less feats. Swashbuckler can be a\ngreat class dip for weapon finesse builds, but single-class Swashbucklers are\ndismally ineffective. With some clever build choices, the Swashbuckler can be a\nDefender, Face, and Striker.<\/p>\n\n\n

          Swashbuckler Class Features<\/h3>\n\n\nHit Points<\/span>: d10 hit points is standard for a\nmelee class.\n\nBase Attack Bonus<\/span>: Full BAB.\n\nSaves<\/span>: Fortitude is the Swashbuckler’s only\ngood save, but Grace and a dependence on Dexterity will help with the\nSwashbuckler’s Reflex saves.\n\nProficiencies<\/span>: Martial weapons and light\narmor, but no shields. None of the Swashbuckler’s class features require that\nyou don’t use a shield, so you can use a masterwork buckler or a bigger shield\nif you pick up shield proficiency.\n\nSkills<\/span>: 4+ skill ranks is better than a\nFighter, and Swashbucklers get every social skill except Intimidate, which\nallows you to be a Face as long as you’re not trying to intimidate people.\n\nWeapon Finesse<\/span> (Ex): A good reason for finesse\nfighters to take a single level dip into Swashbuckler. Largely defines the play\nstyle of the class.\n\nGrace<\/span> (Ex): The bonus is puny, but it helps\nmake the Swashbuckler’s Reflex saves decent.\n\nInsightful Strike<\/span> (Ex): This is a cool idea,\nbut the actual damage bonus will generally be pretty small, especially compared\nto things like Power Attack.\n\nDodge Bonus<\/span> (Ex): This would be great if it\nwere a flat Dodge bonus to AC, but instead it only applies to melee attacks from\none enemy at a time.\n\nAcrobatic Charge<\/span> (Ex): Very situational, and\nyou can jump over difficult terrain as part of a charge.\n\nImproved Flanking<\/span> (Ex): Fairly useless unless\nyou have Daring Outlaw and can capitalize on Sneak Attack.\n\nLucky<\/span> (Ex): Rerolls are great at any level,\nbut a Cleric can do this at first level. Save this for save or suck effects.\n\nAcrobatic Skill Mastery<\/span> (Ex): Nice if you\ntake Leap Attack, but generally not useful.\n\nWeakening Critical<\/span> (Ex): Strength damage is\nnice at any level, especially against weaker creatures. Combine this with ambush\nfeats and you can put out quite a bit of ability damage.\n\nSlippery Mind<\/span> (Ex): Rerolls are great, but this\nonly affects on school of magic, making it very situational.\n\nWounding Critical<\/span> (Ex): Constitution damage is\nalways nice, but there are much better ways to do it, including ambush feats.\n

            Abilities<\/h3>\n\n\n

            The Swashbuckler’s abilities look like a finesse Fighter. Dexterity is crucial, but everything else is largely secondary.<\/p>\n\n\n\nStr<\/span>: Take 13 for Power Attack, but you don’t\nneed any more. The Swashbuckler can’t afford a ton of Strength for damage, so we\nneed to get it from somewhere else. Take 15 if you plan to be permanently\nreduced.\n\nDex<\/span>: The Swashbuckler’s primary ability.\n\nCon<\/span>: Hit points are crucial for hit points.\n\nInt<\/span>: 4 skill ranks is plenty to get all of\nthe Swashbuckler’s good skills, but Precise Strike allows you to apply\nIntelligence to Damage. If you have extra skill ranks, pick up Spot and Search\nas cross-class skills. You will likely want 13 so that you qualify for Combat\nExpertise.\n\nWis<\/span>: A bit of Wisdom for saves is nice, but\nyou don’t need much.\n\nCha<\/span>: Despite the theme of the class, the\nSwashbuckler doesn’t strictly need<\/em> Charisma. Take a bit if you plan to\nplay a Face, but otherwise you can dump it.\n\n\n

            Races<\/h3>\n\n\n

            Dexterity bonuses are crucial. If you’re going for a Defender build, you may want to be medium so that you can be Enlarged, but the Dexterity penalty makes that unappealing. Being Medium is still nice if you want to be Reduced, but it’s hardly necessary.<\/p>\n\n\n\nDwarf<\/span>: Doesn’t really fit the theme of the\nclass, and doesn’t really offer anything specifically useful.\n\nElf<\/span>: The Dexterity bonus works, and the Elf’s\nother class features can be nice, but be careful with the Constitution penalty.\n\nGnome<\/span>: Nothing useful for the Swashbuckler.\n\nHalf-Elf<\/span>: Awful.\n\nHalf-Orc<\/span>: The only really interesting thing\noffered by the Half-Orc is Darkvision.\n\nHalfling<\/span>: The Dexterity bonus is great, and\nthe Strength penalty is easily overcome, but the Halfling’s affinity for Stealth\nis wasted on the Swashbuckler.\n\nHuman<\/span>: Bonus feat and bonus skill rank.\n\n\n
