Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

The Mobile Fighter is a fantastic archetype. It fixes the Fighter’s issues with mobility, and makes the Fighter one of the most mobile characters in the game. Even with this improvement, the Mobile Fighter doesn’t sacrifice the Fighter’s excellent build versatility, and in many ways enhances the Fighter’s already formidable capabilities as a Defender and Striker.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Taking inspiration from the Mobile Fighter’s abilities, we can divine Mobile Fighter builds into three options: TWF builds (lots of movement and lots of attacks), Two-Handed builds (a little movement, few attacks with high damage, and whirlwind attack), and the Switch Hitter (Double weapons to fight like TWF, but Whirlwind and make Attacks of Opportunity Two-Handed).<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Fighter Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Hit Points<\/span>: d10 hit points like nearly\n\tevery other front-line class.<\/p>\n

    Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: Full BAB.<\/p>\n

    Saves<\/span>: Good fortitude saves, but many\n\tfighters have major problems with Reflex and Will saves.<\/p>\n

    Proficiencies<\/span>: All armor and shields,\n\tinclude Tower shields, and martial weapons. The fighter has a huge variety of\n\toptions, and with plenty of bonus feats they can supplement these options with\n\texotic weapon proficiency.<\/p>\n

    Skills<\/span>: Likely the worst skill list in the\n\tgame, and Fighters often dump intelligence to 7. 2+ skill ranks is almost\n\tnothing, but many fighters still can’t find a good way to use all of their\n\tskill ranks.<\/p>\n

    Bonus Feats<\/span>: Bonus feats are why most people\n\tplay a Fighter (or at least take a class dip). With a feat at level one and\n\tanother at every even-numbered class level, the Fighter gets a total of 22\n\tfeats by level 20 (23 for humans), giving you a dizzying number of options. On\n\ttop of this, the fighter gets a free retraining option every 4 levels which\n\tallows you to pick up feats with high prerequisites by trading in low-level\n\tfeats which aren’t paying off anymore. Do silly things like trade in Toughness\n\tfor Improved Critical at level 8.<\/p>\n

    Armor Training<\/span> (Ex): This greatly reduces\n\tthe need for Mithral armor, and makes Dexterity much more important for\n\tFighters than it is for other heavily armored characters. Unfortunately you\n\tonly get the first two steps, but the ability to move at full speed in armor\n\tworks very well for the mobile fighter.<\/p>\n

    Armor Mastery<\/span> (Ex): A bit late in the game,\n\tbut DR\/- is always welcome.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

    Mobile Fighter Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Agility<\/span> (Ex): Situational. About as useful\n\tas Bravery.<\/p>\n

    Leaping Attack<\/span> (Ex): Use your 5 foot step,\n\tand get weapon training with any weapon. This is great if you like to use\n\tdifferent weapons to fit the situation, or if you’re avoiding weapon\n\tfocus\/specialization.<\/p>\n

    Rapid Attack<\/span> (Ex): Like a beautiful\n\tcombination of pounce and spring attack. You give up one attack at your\n\thighest attack bonus, but you could run around with two weapons and attack\n\teveryone in the room if you felt the need to do so.<\/p>\n

    Fleet Footed<\/span> (Ex): Very suddenly you’re fast\n\tand good at acrobatics. Acrobatics isn’t a class skill, which is annoying, but\n\tyou can fix that with a trait.<\/p>\n

    Whirlwind Blitz<\/span> (Ex): Almost as good as\n\tpounce if you just want full attacks, and it makes Whirlwind Attack\n\tconsiderably better.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

    The Mobile Fighter’s abilities strongly resemble those of other Fighters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Str<\/span>: Strength is key for Two-Handed Mobile\n\tFighters, and even TWF Mobile Fighters should consider a bit of Strength to\n\tget Power Attack. Most TWF characters don’t rely on Power Attack for damage\n\tbecause they can’t spare the attack penalty, but Fighters get enough bonuses\n\tto hit that Power Attack is viable.<\/p>\n

    Dex<\/span>: Dexterity is great for the Mobile\n\tFighter. Two-Handed Mobile Fighters should start with 12 to max out their Full\n\tPlate, but TWF mobile fighters will want considerably more to qualify for\n\tfeats.\n<\/p>\n

    Con<\/span>: Hit points are crucial for Fighters\n\tbecause they tend to be the party’s front line.<\/p>\n

    Int<\/span>: The Fighter’s skills are garbage, and\n\tyou can’t get less than one skill rank.<\/p>\n

    Wis<\/span>: Only needed for Will saves, but don’t\n\tdump it.<\/p>\n

    Cha<\/span>: Worthless unless you plan to use\n\tIntimidate.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
