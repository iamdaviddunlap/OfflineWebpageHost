Way of Mercy Monk Handbook Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Coming to the game from Tasha\u2019s as something of an unarmored DPS paladin, Way of Mercy is one of only two blue-rated Monk subclasses on the site for its incredible utility and versatility. It has some tools to improve the Monk\u2019s core competencies as a Striker, but also gives it some battlefield control and, iconically, healing and Support capabilities. Way of Mercy does still suffer from the MADness that goes with most Monk subclasses, but what you get for being willing to invest in those abilities is pretty great.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The flavor of Way of Mercy is definitely strange. They \u201c…[present] themselves as the faceless bringers of life and death,\u201d which feels really out of place with what a monastic tradition is supposed to be. This I can quote more broadly because it\u2019s from the basic rules, and I really want to because it\u2019s a hilarious moment showing how WOTC really shot themselves in the foot:<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
\n

\u201cThree traditions of monastic pursuit are common in the monasteries scattered across the multiverse. Most monasteries practice one tradition exclusively, but a few honor the three traditions and instruct each monk according to his or her aptitude and interest. All three traditions rely on the same basic techniques, diverging as the student grows more adept. Thus, a monk need choose a tradition only upon reaching 3rd level.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n\n\n\n\n

<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

So, clearly they didn\u2019t futureproof this section. Regardless, it just feels very strange that there would be a little subsection of a typical monastery devoted to wearing a different flavor of odd clothing and channeling the forces of life and death rather than something more \u201cnormal\u201d like elements.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        Way of Mercy Features<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
        <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
          \n\t
        1. Implements of Mercy<\/span>: Insight and Medicine\n\t\tare both Wisdom-based, which makes them good skills for the Monk. Unfortunately, Medicine is borderline useless (although check out some of our \nsuggestions<\/a>\nto fix that), as its sole written function is made\n\t\tobsolete by the Healer’s Kit. Herbalism Kit does allow you to craft Potions\n\t\tof Healing, though, which is nice.\n\t\t

          You also get a neat mask as a class feature. It doesn’t do anything. You\n\t\t\tdon’t need it to do anything and technically you don’t even need to wear\n\t\t\tit.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

        2. Hand of Healing<\/span>: Your Martial Arts die\n\t\tplus your Wisdom modifier is roughly equivalent to a 1st-level Cure Wounds.\n\t\tThat’s not a ton of healing all at once, but you get a ton of Ki Points per\n\t\tshort rest as you gain levels, and the action economy around this is\n\t\tfantastic. This is also a great way to expend your remaining Ki Points\n\t\twhenever you reach a Short Rest.\n\t\t

          Cure Wounds is normally a hard choice in combat because you’re spending\n\t\t\tyour Action to not damage your enemies when Healing Word is available, so\n\t\t\tthe Action Economy makes it hard to justify. But, while you\n\t\t\tcan<\/em> use Hand of Healing as an Action, there’s almost no reason to\n\t\t\tdo so in combat.<\/p>\n\t\t

          Generally you’ll use Hand of Healing during combat as part of a Flurry of\n\t\t\tBlows. When you do this, you spend the Ki Point to use Flurry of Blows,\n\t\t\tthen you can decide to replace one of the unarmed strikes with Hand of\n\t\t\tHealing. As far as I can tell, you can make this decision at any point so\n\t\t\tyou could make one attack and see the results before deciding if you want\n\t\t\tto make another attack or heal something.<\/p>\n\t\t

          Since you can move between attacks, I believe that you can also move\n\t\t\tbetween attacking and using Hand of Healing, so you could Attack, use\n\t\t\tFlurry of Blows to heal an ally, then run over to an enemy and attack\n\t\t\tthem some more.<\/p>\n\t\t

          In many ways, this is like Healing Word, and until your Ki Pool is fairly\n\t\t\tlarge, that’s how you should consider Hand of Healing: a tiny amount of\n\t\t\thealing, but enough to bring a dying ally back into a fight. As you gain\n\t\t\tmore levels you can afford to use this more often, and Physician’s Touch\n\t\t\tallows you to quickly remove some problematic status conditions.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

        3. Hand of Harm<\/span>: One Ki point to\n\t\tautomatically deal as much damage as an unarmed strike is more efficient\n\t\tthan spending a Ki Point to perform a Flurry of Blows which has a chance to\n\t\tmiss. This is also necrotic damage, which is much less-commonly resisted\n\t\tthan bludgeoning damage (or whatever type your weapon deals).\n\t\t

          Since the damage is “extra damage” added to the attack, the extra damage\n\t\t\tdie is multiplied, much like Divine Smite or Sneak Attack, so the most\n\t\t\teffective time to use Hand of Harm is when you score a critical hit.<\/p>\n\t\t

          However, this only works after you hit with an unarmed strike so if\n\t\t\tyou’re using a weapon (spears are my go-to, and I recommend them for\n\t\t\tnearly every monk) there’s an incentive to fight unarmed even if your\n\t\t\tMartial Arts die would deal less damage than a weapon. Of course, Flurry\n\t\t\tof Blows and Martial Arts’ Bonus Action attack only allow unarmed strikes,\n\t\t\tso there’s always going to be some portion of your attacks where you can\n\t\t\tapply this.<\/p>\n\t\t

          Physician’s Touch later improves Hand of Harm to add the Poisoned\n\t\t\tcondition, making it an amazing way to briefly debuff martial enemies\n\t\t\twithout a save.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

        4. Physician’s Touch<\/span>: Hand of Healing adds\n\t\tthe effect of Lesser Restoration and can also remove the Stunned condition.\n\t\tIf this was an either\/or it would be fantastic, but you get to both remove\n\t\tconditions and<\/em> heal the target.\n\t\t

          Hand of Harm adds the ability to make the target Poisoned with no save.\n\t\t\tPoisoned is an absolutely fantastic debuff against most enemies (though\n\t\t\tspellcasters who don’t rely on attack rolls can largely ignore it). If you\n\t\t\thit an enemy with an unarmed strike, you can activate Hand of Harm to deal\n\t\t\tbonus damage and make the target poisoned, then, while they’re at\n\t\t\tDisadvantage on Ability Checks due to being Poisoned, you can Shove them\n\t\t\tprone and\/or grapple them, then make any remaining attacks at Advantage.\n\t\t\tIf they’re Grappled, they’ll have a hard time escaping (Disadvantage\n\t\t\tthanks to Poisoned), so you’re likely to keep them on the ground until\n\t\t\tyour next turn when you can drop a full Flurry of Blows on them at\n\t\t\tAdvantage and if they don’t die you can always hit them with Hand of Harm\n\t\t\tagain to keep them Poisoned.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

        5. Flurry of Healing and Harm<\/span>: This\n\t\tdramatically reduces the rate at which you spend Ki to activate Hand of Harm\n\t\tand Hand of Healing. You still need to spend 1 Ki Point to use Flurry of\n\t\tBlows, but after that you can use Hand of Harm\/Healing for free. You could\n\t\theal twice in one turn, heal once and attack (and hopefully use Hand of\n\t\tHarm), or attack once to see what happens, then decide to either heal or\n\t\tattempt another attack.\n\t\t

          The wording around how Hand of Healing works here is somewhat clumsy. It\n\t\t\tspecifies that you can use Hand of Healing with Flurry of Blows and\n\t\t\treplace both attacks without spending Ki. You could already replace one,\n\t\t\tbut the text is trying to say that you can now replace both attacks, but\n\t\t\tyou still don’t need to spend additional Ki to do so.<\/p>\n\t\t

          Using Hand of Healing twice during a Flurry of Blows doubles your ability\n\t\t\tto output healing. Weirdly, this also means that rather than spending an\n\t\t\tAction to use Hand of Healing outside of combat it’s more efficient to\n\t\t\tpunch the air or attack the darkness, then follow with a Flurry of Blows\n\t\t\tand punch yourself and your party until everyone feels better.         6. Hand of Ultimate Mercy<\/span>: Have a dead party\n\t\tmember? Take a Short Rest to get your Ki Points back if you need to, then\n\t\tpunch them back to life.
