Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

Where the Fighter’s roles are purely combative, the Cavalier is much more tactical and intellectual martial class. With an interesting mix of options, the Cavalier can succeed as a Defender, Face, or Striker, and includes a few Support abilities attached to the Cavalier’s banner.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        Cavalier Class Features<\/h2>\n\n\n

        Hit Points<\/span>: d10 hit points like nearly\n\tevery other front-line class.<\/p>\n

        Base Attack Bonus<\/span>: Full BAB.<\/p>\n

        Saves<\/span>: With heavy armor and no need for\n\tWisdom, the Cavalier really needs help with Reflex and Will saves.<\/p>\n

        Proficiencies<\/span>: .<\/p>\n

        Skills<\/span>: 4+ skill ranks and all of the\n\tskills to be the party’s Face, but almost no other skills. Remember that your\n\tchoice of Order will add two additional class skills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

        Challenge<\/span> (Ex): Similar to the Paladin’s\n\tSmite Evil, but the damage doesn’t double against major foes, and you lose AC\n\tinstead of getting a bonus to AC against the target. Note that your choice of\n\tOrder adds additional effects to your Challenge.<\/p>\n

        Mount<\/span> (Ex): The Cavalier’s combat tactics\n\tare, as you might expect based on the name, centered around mounted combat,\n\tand the Cavalier’s Mount is an essential part of those tactics. For help with\n\tthe Cavalier’s Mount, see my\n\tPractical Guide to Animal Companions<\/a>.<\/p>\n

        Order<\/span> (Ex): Cavalier Orders define the\n\tCavalier as much as a Wizard’s school defines the Wizard. Orders provide a\n\tcollection of abilities and two additional class skills. For help selecting a\n\tCavalier Order, see my\n\tCavalier Order Breakdown<\/a>.<\/p>\n

        Tactician<\/span> (Ex): Teamwork Feats can be very\n\tgood, but at first level you won’t qualify for anything particularly\n\tinteresting. For help with Teamwork Feats, see my\n\tPractical Guide to Teamwork Feats<\/a>.<\/p>\n

        Cavalier’s Charge<\/span> (Ex): Mounted charges are\n\tthe Cavalier’s first and best option in combat, and this makes them even\n\tbetter.<\/p>\n

        Expert Trainer<\/span> (Ex): The bonus to Handle\n\tAnimal is nice if your GM knows (and follows as written) the mounted combat\n\tand animal rules. Animals usually freak out and run away when wounded, and you\n\tneed to make Handle Animal checks to keep them in combat. This bonus to Handle\n\tAnimal can help make your mount more reliable. The ability to train stuff\n\tfaster is very situational.<\/p>\n

        Banner<\/span> (Ex): A fairly small and situational\n\tbonus, but charging is big part of the cavalier, and\n\tYou count as your own ally<\/a>, so you get the bonus to charges too.<\/p>\n

        Bonus Feat<\/span>: You only get 3, but bonus feats\n\tare always welcome, especially for feat-heavy builds like mounted combat.<\/p>\n

        Greater Tactician<\/span> (Ex): By this level you\n\tcan qualify for a lot of interesting Teamwork Feats, and granting them as a\n\tSwift Action means that you can do it at the beginning of every combat.<\/p>\n

        Mighty Charge<\/span> (Ex): Doubling the threat\n\trange on your lance won’t help you much, but a free combat maneuver on a\n\tsuccessful charge can be very helpful. Even if you haven’t put any effort into\n\tit, a Trip attempt will give your mount a nice +4 bonus on your mount’s\n\tattacks (if any), and will make it easier to move away unchallenged. If you\n\tenemy is prone, they will also have more trouble moving out of the way of your\n\tnext charge.<\/p>\n

        Demanding Challenge<\/span> (Ex): If you have other\n\tweapon-based characters in the party, this is a nice way to bring down major\n\tenemies with big AC’s.<\/p>\n

        Greater Banner<\/span> (Ex): The bonuses against\n\tcharm\/compulsion covers nearly every Enchantment spell and effect. The ability\n\tto wave your banner and provide rerolls on saves is huge. You can allow\n\trerolls against domination, poison, disease, or any number of other horrifying\n\teffects. This is especially useful against effects which only allow one save\n\tat the start of the effect.<\/p>\n

        Master Tactician<\/span> (Ex): Another improvement\n\ton tactician, this time you can grant any two Teamwork Feats which you know,\n\tnot just those granted as bonus feats by Tactician.<\/p>\n

        Supreme Charge<\/span> (Ex): Remember that\n\tmultipliers always stack additively, so if you have Spirited Charge and a\n\tlance you now deal x4 normal damage. 4x normal damage will match (or likely\n\texceed) the damage of a fighter making a full attack with a two-handed weapon,\n\tand is considerably more reliably because you don’t depend on iterative\n\tattacks made at -5\/-10\/-15. The ability to stun the target on a crit is nice,\n\tbut with a 19-20 threat range on your lance it’s hardly reliable.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

        Abilities<\/h2>\n\n\n

        The Cavalier’s abilities fall somewhere between the Fighter and the Paladin. Melee combat defines the Cavalier, but Charisma helps the Cavalier function as a Face and adds to many Cavalier Order abilities.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Str<\/span>: As a primarily melee character,\n\tStrength is your primary ability. You can use a lance one-handed while\n\tmounted, so the difference between 16 and 18 strength isn’t big enough that\n\tyou need to bend over backwards to hit 18 at level 1.<\/p>\n

        Dex<\/span>: Dexterity adds to Ride and the\n\tCavalier’s bad Reflex saves, but because the Cavalier is likely in full plate\n\tyou likely won’t have high Dexterity.<\/p>\n

        Con<\/span>: As a front-line martial character, hit\n\tpoints are essential.<\/p>\n

        Int<\/span>: Only needed for skill ranks. Dump as low\n\tas you can while still maximizing your essential skills.<\/p>\n

        Wis<\/span>: Only needed for Will saves, but the\n\tCavalier is very vulnerable to Will save effects, and needs all the help it\n\tcan get.<\/p>\n

        Cha<\/span>: The Cavalier can serve as a Face, and\n\tmany Order abilities depend on Charisma. You don’t need nearly as much as a\n\tPaladin, but 14 Charisma will go a long way. If you don’t play a Face and\n\tavoid Orders which require Charisma, you can dump Charisma to 7.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
