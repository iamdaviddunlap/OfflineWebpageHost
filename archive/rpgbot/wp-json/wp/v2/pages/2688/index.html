                                Immunity to hit point\n\t\t\treduction removes that cost, so your Homunculus can perpetually have half\n\t\t\tof your hit dice at no cost to your hit point maximum, and so long as you\n\t\t\thave the gold, you can create a small army of magens to serve you where\n\t\t\tundead might not do the job (i.e. almost anywhere with living people). I\n\t\t\tdon’t think that this was an intentional rules interaction, so your DM\n\t\t\tmight not be thrilled about it. Please be careful not to abuse it too\n\t\t\theavily.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n\t

                              5. Command Undead<\/span>: This is a very useful\n\t\tability if you have enemies which command undead or which are undead\n\t\tthemselves, but the fact that intelligent undead (very common by this level)\n\t\tget Advantage on the save makes this ability hard to use effectively. Some\n\t\thigh-CR undead like the Nightwalker are tempting targets, but unless you’re\n\t\tactively seeking them out (and your DM is silly enough to let you find and\n\t\tenslave one) you can’t count on just stumbling upon a suitable target.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n\n
                                <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                                School of Transmutation<\/a> (PHB<\/a>)<\/sup><\/h3>\n\n\n\n
                                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                                Transmutation gets you very little which you couldn’t already get more easily from other places, often at a lower level. Transmuter’s Stone is arguably the best part of the subclass, but you can replace it with the Resilient feat or by taking your first level in the Artificer, Fighter, or Sorcerer classes to get proficiency in Constitution saves, then learn some basic spells that any sensible wizard should know anyway. Master Transmuter is the next high point of the class, but waiting until level 14 to get a good subclass feature is not a good choice. Even then, the best benefits are made totally obsolete by Wish three levels later.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                School of Transmutation Wizard Handbook<\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                                <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
                                  \n\t
                                1. Transmutation Savant<\/span>: Standard for PHB\n\t\twizard subclasses. Strangely, this feature incentivizes you to use your two\n\t\tfree spells learned per level to learn literally anything except spells from\n\t\tyour favorite school.<\/li>\n\t
                                2. Minor Alchemy<\/span>: It’s hard to say how best to\n\t\tuse this, but at the very least you could turn a block of wood into silver\n\t\tand sell it before it reverts and you’re run out of town.<\/li>\n\t
                                3. Transmuter’s Stone<\/span>: Several excellent\n\t\tbuffs to choose from, and you can change the selected buff easily. If you\n\t\tenjoy Concentration spells like Polymorph, proficiency in Constitution saving throws is\n\t\thuge. You might also share that benefit with a cleric if there’s one in the\n\t\tparty since clerics have tons of good Concentration spells and tend to be on\n\t\tthe front lines a lot but also don’t get proficiency in Constitution saves.\n\t\tIf you want Darkvision, cast Darkvision. If you want the damage resistance,\n\t\tAbsorb Elements or Protection From Energy work, but they may be more costly.\n\t\tRemember that you can change the benefit when you cast a leveled transmutation\n\t\tspell, so don’t be afraid to change it when the situation calls for it.<\/li>\n\t
                                4. Shapechanger<\/span>: Helpful for scouting and\n\t\tutility purposes, but it won’t get you through a fight, and you can use a\n\t\tRogue or a Divination spell for scouting. You can use it for overland travel\n\t\tby turning into something that flies, but stick to Giant Eagle, Giant Owl,\n\t\tor Giant Vulture because most beasts have an Intelligence score low enough\n\t\tthat you’re likely to forget where you were going.<\/li>\n\t
                                5. Master Transmuter<\/span>: The Major\n\t\tTransformation option can do curious things like making adamantine weapons,\n\t\tbut can’t create adamantine armor because adamantine armor is a magic item\n\t\teven though adamantine weapons aren’t.\n\t\t

                                  The Panacea and Restore Life options are very good, and giving up your\n\t\t\tTransmuter’s Stone to get the benefits is worth the cost since you can\n\t\t\tcreate another one at no cost except time. Wizards don’t have many healing\n\t\t\toptions and can’t raise creatures from the dead until you can cast Wish,\n\t\t\tso these are useful options, especially in a party without a cleric.<\/p>\n\t\t

                                  Restore Youth is amusing but it’s basically a party trick.<\/p><\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n\n

                                  War Magic<\/a> (XGtE<\/a>)<\/sup><\/h3>\n\n\n\n
                                  <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

                                  Despite the flavor text, War Magic is primarily defensive. Power Surge (and\n\targuably Deflecting Shroud) are the only truly offensive abilities, and the\n\tdamage provided is fairly small. War Magic has a lot of overlap with School of\n\tAbjuration, but where School of Abjuration focuses more on countering spells\n\tand defending your allies, War Magic focuses more on defending yourself and\n\tkeeping yourself in a fight.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                  War Magic’s focus on your own durability helps solve the Wizard’s biggest\n\tproblem: their frailty. Wizards get no armor except Mage Armor and have just\n\td6 hit dice, making them the least durable class in the game. They are very\n\tmuch “glass cannons”, and War Magic does a bit to improve on that pain point.\n\tThis is appealing if you’re planning to play a wizard in tight quarters like\n\tdungeons or caves where running and hiding may be difficult, or if you’re\n\tbrave and want to wade into close quarters and rely on short-range spells like\n\tBurning Hands.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                  If you want to play an extra durably wizard, consider adding other mechanisms\n\tto boost your AC and ideally your Constitution saves. Starting with a level in\n\tArtificer accomplishes both, but you could also choose Githyanki, Mountain\n\tDwarf, or Tortle as your race to get better AC, or you could go for Custom\n\tLineage or Variant Human in order to take Resilient or War Caster.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                  War Magic Wizard Handbook<\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n\n

                                  <\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
                                    \n\t
                                  1. Arcane Deflection<\/span>: Shield provides a\n\t\tbetter AC bonus, but the real appeal here is the bonus to saves. +4 is\n\t\tenough to make a huge difference, and features that provide similar bonuses\n\t\t(The Paladin’s Aura of Protection, the Artificer’s Flash of Genius, etc.)\n\t\tare never<\/em> available this early in your career.\n\t\t

                                    However, Arcane Deflection comes with an opportunity cost beyond your\n\t\t\tReaction: You can’t cast leveled spells during your next turn. Consider\n\t\t\tthat casting leveled spells is your primary function as a wizard, that’s a\n\t\t\tlot to give up. When you use Arcane Deflection, you need to be ready to\n\t\t\tspend your next turn using cantrips or something. At low levels where your\n\t\t\tspell slots are few in number that’s not a problem, but as you gain levels\n\t\t\tthe opportunity cost grows considerably. You can’t even use items like\n\t\t\twands to get around this because those items still cause you to cast a\n\t\t\tspell.<\/p>\n\t\t

                                    There may still be times where Absorb Elements or Shield make more sense\n\t\t\tso that you can cast leveled spells on your turn, and fortunately as the\n\t\t\topportunity cost to use Arcane Deflection increases, the opportunity cost\n\t\t\tto prepare Absorb Elements and Shield is reduced as your number of\n\t\t\tprepared spells increases.<\/p><\/li>\n\t

                                  2. Tactical Wit<\/span>: Initiative bonuses are\n\t\talways great, and getting to go early means that you can quickly cast buff\n\t\tor control spells which will determine how the fight plays out.<\/li>\n\t
                                  3. Power Surge<\/span>: The damage isn’t great, and\n\t\tunless you’re facing a lot of enemy spellcasters it will be hard to charge\n\t\tyour power surges. You could ask your allies to waste low-level spells\n\t\t(ideally a cantrip with a non-instantaneous duraiton like Guidance or\n\t\tShillelagh) so that you can dispel or counterspell them, but it’s probably\n\t\tnot worth your spell slots do this in order to recharge your Power Surge\n\t\tpool.<\/li>\n\t
                                  4. Durable Magic<\/span>: If you’re not maintaining\n\t\tConcentration on a spell, you should be casting a spell that requires\n\t\tConcentration. There are too many good Concentration spells for you to not\n\t\thave one running. The bonus to saves notably applies to your save to\n\t\tmaintain Concentration, and combined with Arcane Deflection you’re very\n\t\teffective at maintaining Concentration.\n\t\t

                                    If you’re worried about the cost to constantly cast and maintain\n\t\t\tConcentration, look at summoning spells. They typically have a 1-hour\n\t\t\tduration and many are very effective, so it’s easy to fit them into your\n\t\t\ttactics. Another useful option is running Enhance Ability (Dexterity) so that when combat starts you or another party member, or both if upcast, have Advantage on the Initiative check.<\/p>\n<\/li>\n\t

                                  5. Deflecting Shroud<\/span>: You only get to use\n\t\tArcane Deflection if you’re hit by an attack or if you fail a saving throw,\n\t\tbut Deflecting Shroud works even if Arcane Deflection doesn’t negate the\n\t\teffect which triggered it, so you’re always guaranteed the damage.\n\t\t

                                    The damage from Deflecting Shroud isn’t great, but there’s no usage\n\t\t\tlimitation and since it hits three targets at range it can easily\n\t\t\tout-damage cantrips. If you’re facing groups of weak enemies they might\n\t\t\thit you sheerly by making a large number of attacks or by including you in\n\t\t\tan area damage effect, and this will allow you to thin their numbers\n\t\t\twithout spending expensive spell slots.<\/p>\n\t\t

                                    The damage also helps justify using Arcane Deflection at high levels by\n\t\t\ttaking some of the sting out of giving up leveled spells for a turn. By\n\t\t\tthis level your pool of spell slots is enormous, so limiting yourself to\n\t\t\tcantrips in many circumstances means that your turn is reduced to a tiny\n\t\t\tsplash of damage where you could instead throw a leveled spell like\n\t\t\tFireball or Chain Lightning.<\/p>\n\t\t

                                    If you want to trigger this easily, there’s an ally can make an unarmed\n\t\t\tstrike to hit you for 1+Strength damage to set this off, allowing you to\n\t\t\tget a bunch of extra damage before a turn where you may have already\n\t\t\tplanned to cast a cantrip. If you don’t have an ally handy, keep an angry\n\t\t\trat in a sack, stick your hand into the sack, and wait for the rat to bite\n\t\t\tyou.<\/p>\n\t<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n\n
