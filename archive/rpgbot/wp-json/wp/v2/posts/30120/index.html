Looking Back at 2024<\/h2>\n\n\n

The 2024 release schedule was a little odd. With the impending release of the 2024 rules and the 50th anniversary of Dungeons and Dragons, the last few pieces of 2014 content were part send-off and part celebration of DnD\u2019s storied history.<\/p>\n\n\n

Vecna: Eve of Ruin – May, 2024<\/h3>\n\n\n

The first high-level adventure published for 5e, Vecna: Eve of Ruin takes players on a plane-hopping adventure to foil a plot by Vecna, evil god of secrets, to remake the universe in his image. We reviewed it on the RPGBOT.Podcast<\/a> and liked it a lot. Facing a villain dating back to DnD\u2019s early days and meeting some major characters along the way was a fun romp.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

[Spoilers] The reception to the adventure was middling. Many players felt that Vecna\u2019s extremely late appearance in the module was unsatisfying, and Vecna\u2019s stat block wasn\u2019t suitably challenging for a party of level 20 adventurers. I still think it\u2019s an exciting boss fight if the GM uses the battle map appropriately, but I haven\u2019t gotten to test it yet.<\/p>\n\n\n

Quests from the Infinite Staircase – July 2024<\/h3>\n\n\n

A 5e update for 7 classic adventures dating back to DnD\u2019s earliest days, this was another adventure anthology in the vein of Tales from the Yawning Portal. Adventures are nominally linked together by travel through the Infinite Staircase, but they\u2019re functionally independent.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Among these updated adventures is Expedition to the Barrier Peaks, the first official DnD adventure to introduce science fiction elements like laser pistols and robots to DnD. We reviewed it on the RPGBOT.Podcast<\/a> and had a lot of fun with it.<\/p>\n\n\n

2024 DnD Player\u2019s Handbook – September, 2024<\/h3>\n\n\n

After over a year of OneDnD playtest documents, people were nervous about the new Player\u2019s Handbook. The DnD design team took some big swings during the playtest, and responses were mixed. The final version made a lot of exciting improvements, and folks are mostly happy<\/a> with the changes.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

There are certainly some rough edges, but there have also been some great improvements. If nothing else, Weapon Mastery is really fun, and the Monk is really good now.<\/p>\n\n\n

2024 Dungeon Master\u2019s Guide – November, 2024<\/h3>\n\n\n

The 2014 Dungeon Master\u2019s guide had a lot of issues, and most players rarely saw a need to open it for anything other than magic items. The 2024 DMG attempts to make it more of a go-to resource, and it certainly made some improvements in that respect. Initial responses have been largely positive, and the newly-added sections are excellent additions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But the new Dungeon Master\u2019s Guide is nowhere near perfect<\/a>. Most of the book is disorganized, a huge amount of content from the 2014 DMG was cut, and some frequently repeated promises were not delivered upon. Most notably, magic items prices are still nonsense, and the promised update to the monster creation rules is now just \u201creskin it, change a couple traits, and give it a new name\u201d rather than an actual robust solution for designing new monsters.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

As of this writing, the 2024 Dungeon Master\u2019s Guide is also suffering significant delays with international shipping. Players in our Discord community outside of the US have reported that their local game stores had their promised supply of books repeatedly delayed, then had the amount drastically reduced to the point that stores couldn\u2019t fulfill preorders.<\/p>\n\n\n
