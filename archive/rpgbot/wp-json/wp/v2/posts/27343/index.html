Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

We\u2019re taking a look at building single-class parties. Building a party around a single class presents unique challenges often due individual classes\u2019 limited capabilities. DnD is fundamentally a game about a party of diverse characters pooling their abilities to become more than the sum of their parts. The single-class party flips that on its head, introducing fun new challenges.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Artificer is an extremely versatile class capable of filling many roles in the party. While it doesn\u2019t have the incredible spellcasting of the Wizard, its combination of spellcasting and durability makes it much more survivable.<\/p>\n\n\n

The Rules<\/h2>\n\n\n
    \n
  • No multiclassing<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 4 party members<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Must attempt to cover all party roles<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

    Strengths and Weaknesses the All-Artificer Party<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Artificers have great Control options like Web, Support options like Aid and Faerie Fire, and even a few Blaster options like Tasha\u2019s Caustic Brew and Shatter. Since the whole party is Intelligence-based, we can distribute the Scholar role among the party. Artificers are ritual casters and have a ton of utility spells and tool proficiencies, so we can easily manage any Utility Caster tasks with whatever the party has on hand.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Perhaps the Artificer\u2019s biggest shortcoming is the lack of subclass options, limiting our ability to build a diverse party. There\u2019s also not one person in the party predisposed to Charisma, so a Face is a struggle.<\/p>\n\n\n

    The Party<\/h2>\n\n

    Member 1: Alchemist Artificer<\/h3>\n\n\n

    We need a healer, and the Alchemist is the only version of the Artificer which gets access to Healing Word. Experimental Elixir also provides some healing and some buffs. Between the two we can firmly claim the Healer and Support roles. We can also pick up Chef\u2019s Tools for the bonus to healing when we take a Short Rest, and we might add even more healing and support with feats like Inspiring Leader and\/or Chef.<\/p>\n\n\n\n


    Alchemist doesn\u2019t get a lot of offensive power, but between Alchemical Savant and access to a Homunculus Familiar, we\u2019re not exactly suffering. We\u2019ll mostly use our spellcasting for area control and support, relying on spells like Faerie Fire and Web.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 2: Armorer Artificer<\/h3>\n\n\n

    The Armorer can be either a Defender or a Scout depending on your choice of Armor Model. In this case we\u2019ll go for Infiltrator<\/a> Armor so that we can fill the Scout role within the party. We\u2019ll grab a Homunculus Familiar to supplement our damage, and we can reasonably call ourselves a Striker. We can use Enhanced Weapon on our lightning launcher and we might even take Sharpshooter to further boost our damage output, making us a decent Striker.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    We\u2019ll use most of our spells for utility and support purposes rather than using them offensively. This lets us focus on Dexterity over Intelligence in order to support our skills. Lightning launcher works with either Dex or Int, so we\u2019re not hurting ourselves much by doing so. Our familiar\u2019s attack is Int-based, unfortunately, but we won\u2019t worry about it too much since we have another Striker in the party.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 3: Artillerist Artificer<\/h3>\n\n\n

    Our party\u2019s Blaster and Striker, the Artillerist\u2019s job is to blow stuff up. Nice and simple. We don\u2019t have a lot of difficult roles to handle or complexity to deal with, so we\u2019ll suck it up and play the party\u2019s Face, too. A splash of Charisma and a few skills won\u2019t kill us, especially if we pick a race like the Half-Elf or the Kenku which can get us extra skills.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    In combat we just need to shoot stuff. Our Force Ballista does good single-target damage, and with Arcane Firearm we can hit crowds with spells like Shatter and Fireball or we can pick of single enemies with Scorching Ray and with cantrips.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 4: Battlesmith Artificer<\/h3>\n\n\n

    Without a secondary melee character, the Battlesmith is a great choice because our Iron Defender can serve as a second character on the front line. We aren\u2019t quite as sticky as an Armorer in Defender Armor, but we can use Booming Blade to solve that problem. Our spellcasting and our Iron Defender also gives us respectable damage output, so we\u2019re not just on the front lines drawing punches.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    As we gain levels we also pick up Arcane Jolt, which can work a bit like Healing Word, allowing us to save dying allies. Having two options here is really helpful in case our Alchemist is knocked out.<\/p>\n\n\n

    The Report Card<\/h2>\n\n\n

    The Face role is our biggest shortcoming, but we can cast spells like Guidance and Enhance Ability, allowing us to compensate for our party\u2019s lack of Charisma. Everything else is well covered, often by multiple party members.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
