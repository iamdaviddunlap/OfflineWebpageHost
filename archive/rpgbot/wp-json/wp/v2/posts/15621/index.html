              Gifts for Newer Players<\/h2>\n\n\n

              Newer players are generally the easiest to shop for. TTRPGs have loads of fun accessories, but veteran players often forget that they’ve spent years accumulating books, dice, and nick-nacks. Newer players typically start small, so adding to their collection is an easy source of gift ideas.<\/p>\n\n\n

              Rulebooks, etc.<\/h3>\n\n

              Dragons of Swormwreck Isle Starter Set<\/h4>\n\n\n
              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
              \n

              The most recent entry in 5e’s starter options (there are now three in total), Dragons of Stormwreck isle is updated to be even more accessible for newcomers to the hobby.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n

              D&D Core Rulebooks and Expansions<\/h4>\n\n\n
              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
              \n

              The core rules are essential for any player. Make sure that your recipient has the core rulebooks, then consider the rules expansion gift set.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Even with One D&D looming, many players won’t immediately move to the new system. Players can expect to get several years of enjoyment out of these before One D&D becomes a thing, and even then much of the content will still be useful.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n

              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
              \n

              The Beginner Box is an excellent introduction the Pathfinder 2nd edition. We even have a Beginner Box Guide<\/a> that will help you get up and running!<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n

              Pathfinder 2nd Edition Core Rulebooks<\/h4>\n\n\n

              Is your recipient ready to go beyond the PF2 Beginner Box? The core rulebooks are the logical choice. If you’re only going to get one of them, I recommend the Core Rulebook, followed by the Bestiary. While the Gamemastery Guide is a truly excellent source book, you can run PF2 with just the Core rulebook, especially if you also get them a published adventure (see below under “Gifts for Dungeon Masters and Game Masters).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Gifts for Long-Time Players<\/h2>\n\n\n

              Players who have been in the hobby for a while can often be difficult to shop for, but there are some really great options that might be “off the beaten path”.<\/p>\n\n\n

              3rd-Party DnD Supplements<\/h3>\n\n\n

              Even long-time players are often nervous to explore 3rd-party content, which is unfortunate because a lot of it is very good.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              Tome of Heroes<\/h4>\n\n\n

              A setting-agnostic compilation of primarily player options, Tome of Heroes<\/a> contains a huge number of new races, subclasses, and feats, as well as exciting new rules for gunpowder<\/a> which involve exploding dice.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              Tome of Beasts 3<\/h4>\n\n\n

              The next hotly-anticipated entry in Kobold Press’s widely popular books of monsters, Tome of Beasts 3 releases soon and everyone here at RPGBOT is excited to get our hands on it.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n

              Board Games<\/h3>\n\n\n

              We don’t talk much about board games on RPGBOT, but we do play a lot of them, and our favorite TTRPG companies frequently make them. Roleplayers also frequently love boards games, and they’re a great way to include people who don’t play TTRPGs or to fill a game night when you can’t get the full party together.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              Betrayal at Baldur’s Gate<\/h4>\n\n\n

              In the same vein as Betrayal at House on the Hill<\/a>, Betrayal at Baldur’s Gate uses similar mechanics with D&D trappings. We’ve played it, and it holds up very well.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              Pathfinder: Level 20<\/h4>\n\n\n

              You are kobolds being attacked by a fighter 5 xp short of level 20. Your goal is to not be the kobold which the fighter kills.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              D&D: Lords of Waterdeep<\/h4>\n\n\n

              A highly-regarded worker placement game, Lords of Waterdeep is a lot of fun and easy to pick up. I’ve played it with non-gamers and everyone has enjoyed it so far.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              Starfinder: Pirates of Skydock<\/h4>\n\n\n

              Want to be a pirate? You’re going to need a ship. Want a good ship? You’re going to have to steal it.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n

              Welcome to Dragon Talk<\/h4>\n\n\n

              Miniatures<\/h3>\n\n\n

              Miniatures are a fantastic gift, and much like dice, people who enjoy them frequently have a bottomless appetite for them, which makes miniatures a perfect gift.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              For minis, you have two great go-to options: pre-made minis and custom minis. If your recipient paints minis or wants to learn, you can also throw in some painting supplies to get them going.<\/p>\n\n\n

              Pre-Made Miniatures<\/h4>\n\n\n

              My go-to option is Reaper’s “Bones”<\/a> line. Inexpensive plastic miniatures, they come in a massive variety of designs and you can’t paint them without priming them (though better painters may recommend otherwise).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Miniature Painting Tools<\/h4>\n\n\n

              If your giftee is an experienced painter, they’re likely very opinionated about their tools, so generally avoid buying them stuff that they haven’t already told you that they want.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              If they’re new to painting or if you’re trying to get them started, a self-contained kit is a great choice.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n

              Their New Favorite RPG<\/h3>\n\n\n

              Most people know Dungeons and Dragons at this point. If you get past that surface layer of TTRPGs, you might know about Pathfinder. But there’s an enormous world of amazing games to play (or at least to steal ideas from for your DnD\/Pathfinder games). <\/p>\n\n\n\n

              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              Alien RPG<\/h4>\n\n\n

              An absolutely fantastic TTRPG adaptation of Alien, the Alien RPG<\/a> brings all the challenges of space travel as well as Alien’s iconic brand of horror.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              Strongly consider grabbing one or more of the cinematic scenarios<\/a>. They’re excellent and present an extremely novel RPG experience.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              M\u00d6RK BORG<\/h4>\n\n\n

              The result of mixing a black metal album with tabletop RPGs, M\u00f6rk Borg<\/a> is dark, brutal, and lethal, in a way that’s novel and extremely entertaining.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              \n
              \n<\/a>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n

              Call of Cthulhu<\/h4>\n\n\n

              Spooky season may be over, but that doesn’t mean that it’s time to stop enjoying spooky things. Call of Cthulhu, the iconic cosmic horror RPG, is a great choice for players who enjoy intrigue, mystery, and horror.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              The new Starter Set<\/a> is an excellent entry point for such a beloved and long-running RPG.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n
