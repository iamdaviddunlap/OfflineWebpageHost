The “Totem Pole Trench<\/a>” is a long-standing trope of cartoons and similarly goofy fiction, often portraying 2-3 children stacked atop one another to disguise themselves as an adult in order to gain access to places where might not otherwise be allowed due to their age. This trope has made its way into tabletop RPGs, but typically features small races like goblins, halflings, or kobolds.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This trope got some extra attention around the release of Icewind Dale: Rime of the Frostmaiden, resulting in WotC’s selling a Three Kobolds in a Trenchcoat Shirt<\/a> to raise money for Extra Life (sadly no longer available), Idle Champions of the Forgotten Realms posting a one-panel comic about the joke<\/a>, and a piece of official promotional art<\/a> for Rime of the Frostmaiden. The adventure even includes a stat block<\/a> with rules for three kobolds passing themselves off as a human!<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But after that, the world sort of let the joke go. Every once in a while it pops back into my head unbidden, though, and recently I tweeted this nonsense:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Character I want to play:

3 kobolds in a trenchcoat, but I’m only 1 kobold on a journey to find kobolds to fill out the trenchcoat. My stats improve as I add kobolds; stats differ by kobold like magic items.

The treasure is literally the friends I make along the way.<\/p>\u2014 RPGBOT.NET (@RPGBOTDOTNET) July 18, 2021<\/a><\/blockquote>
