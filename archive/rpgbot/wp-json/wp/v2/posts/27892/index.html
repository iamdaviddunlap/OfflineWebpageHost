Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

We\u2019re taking a look at building single-class parties. Building a party around a single class presents unique challenges often due individual classes\u2019 limited capabilities. DnD is fundamentally a game about a party of diverse characters pooling their abilities to become more than the sum of their parts. The single-class party flips that on its head, introducing fun new challenges.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

With lots of skills and stealth to spare, an all-Rogue party can be a fun way to explore concepts like a thieves\u2019 guild, an assassin\u2019s guild, a group of bandits, or a merry band of noble, swashbuckling rebels.<\/p>\n\n\n

The Rules<\/h2>\n\n\n
    \n
  • No multiclassing<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 4 party members<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Must attempt to cover all party roles<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

    Strengths and Weaknesses of the All-Rogue Party<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Rogues have more skills than any other class, they\u2019re surprisingly durable thanks to their high Dexterity saves and Uncanny Dodge, and Cunning Action means that they\u2019re often hard to target and can readily gain Advantage on attacks. With Expertise, relatively low ability scores don\u2019t significantly inhibit skill-based roles. Aside from a near total lack of ability to do magic, the Rogue is a great class for a single-class party.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    However, Rogues have extremely fixed damage progression. A Rogue with no subclass features or feats will sit consistently in the middle of the Target DPR range, never quite reaching High DPR. We can boost the Rogue\u2019s DPR with feats, subclass features, and Opportunity Attacks, but it\u2019s harder to force Opportunity Attacks or attack outside of our turn without non-Rogue allies. Despite their conceptual place as a Striker, the Rogue\u2019s actual DPR isn\u2019t especially impressive without some work.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Relying on Cunning Action can also make combat work in very strange ways. If the party leans into hiding with Cunning Action, you\u2019re essentially playing Russian Roulette. The first person to roll poorly on Stealth is going to get dogpiled by every monster in sight, likely resulting in their immediate death. Once Reliant Talent comes online, this issues goes away and the party becomes a group of undetectable snipers, turning D&D into a weird, turn-based sniper simulator.<\/p>\n\n\n

    The Party<\/h2>\n\n

    Member 1: Fairy Arcane Trickster Rogue<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

    Our only caster, the Arcane Trickster has to carry a lot of responsibility. We\u2019re the only one with any hope to serve as a Blaster, a Controller, or a Utility Caster, so we\u2019ll cover those roles to the best of our ability with our limited number of spells known. Fairy\u2019s ability to cast both Faerie Fire and Enlarge\/Reduce gives us some great Support options, too.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    In combat, using spells offensively will still be rare, but we might use options like Hypnotic pattern to thin herds, especially once we get Magical Ambush.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Since they need Intelligence for our spells, our Arcane Trickster is a natural Scholar. Use Expertise to close the gap between our 14-ish Intelligence and a smarter character.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 2: Phantom Rogue<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

    Wails from the Grave can boost our damage output by almost half, which is huge in a party that has few ways to boost their damage output. Hitting is crucial, so either use Cunning Action to hide or use Two-Weapon Fighting so that you get a second attack.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    With the easiest roles in the party, our Phantom Rogue gets saddled with the hardest single role for the Rogue: Healer. We can do this with the Gift of the Gem Dragon feat, the Healer feat, and the Aasimar race. Healer provides the most healing, so we\u2019ll make that our go-to choice. We still can\u2019t do a thing about status conditions, unfortunately.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 3: Scout Rogue<\/h3>\n\n\n

    In a party of characters who can fill the Scout role, our Scout Rogue is our best Scout character. (Look, I named the role before we had a Scout subclass). We\u2019ll have them focus on Stealth, Perception, and Investigation. We get proficiency in Nature for free, but that doesn\u2019t make us a Scholar.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    In combat, we\u2019re all about hit-and-run tactics and sniping. We want to be highly mobile, draw melee enemies away from allies, and move to target vulnerable enemies. If we\u2019re feeling brave, we can combine Cunning Action with Elven Accuracy and Sharpshooter to boost our damage output.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 4: Swashbuckler Rogue<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

    Built to face enemies in single combat, our swashbuckler is our best option for a Defender. Building our swashbuckler as something durable like a hill dwarf will help them stay on the front lines for extended periods as the rest of the party darts in and out of combat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    The Swashbuckler\u2019s dependence on Charisma also makes them an ideal Face, so we\u2019ll focus their skills on Charisma-based skills and Insight.<\/p>\n\n\n

    The Report Card<\/h2>\n\n\n

    We just barely managed to cover every role, but it\u2019s definitely a stretch for some of them.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
