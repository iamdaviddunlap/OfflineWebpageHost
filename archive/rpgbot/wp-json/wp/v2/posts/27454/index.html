Introduction<\/h2>\n\n\n

We\u2019re taking a look at building single-class parties. Building a party around a single class presents unique challenges often due individual classes\u2019 limited capabilities. DnD is fundamentally a game about a party of diverse characters pooling their abilities to become more than the sum of their parts. The single-class party flips that on its head, introducing fun new challenges.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

So weird that they get their own spellcasting mechanic, the Warlock handles the single-class party situation a bit differently from other classes. While everyone in the party will have some of the staple options like Eldritch Blast, we otherwise have a ton of room to customize in order to suit the needs of our role within the party.<\/p>\n\n\n

The Rules<\/h2>\n\n\n
    \n
  • No multiclassing<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 4 party members<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Must attempt to cover all party roles<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n

    Strengths and Weaknesses of the All-Warlock Party<\/h2>\n\n\n

    Warlocks are highly customizable, and the diversity of Pact Boons and subclasses offers a lot of options which can cover a wide range of capabilities. Eldritch Blast builds are consistently effective Strikers, but that\u2019s certainly not the limit of the Warlock\u2019s capabilities. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Where warlocks struggle is from levels 12 to 20. Mystic Arcanum isn\u2019t as good as what full spellcasters get, and, for the most part, their other features stop advancing at level 11.<\/p>\n\n\n

    The Party<\/h2>\n\n

    Member 1: Hexblade Warlock<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

    Yes, this one was inevitable. It\u2019s the only warlock with better than light armor, so natural this is our Defender. We\u2019ll build around melee with Pact of the Blade and the associated Invocations, and we\u2019ll put feats into options like Polearm Master and Sentinel.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 2: Celestial Warlock<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

    Another inevitability since this is the only Warlock with meaningful healing, our Celestial Warlock will be our Healer. The subclass spell list also includes Wall of Fire, so we\u2019ll call this our Controller, too, since they get Wall of Fire on top of great Controller options like Hunger of Hadar. <\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 3: Genie Warlock<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

    The Efreeti\u2019s spell list offers go-to Blaster options, including Fireball. The main genie spell list also offers some Utility options on top of the utility provided by the other subclass features. We can throw in Pact of the Tome, too, to get ritual spellcasting.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    We\u2019ll also make this our Scholar. A small investment in Intelligence and the right skills is likely all that we can manage, but the combination of Pact of the Tome and Intelligence-based skills seems natural.<\/p>\n\n\n

    Member 4: Great Old One Warlock<\/a><\/h3>\n\n\n

    With telepathy and a lot of ability to cause mischief outside of combat, the Goolock seems like our best choice as a Scout. We\u2019ll need additional skills from our race or from Eldritch Invocations, but otherwise we\u2019ll do just fine.<\/p>\n\n\n
